**muddle** *n*
**1** a state of untidiness or [[confusion]]: the files are in a terrible muddle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[confusion]], [[mess]], [[disorder]], [[chaos]], [[tangle]], [[mix-up]], [[disarray]], [[predicament]]
**2** a state of mental [[confusion]] or uncertainty: the government are in a muddle over the economy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[confusion]], [[mess]], [[disorder]], [[chaos]], [[tangle]], [[mix-up]], [[disarray]], [[predicament]]
 *vb* **-dling**, **-dled**
**3** Also: **muddle up** to [[mix up]] or [[confuse]] (objects or items): you've got your books all [[muddled]] up with mine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jumble]], [[disorder]], [[scramble]], [[tangle]], [[mix up]]
**4** to make (someone) confused: don't muddle her with too many suggestions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[confuse]], [[bewilder]], [[daze]], [[confound]], [[perplex]], [[disorient]], [[stupefy]], [[befuddle]]
 **[[muddled]]** *adj*
