**present**^2 *n* (**prez**-int)
**1** a [[gift]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gift]], [[offering]], [[grant]], [[donation]], [[hand-out]], [[endowment]], [[boon]] (*archaic*), [[gratuity]], [[bonsela]] (*S African*), koha (*NZ*)
 *vb* (pri-**zent**)
**2** to [[introduce]] (a person) formally to another
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[introduce]], [[make known]], [[acquaint someone with]]
**3** to [[introduce]] to the public: the Museum of Modern Art is presenting a retrospective of his work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put on]], [[stage]], [[perform]], [[give]], [[show]], [[render]]
**4** to [[introduce]] and compere (a radio or television [[show]])
**5** to [[show]] or exhibit: they took advantage of every tax dodge that presented itself
**6** to bring about: the case presented a large number of legal difficulties
**7** to put forward or submit: they presented a petition to the Prime Minister
**8** to [[give]] or offer formally: he was presented with a watch to celebrate his twenty-five years with the company
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give]], [[award]], [[hand over]], [[grant]], [[hand out]], [[confer]], [[bestow]]
**9** to [[hand over]] for action or payment: to present a bill
**10** to portray in a particular way: his lawyer presented him as a naive young man who had got into bad company
**11** to aim (a weapon)
**12** **present arms** to salute with one's weapon
