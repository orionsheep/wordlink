**relish** *vb*
**1** to [[savour]] or [[enjoy]] (an experience) to the full
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[enjoy]], [[like]], [[savour]], [[revel in]]
**2** to anticipate eagerly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[look forward to]], [[fancy]] (*informal*), delight in
 *n*
**3** [[liking]] or [[enjoyment]]: he has an enormous relish for life
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[enjoyment]], [[liking]], [[love]], [[taste]], [[fancy]], [[penchant]], [[fondness]], [[gusto]]
**4** pleasurable anticipation: his early relish for a new challenge
**5** an appetizing or spicy food, such as a pickle, added to a main dish to improve its flavour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[condiment]], [[seasoning]], [[sauce]]
**6** a zestful quality: he tells stories with great relish
