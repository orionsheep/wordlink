**entry** *n*, *pl* **-tries**
**1** something, such as a [[door]] or [[gate]], through which it is possible to enter a place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[way in]], [[opening]], [[door]], [[approach]], [[access]], [[gate]], [[passage]], [[entrance]]
**2** the act of [[coming in]] to a place, esp. with reference to the [[way in]] which it is done
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[coming in]], [[entering]], [[appearance]], [[arrival]], [[entrance]]
**3** the right to enter a place: he was often refused entry to Boston's bars because he looked so young
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[admission]], [[access]], [[entrance]], [[admittance]], [[entrée]], [[permission to enter]], [[right of entry]]
**4** the act of joining an organization or group: Poland's entry into the EU
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[introduction]], [[presentation]], [[initiation]], [[inauguration]], [[induction]], [[debut]], [[investiture]]
**5** a brief [[note]], article, or group of figures in a diary, book, or computer file
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[record]], [[listing]], [[account]], [[note]], [[statement]], [[item]]
**6** a quiz form, painting, etc. submitted in an attempt to win a competition
**7** a person, horse, car, etc. [[entering]] a competition
 *adj*
**8** necessary in order to enter something: entry fee
