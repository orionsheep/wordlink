**scramble** *vb* **-bling**, **-bled**
**1** to [[climb]] or [[crawl]] hurriedly by using the hands to aid movement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[struggle]], [[climb]], [[crawl]], [[swarm]], [[scrabble]]
**2** to go hurriedly or in a disorderly manner
**3** to compete with others in a rough and undignified way: spectators scrambled for the best seats
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strive]], [[rush]], [[contend]], [[vie]], [[run]], [[push]], [[jostle]]
**4** to [[jumble]] together in a haphazard manner
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jumble]], [[mix up]], [[muddle]], [[shuffle]]
**5** to cook (eggs that have been whisked up with milk) in a pan
**6** (*military*) (of a crew or aircraft) to take off quickly in an emergency
**7** to make (transmitted speech) unintelligible by the use of an electronic scrambler
 *n*
**8** the act of scrambling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clamber]], [[ascent]]
**9** a [[climb]] or trek over difficult ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clamber]], [[ascent]]
**10** a rough and undignified [[struggle]] to gain possession of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[race]], [[competition]], [[struggle]], [[rush]], [[confusion]], [[commotion]], melee *or* mêlée
**11** (*military*) an immediate takeoff of crew or aircraft in an emergency
**12** (*Brit*) a motorcycle [[race]] across rough open ground
