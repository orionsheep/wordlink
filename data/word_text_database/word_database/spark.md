**spark** *n*
**1** a fiery particle thrown out from a fire or caused by friction
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flicker]], [[flash]], [[gleam]], [[glint]], [[flare]]
**2** a short [[flash]] of light followed by a sharp crackling noise, produced by a sudden electrical discharge through the air
**3** a [[trace]] or [[hint]]: a spark of goodwill
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trace]], [[hint]], [[scrap]], [[atom]], [[jot]], [[vestige]]
**4** liveliness, enthusiasm, or humour: that spark in her eye
 *vb*
**5** to give off sparks
**6** to cause to [[start]]; [[trigger]]: the incident sparked off an angry exchange
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[start]], [[stimulate]], [[provoke]], [[inspire]], [[trigger]] (off), [[set off]], [[precipitate]]
