**honour** * or * (*US*) **honor** *n*
**1** allegiance to moral principles
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[integrity]], [[morality]], [[honesty]], [[goodness]], [[fairness]], [[decency]], [[probity]] (*formal*), [[rectitude]]
**2** a person's good [[reputation]] and the [[respect]] they are given by other people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reputation]], [[standing]], [[prestige]], [[image]], [[status]], [[stature]], [[good name]], [[cachet]]
**3**
**A** [[fame]] or [[glory]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prestige]], [[credit]], [[reputation]], [[glory]], [[fame]], [[distinction]], [[dignity]], renown
 **B** a person who wins [[fame]] or [[glory]] for his or her country, [[school]], [[etc.: he was an honour to his nation]]
**4** great [[respect]] or [[esteem]], or an outward sign of this
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[acclaim]], [[praise]], [[recognition]], [[compliments]], [[homage]], [[accolades]], [[commendation]]
**5** a [[privilege]] or [[pleasure]]: it was an honour to meet him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[privilege]], [[credit]], [[pleasure]], [[compliment]]
**6** (*old-fashioned*) a woman's virginity
**7** (*bridge*, *whist*) any of the top four or five cards in a suit
**8** (*golf*) the right to tee off first
**9** **in honour of** out of [[respect]] for
**10** **on one's honour** under a moral obligation
 *vb*
**11** to hold someone in [[respect]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[respect]], [[value]], [[esteem]], [[prize]], [[appreciate]], [[adore]]
**12** to give (someone) special [[praise]], attention, or an award
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[acclaim]], [[praise]], [[decorate]], [[commemorate]], [[commend]]
**13** to [[accept]] and then [[pay]] (a cheque or bill)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pay]], [[take]], [[accept]], [[pass]], [[acknowledge]]
**14** to [[keep]] (one's promise); [[fulfil]] (a previous agreement)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fulfil]], [[keep]], [[carry out]], [[observe]], [[discharge]], [[live up to]], [[be true to]]
