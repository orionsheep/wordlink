**arise** *vb* **arising**, **arose**, **arisen**
**1** to come into being: the opportunity for action did not arise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[happen]], [[start]], [[begin]], [[follow]], [[result]], [[develop]], [[emerge]], [[occur]]
**2** to come into notice: people can seek answers to their problems as and when they arise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[happen]], [[start]], [[begin]], [[follow]], [[result]], [[develop]], [[emerge]], [[occur]]
**3** **arise from** to [[happen]] as a [[result]] of
**4** (*old-fashioned*) to get or [[stand up]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get to your feet]], [[get up]], [[rise]], [[stand up]], [[spring up]], [[leap up]]
