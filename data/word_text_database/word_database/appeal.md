**appeal** *vb*
**1** to make an earnest [[request]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plead]], [[ask]], [[request]], [[pray]], [[beg]], [[entreat]]
**2** **appeal to** to attract, please, or interest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[appeal to someone]]**: attract, interest, draw (*informal*), please, [[charm]], fascinate, tempt, lure
**3** (*law*) to apply to a higher court to review (a case or issue decided by a lower court)
**4** to resort to a higher authority to change a decision
**5** to [[call]] on in support of an earnest [[request]]: he appealed for volunteers to help in relief work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plead]], [[ask]], [[request]], [[pray]], [[beg]], [[entreat]]
**6** (*cricket*) to [[request]] the umpire to declare a batsman out
 *n*
**7** an earnest [[request]] for money or help
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plea]], [[call]], [[application]], [[request]], [[prayer]], [[petition]], [[overture]], [[entreaty]]
**8** the power to attract, please, or interest people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attraction]], [[charm]], [[fascination]], [[beauty]], [[allure]]
**9** (*law*) a [[request]] for a review of a lower court's decision by a higher court
**10** an [[application]] to a higher authority to change a decision that has been made
**11** (*cricket*) a [[request]] to the umpire to declare the batsman out
