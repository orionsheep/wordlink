**evacuate** *vb* **-ating**, **-ated**
**1** to send away from a dangerous place to a safe place: 200 people were evacuated from their homes because of the floods
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[remove]], [[clear]], [[withdraw]], [[expel]], [[move out]], [[send to a safe place]]
**2** to empty (a place) of people because it has become dangerous: the entire street was evacuated until the fire was put out
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[abandon]], [[leave]], [[clear]], [[desert]], [[quit]], [[withdraw from]], [[pull out of]], [[move out of]]
**3** (*physiol*) to discharge waste from the body
 **[[evacuation]]** *n* 
 **[[evacuee]]** *n*
