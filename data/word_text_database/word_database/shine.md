**shine** *vb* **shining**, **shone**
**1** to give off or reflect light
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gleam]], [[flash]], [[beam]], [[glow]], [[sparkle]], [[glitter]], [[glare]], [[radiate]]
**2** to direct the light of (a lamp or torch): I shone a torch at the ceiling
**3** *past tense* * & **past participle* **shined** to make clean and bright by polishing: they earned money by shining shoes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[polish]], [[buff]], [[burnish]], [[brush]]
**4** to be very good at something: she shone in most subjects; she shone at school
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be outstanding]], [[stand out]], [[excel]], [[be conspicuous]]
**5** to appear very bright and clear: her hair shone like gold
 *n*
**6** brightness or [[lustre]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[polish]], [[gloss]], [[sheen]], [[lustre]]
**7** **take a shine to someone** (*informal*) to take a liking to someone
