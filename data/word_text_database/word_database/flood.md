**flood** *n*
**1** an overflowing of water on an area that is normally dry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deluge]], [[downpour]], [[inundation]], [[tide]], [[overflow]], [[torrent]], [[spate]]
**2** a large amount of water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[torrent]], [[flow]], [[rush]], [[stream]], [[tide]], [[abundance]], [[glut]], [[profusion]]
**3** the rising of the [[tide]] from low to high water. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**diluvial**, **diluvian**
**4** a large amount: a flood of letters
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[series]], [[stream]], [[avalanche]], [[barrage]], [[spate]], [[torrent]]
**5** (*theatre*) short for **floodlight**
 *vb*
**6** to cover or become covered with water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[immerse]], [[swamp]], [[submerge]], [[inundate]], [[drown]], [[cover with water]]
**7** to [[fill]] to overflowing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pour over]], [[swamp]], [[run over]], [[overflow]], [[inundate]]
**8** to put a large number of goods on sale on (a market) at the same time, often at a cheap price: the US was flooded with cheap televisions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[saturate]], [[fill]], [[choke]], [[swamp]], [[glut]], [[oversupply]], [[overfill]]
**9** to [[flow]] or [[surge]]: the memories flooded back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stream]], [[flow]], [[rush]], [[pour]], [[surge]]
**10** to supply excess petrol to (a petrol engine) so that it cannot work properly
**11** to bleed profusely from the womb
 **[[flooding]]** *n*
