**pinch** *vb*
**1** to [[squeeze]] (something, esp. flesh) between a finger and thumb
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nip]], [[press]], [[squeeze]], [[grasp]], [[compress]]
**2** to [[squeeze]] by being too tight: shoes that pinch
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hurt]], [[crush]], [[squeeze]], [[pain]], [[cramp]]
**3** to cause stinging [[pain]] to: the cold pinched his face
**4** to make thin or drawn-looking, such as from grief or cold
**5** (*informal*) to [[steal]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[steal]], [[lift]] (*informal*), nick (*slang*, *chiefly* *Brit*), [[swipe]] (*slang*), [[knock off]] (*slang*), [[pilfer]], [[purloin]], [[filch]]
**6** (*informal*) to arrest
**7** (usually foll. by *out* or *back*) to remove the tips of (a plant shoot) to correct or encourage growth
 *n*
**8** a [[squeeze]] or sustained [[nip]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nip]], [[squeeze]]
**9** the quantity that can be taken up between a thumb and finger: a pinch of ground ginger
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dash]], [[bit]], [[mite]], [[jot]], [[speck]], [[soupçon]] (*French*)
**10** extreme stress or need: most companies are feeling the pinch of recession
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[emergency]], [[crisis]], [[difficulty]], [[plight]], [[scrape]] (*informal*), [[strait]], [[uphill]] (*S African*), [[predicament]]
**11** **at a pinch** if absolutely necessary
**12** **feel the pinch** to be forced to economize
