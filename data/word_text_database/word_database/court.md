**court** *n*
**1** (*law*)
**A** a judicial body that hears and makes decisions on legal cases
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[law court]], [[bar]], [[bench]], [[tribunal]]
 **B** the room or building in which such a body meets
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[law court]], [[bar]], [[bench]], [[tribunal]]
**2** a marked area used for playing a racket game
**3** an area of ground wholly or partly surrounded by walls or buildings
**4** a name given to some short streets, blocks of flats, or large country houses as a part of their address: Carlton Court
**5** the residence or retinue of a sovereign
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[palace]], [[hall]], [[castle]], [[manor]]
**6** any formal assembly held by a sovereign
**7** **go to court** to take legal action
**8** **hold court** to preside over a group of admirers
**9** **out of court** without a trial or legal case
**10** **pay court to** to give flattering attention to
 *vb*
**11** to attempt to gain the love of
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[woo]], go (out) with, [[date]], [[take out]], [[run after]], [[walk out with]], [[set your cap at]], [[step out with]] (*informal*)
**12** to pay attention to (someone) in order to gain favour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cultivate]], [[seek]], [[flatter]], [[solicit]], [[pander to]], [[curry favour with]], [[fawn upon]]
**13** to try to obtain (something): he has not courted controversy, but he has certainly attracted it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[invite]], [[seek]], [[attract]], [[prompt]], [[provoke]], [[bring about]], [[incite]]
**14** to make oneself open or vulnerable to: courting disaster
