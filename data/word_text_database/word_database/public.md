**public** *adj*
**1** relating to the [[people]] as a whole
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[general]], [[popular]], [[national]], [[shared]], [[common]], [[widespread]], [[universal]], [[collective]]
**2** provided by the [[government]]: public service
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[civic]], [[government]], [[state]], [[national]], [[local]], [[official]], [[community]], [[social]]
**3** [[open]] to all: public gardens
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[open]], [[accessible]], [[communal]], [[unrestricted]]
**4** [[well-known]]: a public figure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[well-known]], [[leading]], [[important]], [[respected]], [[famous]], [[celebrated]], [[recognized]], [[distinguished]]
**5** performed or made openly: a public proclamation
**6** maintained by and for the [[community]]: a public library
**7** [[open]], [[acknowledged]], or notorious: a public scandal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[known]], [[open]], [[obvious]], [[acknowledged]], [[plain]], [[patent]], [[overt]]
**8** **go public**
**A** (of a private company) to offer shares for sale to the public: few German firms have gone public in recent years
 **B** to make information, plans, etc., [[known]]: the group would not have gone public with its suspicions unless it was fully convinced of them
 *n*
**9** the [[community]] or [[people]] in [[general]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[people]], [[society]], [[community]], [[nation]], [[everyone]], [[citizens]], [[electorate]], [[populace]]
**10** a particular section of the [[community]]: the racing public
 **[[publicly]]** *adv*
