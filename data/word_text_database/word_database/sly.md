**sly** *adj* **slyer**, **slyest*** or ***slier**, **sliest**
**1** (of a person's remarks or gestures) indicating that he or she knows something of which other people may be unaware: she had the feeling they were poking sly fun at her
**2** secretive and skilled at deception: a sly trickster
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cunning]], [[scheming]], [[devious]], [[secret]], [[clever]], [[subtle]], [[wily]], [[crafty]]
**3** [[roguish]]: sly comedy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[roguish]], [[knowing]], [[arch]], [[mischievous]], impish
 *n*
**4** **[[on the sly]]** secretively: they were smoking [[on the sly]] behind the shed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[on the sly]]**: secretly, privately, covertly, surreptitiously, on the quiet
 **[[slyly]]** *adv*
