**call** *vb*
**1** to [[name]]: a town called Eyemouth
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[name]], [[entitle]], [[dub]], [[designate]], [[term]], [[style]], [[label]], [[describe as]]
**2** to describe (someone or something) as being: they called him a Hitler
**3** to speak loudly so as to attract attention
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cry]], [[shout]], [[scream]], [[yell]], [[whoop]]
**4** to [[telephone]]: he left a message for Lynch to call him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[phone]], [[telephone]], [[ring]] (up) (*informal*, *chiefly* *Brit*), [[Skype]] (*trademark*), [[video call]]
**5** to summon: a doctor must be called immediately
**6** to pay someone a [[visit]]: the social worker called and she didn't answer the door
**7** to arrange: the meeting was called for the lunch hour
**8** **call someone's bluff** See **bluff**
 *n*
**9** a [[cry]] or [[shout]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cry]], [[shout]], [[scream]], [[yell]], [[whoop]]
**10** the [[cry]] made by a bird or animal
**11** a communication by [[telephone]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[telephone call]], [[bell]] (*informal*), [[phone call]], [[buzz]] (*informal*), [[ring]] (*informal*), [[video call]], [[Skype]] (*trademark*)
**12** a short [[visit]]: I paid a call on an old friend
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[visit]]
**13** a summons or [[invitation]]: the police and fire brigade continued to respond to calls
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[request]], [[order]], [[demand]], [[appeal]], [[notice]], [[command]], [[invitation]], [[plea]]
**14** [[need]], [[demand]], or desire: a call for economic sanctions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[need]], [[cause]], [[reason]], [[grounds]], [[occasion]], [[excuse]], [[justification]]
**15** [[allure]] or fascination: the call of the open road
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attraction]], [[pull]] (*informal*), [[appeal]], [[lure]], [[allure]], [[magnetism]]
**16** **on call** available when summoned: there's a doctor on call in town
 See also **call for**
