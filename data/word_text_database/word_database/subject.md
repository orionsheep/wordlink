**subject** (**sub**-ject) *n*
**1** the person, thing, or [[topic]] being dealt with or discussed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[topic]], [[question]], [[issue]], [[matter]], [[point]], [[business]], [[affair]], [[object]]
**2** any branch of learning considered as a course of study
**3** a person, [[object]], idea, or scene portrayed in a work of art
**4** (*grammar*) a word or phrase that represents the person or thing performing the action of the verb in a sentence; for example, *the cat* in the sentence *The cat catches mice*
**5** a person or thing that undergoes an experiment or treatment
**6** a person under the rule of a monarch or government: Zambian subjects
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[citizen]], [[resident]], [[native]], [[inhabitant]], national
 *adj*
**7** being under the rule of a monarch or government: a subject race
**8** **[[subject to]]**
**A** showing a tendency towards: they are expensive and [[subject to]] overruns in cost and time
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[subject to]]**: liable to, open to, exposed to, vulnerable to, prone to, susceptible to
 **B** exposed or vulnerable to: [[subject to]] ridicule
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[subject to]]**: liable to, open to, exposed to, vulnerable to, prone to, susceptible to
 **C** conditional upon: pay is [[subject to]] negotiation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[subject to]]**: dependent on, contingent on, controlled by, conditional on
 *adv*
**9** **[[subject to]]** under the condition that something takes place: my visit was agreed [[subject to]] certain conditions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[subject to]]**: bound by
 *vb* (sub-**ject**)
**10** **[[subject to]]**
**A** to cause (someone) to experience (something unpleasant): they were subjected to beatings
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put through]], [[expose]], [[submit]], [[lay open]]
 **B** to bring under the control or authority of: to subject a soldier to discipline
