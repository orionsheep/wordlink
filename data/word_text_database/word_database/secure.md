**secure** *adj*
**1** free from danger or damage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[safe]], [[protected]], [[immune]], [[unassailable]]
**2** free from fear, doubt, or care
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[confident]], [[sure]], [[easy]], [[certain]], [[assured]], [[reassured]]
**3** tightly locked or well [[protected]]
**4** [[fixed]] or tied firmly in position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fast]], [[firm]], [[fixed]], [[stable]], [[steady]], [[fastened]], [[immovable]]
**5** able to be relied on: secure profits
 *vb* **-curing**, **-cured**
**6** to [[obtain]]: to secure a change in German policy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[obtain]], [[get]], [[acquire]], [[score]] (*slang*), [[gain]], [[procure]]
**7** to make or become free from danger or fear
**8** to make [[safe]] from loss, theft, or attack
**9** to [[attach]]; make [[fast]] or [[firm]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attach]], [[stick]], [[fix]], [[bind]], [[fasten]]
**10** to guarantee (payment of a loan) by giving something as security
 **[[securely]]** *adv*
