**plug** *n*
**1** an object used to [[block]] up holes or waste pipes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stopper]], [[cork]], [[bung]], [[spigot]]
**2** a device with one or more pins which connects an appliance to an electricity supply
**3** (*informal*) a favourable [[mention]] of a product, etc., for example on television, to encourage people to buy it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mention]], [[advertisement]], [[advert]] (*Brit*), [[push]], [[publicity]], [[hype]]
**4** See **spark plug**
**5** a piece of tobacco for chewing
 *vb* **plugging**, **plugged**
**6** to [[block]] or [[seal]] (a hole or gap) with a plug
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[seal]], [[close]], [[stop]], [[fill]], [[block]], [[stuff]], [[pack]], [[cork]]
**7** (*informal*) to make frequent favourable mentions of (a product, etc.), for example on television
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mention]], [[push]], [[promote]], [[publicize]], [[advertise]], [[build up]], [[hype]]
**8** (*slang*) to shoot
**9** (*slang*) to punch
**10** (foll. by *along* or *away* etc) (*informal*) to work steadily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[plug away]]**: slog away, labour, toil away, grind away (*informal*), peg away, plod away
 See also **plug in**
