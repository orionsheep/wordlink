**intervene** *vb* **-vening**, **-vened**
**1** (often foll. by *in*) to involve oneself in a situation, esp. to prevent conflict
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[step in]] (*informal*), [[interfere]], [[mediate]], [[intrude]], [[intercede]], [[arbitrate]], [[take a hand]] (*informal*)
**2** to [[interrupt]] a conversation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[interrupt]], [[involve yourself]]
**3** to [[happen]] so as to stop something: he hoped to play but a serious injury intervened
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[happen]], [[occur]], [[take place]], [[follow]], [[arise]], [[ensue]], [[befall]], [[materialize]]
**4** to come or be among or between: ten years had intervened since he had seen Joe
