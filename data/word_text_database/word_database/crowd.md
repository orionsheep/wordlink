**crowd** *n*
**1** a large number of things or people gathered together
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[multitude]], [[mass]], [[throng]], [[army]], [[host]], [[pack]], [[mob]], [[swarm]]
**2** a particular [[group]] of people: we got to know a French crowd from Lyons
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[group]], [[set]], [[lot]], [[circle]], [[gang]], [[bunch]] (*informal*), [[clique]]
**3** **the crowd** the masses
 *vb*
**4** to [[gather]] together in large numbers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flock]], [[mass]], [[collect]], [[gather]], [[stream]], [[surge]], [[swarm]], [[throng]]
**5** to press together into a confined space
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[squeeze]], [[pack]], [[pile]], [[bundle]], [[cram]]
**6** to fill or occupy fully
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[congest]], [[pack]], [[cram]]
**7** (*informal*) to make (someone) uncomfortable by coming too close
 **[[crowded]]** *adj*
