**weary** *adj* **-rier**, **-riest**
**1** very [[tired]]; lacking energy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tired]], [[exhausted]], [[drained]], [[worn out]], [[done in]] (*informal*), [[flagging]], [[fatigued]], [[sleepy]], [[clapped out]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*)
**2** caused by or suggestive of [[weariness]]: he managed a weary smile
**3** causing exhaustion: a long weary struggle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tiring]], [[arduous]], [[tiresome]], [[laborious]], [[wearisome]]
**4** **weary of** discontented or bored with: he was weary of the war
 *vb* **-ries**, **-rying**, **-ried**
**5** to make weary
**6** **weary of** to become discontented or bored with: he seems to have wearied of her possessiveness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grow tired]], [[tire]], [[become bored]]
 **[[wearily]]** *adv* 
 **[[weariness]]** *n* 
 **[[wearying]]** *adj*
