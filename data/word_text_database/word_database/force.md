**force** *n*
**1** [[strength]] or [[power]]: the force of the impact had thrown him into the fireplace
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[power]], [[might]], [[pressure]], [[energy]], [[strength]], [[momentum]], [[impulse]], [[vigour]]
**2** exertion or the use of exertion against a person or thing that resists: they used force and repression against those who opposed their policies
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[compulsion]], [[pressure]], [[violence]], [[constraint]], [[oppression]], [[coercion]], [[duress]], [[arm-twisting]] (*informal*)
**3** (*physics*) an influence that changes a body from a state of rest to one of motion or changes its rate of motion. <small>SYMBOL: </small>***F***
**4**
**A** intellectual or moral influence: the Superintendent acknowledged the force of the Chief Constable's argument
 **B** a person or thing with such influence: the major force behind the group's success 
**5** [[drive]] or [[intensity]]: she reacted with frightening speed and force
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intensity]], [[vigour]], [[vehemence]], [[fierceness]], [[emphasis]]
**6** a group of people organized for particular duties or tasks: a UN peacekeeping force
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[army]], [[unit]], [[company]], [[host]], [[troop]], [[squad]], [[patrol]], [[regiment]]
**7** **[[in force]]**
**A** (of a law) having legal validity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[in force]]**: valid, working, current, effective, binding, operative, operational, in operation
 **B** in great [[strength]] or numbers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[in force]]**: in great numbers, all together, in full [[strength]]
 *vb* **forcing**, **forced**
**8** to [[compel]] (a person, group, etc.) to do something through effort, superior [[strength]], etc.: forced into an arranged marriage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[compel]], [[make]], [[drive]], [[press]], [[oblige]], [[constrain]], [[coerce]], [[impel]]
**9** to acquire or produce through effort, superior [[strength]], etc.: I forced a smile
**10** to [[propel]] or [[drive]] despite resistance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[push]], [[thrust]], [[propel]]
**11** to break down or open (a lock, door, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[break open]], [[blast]], [[wrench]], [[prise]], [[wrest]]
**12** to impose or inflict: a series of opposition strikes forced the appointment of a coalition government
**13** to cause (plants or farm animals) to grow at an increased rate
