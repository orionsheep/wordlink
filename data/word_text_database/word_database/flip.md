**flip** *vb* **flipping**, **flipped**
**1** to [[throw]] (something light or small) carelessly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[toss]], [[throw]], [[flick]], [[fling]], [[sling]] (*informal*)
**2** to [[turn]] (something) over: flip the fish on its back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spin]], [[turn]], [[overturn]], [[turn over]], [[roll over]]
**3** to [[turn]] (a device or machine) on or off by quickly pressing a [[switch]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flick]], [[switch]], [[snap]], [[click]]
**4** to [[throw]] (an object such as a coin) so that it turns in the air
**5** to buy and sell an asset (often property) quickly for profit
**6** **flip through** to look at (a book or magazine) idly
**7** Also: **flip one's lid** (*slang*) to fly into an emotional outburst
 *n*
**8** a [[snap]] or tap, usually with the fingers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[toss]], [[throw]], [[spin]], [[snap]], [[flick]]
 *adj*
**9** (*informal*) flippant or pert
