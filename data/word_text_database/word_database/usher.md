**usher** *n*
**1** an official who shows people to their seats, as in a church
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attendant]], [[guide]], [[doorman]], [[escort]], [[doorkeeper]]
**2** a person who acts as [[doorkeeper]] in a court of law
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attendant]], [[guide]], [[doorman]], [[escort]], [[doorkeeper]]
 *vb*
**3** to [[conduct]] or [[escort]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[escort]], [[lead]], [[direct]], [[guide]], [[conduct]]
**4** (foll. by *in*) to happen immediately before something or cause it to happen; herald: the French Revolution ushered in a new age
