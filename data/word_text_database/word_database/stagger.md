**stagger** *vb*
**1** to walk unsteadily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[totter]], [[reel]], [[sway]], [[lurch]], [[wobble]]
**2** to [[amaze]] or [[shock]] (someone): it staggered her that there was any liaison between them
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[astound]], [[amaze]], [[stun]], [[shock]], [[shake]], [[overwhelm]], [[astonish]], [[confound]]
**3** to arrange (events) so as not to happen at the same time: staggered elections
 *n*
**4** a [[staggering]]
 **[[staggering]]** *adj* 
 **[[staggeringly]]** *adv*
