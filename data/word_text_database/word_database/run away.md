**[[run away]]** *vb*
**1** to go away
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run away]]**: flee, escape, bolt, abscond, do a runner (*slang*), make a run for it, scram (*informal*), fly the coop (*US** & **Canad* *informal*), do a Skase (*Austral* *informal*)
**2** to escape
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run away]]**: flee, escape, bolt, abscond, do a runner (*slang*), make a run for it, scram (*informal*), fly the coop (*US** & **Canad* *informal*), do a Skase (*Austral* *informal*)
**3** (of a horse) to gallop away uncontrollably: the horse ran away with him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run away]]**: flee, escape, bolt, abscond, do a runner (*slang*), make a run for it, scram (*informal*), fly the coop (*US** & **Canad* *informal*), do a Skase (*Austral* *informal*)
**4** **[[run away]] with**
**A** to abscond or elope with: I ran away with David
 **B** to escape from the control of: he let his imagination [[run away]] with him
 **C** to win easily or be certain of victory in (a competition): the Spaniards at one stage seemed to be running away with the match
 *n* **runaway**
**5** a person or animal that runs away
 *adj* **runaway**
**6** no longer under control: a runaway train
**7** (of a race or victory) easily won
