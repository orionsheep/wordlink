**pull in** *vb*
**1** Also: **pull over** (of a motor vehicle) to draw in to the side of the road
**2** (often foll. by *to*) to reach a destination: the train pulled in to the station
**3** to attract: his appearance will pull in the crowds
**4** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*) to arrest
**5** to earn (money): he pulls in at least thirty thousand a year
