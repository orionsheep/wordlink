**comedown** *n*
**1** a decline in status or prosperity
**2** (*informal*) a disappointment
 *vb* **come down**
**3** (of prices) to become lower
**4** to reach a decision: a 1989 court ruling came down in favour of three councils who wanted Sunday trading banned
**5** to be handed down by tradition or inheritance
**6** **come down in the world** to lose status or prosperity
**7** **come down on** to reprimand sharply
**8** **come down to** to amount to: at the end the case came down to the one simple issue
**9** **come down with** to begin to suffer from (illness)
