**rage** *n*
**1** intense [[anger]] or [[passion]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[anger]], [[passion]], [[madness]], [[wrath]], [[ire]]
**2** a [[fashion]] or [[craze]]: the dance was the rage of Europe
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[craze]], [[fashion]], [[enthusiasm]], [[vogue]], [[fad]] (*informal*), [[latest thing]]
**3** aggressive behaviour associated with a specified activity or environment: road rage; school rage
**4** **all the rage** (*informal*) very popular
**5** (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) a dance or party
 *vb* **raging**, **raged**
**6** to feel or show intense [[anger]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be furious]], [[blow up]] (*informal*), [[fume]], [[lose it]] (*informal*), [[seethe]], [[lose the plot]] (*informal*), [[go ballistic]] (*slang*), [[lose your temper]]
**7** to proceed violently and without restraint: the argument was still raging
