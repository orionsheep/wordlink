**drain** *n*
**1** a [[pipe]] that carries off water or sewage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sewer]], [[channel]], [[pipe]], [[sink]], [[ditch]], [[trench]], [[conduit]], [[duct]]
**2** a cause of a continuous [[reduction]] in energy or resources: the expansion will be a drain on resources
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reduction]], [[strain]], [[drag]], [[exhaustion]], [[sapping]], [[depletion]]
**3** a metal grid on a road or pavement through which rainwater flows
**4** **down the drain** wasted
 *vb*
**5** to [[draw]] off or [[remove]] (liquid) from
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[remove]], [[draw]], [[empty]], [[withdraw]], [[tap]], [[pump]], [[bleed]] (*informal*)
**6** to flow (away) or filter (off)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flow out]], [[leak]], [[trickle]], [[ooze]], [[seep]], [[exude]], [[well out]], [[effuse]]
**7** to dry or be emptied as a result of liquid running off or flowing away
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[empty]]
**8** to drink the entire contents of (a glass or cup)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drink up]], [[swallow]], [[finish]], [[put away]] (*informal*), [[quaff]], [[gulp down]]
**9** to make constant demands on (energy or resources); [[exhaust]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[consume]], [[exhaust]], [[empty]], [[use up]], [[sap]], [[dissipate]]
**10** (of a river) to carry off the surface water from (an area)
