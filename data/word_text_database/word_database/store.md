**store** *vb* **storing**, **stored**
**1** to [[keep]], set aside, or gather (things) for future use
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put by]], [[save]], [[hoard]], [[keep]], [[reserve]], [[deposit]], [[garner]], [[stockpile]]
**2** to place (furniture or other possessions) in a [[warehouse]] for safekeeping
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put away]], [[put in storage]], [[put in store]]
**3** to [[supply]] or [[stock]] (certain goods)
**4** (*computers*) to enter or [[keep]] (information) in a storage device
 *n*
**5** a [[shop]] (in Britain usually a large one)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shop]], [[outlet]], [[market]], [[mart]]
**6** a large [[supply]] or [[stock]] kept for future use
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[supply]], [[stock]], [[reserve]], [[fund]], [[quantity]], [[accumulation]], [[stockpile]], [[hoard]]
**7** short for **department store**
**8** a storage place, such as a [[warehouse]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[repository]], [[warehouse]], [[depository]], [[storeroom]]
**9** (*computers*, *chiefly* *Brit*) same as **memory**
**10** **in store** about to happen; forthcoming: you've got a treat in store
**11** **set great store by something** to value something as important
