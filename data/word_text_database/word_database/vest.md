**vest** *n*
**1** (*Brit*) an undergarment covering the top half of the body
**2** (*US** & **Canad** & **Austral*) a waistcoat
 *vb*
**3** **vest in** to settle (power or property) on: by the power vested in me, I pronounce you married
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[vest in something or someone]]**: place, invest, entrust, settle, confer, endow, bestow, consign
**4** **vest with** to bestow on: the sponsorship has vested these matches with a new interest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[vest with something]]**: endow with, entrust with
