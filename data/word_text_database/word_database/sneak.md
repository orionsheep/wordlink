**sneak** *vb*
**1** to move quietly, trying not be noticed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slink]], [[slip]], [[steal]], [[pad]], [[skulk]]
**2** to behave in a cowardly or underhand manner
**3** to bring, take, or put secretly: we sneaked him over the border
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slip]], [[smuggle]], [[spirit]]
**4** (*informal*, *chiefly* *Brit** & **NZ*) (esp. in schools) to tell tales
 *n*
**5** a person who acts in an underhand or cowardly manner
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[informer]], [[betrayer]], [[telltale]], [[Judas]], [[accuser]], [[stool pigeon]], [[nark]] (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*), fizgig (*Austral* *slang*)
 *adj*
**6** without warning: a sneak attack
