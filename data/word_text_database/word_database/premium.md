**premium** *n*
**1** an extra sum of money added to a standard rate, price, or wage: the superior taste persuades me to pay the premium for bottled water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[surcharge]], [[extra charge]], additional [[fee]] *or* [[charge]]
**2** the (regular) amount paid for an insurance policy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fee]], [[charge]], [[payment]], [[instalment]]
**3** the amount above the usual value at which something sells: some even pay a premium of up to 15% for the privilege
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bonus]], [[reward]], [[prize]], [[perk]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[bounty]], [[perquisite]] (*formal*)
**4** great value or regard: we do put a very high premium on common sense
**5** **[[at a premium]]**
**A** in great demand, usually because of scarcity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[at a premium]]**: in great demand, rare, scarce, in short supply, hard to come by
 **B** at a higher price than usual
