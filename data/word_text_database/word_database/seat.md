**seat** *n*
**1** a piece of furniture designed for sitting on, such as a [[chair]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[chair]], [[bench]], [[stall]], [[stool]], [[pew]], [[settle]]
**2** the part of a [[chair]] or other piece of furniture on which one sits
**3** a [[place]] to [[sit]] in a theatre, esp. one that requires a ticket: there were two empty front-row seats at the pageant
**4** the buttocks
**5** the part of a garment covering the buttocks
**6** the part or surface on which an object rests
**7** the [[place]] or [[centre]] in which something is based: the seat of government
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[centre]], [[place]], [[site]], [[heart]], [[capital]], [[situation]], [[source]], [[hub]]
**8** (*Brit*) a country [[mansion]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mansion]], [[house]], [[residence]], [[abode]], [[ancestral hall]]
**9** a [[membership]] or the right to [[membership]] of a legislative or administrative body: a seat on the council
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[membership]], [[place]], [[constituency]], [[chair]], [[incumbency]]
**10** (*chiefly* *Brit*) a parliamentary [[constituency]]
**11** the manner in which a rider sits on a horse
 *vb*
**12** to bring to or [[place]] on a seat
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sit]], [[place]], [[settle]], [[set]], [[fix]], [[locate]], [[install]]
**13** to provide seats for: the dining hall seats 150 people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hold]], [[take]], [[accommodate]], [[sit]], [[contain]], [[cater for]]
**14** to [[set]] firmly in [[place]]
