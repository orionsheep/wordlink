**[[wind up]]** *vb*
**1** to bring to a conclusion: we want to wind this conflict up as quickly as possible
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[wind something up]]**: end, finish, settle, conclude, tie up, wrap up, finalize
**2** (*informal*) to dissolve (a company) and divide its assets among creditors
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[wind something up]]**: close down, close, dissolve, terminate, put something into liquidation
**3** to tighten the spring of (a clockwork mechanism) by turning a key or knob
**4** to move (a car window) upwards by turning a handle
**5** (*informal*) to end up: to [[wind up]] in the hospital
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[wind up]]**: end up, be left, finish up, fetch up (*informal*), land up
**6** (*informal*) to make nervous or tense: as crisis after crisis broke, I became increasingly wound up
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[wind someone up]]**: irritate, excite, anger, annoy, exasperate, nettle, work someone up, pique
**7** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*) to tease or annoy: that really used to wind my old man up something rotten
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[wind someone up]]**: tease, kid (*informal*), have someone on (*informal*), annoy, rag (*informal*), rib (*informal*), josh (*informal*), vex
 *adj* **wind-up**
**8** operated by clockwork: a wind-up toy
 *n* **wind-up**
**9** a light-hearted hoax
**10** the finish
