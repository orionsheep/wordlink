**plough** * or * (*US*) **plow** *n*
**1** an agricultural tool for cutting or turning over the earth
**2** a similar tool used for clearing snow
 *vb*
**3** to [[turn over]] (the soil) with a plough
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn over]], [[dig]], [[till]], [[cultivate]]
**4** to make (furrows or grooves) in (something) with or as if with a plough
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn over]], [[dig]], [[till]], [[cultivate]]
**5** (sometimes foll. by *through*) to move (through something) in the manner of a plough: the ship ploughed through the water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[plough through something]]**: forge, cut, drive, press, push, plunge, wade
**6** (foll. by *through*) to work at slowly or perseveringly
**7** to invest (money): he ploughed the profits back into the business
**8** **plough into** (of a vehicle, plane, etc.) to go out of control and crash into (something): the aircraft ploughed into a motorway embankment
