**play** *vb*
**1** to occupy oneself in (a [[sport]] or recreation)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amuse yourself]], [[have fun]], [[sport]], [[fool]], [[romp]], [[revel]], [[trifle]], [[entertain yourself]]
**2** to [[compete against]] (someone) in a [[sport]] or game: I saw Brazil play Argentina recently
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[compete against]], [[challenge]], [[take on]], [[oppose]], [[contend against]]
**3** to fulfil (a particular role) in a team game: he usually plays in midfield
**4** (often foll. by *about* or *around*) to behave carelessly: he's only playing with your affections, you know
**5** to [[act]] the part (of) in a dramatic [[piece]]: he has played Hamlet to packed Broadway houses
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[act]], [[portray]], [[represent]], [[perform]], [[act the part of]]
**6** to [[perform]] (a dramatic [[piece]])
**7**
**A** to [[perform]] (music) on a musical instrument
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[perform on]], [[strum]], make music on
 **B** to be able to [[perform on]] (a musical instrument): she plays the bassoon
**8** to send out (water) or cause to send out (water): they played a hose across the wrecked building
**9** to cause (a radio, etc.) to emit sound
**10** to move freely or quickly: the light played across the water
**11** (*Stock Exchange*) to speculate for gain in (a market)
**12** (*angling*) to tire (a hooked fish) by alternately letting out and reeling in the line
**13** to put (a card) into play
**14** to gamble
**15** **play fair *or* false with** to [[act]] fairly *or* unfairly with
**16** **play for time** to gain time to one's advantage by the use of delaying tactics
**17** **play into the hands of** to [[act]] unwittingly to the advantage of (an opponent)
**18** **play politics**
**A** to negotiate politically
 **B** to exploit an important issue merely for political gain
 **C** to make something into a political issue
 *n*
**19**
**A** a dramatic [[piece]] written for performance by actors
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drama]], [[show]], [[piece]], [[comedy]], [[tragedy]], [[farce]], soapie *or* soapy (*Austral* *slang*), [[pantomime]]
 **B** the performance of such a [[piece]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drama]], [[show]], [[piece]], [[comedy]], [[tragedy]], [[farce]], soapie *or* soapy (*Austral* *slang*), [[pantomime]]
**20** [[games]] or other activity undertaken for [[pleasure]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amusement]], [[pleasure]], [[leisure]], [[games]], [[sport]], [[fun]], [[entertainment]], [[relaxation]], [[me-time]]
**21** the playing of a game or the time during which a game is in progress: rain stopped play
**22** conduct: fair play
**23** gambling
**24** activity or operation: radio allows full play to your imagination
**25** scope for freedom of movement: there was a lot of play in the rope
**26** free or rapidly shifting motion: the play of light on the water
**27** [[fun]] or jest: I used to throw cushions at her in play
**28** **in *or* out of play** (of a ball in a game) in *or* not in a position for continuing play according to the rules
**29** **make a play for** (*informal*) to make an obvious attempt to gain (something)
 **[[playable]]** *adj*
