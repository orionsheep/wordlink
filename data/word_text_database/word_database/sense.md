**sense** *n*
**1** any of the faculties (sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell) by which the mind receives information about the external world or the state of the body
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[faculty]]
**2** the ability to [[perceive]]
**3** a [[feeling]] perceived through one of the senses: a sense of warmth
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[feeling]], [[impression]], [[perception]], [[awareness]], [[consciousness]], [[atmosphere]], [[aura]]
**4** a mental [[perception]] or [[awareness]]: a sense of security
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[feeling]], [[impression]], [[perception]], [[awareness]], [[consciousness]], [[atmosphere]], [[aura]]
**5** ability to make moral judgments: a sense of honour
**6** (*usually pl*) sound practical [[judgment]] or [[intelligence]]: you must have lost your senses when you bought that
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intelligence]], [[reason]], [[understanding]], [[brains]] (*informal*), [[judgment]], [[wisdom]], [[wit]](s), [[common sense]]
**7** [[reason]] or purpose: no sense in continuing
**8** general [[meaning]]: he couldn't [[understand]] every word but he got the sense of what they were saying
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meaning]], [[significance]], [[import]], [[implication]], [[drift]], [[gist]]
**9** specific [[meaning]]; definition: the three senses of the word
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meaning]], [[significance]], [[import]], [[implication]], [[drift]], [[gist]]
**10** **make sense** to be understandable or practical
 *vb* **sensing**, **sensed**
**11** to [[perceive]] without the evidence of the senses: he sensed that she was impressed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[perceive]], [[feel]], [[understand]], [[pick up]], [[realize]], [[be aware of]], [[discern]], [[get the impression]]
**12** to [[perceive]] through the senses
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[perceive]], [[feel]], [[understand]], [[pick up]], [[realize]], [[be aware of]], [[discern]], [[get the impression]]
