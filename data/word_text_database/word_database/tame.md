**tame** *adj*
**1**
**A** (of an animal) changed by humans from a wild state into a [[domesticated]] state
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[domesticated]], [[docile]], [[broken]], [[gentle]], [[obedient]], [[amenable]], [[tractable]]
 **B** (of an animal) not afraid of or aggressive towards humans
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[domesticated]], [[docile]], [[broken]], [[gentle]], [[obedient]], [[amenable]], [[tractable]]
**2** (of a person) tending to do what one is told without questioning or criticizing it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[submissive]], [[meek]], [[compliant]], [[subdued]], [[manageable]], [[obedient]], [[docile]], [[unresisting]]
**3** mild and [[unexciting]]: the love scenes are fairly tame by modern standards
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unexciting]], [[boring]], [[dull]], [[bland]], [[uninspiring]], [[humdrum]], [[uninteresting]], insipid
 *vb* **taming**, **[[tamed]]**
**4** to make (an animal) tame; [[domesticate]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[domesticate]], [[train]], [[break in]], [[house-train]]
**5** to bring under control; make less extreme or dangerous: many previously deadly diseases have been [[tamed]] by antibiotics
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[subdue]], [[suppress]], [[master]], [[discipline]], [[humble]], [[conquer]], [[subjugate]]
