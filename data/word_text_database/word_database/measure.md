**measure** *n*
**1** the size, [[quantity]], or degree of something, as discovered by measurement or calculation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quantity]], [[share]], [[amount]], [[allowance]], [[portion]], [[quota]], [[ration]], [[allotment]]
**2** a device for measuring distance, volume, etc., such as a graduated [[scale]] or container
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gauge]], [[rule]], [[scale]], [[metre]], [[ruler]], [[yardstick]]
**3** a system or unit of measurement: the joule is a measure of energy
**4** an [[amount]] of alcoholic drink, esp. that served as standard in a bar
**5** degree or extent: a measure of success
**6** a particular [[action]] intended to achieve an effect: radical measures are needed to cut unemployment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[action]], [[act]], [[step]], [[procedure]], [[means]], [[control]], [[initiative]], [[manoeuvre]]
**7** a legislative [[bill]], [[act]], or [[resolution]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[law]], [[act]], [[bill]], [[legislation]], [[resolution]], [[statute]]
**8** (*music*) same as **bar**
**9** (*prosody*) poetic rhythm or [[metre]]
**10** (*prosody*) a metrical foot
**11** (*old-fashioned*) a dance
**12** **for good measure** as an extra precaution or beyond requirements
 *vb* **-suring**, **-sured**
**13** to [[determine]] the size, [[amount]], etc., of by measurement: he measured the room for a new carpet
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quantify]], [[determine]], [[assess]], [[weigh]], [[calculate]], [[evaluate]], [[compute]], [[gauge]]
**14** to indicate or record the size, speed, force, etc., of: this dial measures the pressure in the pipe
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quantify]], [[determine]], [[assess]], [[weigh]], [[calculate]], [[evaluate]], [[compute]], [[gauge]]
**15** to have the size, [[quantity]], etc., specified: the room measures six feet
**16** to estimate or [[assess]]: you cannot measure intelligence purely by exam results
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quantify]], [[determine]], [[assess]], [[weigh]], [[calculate]], [[evaluate]], [[compute]], [[gauge]]
**17** to function as a measurement of: the ohm measures electrical resistance
**18** to bring into competition or conflict with: he measured his strength against that of his opponent
 **[[measurable]]** *adj*
