**number** *n*
**1** a concept of [[quantity]] that is or can be derived from a single unit, a sum of units, or zero
**2** the word or symbol used to represent a number
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[numeral]], [[figure]], [[character]], [[digit]], [[integer]]
**3** a [[numeral]] or string of numerals used to identify a person or thing: an [[account]] number
**4** the person or thing so identified: he was seeded number two
**5** sum or [[quantity]]: a very large number of people have telephoned
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amount]], [[quantity]], [[collection]], [[aggregate]]
**6** one of a series, as of a magazine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[issue]], [[copy]], [[edition]], [[imprint]], [[printing]]
**7** a self-contained piece of pop or jazz music
**8** a [[group]] of people: one of their number might be willing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[group]], [[set]], [[band]], [[crowd]], [[gang]]
**9** (*informal*) an admired article: that little number is by Dior
**10** (*grammar*) classification of words depending on how many people or things are referred to
**11** **any number of** many
**12** **beyond *or* without number** innumerable
**13** **have someone's number** (*informal*) to have discovered someone's true [[character]] or intentions
**14** **one's number is up** (*Brit** & **Austral* *informal*) one is about to die
 *vb*
**15** to [[count]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[calculate]], [[account]], [[reckon]], [[compute]], [[enumerate]]
**16** to assign a number to: numbered seats
**17** to [[add up to]]: the illustrations numbered well over fifty
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amount to]], [[come to]], [[total]], [[add up to]]
**18** to [[include]] in a [[group]]: he numbered several Americans among his friends
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[include]], [[count]]
**19** **one's days are numbered** something unpleasant, such as death, is likely to happen to one soon
