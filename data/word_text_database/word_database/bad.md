**bad** *adj* **worse**, **worst**
**1** not good; of [[poor]] quality
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harmful]], [[damaging]], [[dangerous]], [[destructive]], [[unhealthy]], [[detrimental]], [[hurtful]], [[ruinous]]
**2** lacking skill or talent: I'm so bad at that sort of thing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[incompetent]], [[poor]], [[useless]] (*informal*), [[incapable]], [[unfit]], [[inexpert]]
**3** [[harmful]]: smoking is bad for you
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harmful]], [[damaging]], [[dangerous]], [[destructive]], [[unhealthy]], [[detrimental]], [[hurtful]], [[ruinous]]
**4** [[evil]] or [[immoral]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wicked]], [[criminal]], [[evil]], [[corrupt]], [[immoral]], [[sinful]], [[depraved]]
**5** [[naughty]] or [[mischievous]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[naughty]], [[defiant]], [[wayward]], [[mischievous]], [[wicked]], [[unruly]], [[impish]], [[undisciplined]]
**6** [[rotten]] or decayed: a bad egg
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rotten]], [[off]], [[rank]], [[sour]], [[rancid]], [[mouldy]], [[putrid]], [[festy]] (*Austral* *slang*)
**7** severe: a bad headache
**8** incorrect or faulty: bad grammar
**9** sorry or upset: I feel bad about saying no
**10** [[unfavourable]] or [[distressing]]: bad news
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unfavourable]], [[distressing]], [[unfortunate]], [[grim]], [[unpleasant]], [[gloomy]], [[adverse]]
**11** offensive or [[unpleasant]]: bad language
**12** not valid: a bad cheque
**13** not recoverable: a bad debt
**14** **badder**, **baddest** (*slang*) good; excellent
**15** **not bad** *or ***not so bad** (*informal*) fairly good
**16** **too bad** (*informal*) (often used dismissively) regrettable
 *n*
**17** [[unfortunate]] or [[unpleasant]] events: you've got to take the good with the bad
 *adv*
**18** (*not standard*) badly: to want something bad
 **[[badness]]** *n*
