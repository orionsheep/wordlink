**principal** *adj*
**1** [[first]] in importance, rank, or value: salt is the principal source of sodium in our diets; the Republic's two principal parties
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[main]], [[leading]], [[chief]], [[prime]], [[first]], [[key]], [[essential]], primary
 *n*
**2** the [[head]] of a school or other educational institution
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: headmaster *or* headmistress, [[head]] (*informal*), [[dean]], [[head teacher]], [[rector]], master *or* mistress
**3** a person who holds one of the most important positions in an organization: she became a principal in the home finance department
**4** the [[leading]] actor in a play
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[star]], [[lead]], [[leader]], [[prima ballerina]], [[leading]] man *or* lady, [[coryphée]]
**5** (*law*)
**A** a person who engages another to act as his or her agent
 **B** a person who takes an active part in a crime
 **C** the person held responsible for fulfilling an obligation
**6** (*finance*)
**A** [[capital]] or property, as contrasted with income
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[capital]], [[money]], [[assets]], working [[capital]]
 **B** the original amount of a debt on which interest is calculated
 **[[principally]]** *adv*
