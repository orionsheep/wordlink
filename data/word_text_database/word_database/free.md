**free** *adj* **freer**, **freest**
**1** [[able]] to act at will; not under compulsion or restraint
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[allowed]], [[permitted]], [[unrestricted]], [[unimpeded]], [[clear]], [[able]]
**2** not enslaved or confined
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[at liberty]], [[loose]], [[liberated]], [[at large]], [[on the loose]]
**3** (of a country) independent
**4** (of a translation) not exact or literal
**5** provided [[without charge]]: free school meals
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[complimentary]], [[for free]] (*informal*), [[for nothing]], [[unpaid]], [[for love]], [[free of charge]], [[on the house]], [[without charge]], open *or* open source (*computers*)
**6** not occupied or in use; [[available]]: is this seat free?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[available]], [[empty]], [[spare]], [[vacant]], [[unused]], [[unoccupied]], [[untaken]]
**7** (of a person) not busy
**8** open or [[available]] to all
**9** not fixed or joined; [[loose]]: the free end
**10** without obstruction or blockage: the free flow of capital
**11** (*chem*) chemically uncombined: free nitrogen
**12** **free and easy** casual or tolerant
**13** **free from** not subject to: free from surveillance
**14** **free with** using or giving (something) a lot: he was free with his tongue
**15** **make free with** to behave too familiarly towards
 *adv*
**16** in a free manner
**17** [[without charge]] or cost
 *vb* **freeing**, **freed**
**18** to [[release]] or liberate
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clear]], [[disengage]], [[cut loose]], [[release]], [[rescue]], [[extricate]]
**19** to remove obstructions or impediments from
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disentangle]], [[extricate]], [[disengage]], [[loose]], [[unravel]], [[disconnect]], [[untangle]]
**20** to make [[available]] or usable: capital freed by the local authority
**21** **free of *or* from** to relieve or rid of (obstacles, pain, etc.)
 **[[freely]]** *adv*
