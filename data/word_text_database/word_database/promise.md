**promise** *vb* **-mising**, **-mised**
**1** to say that one will definitely do or not do something: I promise I'll have it finished by the end of the week
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guarantee]], [[pledge]], [[vow]], [[swear]], [[contract]], [[assure]], [[undertake]], [[warrant]]
**2** to [[undertake]] to give (something to someone): he promised me a car for my birthday
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guarantee]], [[pledge]], [[vow]], [[swear]], [[contract]], [[assure]], [[undertake]], [[warrant]]
**3** to [[show signs of]]; [[seem likely]]: she promises to be a fine singer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[seem likely]], [[look like]], [[show signs of]], [[augur]], [[betoken]]
**4** to [[assure]] (someone) of the certainty of something: everything's fine, I promise you
 *n*
**5** an [[undertaking]] to do or not do something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guarantee]], [[word]], [[bond]], [[vow]], [[commitment]], [[pledge]], [[undertaking]], [[assurance]]
**6** indication of future success: a young player who shows great promise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[potential]], [[ability]], [[talent]], [[capacity]], [[capability]], [[aptitude]]
