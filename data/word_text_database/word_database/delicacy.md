**delicacy** *n*, *pl* **-cies**
**1** fine or subtle quality, construction, etc.: delicacy of craftsmanship
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fragility]], [[flimsiness]]
**2** fragile or graceful beauty
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[daintiness]], [[charm]], [[grace]], [[elegance]], [[neatness]], [[prettiness]], [[slenderness]], [[exquisiteness]]
**3** something that is considered particularly nice to eat
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[treat]], [[luxury]], [[savoury]], [[dainty]], [[morsel]], [[titbit]]
**4** frail health
**5** refinement of feeling, manner, or appreciation: the delicacy of the orchestra's playing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sensitivity]], [[understanding]], [[consideration]], [[diplomacy]], [[discretion]], [[tact]], [[thoughtfulness]], [[sensitiveness]]
**6** need for careful or tactful treatment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[difficulty]]
