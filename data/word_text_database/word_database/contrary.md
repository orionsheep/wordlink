**contrary** *n*, *pl* **-ries**
**1** **on *or* to the contrary** in opposition to what has just been said or implied
 *adj*
**2** [[opposed]]; completely [[different]]: a contrary view, based on equally good information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[opposite]], [[different]], [[opposed]], [[clashing]], [[counter]], [[reverse]], [[adverse]], [[contradictory]]
**3** [[perverse]]; [[obstinate]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[perverse]], [[difficult]], [[awkward]], [[intractable]], [[obstinate]], [[stroppy]] (*Brit* *slang*), [[cantankerous]], [[disobliging]]
**4** (of the wind) unfavourable
 *adv* **contrary to**
**5** in opposition or contrast to: contrary to popular belief
**6** in conflict with: contrary to nature
 **[[contrariness]]** *n*
