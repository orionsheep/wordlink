**travel** *vb* **-elling**, **-elled** (*US*) **-eling**, **-eled**
**1** to [[go]] or [[move]] from one place to another
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go]], [[journey]], [[move]], [[tour]], [[progress]], [[wander]], [[trek]], [[voyage]]
**2** to [[go]] or [[journey]] through or across (an area, region, etc.): Margaret travelled widely when she was in New Zealand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go]], [[journey]], [[move]], [[tour]], [[progress]], [[wander]], [[trek]], [[voyage]]
**3** to [[go]] at a specified speed or for a specified distance: the car was travelling at 30 mph
**4** to [[go]] from place to place as a salesperson
**5** (of perishable goods) to withstand a [[journey]]: not all wines travel well
**6** (of light or sound) to be transmitted or carried from one place to another: sound travels a long distance in these conditions
**7** (of a machine or part) to [[move]] in a fixed path
**8** (*informal*) (of a vehicle) to [[move]] rapidly
 *n*
**9** the act or a means of travelling: air travel has changed the way people live
**10** a [[tour]] or [[journey]]: his travels took him to Dublin
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[journey]], [[wandering]], [[expedition]], [[globetrotting]], [[tour]], [[trip]], [[voyage]], [[excursion]]
**11** the distance moved by a mechanical part, such as the stroke of a piston
