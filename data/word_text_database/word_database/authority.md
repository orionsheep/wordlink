**authority** *n*, *pl* **-ties**
**1** the [[power]] to [[command]], [[control]], or judge others
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prerogative]], [[influence]], [[power]], [[control]], [[weight]], [[direction]], [[command]], [[licence]], [[mana]] (*NZ*)
**2** a person or group with this [[power]]: a third escapee turned himself in to the authorities
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[powers that be]], [[government]], [[police]], [[officials]], [[the state]], [[management]], [[administration]], [[the system]]
**3** a decision-making organization or [[government]] department: the local authority
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[powers that be]], [[government]], [[police]], [[officials]], [[the state]], [[management]], [[administration]], [[the system]]
**4** an [[expert]] in a particular field
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[expert]], [[specialist]], [[professional]], [[master]], [[guru]], [[virtuoso]], [[connoisseur]], [[fundi]] (*S African*)
**5** official permission: she had no authority to negotiate
**6** a position that has the [[power]] to [[command]], [[control]], or judge others: people in authority
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[command]], [[power]], [[control]], [[rule]], [[management]], [[direction]], [[mastery]]
**7** **on good authority** from reliable evidence
**8** confidence resulting from expertise
