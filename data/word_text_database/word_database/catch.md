**catch** *vb* **catching**, **caught**
**1** to [[seize]] and hold
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[seize]], [[get]], [[grab]], [[snatch]]
**2** to [[capture]] (a person or a fish or animal)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[capture]], [[arrest]], [[trap]], [[seize]], [[snare]], [[apprehend]], [[ensnare]], [[entrap]]
**3** to [[surprise]] in an act: two boys were caught stealing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[discover]], [[surprise]], [[find out]], [[expose]], [[detect]], [[catch in the act]], [[take unawares]]
**4** to reach (a bus, train, or plane) in time to board it
**5** to see or hear: you'll have to be quick if you want to catch her DJ-ing
**6** to be infected with (an illness)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[contract]], [[get]], [[develop]], [[suffer from]], [[incur]], [[succumb to]], [[go down with]]
**7** to entangle or become entangled
**8** to attract (someone's attention, imagination, or interest)
**9** to comprehend or make out: you have to work hard to catch his tone and meaning
**10** to reproduce (a quality) accurately in a work of art
**11** (of a fire) to start burning
**12** (*cricket*) to dismiss (a batsman) by catching a ball struck by him or her before it touches the ground
**13** **catch at**
**A** to attempt to grasp
 **B** to take advantage of (an opportunity)
**14** **catch it** (*informal*) to be punished
 *n*
**15** a device such as a hook, for fastening a door, window, or box
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fastener]], [[clip]], [[bolt]], [[latch]], [[clasp]]
**16** the total number of fish caught
**17** (*informal*) a concealed or unforeseen [[drawback]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drawback]], [[trick]], [[trap]], [[disadvantage]], [[hitch]], [[snag]], [[stumbling block]], [[fly in the ointment]]
**18** an emotional break in the voice
**19** (*informal*) a person considered worth having as a husband or wife
**20** (*cricket*) the act of catching a ball struck by a batsman before it touches the ground, resulting in him or her being out
 See also **catch on**, **catch out**, **catch up**
