**idle** *adj*
**1** not doing anything
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unoccupied]], [[unemployed]], [[redundant]], [[inactive]]
**2** not operating or being used
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unused]], [[inactive]], [[out of order]], [[out of service]]
**3** not wanting to work; [[lazy]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lazy]], [[slow]], [[slack]], [[sluggish]], [[lax]], [[negligent]], [[inactive]], [[inert]]
**4** [[ineffective]] or [[useless]]: it would be idle to look for a solution at this stage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[useless]], [[vain]], [[pointless]], [[unsuccessful]], [[ineffective]], [[worthless]], [[futile]], [[fruitless]]
**5** frivolous or trivial: idle pleasures
**6** without basis; unfounded: idle rumours
 *vb* **idling**, **idled**
**7** (often foll. by *away*) to waste or pass (time) in [[idleness]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fritter]], [[lounge]], [[potter]], [[loaf]], [[dally]], [[loiter]], [[dawdle]], [[laze]]
**8** (of an engine) to run at low speed without transmitting any power
 **[[idleness]]** *n* 
 **[[idler]]** *n* 
 **[[idly]]** *adv*
