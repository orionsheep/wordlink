**gut** *n*
**1** same as **intestine**
**2** (*slang*) the [[belly]]; [[paunch]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[paunch]] (*informal*), [[belly]], [[spare tyre]] (*Brit* *slang*), [[potbelly]], [[puku]] (*NZ*)
**3** short for **catgut**
**4** a silky fibrous substance extracted from silkworms and used in the manufacture of fishing tackle
 *vb* **gutting**, **gutted**
**5** to remove the internal organs from (a dead animal or fish)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disembowel]], [[clean]]
**6** (of a fire) to destroy the inside of (a building): a local pub was gutted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ravage]], [[empty]], [[clean out]], despoil (*formal*)
 *adj*
**7** (*informal*) [[basic]], essential, or [[natural]]: I have a gut feeling she's after something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[instinctive]], [[natural]], [[basic]], [[spontaneous]], [[intuitive]], [[involuntary]], [[heartfelt]], [[unthinking]]
