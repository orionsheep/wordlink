**tie** *vb* **tying**, **tied**
**1** to [[fasten]] or be fastened with string, rope, etc.: a parcel tied with string
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fasten]], [[bind]], [[join]], [[link]], [[connect]], [[attach]], [[knot]]
**2** to make a [[knot]] or bow in (something): hang on while I tie my laces
**3** to [[restrict]] or [[limit]]: they had children and were consequently tied to the school holidays
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[restrict]], [[limit]], [[confine]], [[bind]], [[restrain]], [[hamper]], [[hinder]]
**4** to equal the score of a competitor or fellow candidate: three players tied for second place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[draw]], [[be level]], [[match]], equal
 *n*
**5** a long narrow piece of material worn, esp. by men, under the collar of a shirt, tied in a [[knot]] close to the throat with the ends hanging down the front
**6** a [[bond]] or [[link]]: he still has close ties to the town where he grew up
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bond]], [[relationship]], [[connection]], [[commitment]], [[liaison]], [[allegiance]], [[affiliation]]
**7** a string, wire, etc., with which something is tied
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fastening]], [[binding]], [[link]], [[bond]], [[knot]], [[cord]], [[fetter]], [[ligature]]
**8** (*Brit*, *sport*) a [[match]] in a knockout competition: whoever wins the tie will play Australia in the semi-finals
**9**
**A** a result in a [[match]] or competition in which the scores or times of some of the competitors are the same: a tie for second place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[draw]], [[dead heat]], [[deadlock]], [[stalemate]]
 **B** the [[match]] or competition in which the scores or results are equal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[draw]], [[dead heat]], [[deadlock]], [[stalemate]]
**10** a regular [[commitment]] that limits a person's freedom: it's a bit of a tie having to visit him every day
**11** something which supports or links parts of a structure
**12** (*US** & **Canad*) a sleeper on a railway track
**13** (*music*) a curved line connecting two notes of the same pitch indicating that the sound is to be prolonged for their joint time value
