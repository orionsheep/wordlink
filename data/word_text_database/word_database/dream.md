**dream** *n*
**1** an imagined series of events experienced in the mind while asleep
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vision]], [[illusion]], [[delusion]], [[hallucination]]
**2** a [[daydream]]: she escaped into dreams of the perfect job
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[daydream]]
**3** a goal or aim: unity has been their constant dream
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ambition]], [[wish]], [[fantasy]], [[desire]], [[pipe dream]]
**4** a wonderful person or thing: her house is a dream
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[delight]], [[pleasure]], [[joy]], [[beauty]], [[treasure]], [[gem]], [[marvel]], [[pearler]] (*Austral* *slang*), beaut (*Austral** & **NZ* *slang*)
 *vb* **dreaming**, **dreamed*** or ***dreamt**
**5** to experience (a dream)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[have dreams]], [[hallucinate]]
**6** to indulge in daydreams
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[daydream]], [[stargaze]], build castles in the air *or* in Spain
**7** to be unrealistic: you're dreaming if you think we can win
**8** **dream of** to consider the possibility of: she would not dream of taking his advice
**9** **dream of *or* about** to have an image of or [[fantasy]] about: they often dream about what life will be like for them on the outside
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[dream of something or someone]]**: [[daydream]] about, fantasize about
 *adj*
**10** beautiful or pleasing: a dream kitchen
 **[[dreamer]]** *n*
