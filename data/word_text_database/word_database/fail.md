**fail** *vb*
**1** to [[be unsuccessful]] in an attempt
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be unsuccessful]], [[founder]], [[fall]], [[break down]], [[flop]] (*informal*), [[fizzle out]] (*informal*), [[come unstuck]], [[miscarry]]
**2** to [[stop]] operating
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stop working]], [[stop]], [[die]], [[break down]], [[stall]], [[cut out]], [[malfunction]], [[conk out]] (*informal*), [[crash]]
**3** to judge or be judged as being below the officially accepted standard required in a course or examination
**4** to prove disappointing or useless to (someone): the government has failed the homeless
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disappoint]], [[abandon]], [[desert]], [[neglect]], [[omit]], [[let down]], [[forsake]], [[be disloyal to]]
**5** to [[neglect]] or be unable (to do something): he failed to repair the car
**6** to [[go bankrupt]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go bankrupt]], [[collapse]], [[fold]] (*informal*), [[close down]], [[go under]], [[go bust]] (*informal*), [[go out of business]], be wound up
 *n*
**7** a failure to attain the required standard
**8** **[[without fail]]**
**A** regularly or without exception: use this shampoo once a week [[without fail]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[without fail]]**: without exception, regularly, constantly, invariably, religiously, unfailingly, conscientiously, like clockwork
 **B** definitely: they agreed to enforce the embargo [[without fail]]
