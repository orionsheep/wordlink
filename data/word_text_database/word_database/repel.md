**repel** *vb* **-pelling**, **-pelled**
**1** to cause (someone) to feel disgusted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disgust]], [[offend]], [[revolt]], [[sicken]], [[nauseate]], [[gross you out]] (*US* *slang*)
**2** to force or drive back (someone or something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drive off]], [[fight]], [[resist]], [[parry]], [[hold off]], [[rebuff]], [[ward off]], [[repulse]]
**3** to be effective in keeping away or controlling: these buzzers are claimed to repel female mosquitoes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drive off]], [[fight]], [[resist]], [[parry]], [[hold off]], [[rebuff]], [[ward off]], [[repulse]]
**4** to fail to mix with or absorb: water and oil repel each other
**5** to reject or spurn: she repelled his advances
