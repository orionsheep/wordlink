**place** *n*
**1** a particular part of a [[space]] or of a surface
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spot]], [[point]], [[position]], [[site]], [[area]], [[location]], [[venue]], [[whereabouts]]
**2** a geographical [[point]], such as a town or city
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[region]], [[quarter]], [[district]], [[neighbourhood]], [[vicinity]], [[locality]], [[locale]], [[dorp]] (*S African*)
**3** a [[position]] or rank in a sequence or order
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[position]], [[point]], [[spot]], [[location]]
**4** an open square lined with houses in a city or town
**5** [[space]] or [[room]]
**6** a [[house]] or living quarters: he's buying his own place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[home]], [[house]], [[room]], [[property]], [[accommodation]], pad (*slang*, *old-fashioned*), [[residence]], dwelling (*formal*, *literary*), [[bachelor apartment]] (*Canad*)
**7** any building or [[area]] [[set]] aside for a specific purpose
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spot]], [[point]], [[position]], [[site]], [[area]], [[location]], [[venue]], [[whereabouts]]
**8** the [[point]] reached in reading or speaking: her finger was pressed to the page as if marking her place
**9** [[right]] or [[duty]]: it's not my place to do their [[job]] for them
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[duty]], [[right]], [[job]], [[charge]], [[concern]], [[role]], [[affair]], [[responsibility]]
**10** appointment, [[position]], or [[job]]: she won a place at university
**11** [[position]], condition, or state: if I were in your place, I'd change things
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[in your/his/her/their place]]**: situation, [[position]], circumstances, shoes (*informal*)
**12** a [[space]] or [[seat]], as at a dining table
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[space]], [[position]], [[seat]], [[chair]]
**13** (*maths*) the relative [[position]] of a digit in a number
**14** **all over the place** in disorder or disarray
**15** **go places** (*informal*) to become successful
**16** **in *or* out of place** in or out of the proper or customary [[position]]
**17** **in place of**
**A** instead of: leeks can be used in place of the broccoli
 **B** in exchange for: he gave her it in place of her ring
**18** **[[know one's place]]** to be aware of one's inferior [[position]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[know one's place]]**: know one's rank, know one's standing, know one's [[position]], know one's footing, know one's [[station]], know one's status, know one's grade, know one's niche
**19** **[[put]] someone in his *or* her place** to humble someone who is arrogant, conceited, etc.
**20** **[[take place]]** to happen or occur
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[take place]]**: happen, occur, go on, go down (*US** & **Canad*), arise, come about, crop up, transpire (*informal*)
**21** **take the place of** to be a substitute for
 *vb* **placing**, **placed**
**22** to [[put]] in a particular or appropriate place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lay]] (down), [[put]] (down), [[set]] (down), [[stand]], [[position]], [[rest]], [[station]], [[stick]] (*informal*)
**23** to find or indicate the place of: I bet you the media couldn't have placed Neath on the map before the by-election
**24** to [[identify]] or classify by linking with an appropriate context: I felt I should know him, but could not quite place him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[identify]], [[remember]], [[recognize]], [[pin someone down]], [[put your finger on]], [[put a name to]]
**25** to make (an order or bet)
**26** to find a [[home]] or [[job]] for (someone)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entrust to]], [[give to]], [[assign to]], [[appoint to]], [[allocate to]], [[find a home for]]
**27** (often foll. by *with*) to [[put]] under the care (of)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entrust to]], [[give to]], [[assign to]], [[appoint to]], [[allocate to]], [[find a home for]]
**28** (of a racehorse, greyhound, athlete, etc.) to arrive in first, second, third, or sometimes fourth place
