**scratch** *vb*
**1** to [[mark]] or [[cut]] (the surface of something) with a rough or sharp instrument
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mark]], [[cut]], [[score]], [[damage]], [[grate]], [[graze]], [[etch]], [[lacerate]]
**2** (often foll. by *at* or *out* etc) to tear or dig with the nails or claws
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rub]], [[scrape]], [[claw at]]
**3** to [[scrape]] (the surface of the skin) with the nails to relieve itching
**4** to [[rub]] against (the skin) causing a slight [[cut]]
**5** to make or cause to make a grating sound
**6** (sometimes foll. by *out*) to erase or cross out
**7** to withdraw from a race or (in the US) an election
 *n*
**8** the act of scratching
**9** a slight [[cut]] on a person's or an animal's body
**10** a [[mark]] made by scratching
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mark]], [[scrape]], [[graze]], [[blemish]], [[gash]], [[laceration]], [[claw mark]]
**11** a slight grating sound
**12** **from scratch** (*informal*) from the very beginning
**13** **[[not up to scratch]]** (*informal*) not up to standard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[not up to scratch]]**: inadequate, unacceptable, unsatisfactory, insufficient, not up to standard
 *adj*
**14** put together at short notice: a scratch team
**15** (*sport*) with no handicap allowed: a scratch golfer
 **[[scratchy]]** *adj*
