**deep** *adj*
**1** extending or situated far down from a surface: a deep ditch
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[big]], [[wide]], [[broad]], [[profound]], [[yawning]], [[bottomless]], [[unfathomable]]
**2** extending or situated far inwards, backwards, or sideways
**3** of a specified dimension downwards, inwards, or backwards: six metres deep
**4** coming from or penetrating to a [[great]] depth
**5** difficult to understand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[secret]], [[hidden]], [[mysterious]], [[obscure]], [[abstract]], [[esoteric]], [[mystifying]], [[arcane]]
**6** of [[great]] intensity: deep doubts
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intense]], [[great]], [[serious]] (*informal*), [[acute]], [[extreme]], [[grave]], [[profound]], [[heartfelt]]
**7** **deep in** totally absorbed in: deep in conversation
**8** (of a colour) [[intense]] or [[dark]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dark]], [[strong]], [[rich]], [[intense]], [[vivid]]
**9** [[low]] in pitch: a deep laugh
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[low]], [[booming]], [[bass]], [[resonant]], [[sonorous]], [[low-pitched]]
**10** **go off [[the deep]] end** (*informal*) to lose one's temper
**11** **in deep water** (*informal*) in a tricky position or in trouble
 *n*
**12** any deep place on land or under water
**13** **[[the deep]]**
**A** (*poetic*) the ocean
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[the deep]]**: the ocean, the sea, the waves, the main, the high seas, the briny (*informal*)
 **B** (*cricket*) the area of the field relatively far from the pitch
**14** the most [[profound]], [[intense]], or central part: [[the deep]] of winter
 *adv*
**15** late: deep into the night
**16** profoundly or intensely: deep down I was afraid it was all my fault
 **[[deeply]]** *adv*
