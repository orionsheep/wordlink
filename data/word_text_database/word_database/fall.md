**fall** *vb* **falling**, **fell**, **fallen**
**1** to descend by the force of gravity from a higher to a lower place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[plunge]], [[tumble]], [[plummet]], [[collapse]], [[sink]], [[go down]], [[come down]]
**2** to [[drop]] suddenly from an upright position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[plunge]], [[tumble]], [[plummet]], [[collapse]], [[sink]], [[go down]], [[come down]]
**3** to [[collapse]] to the ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[plunge]], [[tumble]], [[plummet]], [[collapse]], [[sink]], [[go down]], [[come down]]
**4** to become less or lower in number or quality: inflation fell by one percentage point
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decrease]], [[drop]], [[decline]], [[go down]], [[slump]], [[diminish]], [[dwindle]], [[lessen]]
**5** to slope downwards
**6** to be badly wounded or killed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be killed]], [[die]], [[perish]], [[meet your end]]
**7** to give in to attack: in 1939 Barcelona fell to the Nationalists
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be overthrown]], [[surrender]], [[succumb]], [[submit]], [[capitulate]], [[be conquered]], [[pass into enemy hands]]
**8** to lose power or status
**9** to pass into a specified condition: I fell asleep
**10** to adopt a downhearted expression: his face fell and he pouted like a child
**11** (of night or darkness) to begin
**12** to [[occur]] at a specified time: Christmas falls on a Sunday
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[occur]], [[happen]], [[come about]], [[chance]], [[take place]], [[befall]], [[come to pass]]
**13** to give in to temptation or sin
**14** **fall apart**
**A** to break owing to long use or poor construction: the chassis is falling apart
 **B** to become disorganized and ineffective: since you resigned, the office has fallen apart
**15** **fall short** to prove inadequate
**16** **fall short of** to fail to reach (a standard)
 *n*
**17** an instance of falling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[slip]], [[plunge]], [[dive]], [[tumble]], [[descent]], [[plummet]], [[nose dive]]
**18** an amount of something, such as snow or soot, that has fallen
**19** a [[decrease]] in value or number
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decrease]], [[drop]], [[lowering]], [[decline]], [[reduction]], [[slump]], [[dip]], [[lessening]]
**20** a [[decline]] in status or importance: the town's fall from prosperity
**21** a capture or [[overthrow]]: the fall of Budapest in February 1945
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[collapse]], [[defeat]], [[downfall]], [[ruin]], [[destruction]], [[overthrow]], [[submission]], [[capitulation]]
**22** (*wrestling*) a scoring move, pinning both shoulders of one's opponent to the floor for a specified period
**23** (*chiefly* *US*) autumn
