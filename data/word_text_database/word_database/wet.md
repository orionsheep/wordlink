**wet** *adj* **wetter**, **wettest**
**1** moistened, covered, or soaked with [[water]] or some other liquid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[damp]], [[soaking]], [[saturated]], [[moist]], [[watery]], [[soggy]], [[sodden]], [[waterlogged]]
**2** not yet dry or solid: wet paint
**3** [[rainy]]: the weather was cold and wet
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rainy]], [[damp]], [[drizzly]], [[showery]], [[raining]], [[pouring]], [[drizzling]], [[teeming]]
**4** (*Brit** & **NZ* *informal*) [[feeble]] or foolish
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[feeble]], [[soft]], [[weak]], [[ineffectual]], [[weedy]] (*informal*), [[spineless]], [[effete]], [[timorous]]
**5** **wet behind the ears** (*informal*) immature or inexperienced
 *n*
**6** [[rainy]] weather
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rain]], [[drizzle]]
**7** (*Brit* *informal*) a [[feeble]] or foolish person
 *vb* **wetting**, **wet*** or ***wetted**
**8** to make wet: wet the brush before applying the paint
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[moisten]], [[spray]], [[dampen]], [[water]], [[soak]], [[saturate]], [[douse]], [[irrigate]], [[fertigate]] (*Austral*)
**9** to urinate in (one's clothes or bed)
**10** **wet oneself** to urinate in one's clothes
 **[[wetly]]** *adv* 
 **[[wetness]]** *n*
