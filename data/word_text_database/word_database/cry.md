**cry** *vb* **cries**, **crying**, **cried**
**1** to [[shed tears]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[weep]], [[sob]], [[shed tears]], [[blubber]], [[snivel]]
**2** to make a loud vocal sound, usually to express pain or fear or to appeal for help
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shout]], [[scream]], [[roar]], [[yell]], [[howl]], [[call out]], [[exclaim]], [[shriek]]
**3** to utter loudly or [[shout]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shout]], [[scream]], [[roar]], [[yell]], [[howl]], [[call out]], [[exclaim]], [[shriek]]
**4** (of an animal or bird) to utter loud characteristic sounds
**5** **cry out for** to need urgently
 *n*, *pl* **cries**
**6** a fit of weeping
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[weep]], [[sob]], [[bawl]], [[blubber]]
**7** the act or sound of crying
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shout]], [[call]], [[scream]], [[roar]], [[yell]], [[howl]], [[shriek]], [[bellow]]
**8** the characteristic utterance of an animal or bird
**9** an urgent appeal: a cry for help
**10** a public demand: a cry for more law and order on the streets
**11** **a far cry from** something very different from
**12** **in full cry**
**A** in eager pursuit
 **B** in the middle of talking or doing something
 See also **cry off**
