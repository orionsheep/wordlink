**send** *vb* **sending**, **sent**
**1** to cause (a person or thing) to go or be taken or transmitted to another place: send a message
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dispatch]], [[forward]], [[direct]], [[convey]], [[remit]]
**2** **send for** to [[dispatch]] a request or command for (someone or something): she had sent for me
**3** to cause to go to a place or point: the bullet sent him flying into the air
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[propel]], [[hurl]], [[fling]], [[shoot]], [[fire]], [[cast]], [[let fly]]
**4** to bring to a state or condition: his schemes to send her mad
**5** to cause to happen or come: the thunderstorm sent by the gods
**6** (*old-fashioned*, *slang*) to move to excitement or rapture: this music really sends me
 **[[sender]]** *n*
