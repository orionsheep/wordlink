**deliver** *vb*
**1** to [[carry]] (goods or mail) to a destination
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bring]], [[carry]], [[bear]], [[transport]], [[distribute]], [[convey]], [[cart]]
**2** to [[hand over]]: the tenants were asked to deliver up their keys
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hand over]], [[commit]], [[give up]], [[yield]], [[surrender]], [[turn over]], [[relinquish]], [[make over]]
**3** to aid in the birth of (offspring)
**4** to [[present]] (a lecture or speech)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give]], [[read]], [[present]], [[announce]], [[declare]], [[utter]]
**5** to [[release]] or [[rescue]] (from captivity or danger)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[release]], [[free]], [[save]], [[rescue]], [[loose]], [[liberate]], [[ransom]], [[emancipate]]
**6** to [[strike]] (a blow) suddenly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strike]], [[give]], [[deal]], [[launch]], [[direct]], [[aim]], [[administer]], [[inflict]]
**7** (*informal*) Also: **deliver the goods** to produce something promised
 **[[deliverance]]** *n*
