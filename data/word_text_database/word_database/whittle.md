**whittle** *vb* **-tling**, **-tled**
**1** to make (an object) by cutting or shaving pieces from (a piece of wood) with a small knife
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[carve]], [[cut]], [[hew]] (*old-fashioned*), [[shape]], [[trim]], [[shave]], [[pare]]
**2** **whittle down *or* away** to reduce in size or effectiveness gradually: my self-confidence had been whittled away to almost nothing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[whittle something away]]**: undermine, reduce, consume, erode, eat away, wear away, [[cut]] down, [[cut]], decrease, prune, scale down
