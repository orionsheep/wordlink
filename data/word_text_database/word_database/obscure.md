**obscure** *adj*
**1** not well-known: the concerts feature several obscure artists
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unknown]], [[little-known]], [[humble]], [[unfamiliar]], [[out-of-the-way]], [[lowly]], [[unheard-of]], [[undistinguished]]
**2** not easily understood: the contracts are written in obscure language
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[abstruse]], [[complex]], [[confusing]], [[mysterious]], [[vague]], [[unclear]], [[ambiguous]], [[enigmatic]]
**3** [[unclear]] or indistinct
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unclear]], [[uncertain]], [[confused]], [[mysterious]], [[doubtful]], indeterminate
 *vb* **-scuring**, **-scured**
**4** to make [[unclear]] or [[vague]]; [[hide]]: no amount of bluster could obscure the fact that the prime minister had run out of excuses
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hide]], [[screen]], [[mask]], [[disguise]], [[conceal]], [[veil]], [[cloak]], [[camouflage]]
**5** to cover or cloud over
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[obstruct]], [[hinder]]
 **[[obscuration]]** *n* 
 **[[obscurity]]** *n*
