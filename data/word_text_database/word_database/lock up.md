**lock up** *vb*
**1** to imprison
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[lock someone up]]**: imprison, jail, confine, cage, detain, shut up, incarcerate, send down (*informal*)
**2** to secure a building by locking all the doors and windows
 *n* **lockup**
**3** a jail
**4** (*Brit*) a garage or store separate from the main premises
**5** (*Brit*) a small shop with no attached quarters for the owner
 *adj* **lock-up**
**6** (*Brit** & **NZ*) (of premises) without living quarters: a lock-up garage
