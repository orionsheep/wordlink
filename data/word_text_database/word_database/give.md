**give** *vb* **giving**, **gave**, **given**
**1** to [[present]] or hand (something) to someone
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[present]], [[contribute]], [[donate]], [[provide]], [[supply]], [[award]], [[grant]], [[deliver]]
**2** to pay (an amount of money) for a purchase
**3** to [[grant]] or [[provide]]: to give an answer
**4** to [[utter]] (a shout or cry)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[communicate]], [[announce]], [[transmit]], [[pronounce]], [[utter]], [[issue]]
**5** to [[perform]], make, or [[do]]: the prime minister gave a speech
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[perform]], [[do]], [[carry out]], [[execute]]
**6** to host (a party)
**7** to sacrifice or [[devote]]: comrades who gave their lives for their country
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[surrender]], [[yield]], [[devote]], [[hand over]], [[relinquish]], [[part with]]
**8** to [[concede]]: he was very efficient, I have to give him that
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[concede]], [[allow]], [[grant]]
**9** to [[yield]] or break under pressure: something has got to give
**10** **give or take** plus or minus: about one hundred metres, give or take five
 *n*
**11** a tendency to [[yield]] under pressure; elasticity
 See also **give away**, **give in** etc
