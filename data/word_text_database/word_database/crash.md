**crash** *n*
**1** a [[collision]] involving a vehicle or vehicles
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[collision]], [[accident]], [[smash]], [[wreck]], [[prang]] (*informal*), [[bump]], [[pile-up]] (*informal*)
**2** a sudden descent of an aircraft as a result of which it crashes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[collision]], [[accident]], [[smash]], [[wreck]], [[prang]] (*informal*), [[bump]], [[pile-up]] (*informal*)
**3** a sudden loud noise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[smash]], [[clash]], [[boom]], [[bang]], [[thunder]], [[racket]], [[din]], [[clatter]]
**4** a breaking and falling to pieces
**5** the sudden [[collapse]] of a business or stock exchange
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[collapse]], [[failure]], [[depression]], [[ruin]], downfall
 *vb*
**6** to cause (a vehicle or aircraft) to collide with another vehicle, the ground, or some other object or (of vehicles or aircraft) to be involved in a [[collision]]
**7** to make or cause to make a loud smashing noise
**8** to drop with force and break into pieces with a loud noise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plunge]], [[hurtle]]
**9** to break or [[smash]] into pieces with a loud noise
**10** (of a business or stock exchange) to [[collapse]] or [[fail]] suddenly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[collapse]], [[fail]], [[go under]], [[be ruined]], [[go bust]] (*informal*), [[fold up]], [[go to the wall]], [[go belly up]] (*informal*)
**11** to move violently or noisily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fall]], [[plunge]], [[topple]], [[lurch]], [[hurtle]], [[overbalance]], [[fall headlong]]
**12** (of a computer system or program) to [[fail]] suddenly because of a malfunction
**13** (*Brit** & **Austral* *informal*) to gate-crash
 *adj*
**14** requiring or using great effort in order to achieve results quickly: a crash course
