**release** *vb* **-leasing**, **-leased**
**1** to [[free]] (a person or animal) from captivity or imprisonment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[set free]], [[free]], [[discharge]], [[liberate]], [[drop]], [[loose]], [[undo]], [[extricate]]
**2** to [[free]] (someone) from obligation or duty
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[acquit]], [[let go]], [[let off]], [[exonerate]], [[absolve]]
**3** to [[free]] (something) from (one's grip)
**4** to allow (news or information) to be made public or available
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[issue]], [[publish]], [[make public]], [[make known]], [[launch]], [[distribute]], [[put out]], [[circulate]]
**5** to allow (something) to move freely: she released the handbrake
**6** to [[issue]] (a music recording, film, or book) for sale or public showing: When was the film originally released?
**7** to give out (heat, energy, radiation, etc.): the explosion released a cloud of toxic gas
 *n*
**8** the act of freeing or state of being freed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[liberation]], [[freedom]], [[liberty]], [[discharge]], [[emancipation]], [[deliverance]]
**9** a statement to the press
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[issue]], [[publication]], [[proclamation]]
**10** the act of issuing for sale or [[publication]]
**11** something issued for sale or public showing
