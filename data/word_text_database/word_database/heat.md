**heat** *vb*
**1** to make or become hot or [[warm]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[warm]] (up), [[cook]], [[boil]], [[roast]], [[reheat]], make hot
 *n*
**2** the state of being hot
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[warmth]], [[hotness]], [[temperature]]
**3** the energy transferred as a result of a difference in [[temperature]]. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVES: </small>**thermal**, **calorific**
**4** [[hot weather]]: he loves the heat of Africa
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hot weather]], [[warmth]], [[closeness]], [[high temperature]], [[heatwave]], [[warm weather]], [[hot climate]], [[mugginess]]
**5** [[intensity]] of feeling: the heat of their argument
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[passion]], [[excitement]], [[intensity]], [[fury]], [[fervour]], [[vehemence]]
**6** the most intense part: in the heat of an election campaign
**7** pressure: political heat on the government
**8** (*sport*) a preliminary eliminating contest in a competition
**9** **on *or* in heat** (of some female mammals) ready for mating
 **[[heating]]** *n*
