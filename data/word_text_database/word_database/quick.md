**quick** *adj*
**1** characterized by rapidity of movement or action; [[fast]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fast]], [[swift]], [[speedy]], [[express]], [[cracking]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[smart]], [[rapid]], [[fleet]]
**2** lasting or taking a short time
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[brief]], [[passing]], [[hurried]], [[flying]], [[fleeting]], [[summary]], [[lightning]], [[short-lived]]
**3** [[immediate]] or [[prompt]]: her quick action minimized the damage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[immediate]], [[instant]], [[prompt]], [[sudden]], [[abrupt]], [[instantaneous]]
**4** eager or ready to perform (an action): quick to condemn
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[excitable]], [[passionate]], [[irritable]], [[touchy]], [[irascible]], [[testy]]
**5** responsive to stimulation; [[alert]]; lively: they were impressed by his quick mind
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intelligent]], [[bright]], [[alert]], [[sharp]], [[acute]], [[smart]], [[clever]], [[shrewd]]
**6** easily excited or aroused: he is impulsive and has a quick temper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[excitable]], [[passionate]], [[irritable]], [[touchy]], [[irascible]], [[testy]]
**7** nimble in one's movements or actions; deft: she has quick hands
 *n*
**8** any area of sensitive flesh, esp. that under a nail
**9** **cut someone to the quick** to hurt someone's feelings deeply
**10** **the quick** (*archaic*) living people
 *adv*
**11** in a [[rapid]] manner; swiftly
 **[[quickly]]** *adv*
