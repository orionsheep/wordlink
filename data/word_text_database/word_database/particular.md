**particular** *adj*
**1** of, belonging to, or being one person or thing; [[specific]]: the particular type of tuition on offer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[specific]], [[special]], [[exact]], [[precise]], [[distinct]], [[peculiar]]
**2** [[exceptional]] or [[special]]: the report voices particular concern over the state of the country's manufacturing industry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[special]], [[exceptional]], [[notable]], [[uncommon]], [[marked]], [[unusual]], [[remarkable]], [[singular]]
**3** providing [[specific]] details or circumstances: a particular account
**4** difficult to please; [[fussy]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fussy]], [[demanding]], [[fastidious]], [[choosy]] (*informal*), [[picky]] (*informal*), [[finicky]], [[pernickety]] (*informal*), nit-[[picky]] (*informal*)
 *n*
**5** a separate [[distinct]] [[item]] as opposed to a generalization: moving from the general to the particular
**6** an [[item]] of information; [[detail]]: she refused to go into particulars
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[detail]], [[fact]], [[feature]], [[item]], [[circumstance]], [[specification]]
**7** **in particular** especially or exactly: three painters in particular were responsible for these developments
 **[[particularly]]** *adv*
