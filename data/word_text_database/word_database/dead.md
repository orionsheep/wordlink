**dead** *adj*
**1** no longer alive
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deceased]], [[departed]] (*euphemistic*), [[late]], [[perished]], [[extinct]], [[defunct]], [[passed away]]
**2** no longer in use or finished: a dead language; a dead match
**3** unresponsive
**4** (of a limb) [[numb]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[numb]], [[frozen]], [[paralysed]], [[insensitive]], [[inert]], [[deadened]], [[immobilized]], [[unfeeling]]
**5** [[complete]] or [[absolute]]: there was dead silence
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[total]], [[complete]], [[absolute]], [[utter]], [[outright]], [[thorough]], [[unqualified]]
**6** (*informal*) very [[tired]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exhausted]], [[tired]], [[worn out]], [[spent]], [[done in]] (*informal*), [[all in]] (*slang*), [[drained]], [[knackered]] (*slang*)
**7** (of a place) lacking activity
**8** (*electronics*)
**A** [[drained]] of electric charge
 **B** not connected to a source of electric charge
**9** (*sport*) (of a ball) out of play
**10** **dead from the neck up** (*informal*) stupid
**11** **dead to the world** (*informal*) fast asleep
 *n*
**12** a period during which coldness or darkness is most intense: the dead of winter
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[middle]], [[heart]], [[depth]], midst
 *adv*
**13** (*informal*) extremely: dead easy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exactly]], [[completely]], [[totally]], [[directly]], [[fully]], [[entirely]], [[absolutely]], [[thoroughly]]
**14** suddenly and abruptly: stop dead
**15** **dead on** [[exactly]] right
