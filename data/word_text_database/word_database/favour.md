**favour** * or * (*US*) **favor** *n*
**1** an approving attitude: the company looked with favour on his plan
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[approval]], [[goodwill]], [[commendation]], [[approbation]]
**2** an act done out of [[goodwill]] or generosity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[good turn]], [[service]], [[benefit]], [[courtesy]], [[kindness]], [[indulgence]], [[boon]] (*archaic*), [[good deed]]
**3** bias at the expense of others: his fellow customs officers, showing no favour, demanded to see his luggage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[favouritism]], [[preferential treatment]]
**4** **in *or* out of favour** regarded with [[approval]] *or* disapproval
**5** **in favour of**
**A** approving
 **B** to the [[benefit]] of
 *vb*
**6** to [[prefer]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prefer]], [[opt for]], [[like better]], [[incline towards]], [[choose]], [[pick]], [[desire]], [[go for]]
**7** to show bias towards (someone) at the expense of others: parents sometimes favour the youngest child in the family
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indulge]], [[reward]], [[side with]], [[smile upon]]
**8** to [[support]] or agree with (something): he favours the abolition of capital punishment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[support]], [[champion]], [[encourage]], [[approve]], [[advocate]], [[subscribe to]], [[commend]], [[stand up for]]
 **[[favoured]]** * or * (*US*) **[[favored]]** *adj*
