**[[drop off]]** *vb*
**1** to set down (passengers or goods)
**2** to fall asleep
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[drop off]]**: fall asleep, nod (off), doze (off), snooze (*informal*), have forty winks (*informal*)
**3** to decrease or decline: sales dropped off during our period of transition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[drop off]]**: decrease, lower, decline, shrink, diminish, dwindle, lessen, subside
