**natural** *adj*
**1** as is normal or to be expected: the natural consequence
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[logical]], [[valid]], [[legitimate]]
**2** [[genuine]] or [[spontaneous]]: talking in a relaxed, natural manner
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unaffected]], [[open]], [[genuine]], [[spontaneous]], [[unpretentious]], [[unsophisticated]], [[dinkum]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*), [[ingenuous]], [[real]]
**3** of, according to, existing in, or produced by nature: natural disasters
**4** not acquired; [[inborn]]: their natural enthusiasm
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[innate]], [[native]], [[characteristic]], [[inherent]], [[instinctive]], [[intuitive]], [[inborn]], [[essential]]
**5** not created by human beings
**6** not synthetic: natural fibres such as wool
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pure]], [[plain]], [[organic]], [[whole]], [[unrefined]]
**7** (of a parent) not adoptive
**8** (*music*) not sharp or flat: F natural
 *n*
**9** (*informal*) a person with an [[inborn]] talent or skill: she's a natural at bridge
**10** (*music*) a note that is neither sharp nor flat
 **[[naturalness]]** *n*
