**exercise** *n*
**1** physical [[exertion]], esp. for [[training]] or keeping fit
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exertion]], [[training]], [[activity]], [[work]], [[labour]], [[effort]], [[movement]], [[toil]]
**2** an [[activity]] planned to achieve a particular purpose: the group's meeting was mainly an exercise in mutual reassurance
**3** a set of movements, tasks, etc. designed to improve or test one's ability or fitness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[task]], [[problem]], [[lesson]], [[assignment]], [[practice]]
**4** the [[use]] or [[practice]] of (a right, power, or authority)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[use]], [[practice]], [[application]], [[operation]], [[discharge]], [[implementation]], [[fulfilment]], [[utilization]]
**5** (*military*) a [[manoeuvre]] or simulated combat [[operation]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[manoeuvre]], [[campaign]], [[operation]], [[movement]], deployment
 *vb* **-cising**, **-cised**
**6** to put into [[use]]; make [[use]] of: we urge all governments involved to exercise restraint
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put to use]], [[use]], [[apply]], [[employ]], [[exert]], [[utilize]], [[bring to bear]], [[avail yourself of]]
**7** to take exercise or perform exercises
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[train]], [[work out]], [[practise]], [[keep fit]], [[do exercises]]
**8** to [[practise]] using in order to develop or [[train]]: to exercise one's voice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[train]], [[work out]], [[practise]], [[keep fit]], [[do exercises]]
**9** to worry or vex: the government has been exercised by the threat from international criminal groups
**10** (*military*) to carry out simulated combat, manoeuvres, etc.
 **[[exerciser]]** *n*
