**remove** *vb* **-moving**, **-moved**
**1** to [[take away]] and place elsewhere
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[take away]], [[detach]], [[displace]]
**2** to take (clothing) off
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[take off]]
**3** to [[get rid of]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[erase]], [[eliminate]], [[take out]]
**4** to [[dismiss]] (someone) from office
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dismiss]], [[eliminate]], [[get rid of]], [[discharge]], [[abolish]], [[expel]], [[throw out]], [[oust]]
**5** (*formal*) to change the location of one's home or place of business
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move]], [[depart]], [[relocate]], flit (*Scot** & **N English* *dialect*)
 *n*
**6** the degree of difference: one remove away from complete rebuttal
**7** (*Brit*) (in certain schools) a class or form designed to prepare pupils for senior classes
