**manoeuvre** * or * (*US*) **maneuver** (man-**noo**-ver) *vb* **-vring**, **-vred*** or ***-vering**, **-vered**
**1** to move or do something with dexterity and skill: she manoeuvred the car easily into the parking space
**2** to manipulate a situation in order to gain some advantage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[scheme]], [[wangle]] (*informal*), [[machinate]]
**3** to perform a manoeuvre or manoeuvres
 *n*
**4** a [[movement]] or action requiring dexterity and skill
**5** a contrived, complicated, and possibly deceptive plan or action
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stratagem]], [[scheme]], [[trick]], [[tactic]], [[intrigue]], [[dodge]], [[ploy]], [[ruse]]
**6** **manoeuvres** military or naval exercises, usually on a large scale
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[movement]], [[operation]], [[exercise]], [[war game]]
**7** a change in course of a ship or aircraft, esp. a complicated one
**8** **room for manoeuvre** the possibility of changing one's plans or behaviour if it becomes necessary or desirable
 **[[manoeuvrable]]** * or * (*US*) **[[maneuverable]]** *adj* 
 **[[manoeuvrability]]** * or * (*US*) **[[maneuverability]]** *n*
