**conduct** *n*
**1** [[behaviour]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[behaviour]], [[ways]], [[bearing]], [[attitude]], [[manners]], [[demeanour]], [[deportment]]
**2** the [[management]] or [[handling]] of an activity or business
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[management]], [[running]], [[control]], [[handling]], [[administration]], [[direction]], [[organization]], guidance
 *vb*
**3** to [[carry out]]: the police are conducting an investigation into the affair
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[carry out]], [[run]], [[control]], [[manage]], [[direct]], [[handle]], [[organize]], [[administer]]
**4** **conduct oneself** to behave (oneself)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[conduct yourself]]**: behave yourself, act, carry yourself, acquit yourself, deport yourself, comport yourself
**5** to [[control]] (an orchestra or choir) by the movements of the hands or a baton
**6** to [[accompany]] and [[guide]] (people or a party): a conducted tour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accompany]], [[lead]], [[escort]], [[guide]], [[steer]], [[convey]], [[usher]]
**7** to transmit (heat or electricity)
