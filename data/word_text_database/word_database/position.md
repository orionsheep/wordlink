**position** *n*
**1** [[place]] or [[location]]: the hotel is in an elevated position above the River Wye
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[location]], [[place]], [[point]], [[area]], [[post]], [[situation]], [[station]], [[spot]]
**2** the proper or usual [[place]]
**3** the way in which a person or thing is placed or arranged: an upright position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[posture]], [[attitude]], [[arrangement]], [[pose]], [[stance]]
**4** [[point of view]]; [[attitude]]: the Catholic Church's position on contraception
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attitude]], [[view]], [[perspective]], [[point of view]], [[opinion]], [[belief]], [[stance]], [[outlook]]
**5** social [[status]], esp. high social [[standing]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[status]], [[place]], [[standing]], [[footing]], [[station]], [[rank]], [[reputation]], [[importance]]
**6** a [[job]]; appointment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[job]], [[place]], [[post]], [[opening]], [[office]], [[role]], [[situation]], [[duty]]
**7** (*sport*) a player's allotted [[role]] or [[place]] in the playing [[area]]
**8** (*military*) a [[place]] occupied for tactical reasons
**9** **in a position to** able to: you were not in a position to repay the money
 *vb*
**10** to [[put]] in the proper or usual [[place]]; [[locate]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[place]], [[put]], [[set]], [[stand]], [[arrange]], [[locate]], [[lay out]]
 **[[positional]]** *adj*
