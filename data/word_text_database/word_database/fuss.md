**fuss** *n*
**1** needless activity and [[worry]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[commotion]], [[to-do]], [[bother]], [[stir]], [[excitement]], [[ado]], [[hue and cry]], [[palaver]]
**2** [[complaint]] or [[objection]]: it was silly to make a fuss over seating arrangements
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[complaint]], [[row]], [[protest]], [[objection]], [[trouble]], [[argument]], [[squabble]], [[furore]]
**3** an exhibition of affection or admiration: when I arrived my nephews made a big fuss of me
 *vb*
**4** to [[worry]] unnecessarily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[worry]], [[flap]] (*informal*), [[fret]], [[fidget]], [[take pains]], [[be agitated]], [[get worked up]]
**5** to be excessively concerned over trivial matters
**6** to [[bother]] (a person)
**7** **fuss over** to show great or excessive concern or affection for
