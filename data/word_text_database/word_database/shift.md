**shift** *vb*
**1** to [[move]] from one place or position to another
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move]], [[move around]], [[budge]]
**2** to pass (blame or responsibility) onto someone else: he was trying to shift the blame to me
**3** to [[change]] (gear) in a motor vehicle
**4** to remove or be removed: no detergent can shift these stains
**5** (*US*) to [[change]] for another or others
**6** (*slang*) to [[move]] quickly
 *n*
**7** the act or an instance of [[shifting]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[change]], [[shifting]], [[displacement]]
**8**
**A** a group of workers who work during a specific period
 **B** the period of time worked by such a group
**9** a method or scheme
**10** a loose-fitting straight underskirt or dress
