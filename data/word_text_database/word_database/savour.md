**savour** * or * (*US*) **savor** *vb*
**1** to [[enjoy]] and [[appreciate]] (food or drink) slowly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[enjoy]], [[appreciate]], [[relish]], [[delight in]], [[revel in]], [[luxuriate in]]
**2** to [[enjoy]] (a pleasure) for as long as possible: an experience to be savoured
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[relish]], [[delight in]], [[revel in]], [[luxuriate in]]
**3** **savour of**
**A** to have a suggestion of: that could savour of ostentation
 **B** to possess the [[taste]] or [[smell]] of: the vegetables savoured of coriander
 *n*
**4** the [[taste]] or [[smell]] of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flavour]], [[taste]], [[smell]], [[relish]], [[smack]], [[tang]], [[piquancy]]
**5** a slight but distinctive quality or trace
