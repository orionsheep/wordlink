**weak** *adj*
**1** lacking in physical or mental strength
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[feeble]], [[frail]], [[debilitated]], [[fragile]], [[sickly]], [[puny]], [[unsteady]], [[infirm]]
**2** (of a part of the body) not functioning as well as is normal: a weak heart
**3** liable to collapse or break: weak bridges
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fragile]], [[brittle]], [[flimsy]], [[fine]], [[delicate]], [[frail]], [[dainty]], [[breakable]]
**4** lacking in importance, influence, or strength: a weak government
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unsafe]], [[exposed]], [[vulnerable]], [[helpless]], [[unprotected]], [[defenceless]], [[unguarded]]
**5** (of a currency or shares) falling in price or characterized by falling prices
**6** lacking in moral strength; easily influenced
**7** not convincing: weak arguments
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unconvincing]], [[unsatisfactory]], [[lame]], [[flimsy]], [[pathetic]]
**8** lacking strength or power: his voice was weak
**9** not having a strong flavour: weak coffee
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tasteless]], [[thin]], [[diluted]], [[watery]], [[runny]], insipid
 **[[weakly]]** *adv*
