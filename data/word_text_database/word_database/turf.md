**turf** *n*, *pl* **turfs*** or ***turves**
**1** a layer of thick even [[grass]] with roots and soil attached: a short turf rich in wild flowers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grass]], [[sward]]
**2** a piece cut from this layer: we spent the afternoon digging turves
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sod]]
**3** (*informal*)
**A** the area where a person lives and feels at home: my boyhood turf of east Cork
 **B** a person's area of knowledge or influence: when Kate is at work, she's on her own turf
**4** **[[the turf]]**
**A** a track where horse races are run
 **B** horse racing as a sport or industry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[the turf]]**: horse-racing, the flat, racing
**5** same as **peat**
 *vb*
**6** to cover (an area of ground) with pieces of turf
