**recover** *vb*
**1** (of a person) to [[regain]] health, spirits, or composure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get better]], [[improve]], [[get well]], [[recuperate]], [[heal]], [[revive]], [[mend]], [[convalesce]]
**2** to [[regain]] a former and better condition: real wages have recovered from the recession
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rally]]
**3** to find again or obtain the return of (something lost)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recoup]], [[restore]], [[get back]], [[regain]], [[retrieve]], [[reclaim]], [[redeem]], [[recapture]]
**4** to [[get back]] or make good (expense or loss)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[save]], [[rescue]], [[retrieve]], [[salvage]], [[reclaim]]
**5** to obtain (useful substances) from waste
**6** (*law*) to gain (something) by the judgment of a court: it should be possible to recover damages
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recoup]], [[restore]], [[get back]], [[regain]], [[retrieve]], [[reclaim]], [[redeem]], [[recapture]]
 **[[recoverable]]** *adj*
