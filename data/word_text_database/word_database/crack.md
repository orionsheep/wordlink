**crack** *vb*
**1** to [[break]] or split without complete separation of the parts
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cleave]], [[break]]
**2** to [[break]] with a sudden sharp sound
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[snap]], [[ring]], [[crash]], [[burst]], [[explode]], [[pop]], [[detonate]]
**3** to make or cause to make a sudden sharp sound: the coachman cracked his whip
**4** (of the voice) to become harsh or change pitch suddenly
**5** (*informal*) to fail or [[break down]]: she had cracked under the strain of losing her job
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[break down]], [[collapse]], [[yield]], [[give in]], [[give way]], [[succumb]], [[lose control]], [[be overcome]]
**6** to [[yield]] or cease to resist: he had cracked under torture
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[break down]], [[collapse]], [[yield]], [[give in]], [[give way]], [[succumb]], [[lose control]], [[be overcome]]
**7** to [[hit]] with a forceful or resounding [[blow]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hit]], [[clip]] (*informal*), [[slap]], [[smack]], [[clout]] (*informal*), [[cuff]], [[whack]]
**8** to [[break]] into or force open: it'll take me longer if I have to crack the safe myself
**9** to [[solve]] or [[decipher]] (a code or problem)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[solve]], [[work out]], [[resolve]], [[clear up]], [[fathom]], [[decipher]], [[suss]] (out) (*slang*), [[get to the bottom of]]
**10** (*informal*) to tell (a [[joke]])
**11** to [[break]] (a molecule) into smaller molecules or radicals by heat or catalysis as in the distillation of petroleum
**12** to open (a bottle) for drinking
**13** **crack it** (*informal*) to achieve something
 *n*
**14** a sudden sharp noise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[snap]], [[pop]], [[crash]], [[burst]], [[explosion]], [[clap]], [[report]]
**15** a [[break]] or [[fracture]] without complete separation of the two parts
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[break]], [[chink]], [[gap]], [[fracture]], [[rift]], [[cleft]], [[crevice]], [[fissure]]
**16** a narrow opening or [[fissure]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[break]], [[chink]], [[gap]], [[fracture]], [[rift]], [[cleft]], [[crevice]], [[fissure]]
**17** (*informal*) a sharp [[blow]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[blow]], [[slap]], [[smack]], [[clout]] (*informal*), [[cuff]], [[whack]], [[clip]] (*informal*)
**18** **crack of dawn** daybreak
**19** a broken or cracked tone of voice
**20** (*informal*) an attempt
**21** (*informal*) a gibe or [[joke]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[joke]], [[dig]], [[gag]] (*informal*), [[quip]], [[jibe]], [[wisecrack]], [[witticism]], [[funny remark]]
**22** (*slang*) a highly addictive form of cocaine
**23** (*chiefly* *Irish* *informal*) fun; informal entertainment
**24** **a fair crack of the whip** (*informal*) a fair chance or opportunity
 *adj*
**25** (*slang*) [[first-class]] or [[excellent]]: crack troops
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[first-class]], [[choice]], [[excellent]], [[ace]] (*informal*), [[elite]], [[superior]], [[world-class]], first-rate
 See also **crack down**
