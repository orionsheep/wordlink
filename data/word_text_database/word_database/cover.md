**cover** *vb*
**1** to place something over so as to [[protect]] or [[conceal]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[conceal]], [[hide]], [[mask]], [[disguise]], [[obscure]], [[veil]], [[cloak]], [[shroud]]
**2** to put a garment on; [[clothe]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clothe]], [[dress]], [[wrap]], [[envelop]]
**3** to extend over or lie thickly on the surface of: the ground was covered with dry leaves
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[overlay]], [[blanket]]
**4** (sometimes foll. by *up*) to [[screen]] or [[conceal]]; [[hide]] from view
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[conceal]], [[hide]], [[mask]], [[disguise]], [[obscure]], [[veil]], [[cloak]], [[shroud]]
**5** to [[travel over]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[travel over]], [[cross]], [[traverse]], pass through *or* over
**6** to [[protect]] (an individual or group) by taking up a position from which fire may be returned if those being protected are fired upon
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[protect]], [[guard]], [[defend]], [[shield]]
**7** to keep a gun aimed at
**8**
**A** to insure against loss or risk
 **B** to [[provide for]] (loss or risk) by [[insurance]]
**9** to include or [[deal with]]: the course covers accounting, economics, statistics, law, and computer applications
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[consider]], [[deal with]], [[investigate]], [[describe]], [[tell of]]
**10** to act as reporter or photographer on (a news event) for a newspaper or magazine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[report on]], [[write about]], [[commentate on]], [[relate]], [[tell of]], [[narrate]], [[write up]]
**11** (of a sum of money) to be enough to [[pay for]] (something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pay for]], [[fund]], [[provide for]], [[offset]], [[be enough for]]
**12** (*music*) to record a cover version of
**13** (*sport*) to [[guard]] or obstruct (an opponent, team-mate, or area)
**14** **cover for** to deputize for (a person)
**15** (foll. by *for* or *up for*) to provide an alibi (for): can my men count on your friends at City Hall to cover for us?
 *n*
**16** anything which covers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[covering]], [[case]], [[top]], [[coating]], [[envelope]], [[lid]], [[canopy]], [[wrapper]]
**17** a [[blanket]] or bedspread
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bedclothes]], [[bedding]], [[sheet]], [[blanket]], [[quilt]], [[duvet]], [[eiderdown]]
**18** the outside of a book or magazine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jacket]], [[case]], [[wrapper]]
**19** a [[pretext]] or [[disguise]]: he claimed UN resolutions were being used as a cover for planned American aggression
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disguise]], [[front]] (*informal*), [[screen]], [[mask]], [[veil]], [[façade]], [[pretext]], [[smoke screen]]
**20** an [[envelope]] or other postal wrapping: under plain cover
**21** an individual table setting
**22** [[insurance]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[insurance]], [[protection]], [[compensation]], [[indemnity]], [[reimbursement]]
**23** a cover version
**24** **the covers** (*cricket*) the area roughly at right angles to the pitch on the off side and about halfway to the boundary
**25** **break cover** to come out from a shelter or hiding place
**26** **take cover** to make for a place of safety or shelter
**27** **under cover** protected or in secret
 **[[covering]]** *adj*, *n*
