**dash** *vb*
**1** to move hastily; [[rush]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[run]], [[race]], [[shoot]], [[fly]], [[career]], [[speed]], [[tear]]
**2** to [[hurl]]; crash: deep-sea rollers dashing spray over jagged rocks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw]], [[cast]], [[pitch]], [[slam]], [[toss]], [[hurl]], [[fling]], [[chuck]] (*informal*)
**3** to frustrate: prospects for peace have been dashed
 *n*
**4** a sudden quick movement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[run]], [[race]], [[sprint]], [[dart]], [[spurt]], [[sortie]]
**5** a small amount: a dash of milk
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[little]], [[bit]], [[shot]] (*informal*), [[touch]], [[spot]] (*Brit*), [[trace]], [[hint]]
**6** a mixture of [[style]] and courage: the commander's dash did not impress him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[style]], [[spirit]], [[flair]] (*informal*), [[flourish]], [[verve]], [[panache]], [[élan]], [[brio]]
**7** the punctuation mark (—), used to indicate a change of subject
**8** the symbol (–), used in combination with the symbol *dot* (.) in Morse code
