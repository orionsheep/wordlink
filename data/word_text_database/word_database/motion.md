**motion** *n*
**1** the process of continual change in the position of an object; [[movement]]: the motion of the earth round the sun <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**kinetic**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[movement]], [[mobility]], [[travel]], [[progress]], [[flow]], [[locomotion]]
**2** a [[movement]] or [[gesture]]: he made stabbing motions with the spear
**3** a way or style of moving: massage the back with steady circular motions
**4** a formal [[proposal]] to be discussed and voted on in a debate or meeting
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[proposal]], [[suggestion]], [[recommendation]], [[proposition]], [[submission]]
**5** (*Brit*)
**A** the evacuation of the bowels
 **B** excrement
**6** **go through the motions** to do something mechanically or without sincerity
**7** **set in motion** to make operational or start functioning
 *vb*
**8** to [[signal]] or [[direct]] a person by a [[movement]] or [[gesture]]: she motioned to me to sit down
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gesture]], [[direct]], [[wave]], [[signal]], [[nod]], [[beckon]], [[gesticulate]]
 **[[motionless]]** *adj*
