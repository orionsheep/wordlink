**whole** *adj*
**1** constituting or referring to all of something: I'd spent my whole allowance by Saturday afternoon
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[complete]], [[full]], [[total]], [[entire]], [[uncut]], [[undivided]], [[unabridged]]
**2** [[unbroken]] or [[undamaged]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[undamaged]], [[intact]], [[unscathed]], [[unbroken]], [[untouched]], [[unharmed]], in one piece
 *adv*
**3** in an [[undivided]] or [[unbroken]] piece: truffles are cooked whole
**4** (*informal*) completely or entirely: a whole new theory of treatment
 *n*
**5** all there is of a thing: the whole of my salary
**6** a collection of parts viewed together as a [[unit]]: taking Great Britain as a whole
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unit]], [[ensemble]], [[entirety]], [[totality]]
**7** **[[on the whole]]**
**A** taking all things into consideration: [[on the whole]] he has worked about one year out of twelve
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[on the whole]]**: all in all, altogether, all things considered, by and large
 **B** in general: [[on the whole]] they were not successful
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[on the whole]]**: generally, in general, as a rule, chiefly, mainly, mostly, principally, on average
 **[[wholeness]]** *n*
