**warp** *vb*
**1** (esp. of wooden objects) to be twisted out of shape, for example by heat or damp
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[distort]], [[bend]], [[twist]], [[buckle]], [[deform]], [[disfigure]], [[contort]], [[malform]]
**2** to [[distort]] or influence in a negative way: love warps judgment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pervert]], [[twist]], [[corrupt]], [[degrade]], [[deprave]], [[debase]], [[debauch]], lead astray
 *n*
**3** a fault or an irregularity in the shape or surface of an object
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[twist]], [[bend]], [[defect]], [[flaw]], [[distortion]], [[imperfection]], [[kink]], [[contortion]]
**4** a fault or deviation in someone's character
**5** the yarns arranged lengthways on a loom through which the weft yarns are woven
 **[[warped]]** *adj*
