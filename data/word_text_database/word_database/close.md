**close**^2 *adj*
**1** [[near]] in space or time
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[near]], [[neighbouring]], [[nearby]], [[handy]], [[adjacent]], [[adjoining]], [[cheek by jowl]]
**2** [[intimate]]: we were such close friends in those days
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intimate]], [[loving]], [[familiar]], [[thick]] (*informal*), [[attached]], [[devoted]], [[confidential]], [[inseparable]]
**3** [[near]] in relationship: she seems to have had no close relatives
**4** [[careful]], strict, or searching: their research will not stand up to close scrutiny
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[careful]], [[detailed]], [[intense]], [[minute]], [[thorough]], [[rigorous]], [[painstaking]]
**5** having the parts [[near]] together: a close formation
**6** [[near]] to the surface; short: an NCO's haircut, cropped close on top, shaved clean at sides and back
**7** almost equal: a close game
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[even]], [[level]], [[neck and neck]], [[fifty-fifty]] (*informal*), [[evenly matched]]
**8** not deviating or varying greatly from something: a close resemblance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[noticeable]], [[marked]], [[strong]], [[distinct]], [[pronounced]]
**9** confined or enclosed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stifling]], [[oppressive]], [[suffocating]], [[stuffy]], [[humid]], [[sweltering]], [[airless]], [[muggy]]
**10** [[oppressive]], heavy, or [[airless]]: damp, close weather
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stifling]], [[oppressive]], [[suffocating]], [[stuffy]], [[humid]], [[sweltering]], [[airless]], [[muggy]]
**11** strictly guarded: he had been placed in close arrest
**12** secretive or reticent
**13** miserly; not generous
**14** restricted as to public admission or membership
 *adv*
**15** [[closely]]; tightly
**16** [[near]] or in proximity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[imminent]], [[near]], [[impending]], [[at hand]], [[nigh]]
