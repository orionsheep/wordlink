**surprise** *n*
**1** the act of taking someone unawares: the element of surprise
**2** a sudden or unexpected event, gift, etc.: this is a nice surprise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shock]], [[revelation]], [[jolt]], [[bombshell]], [[eye-opener]] (*informal*)
**3** the feeling of being [[surprised]]; [[astonishment]]: to our great surprise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amazement]], [[astonishment]], [[wonder]], [[incredulity]]
**4** **take someone by surprise** to capture someone unexpectedly or catch someone unprepared
 *adj*
**5** causing surprise: a surprise attack
 *vb* **-prising**, **-prised**
**6** to cause (someone) to feel [[amazement]] or [[wonder]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amaze]], [[astonish]], [[stun]], [[startle]], [[stagger]], [[take aback]]
**7** to come upon or discover (someone) unexpectedly or suddenly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: catch unawares *or* off-guard, [[spring upon]]
**8** to capture or attack (someone) suddenly and without warning
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: catch unawares *or* off-guard, [[spring upon]]
**9** **surprise into** to provoke (someone) to unintended action by a trick or deception
