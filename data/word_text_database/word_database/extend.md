**extend** *vb*
**1** to make bigger or longer than before: they extended the house by building a conservatory
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[widen]], [[increase]], [[expand]], [[add to]], [[enhance]], [[supplement]], [[enlarge]], [[broaden]]
**2** to [[reach]] to a certain distance or in a certain direction: the suburbs extend for many miles
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spread out]], [[reach]], [[stretch]]
**3** to [[last]] for a certain time: in Norway maternity leave extends to 52 weeks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[last]], [[continue]], [[go on]], [[stretch]], [[carry on]]
**4** to [[broaden]] the meaning or scope of: the law was extended to ban all guns
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[widen]], [[increase]], [[expand]], [[add to]], [[enhance]], [[supplement]], [[enlarge]], [[broaden]]
**5** to make something exist or be valid for longer than before: her visa was extended for three months
**6** to [[present]] or [[offer]]: a tradition of extending asylum to refugees
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[offer]], [[present]], [[confer]], [[stick out]], [[impart]], [[proffer]]
**7** to straighten or [[stretch out]] (part of the body): she extended a hand in welcome
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stretch]], [[stretch out]], [[spread out]], [[straighten out]]
**8** **extend oneself** to make use of all one's ability or strength, often because forced to: she'll have to really extend herself if she wants to win
 **[[extendable]]** *adj*
