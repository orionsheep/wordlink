**prejudice** *n*
**1** an unreasonable or unfair dislike or preference
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bias]], [[preconception]], [[partiality]], [[preconceived notion]], [[prejudgment]]
**2** [[intolerance]] of or dislike for people because they belong to a specific race, religion, or group: class prejudice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[discrimination]], [[injustice]], [[intolerance]], [[bigotry]], [[unfairness]], [[chauvinism]], [[narrow-mindedness]], [[faith hate]]
**3** the act or condition of holding such opinions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[discrimination]], [[injustice]], [[intolerance]], [[bigotry]], [[unfairness]], [[chauvinism]], [[narrow-mindedness]], [[faith hate]]
**4** [[harm]] or detriment: conduct to the prejudice of good order and military discipline
**5** **without prejudice** (*law*) without [[harm]] to an existing right or claim
 *vb* **-dicing**, **-diced**
**6** to cause (someone) to have a prejudice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bias]], [[influence]], [[colour]], [[poison]], [[distort]], [[slant]], [[predispose]]
**7** to [[harm]]: the incident prejudiced his campaign
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harm]], [[damage]], [[hurt]], [[injure]], [[mar]], [[undermine]], [[spoil]], [[impair]], crool *or* cruel (*Austral* *slang*)
