**plunge** *vb* **plunging**, **plunged**
**1** (usually foll. by *into*) to thrust or throw (something or oneself) forcibly or suddenly: they plunged into the sea; he plunged the knife in to the hilt
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[submerge]], [[dip]]
**2** to throw or be thrown into a certain condition: the room was plunged into darkness
**3** (usually foll. by *into*) to involve or become involved deeply (in)
**4** to move swiftly or impetuously
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hurtle]], [[charge]], [[career]], [[jump]], [[tear]], [[rush]], [[dive]], [[dash]]
**5** to [[descend]] very suddenly or steeply: temperatures were plunging
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[descend]], [[fall]], [[drop]], [[crash]], [[pitch]], [[sink]], [[dive]], [[tumble]]
 *n*
**6** a leap or [[dive]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dive]], [[jump]], [[duck]], [[descent]]
**7** (*informal*) a swim
**8** a pitching motion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fall]], [[crash]] (*informal*), [[slump]], [[drop]], [[tumble]]
**9** **take the plunge** (*informal*) to make a risky decision which cannot be reversed later
