**tread** *vb* **treading**, **trod**, **trodden*** or ***trod**
**1** to set one's foot down on or in something: he trod on some dog's dirt
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[step]], [[walk]], [[march]], [[pace]], [[stamp]], [[stride]], [[hike]]
**2** to crush or squash by treading (on): treading on a biscuit
**3** to [[walk]] along (a path or road)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[step]], [[walk]], [[march]], [[pace]], [[stamp]], [[stride]], [[hike]]
**4** **tread carefully *or* warily** to proceed in a delicate or tactful manner
**5** **tread water** to stay afloat in an upright position by moving the legs in a walking motion
 *n*
**6** a way of walking or the sound of walking: he walked, with a heavy tread, up the stairs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[step]], [[walk]], [[pace]], [[stride]], [[footstep]], [[gait]], [[footfall]]
**7** the top surface of a [[step]] in a staircase
**8** the pattern of grooves in the outer surface of a tyre that helps it grip the road
**9** the part of a shoe that is generally in contact with the ground
