**bounce** *vb* **bouncing**, **bounced**
**1** (of a ball, etc.) to [[rebound]] from an impact
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rebound]], [[recoil]], [[ricochet]]
**2** to cause (a ball, etc.) to hit a solid surface and [[spring]] back
**3** to move or cause to move suddenly; [[spring]]: I bounced down the stairs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bound]], [[spring]], [[jump]], [[leap]], [[skip]], [[gambol]]
**4** (*slang*) (of a bank) to send (a cheque) back or (of a cheque) to be sent back unredeemed because of lack of funds in the account
 *n*
**5** the action of rebounding from an impact
**6** a [[leap]] or [[jump]]
**7** [[springiness]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[springiness]], [[give]], [[spring]], [[resilience]], [[elasticity]], [[recoil]]
**8** (*informal*) vitality; [[vigour]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[life]], [[go]] (*informal*), [[energy]], [[zip]] (*informal*), [[vigour]], [[exuberance]], [[dynamism]], [[vivacity]]
**9** (*informal*) a temporary increase or rise: a sales bounce
 **[[bouncy]]** *adj*
