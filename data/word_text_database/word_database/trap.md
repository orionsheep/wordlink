**trap** *n*
**1** a device or hole in which something, esp. an animal, is caught: a fox trap
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[snare]], [[net]], [[gin]], [[pitfall]], [[noose]]
**2** a plan for tricking a person into being caught unawares
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trick]], [[set-up]] (*informal*), [[deception]], [[ploy]], [[ruse]], [[trickery]], [[subterfuge]], [[stratagem]]
**3** a situation from which it is difficult to escape: caught in the poverty trap
**4** a bend in a pipe that contains standing water to prevent the passage of gases
**5** a boxlike stall in which greyhounds are enclosed before the start of a race
**6** a device that hurls clay pigeons into the air to be fired at
**7** See **trap door**
**8** a light two-wheeled carriage: a pony and trap
**9** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*) the mouth: shut your trap!
 *vb* **trapping**, **trapped**
**10** to [[catch]] (an animal) in a trap
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[catch]], [[snare]], [[ensnare]], [[entrap]], [[take]], [[corner]], [[bag]], [[lay hold of]]
**11** to [[catch]] (someone) by a [[trick]]: the police trapped the drug dealers by posing as potential customers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[capture]], [[catch]], [[arrest]], [[seize]], [[take]], [[secure]], [[collar]] (*informal*), [[apprehend]]
**12** to hold or confine in an unpleasant situation from which it is difficult to escape: trapped in the rubble of collapsed buildings
