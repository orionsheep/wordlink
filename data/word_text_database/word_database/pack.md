**pack** *n*
**1** a [[bundle]] or [[load]] carried on the back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bundle]], [[parcel]], [[load]], [[burden]], [[rucksack]], [[knapsack]], [[back pack]], [[kitbag]]
**2** (*Brit** & **NZ*) a complete set of playing cards
**3** a [[group]] of animals that hunt together: a pack of hounds
**4** any collection of people or things: a pack of lies
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[group]], [[crowd]], [[company]], [[band]], [[troop]], [[gang]], [[bunch]] (*informal*), [[mob]]
**5** (*chiefly* *US** & **Canad*) same as **[[packet]]**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[packet]], [[box]], [[package]], carton
 *vb*
**6** to put (articles) in a case or container for moving
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[package]], [[load]], [[store]], [[bundle]], [[stow]]
**7** to roll (articles) up into a [[bundle]]
**8** to [[press]] tightly together; [[cram]]: thousands of people packed into the city's main square
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cram]], [[crowd]], [[press]], [[fill]], [[stuff]], [[jam]], [[ram]], [[compress]]
**9** (foll. by *off*) to send away hastily: their young son came in to say good night and was packed off to bed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pack someone off]]**: send away, dismiss, send packing (*informal*)
 See also **pack in**
