**have on** *vb*
**1** to wear: he had a pair of long trousers on
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[have something on]]**: wear, be wearing, be dressed in, be clothed in, be attired in
**2** to have a commitment: what do you have on this afternoon?
**3** (*informal*) to trick or tease: he's having you on
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[have someone on]]**: tease, kid (*informal*), wind up (*Brit* *slang*), trick, deceive, take the mickey, pull someone's leg
**4** to have (information, esp. when incriminating) about (a person): she has something on him
