**note** *n*
**1** a brief informal [[letter]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[message]], [[letter]], [[communication]], [[memo]], [[memorandum]], [[epistle]], email *or* e-mail, [[text]]
**2** a brief [[record]] in writing for future reference
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[record]], [[reminder]], [[memo]], [[memorandum]], [[jotting]], [[minute]]
**3** a critical [[comment]] or explanation in a book
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[annotation]], [[comment]], [[remark]]
**4** an official written [[communication]], as from a government or from a doctor
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[document]], [[form]], [[record]], [[certificate]]
**5** short for **banknote**
**6** (*Brit** & **NZ*) a musical [[sound]] of a particular pitch
**7** a written [[symbol]] representing the pitch and duration of a musical [[sound]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[symbol]], [[mark]], [[sign]], [[indication]], [[token]]
**8** (*chiefly* *Brit*) a key on a piano, organ, or other keyboard instrument
**9** a particular feeling or atmosphere: an optimistic note
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tone]], [[touch]], [[trace]], [[hint]], [[sound]]
**10** a distinctive vocal [[sound]], as of a type of animal
**11** a [[sound]] used as a signal or warning: the note to retreat was sounded
**12** **of note**
**A** distinguished or famous
 **B** important: nothing of note
**13** **strike the right note** to behave appropriately
**14** **take note of** to pay attention to
 *vb* **noting**, **noted**
**15** to [[notice]]; pay attention to: such criticism should be noted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[notice]], [[see]], [[observe]], [[perceive]]
**16** to make a written note of: he noted it in his diary
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[write down]], [[record]], [[scribble]], [[set down]], [[jot down]]
**17** to [[remark]] upon: I note that you do not wear shoes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mention]], [[record]], [[mark]], [[indicate]], [[register]], [[remark]]
