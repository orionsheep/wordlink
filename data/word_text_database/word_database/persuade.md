**persuade** *vb* **-suading**, **-suaded**
**1** to make (someone) do something by reason or charm: we tried to persuade him not to come up the mountain with us
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: talk (someone) into, [[urge]], [[influence]], win (someone) over, [[induce]], [[sway]], [[entice]], [[coax]]
**2** to [[cause to believe]]; [[convince]]: persuading people of the need for enforced environmental protection may be difficult
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[convince]], [[satisfy]], [[assure]], [[cause to believe]]
 **[[persuadable]]** *adj*
