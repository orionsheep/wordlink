**bother** *vb*
**1** to take the time or [[trouble]]: don't bother to come with me
**2** to give annoyance, pain, or [[trouble]] to
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trouble]], [[concern]], [[worry]], [[alarm]], [[disturb]], [[disconcert]], [[perturb]]
**3** to [[trouble]] (a person) by repeatedly disturbing; [[pester]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pester]], [[plague]], [[harass]], [[hassle]] (*informal*), inconvenience
 *n*
**4** a state of [[worry]], [[trouble]], or confusion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trouble]], [[problem]], [[worry]], [[difficulty]], [[fuss]], [[irritation]], [[hassle]] (*informal*), [[nuisance]], [[uphill]] (*S African*)
**5** a person or thing that causes [[fuss]], [[trouble]], or annoyance
**6** (*informal*) a disturbance or fight: a spot of bother
 *interj*
**7** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ*) an exclamation of slight annoyance
