**slash** *vb*
**1** to [[cut]] (a person or thing) with sharp sweeping strokes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cut]], [[slit]], [[gash]], [[lacerate]], [[score]], [[rend]] (*literary*), [[rip]], [[hack]]
**2** to make large gashes in: I slashed the tyres of his van
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cut]], [[slit]], [[gash]], [[lacerate]], [[score]], [[rend]] (*literary*), [[rip]], [[hack]]
**3** to [[reduce]] drastically: to slash costs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reduce]], [[cut]], [[decrease]], [[drop]], [[lower]], [[moderate]], [[diminish]], [[cut down]]
**4** to criticize harshly
 *n*
**5** a sharp sweeping stroke
**6** a [[cut]] made by such a stroke
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cut]], [[slit]], [[gash]], [[rent]], [[rip]], [[incision]], [[laceration]]
