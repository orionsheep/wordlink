**whirl** *vb*
**1** to [[spin]] or [[turn]] [[round]] very fast
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spin]], [[turn]], [[twist]], [[rotate]], [[twirl]]
**2** to seem to [[spin]] from dizziness or [[confusion]]: my mind whirled with half-formed thoughts
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[feel dizzy]], [[swim]], [[spin]], [[reel]], go [[round]]
 *n*
**3** the act or an instance of whirling: he grasps her by the waist and gives her a whirl
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[revolution]], [[turn]], [[roll]], [[spin]], [[twist]], [[swirl]], [[rotation]], [[twirl]]
**4** a [[round]] of intense activity: the social whirl of Paris
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bustle]], [[round]], [[series]], [[succession]], [[flurry]], [[merry-go-round]]
**5** a confused state: my thoughts are in a whirl
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[confusion]], [[daze]], [[dither]] (*chiefly* *Brit*), [[giddiness]]
**6** **give something a whirl** (*informal*) to try something new
