**slick** *adj*
**1** (esp. of speech) easy and persuasive: a slick answer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[glib]], [[smooth]], [[plausible]], [[polished]], [[specious]]
**2** skilfully devised or executed: a slick marketing effort
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[skilful]], [[deft]], [[adroit]], [[dexterous]], [[professional]], [[polished]]
**3** (*informal*, *chiefly* *US** & **Canad*) shrewd; sly
**4** (*informal*) well-made and attractive, but superficial: a slick publication
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[glib]], [[smooth]], [[plausible]], [[polished]], [[specious]]
**5** (*chiefly* *US** & **Canad*) slippery
 *n*
**6** a slippery area, esp. a patch of oil floating on water
 *vb*
**7** to make [[smooth]] or shiny: long hair slicked back with gel
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[smooth]], [[sleek]], [[plaster down]]
