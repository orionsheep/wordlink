**sag** *vb* **sagging**, **sagged**
**1** to [[sink]] in the middle, under weight or pressure: the bed sagged nearly to the floor
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[sink]], [[slump]], [[flop]], [[droop]], [[loll]]
**2** (of courage or spirits) to [[weaken]] or [[tire]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decline]], [[tire]], [[flag]], [[weaken]], [[wilt]], [[wane]], [[droop]]
**3** (of clothes) to [[hang loosely]] or unevenly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sink]], [[bag]], [[droop]], [[fall]], [[slump]], [[dip]], [[give way]], [[hang loosely]]
**4** to [[fall]] in value: the stock market sagged
 *n*
**5** the act or state of sagging
 **[[saggy]]** *adj*
