**handle** *n*
**1** the part of an object that is held or operated in order that it may be used
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grip]], [[hilt]], [[haft]], [[stock]]
**2** a small lever used to open and close a door or window
**3** (*slang*) a person's name
**4** a means of understanding or dealing with something: trying to get a handle on why companies borrow money
**5** **fly off the handle** (*informal*) to become suddenly extremely angry
 *vb* **-dling**, **-dled**
**6** to hold, move, operate, or touch with the hands
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[control]], [[manage]], [[direct]], [[guide]], [[manipulate]], [[manoeuvre]]
**7** to have responsibility for: she handles all their affairs personally
**8** to [[manage]] successfully: I can handle this challenge
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[manage]], [[deal with]], [[tackle]], [[cope with]]
**9** to discuss (a subject)
**10** to [[deal with]] in a specified way: the affair was neatly handled
**11** to trade or deal in (specified merchandise): we handle 1800 properties in Normandy
**12** to react or respond in a specified way to operation or [[control]]: it's light and handles well
 **[[handling]]** *n*
