**turn on** *vb*
**1** to cause (something) to operate by turning a knob, pushing a button, etc.: turn on the radio, please
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[turn something on]]**: switch on, activate, start, start up, ignite, kick-start
**2** to attack (someone), esp. without warning: the Labrador turned on me
**3** (*informal*) to produce suddenly or automatically: turning on that bland smile
**4** (*slang*) to arouse emotionally or sexually
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[turn someone on]]**: arouse, attract, excite, thrill, stimulate, please, titillate
**5** to depend or hinge on: the match turned on a contentious decision
 *n* **turn-on**
**6** (*slang*) a person or thing that causes emotional or sexual arousal
