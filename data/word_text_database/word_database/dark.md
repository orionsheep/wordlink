**dark** *adj*
**1** having little or no light
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dim]], [[murky]], [[shady]], [[shadowy]], [[grey]], [[dingy]], [[unlit]], [[poorly lit]]
**2** (of a colour) reflecting little light: dark brown
**3** (of hair or skin) brown or [[black]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[black]], [[brunette]], [[ebony]], [[dark-skinned]], [[sable]], [[dusky]], [[swarthy]]
**4** (of thoughts or ideas) [[gloomy]] or [[sad]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gloomy]], [[sad]], [[grim]], [[miserable]], [[bleak]], [[dismal]], [[pessimistic]], [[melancholy]]
**5** [[sinister]] or [[evil]]: a dark deed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evil]], [[foul]], [[sinister]], [[vile]], [[wicked]], [[infernal]] (*informal*)
**6** sullen or angry: a dark scowl
**7** [[secret]] or [[mysterious]]: keep it dark
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[secret]], [[hidden]], [[mysterious]], concealed
 *n*
**8** absence of light; [[darkness]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[darkness]], [[shadows]], [[gloom]], [[dusk]] (*poetic*), [[obscurity]], [[murk]], [[dimness]], [[semi-darkness]]
**9** [[night]] or [[nightfall]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[night]], [[twilight]], [[evening]], [[evo]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[dusk]], [[night-time]], [[nightfall]]
**10** **in the dark** in ignorance
 **[[darkly]]** *adv* 
 **[[darkness]]** *n*
