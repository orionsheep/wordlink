**rich** *adj*
**1** owning a lot of money or property
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wealthy]], [[affluent]], [[well-off]], [[loaded]] (*slang*), [[prosperous]], [[well-heeled]] (*informal*), [[well-to-do]], [[moneyed]], [[minted]] (*Brit* *slang*)
**2** well supplied (with a desirable substance or quality): a country rich with cultural interest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[well-stocked]], [[full]], [[productive]], [[ample]], [[abundant]], [[plentiful]], [[copious]], [[well-supplied]]
**3** having an abundance of natural resources, minerals, etc.: a land rich in unexploited minerals
**4** producing abundantly: the island is a blend of hilly moorland and rich farmland
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fruitful]], [[productive]], [[fertile]], [[prolific]]
**5** luxuriant or [[prolific]]: the meadows rich with corn
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[abounding]], [[luxurious]], [[lush]], [[abundant]]
**6** (of food) containing much fat or sugar
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[full-bodied]], [[sweet]], [[fatty]], [[tasty]], [[creamy]], [[luscious]], [[succulent]]
**7** having a [[full-bodied]] flavour: a gloriously rich Cabernet-dominated wine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[full-bodied]], [[sweet]], [[fatty]], [[tasty]], [[creamy]], [[luscious]], [[succulent]]
**8** (of colour) intense or vivid: her hair had a rich auburn tint
**9** (of sound or a voice) [[full]] or resonant
**10** very amusing or ridiculous: a rich joke
**11** (of a fuel-air mixture) containing a relatively high proportion of fuel
 **[[richness]]** *n*
