**empty** *adj* **-tier**, **-tiest**
**1** containing nothing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bare]], [[clear]], [[abandoned]], [[deserted]], [[vacant]], [[free]], [[void]] (*old-fashioned*), [[desolate]]
**2** without inhabitants; unoccupied
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bare]], [[clear]], [[abandoned]], [[deserted]], [[vacant]], [[free]], [[void]] (*old-fashioned*), [[desolate]]
**3** without purpose, substance, or value: he contemplated yet another empty weekend
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[worthless]], [[meaningless]], [[hollow]], [[pointless]], [[futile]], [[senseless]], [[fruitless]], [[inane]]
**4** [[insincere]] or trivial: empty words
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meaningless]], [[cheap]], [[hollow]], [[vain]], [[idle]], [[futile]], [[insincere]]
**5** (*informal*) drained of energy or emotion
**6** (*maths*, *logic*) (of a set or class) containing no members
 *vb* **-ties**, **-tying**, **-tied**
**7** to make or become empty
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clear]], [[drain]], [[void]], [[unload]], [[pour out]], [[unpack]], [[remove the contents of]]
**8** to remove from something: they emptied out the remains of the tin of paint
 *n*, *pl* **-ties**
**9** an empty container, esp. a bottle
 **[[emptiness]]** *n*
