**organization** * or ***-nisation** *n*
**1** an organized [[group]] of people, such as a club, society, union, or business
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[group]], [[company]], [[party]], [[body]], [[association]], [[band]], [[institution]], [[corporation]]
**2** the act of organizing: setting up the European tour took a lot of organization
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[management]], [[running]], [[planning]], [[control]], [[operation]], [[handling]], [[structuring]], [[administration]]
**3** the [[structure]] and [[arrangement]] of the different parts of something: the report recommended radical changes in the organization of the social services department
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[structure]], [[form]], [[pattern]], [[make-up]], [[arrangement]], [[construction]], [[format]], [[formation]]
**4** the state of being organized: the material in this essay lacks any sort of organization
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[structure]], [[form]], [[pattern]], [[make-up]], [[arrangement]], [[construction]], [[format]], [[formation]]
 **[[organizational]]** * or ***-isational** *adj*
