**tunnel** *n*
**1** an underground [[passageway]], esp. one for trains or cars
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[passage]], [[underpass]], [[passageway]], [[subway]], [[channel]], [[hole]], [[shaft]]
**2** any [[passage]] or [[channel]] through or under something: the carpal tunnel
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[passage]], [[underpass]], [[passageway]], [[subway]], [[channel]], [[hole]], [[shaft]]
 *vb* **-nelling**, **-nelled** (*US*) **-neling**, **-neled**
**3** to make one's way through or under (something) by digging a tunnel: ten men succeeded in tunnelling out of the prisoner-of-war camp
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dig]], [[burrow]], [[mine]], [[bore]], [[drill]], [[excavate]]
**4** to [[dig]] a tunnel (through or under something): the idea of tunnelling under the English [[Channel]] had been around for a long time
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dig]], [[burrow]], [[mine]], [[bore]], [[drill]], [[excavate]]
