**introduce** *vb* **-ducing**, **-duced**
**1** to [[present]] (someone) by name (to another person)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[present]], [[acquaint]], [[make known]], [[familiarize]]
**2** to [[present]] (a radio or television programme)
**3** to [[present]] for consideration or approval: he introduced the bill to Parliament in 1967
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suggest]], [[air]], [[advance]], [[submit]], [[bring up]], [[put forward]], [[broach]], [[moot]]
**4** to bring into use: they introduced a new system into our political life
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bring in]], [[establish]], [[set up]], [[start]], [[found]], [[launch]], [[institute]], [[pioneer]]
**5** to [[insert]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[add]], [[insert]], [[inject]], [[throw in]] (*informal*), [[infuse]]
**6** **introduce to** to cause to experience for the first time: his father introduced him to golf
**7** **introduce with** to [[start]]: he introduced his talk with some music
 **[[introducible]]** *adj*
