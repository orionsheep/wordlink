**collapse** *vb* **-lapsing**, **-lapsed**
**1** to [[fall down]] or [[cave in]] suddenly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fall down]], [[fall]], [[give way]], [[subside]], [[cave in]], [[crumple]], [[fall apart at the seams]]
**2** to [[fail]] completely: a package holiday company which collapsed last year
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fail]], [[fold]], [[founder]], [[break down]], [[fall through]], [[come to nothing]], [[go belly-up]] (*informal*)
**3** to [[fall down]] from lack of strength, exhaustion, or illness: he collapsed with an asthma attack
**4** to sit down and rest because of tiredness or lack of energy: she collapsed in front of the telly when she got home
**5** to [[fold]] compactly, esp. for storage
 *n*
**6** the act of [[falling down]] or falling to pieces
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[falling down]], [[ruin]], [[falling apart]], [[cave-in]], [[disintegration]], [[subsidence]]
**7** a sudden [[failure]] or [[breakdown]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[failure]], [[slump]], [[breakdown]], [[flop]] (*informal*), [[downfall]]
