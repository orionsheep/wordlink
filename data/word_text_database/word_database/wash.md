**wash** *vb*
**1** to [[clean]] (oneself, part of one's body, or a thing) with soap or detergent and water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clean]], [[scrub]], [[sponge]], [[rinse]], [[scour]], [[cleanse]]
**2** (of a garment or fabric) to be capable of being washed without damage or loss of colour
**3** to move or be moved in a particular direction by water: houses may be washed away in floods
**4** (of [[waves]]) to flow or sweep against or over (a surface or object), often with a lapping sound
**5** (*informal*) to be acceptable or believable: that excuse won't wash
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be plausible]], [[stand up]], [[hold up]], [[pass muster]], [[hold water]], [[stick]], [[carry weight]], be convincing
 *n*
**6** the act or process of washing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[laundering]], [[cleaning]], [[clean]], [[cleansing]]
**7** all the clothes, etc. to be washed together on one occasion
**8** a thin [[layer]] of paint or ink: a pale wash of blue
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[coat]], [[film]], [[covering]], [[layer]], [[coating]], [[overlay]]
**9** the disturbance in the air or water produced at the rear of an aircraft, boat, or other moving object: we were hit by the wash of a large vessel
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[backwash]], [[slipstream]], [[path]], [[trail]], [[train]], [[track]], [[waves]], [[aftermath]]
**10** **come out in the wash** (*informal*) to become known or apparent in the course of time
 See also **wash out**, **wash up**
 **[[washable]]** *adj*
