**paper** *n*
**1** a flexible material made in sheets from wood pulp or other fibres and used for writing on, decorating walls, or wrapping parcels
**2** short for **[[newspaper]]**, **[[wallpaper]]**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[newspaper]], [[daily]], [[journal]], [[gazette]]
**3** **papers** [[documents]], such as a passport, which can identify the bearer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[documents]], [[records]], [[certificates]], [[identification]], [[deeds]], [[identity papers]], I.D. (*informal*)
**4** a set of [[examination]] questions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[examination]], [[test]], [[exam]]
**5** **papers** the collected [[diaries]] or [[letters]] of someone's private or public life
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[letters]], [[records]], [[documents]], [[file]], [[diaries]], [[archive]], [[paperwork]], [[dossier]]
**6** a lecture or an [[essay]] on a specific subject
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[essay]], [[article]], [[treatise]], [[dissertation]]
**7** **on paper** in theory, as opposed to fact: countless ideas which look good on paper just don't work in practice
 *adj*
**8** made of paper: paper towels; a paper bag
**9** recorded on paper but not yet existing in practice: a paper profit of more than $50 million
 *vb*
**10** to cover (walls) with [[wallpaper]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wallpaper]], [[hang]]
 **[[papery]]** *adj*
