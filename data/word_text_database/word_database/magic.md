**magic** *n*
**1** the supposed [[power]] to make things happen by using supernatural means
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sorcery]], [[wizardry]], [[witchcraft]], [[enchantment]], [[black art]], [[necromancy]]
**2** tricks done to entertain; [[conjuring]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[conjuring]], [[illusion]], [[trickery]], [[sleight of hand]], [[legerdemain]], [[prestidigitation]]
**3** any mysterious or extraordinary quality or [[power]]: the magic of Placido Domingo
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[charm]], [[power]], [[glamour]], [[fascination]], [[magnetism]], [[enchantment]], [[allurement]], [[mojo]] (*slang*)
**4** **like magic** very quickly
 *adj* *also* **[[magical]]**
**5** of magic
**6** possessing or considered to possess mysterious powers
**7** unaccountably [[enchanting]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[miraculous]], [[entrancing]], [[charming]], [[fascinating]], [[marvellous]], [[magical]], [[enchanting]], [[bewitching]]
**8** (*informal*) wonderful or [[marvellous]]
 *vb* **-gicking**, **-gicked**
**9** to transform or produce as if by magic: he had magicked up a gourmet meal at a moment's notice
 **[[magically]]** *adv*
