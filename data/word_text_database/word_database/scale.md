**scale**^3 *n*
**1** a [[sequence]] of marks at regular intervals, used as a reference in making measurements
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[system of measurement]], [[measuring system]]
**2** a measuring instrument with such a scale
**3** the [[ratio]] between the [[size]] of something real and that of a representation of it: the map has a scale of 1:10 000
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ratio]], [[proportion]]
**4** a [[series]] of degrees or graded system of things: the Western wage scale for the same work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ranking]], [[ladder]], [[hierarchy]], [[series]], [[sequence]], [[progression]]
**5** a relative [[degree]] or [[extent]]: growing flowers on a very small scale
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[degree]], [[size]], [[range]], [[extent]], [[dimensions]], [[scope]], [[magnitude]], [[breadth]]
**6** (*music*) a [[sequence]] of notes taken in ascending or descending order, esp. within one octave
**7** (*maths*) the notation of a given number system: the decimal scale
 *vb* **scaling**, **scaled**
**8** to climb to the top of (an object or height): the men scaled a wall
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[climb up]], [[mount]], [[ascend]], [[surmount]], [[clamber up]], [[escalade]]
**9** **scale up *or* down** to increase *or* reduce proportionately in [[size]]: the design can easily be scaled up; after five days the search was scaled down
