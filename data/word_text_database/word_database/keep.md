**keep** *vb* **keeping**, **kept**
**1** to have or [[retain]] possession of (something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hold on to]], [[maintain]], [[retain]], [[save]], [[preserve]], [[nurture]], [[cherish]], [[conserve]]
**2** to have temporary charge of: he'd kept my broken beads in his pocket for me all evening
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[store]], [[put]], [[place]], [[house]], [[hold]], [[deposit]], [[stack]], [[stow]]
**3** to [[store]] in a customary [[place]]: I keep it at the back of the drawer with my journal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[store]], [[put]], [[place]], [[house]], [[hold]], [[deposit]], [[stack]], [[stow]]
**4** to remain or cause (someone or something) to remain in a specified state or condition: keep still
**5** to continue or cause (someone) to continue: keep going straight on
**6** to stay (in, on, or at a [[place]] or position): keep to the paths
**7** to have as part of normal [[stock]]: they keep a small [[stock]] of first-class German wines
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[carry]], [[stock]], [[sell]], [[supply]], [[handle]]
**8** to [[support]] (someone) financially
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[support]], [[maintain]], [[sustain]], [[provide for]], [[mind]], [[fund]], [[finance]], [[feed]]
**9** to [[detain]] (someone)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[delay]], [[detain]], [[hinder]], [[impede]], [[obstruct]], [[set back]]
**10** to be faithful to (something): to keep a promise
**11** (of [[food]]) to stay in good condition for a certain time: fish doesn't keep very well
**12** to observe (a religious festival) with rites or ceremonies
**13** to [[maintain]] by writing regular records in: I keep a nature diary in my spare time
**14** to [[look after]] or [[maintain]] for use, pleasure, or profit: an old man who kept goats and cows
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[raise]], [[own]], [[maintain]], [[tend]], [[farm]], [[breed]], [[look after]], [[rear]]
**15** to associate with: she has started keeping bad company
**16** **how are you keeping?** are you well?
**17** **keep in with** to stay friendly with a person as they may be useful to one
 *n*
**18** the cost of [[food]] and other everyday expenses: I have to earn my keep
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[board]], [[food]], [[maintenance]], [[living]], [[kai]] (*NZ* *informal*)
**19** the main [[tower]] within the walls of a medieval [[castle]] or fortress
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tower]], [[castle]]
**20** **for keeps** (*informal*) permanently
