**engage** *vb* **-gaging**, **-gaged**
**1** Also: **be engaged ** (usually foll. by *in*) to take part or participate: he engaged in criminal and illegal acts; they were engaged in espionage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[participate in]], [[join in]], [[take part in]], [[undertake]], [[embark on]], [[enter into]], [[become involved in]], [[set about]]
**2** to involve (a person or his or her attention) intensely: there's nothing to engage the intellect in this film
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[captivate]], [[catch]], [[arrest]], [[fix]], [[capture]]
**3** to [[employ]] (someone) to do something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[employ]], [[appoint]], [[take on]], [[hire]], [[retain]], [[recruit]], [[enlist]], [[enrol]]
**4** to promise (to do something)
**5** (*military*) to begin a [[battle with]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[begin battle with]], [[attack]], [[take on]], [[encounter]], [[fall on]], [[battle with]], [[meet]], [[assail]]
**6** to bring (part of a machine or other mechanism) into operation, esp. by causing components to interlock
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[set going]], [[apply]], [[trigger]], [[activate]], [[switch on]], [[energize]], [[bring into operation]]
**7** **engage in conversation** to start a conversation with
