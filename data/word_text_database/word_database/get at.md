**get at** *vb*
**1** to succeed in reaching or discovering: the inquiry tried to get at the truth
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[get at something]]**: reach, touch, grasp, get (a) hold of, stretch to
**2** to imply or mean: it is hard to see quite what he is getting at
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[get at something]]**: imply, mean, suggest, hint, intimate, lead up to, insinuate
**3** to annoy or criticize persistently: people who know they're being got at
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[get at someone]]**: criticize, attack, blame, put down, knock (*informal*), nag, pick on, disparage
