**thrash** *vb*
**1** to [[beat]] (someone), esp. with a stick or [[whip]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beat]], [[wallop]], [[whip]], [[belt]] (*informal*), [[cane]], [[flog]], [[scourge]], [[spank]]
**2** to [[defeat]] totally: the All Blacks thrashed England 24–3
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[defeat]], [[beat]], [[crush]], [[slaughter]] (*informal*), [[rout]], [[trounce]], [[run rings around]] (*informal*), [[wipe the floor with]] (*informal*)
**3** to move about in a wild manner: his legs stuck and he fell sideways, thrashing about wildly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thresh]], [[flail]], [[jerk]], [[writhe]], [[toss and turn]]
**4** same as **[[thresh]]**
 *n*
**5** (*informal*) a party
 See also **thrash out**
