**erase** *vb* **erasing**, **erased**
**1** to destroy all traces of: he could not erase the memory of his earlier defeat
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[delete]], [[cancel out]], [[wipe out]], [[remove]], [[eradicate]], [[obliterate]], [[blot out]], [[expunge]] (*formal*)
**2** to rub or [[wipe out]] (something written)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rub out]], [[remove]], [[wipe out]], [[delete]]
**3** to [[remove]] sound or information from (a magnetic tape or disk)
 **[[erasable]]** *adj*
