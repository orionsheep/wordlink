**show** *vb* **showing**, **showed**, **shown*** or ***showed**
**1** to make, be, or become visible or noticeable: to show an interest; excitement showed on everyone's face
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indicate]], [[demonstrate]], [[prove]], [[reveal]], [[display]], [[point out]], [[manifest]], [[testify to]], [[flag up]]
**2** to present for inspection: someone showed me the plans
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[display]], [[exhibit]]
**3** to [[demonstrate]] or [[prove]]: evidence showed that this was the most economical way
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indicate]], [[demonstrate]], [[prove]], [[reveal]], [[display]], [[point out]], [[manifest]], [[testify to]], [[flag up]]
**4** to [[instruct]] by demonstration: she showed me how to feed the pullets
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[demonstrate]], [[describe]], [[explain]], [[teach]], [[illustrate]], [[instruct]]
**5** to [[indicate]]: the device shows changes in the pressure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[express]], [[display]], [[reveal]], [[indicate]], [[register]], [[demonstrate]], [[manifest]]
**6** to behave towards (someone) in a particular way: to show mercy
**7** to [[exhibit]] or [[display]] works of art, goods, etc.: three artists are showing at the gallery
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[display]], [[exhibit]]
**8** to present (a film or play) or (of a film or play) to be presented
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[broadcast]], [[transmit]], [[air]], [[beam]], [[relay]], [[televise]], [[put on the air]], [[podcast]]
**9** to [[guide]] or [[escort]]: he offered to show me around
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guide]], [[lead]], [[conduct]], [[accompany]], [[direct]], [[escort]]
**10** (*informal*) to arrive
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn up]], [[appear]], attend
 *n*
**11** a theatrical or other [[entertainment]]: a magic show
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entertainment]], [[production]], [[presentation]]
**12** a [[display]] or exhibition: a show of paintings
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[display]], [[sight]], [[spectacle]], [[array]]
**13** something done to create an impression: a show of indignation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[appearance]], [[display]], [[pose]], [[parade]]
**14** vain and conspicuous [[display]]: it was nothing but mere show
**15** (*slang*, *chiefly* *Brit*) a thing or affair: jolly good show
 See also **show off**, **show up**
