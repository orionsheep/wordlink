**redeem** *vb*
**1** to [[make up for]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[make up for]], [[compensate for]], [[atone for]], [[make amends for]]
**2** to [[reinstate]] (oneself) in someone's good opinion: he missed a penalty but redeemed himself by setting up the winning goal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reinstate]], [[absolve]], [[restore to favour]]
**3** (*Christianity*) (of Christ as Saviour) to free (humanity) from sin by death on the Cross
**4** to [[buy back]]: she didn't have the money to redeem it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[buy back]], [[recover]], [[regain]], [[retrieve]], [[reclaim]], [[repurchase]]
**5** to pay off (a loan or debt)
**6** to convert (bonds or shares) into cash
**7** to exchange (coupons) for goods
**8** to fulfil (a promise): I vowed to abide by the bill and have redeemed my pledge
 **[[redeemable]]** *adj* 
 **[[redeemer]]** *n*
