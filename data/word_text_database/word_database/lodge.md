**lodge** *n*
**1** (*chiefly* *Brit*) the gatekeeper's house at the entrance to the grounds of a country mansion
**2** a house or [[cabin]] used occasionally by hunters, skiers, etc.: a hunting lodge
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cabin]], [[shelter]], [[cottage]], [[hut]], [[chalet]], [[gatehouse]]
**3** (*chiefly* *Brit*) a [[room]] used by porters in a university or college
**4** a local [[branch]] of certain societies
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[society]], [[group]], [[club]], [[section]], [[wing]], [[chapter]], [[branch]]
**5** a beaver's home
 *vb* **lodging**, **lodged**
**6** to provide or be provided with rented accommodation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stay]], [[room]], [[board]], [[reside]] (*formal*)
**7** to live temporarily in rented accommodation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stay]], [[room]], [[board]], [[reside]] (*formal*)
**8** to embed or be embedded: the bullet lodged in his brain
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stick]], [[remain]], [[implant]], [[come to rest]], [[imbed]]
**9** to leave for safety or storage: he lodged his wages in the bank
**10** to bring (a charge or accusation) against someone: the Brazilians lodged a complaint
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[register]], [[enter]], [[file]], [[submit]], [[put on record]]
**11** (often foll. by *in* or *with*) to place (authority or power) in the control (of someone)
