**whack** *vb*
**1** to [[hit]] hard: that lad whacked him over the head with a bottle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strike]], [[hit]], [[belt]] (*informal*), [[bang]], [[smack]], [[thrash]], [[thump]], [[swipe]] (*informal*)
**2** (usually foll. by *in* or *on*) (*informal*) to put something onto or into (something else) with force or abandon: whack on some sunscreen
 *n*
**3** a hard [[blow]] or the sound of one: a whack with a blunt instrument
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[blow]], [[hit]], [[stroke]], [[belt]] (*informal*), [[bang]], [[smack]], [[thump]], [[swipe]] (*informal*)
**4** (*informal*) a [[share]]: he took his whack of that money
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[share]], [[part]], [[cut]] (*informal*), [[bit]], [[portion]], [[quota]]
**5** **have a whack** to make an attempt
**6** **out of whack** (*informal*) out of order or out of condition: my body is just a little out of whack
