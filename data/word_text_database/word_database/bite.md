**bite** *vb* **biting**, **bit**, **bitten**
**1** to grip, [[cut]] off, or [[tear]] with the teeth or jaws
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nip]], [[cut]], [[tear]], [[wound]], [[snap]], [[pierce]], [[pinch]], [[chew]]
**2** (of animals or insects) to injure by puncturing (the skin) with the teeth or fangs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nip]], [[cut]], [[tear]], [[wound]], [[snap]], [[pierce]], [[pinch]], [[chew]]
**3** (of corrosive material) to eat away or into
**4** to smart or cause to smart; [[sting]]
**5** (*angling*) (of a fish) to take the bait or lure
**6** to take firm hold of or act effectively upon: turn the screw till it bites the wood
**7** (*slang*) to annoy or worry: what's biting her?
 *n*
**8** the act of biting
**9** a thing or amount bitten off
**10** a [[wound]] or [[sting]] inflicted by biting
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wound]], [[sting]], [[pinch]], [[nip]], [[prick]]
**11** (*angling*) an attempt by a fish to take the bait or lure
**12** a [[snack]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[snack]], [[food]], [[piece]], [[taste]], [[refreshment]], [[mouthful]], [[morsel]], [[titbit]]
**13** a stinging or smarting sensation
 **[[biter]]** *n*
