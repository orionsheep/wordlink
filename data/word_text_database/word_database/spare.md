**spare** *adj*
**1** [[extra]] to what is needed: there are some spare chairs at the back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[back-up]], [[reserve]], [[second]], [[extra]], [[additional]], [[auxiliary]]
**2** able to be used when needed: a spare parking space
**3** (of a person) tall and [[thin]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thin]], [[lean]], [[meagre]], [[gaunt]], [[wiry]]
**4** (of a style) plain and without unnecessary decoration or details; austere: a spare but beautiful novel
**5** (*Brit* *slang*) frantic with anger or worry: the boss went spare
 *n*
**6** an [[extra]] thing kept in case it is needed
 *vb* **sparing**, **spared**
**7** to stop from killing, punishing, or injuring (someone)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[have mercy on]], [[pardon]], [[leave]], [[let off]] (*informal*), [[go easy on]] (*informal*), [[save]] (from harm)
**8** to protect (someone) from (something) unpleasant: spare me the sermon
**9** to be able to [[afford]] or [[give]]: can you spare me a moment to talk?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[afford]], [[give]], [[grant]], [[do without]], [[part with]], [[manage without]], [[let someone have]]
**10** **not spare oneself** to try one's hardest
**11** **to spare** more than is required: a few hours to spare
