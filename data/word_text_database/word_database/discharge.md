**discharge** *vb* **-charging**, **-charged**
**1** to [[release]] or [[allow to go]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[release]], [[free]], [[clear]], [[liberate]], [[pardon]], [[allow to go]], [[set free]]
**2** to [[dismiss]] (someone) from duty or employment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dismiss]], [[sack]] (*informal*), [[fire]] (*informal*), [[remove]], [[expel]], [[discard]], [[oust]], [[cashier]], [[kennet]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[jeff]] (*Austral* *slang*)
**3** to [[fire]] (a gun)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fire]], [[shoot]], [[set off]], [[explode]], [[let off]], [[detonate]], [[let loose]] (*informal*)
**4** to cause to [[pour forth]]: the scar was red and swollen and began to discharge [[pus]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pour forth]], [[release]], [[leak]], [[emit]], [[dispense]], [[ooze]], [[exude]], [[give off]]
**5** to [[remove]] (the cargo) from a boat, etc.; unload
**6** to [[meet]] the demands of (a duty or responsibility)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[carry out]], [[perform]], [[fulfil]], [[accomplish]], [[do]], [[effect]], [[realize]], [[observe]]
**7** to [[relieve]] oneself of (a debt)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pay]], [[meet]], [[clear]], [[settle]], [[square]] (up), [[honour]], [[satisfy]], [[relieve]]
**8** (*physics*) to take or supply electrical current from (a cell or battery)
 *n*
**9** something that is discharged
**10** [[dismissal]] or [[release]] from an office, job, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dismissal]], [[notice]] (*Brit*), [[removal]], [[the boot]] (*slang*), [[expulsion]], [[the sack]] (*informal*), [[the push]] (*slang*), [[marching orders]] (*informal*), [[ejection]]
**11** a pouring out of a fluid; [[emission]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[emission]], [[ooze]], [[secretion]], [[excretion]], [[pus]], [[seepage]], [[suppuration]]
**12** (*physics*) a conduction of electricity through a gas
