**regular** *adj*
**1** [[normal]], [[customary]], or [[usual]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[normal]], [[common]], [[usual]], [[ordinary]], [[typical]], [[routine]], [[customary]], [[habitual]]
**2** symmetrical or [[even]]: regular features
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[even]], [[level]], [[balanced]], [[straight]], [[flat]], [[fixed]], [[smooth]], [[uniform]]
**3** according to a [[uniform]] principle, arrangement, or order
**4** occurring at [[fixed]] or prearranged intervals: we run regular advertisements in the press
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[frequent]]
**5** following a set rule or [[normal]] practice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[normal]], [[common]], [[usual]], [[ordinary]], [[typical]], [[routine]], [[customary]], [[habitual]]
**6** (*grammar*) following the [[usual]] pattern of formation in a language: regular verbs
**7** of or serving in the permanent military services: the regular armed forces
**8** (*maths*) (of a polygon) having all its sides and angles the same
**9** officially qualified or recognized: he's not a regular doctor
**10** (*informal*) not constipated: eating fresh vegetables helps keep you regular
**11** (*US** & **Canad* *informal*) likeable: a regular guy
**12** complete or utter: a regular fool
**13** subject to the rule of an established religious community: canons regular
 *n*
**14** a professional long-term serviceman or servicewoman in a military unit
**15** (*informal*) a [[frequent]] customer or visitor
 **[[regularity]]** *n*
