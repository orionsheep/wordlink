**employ** *vb*
**1** to [[hire]] (someone) to do work in return for money
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hire]], [[commission]], [[appoint]], [[take on]], [[retain]], [[engage]], [[recruit]], [[sign up]]
**2** to keep busy or [[occupy]]: she was busily employed cutting the grass
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spend]], [[fill]], [[occupy]], [[involve]], [[engage]], [[take up]], [[make use of]], [[use up]]
**3** to [[use]] as a means: you can employ various methods to cut your heating bills
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[use]], [[apply]], [[exercise]], [[exert]], [[make use of]], [[utilize]], [[ply]], bring to bear
 *n*
**4** **in the employ of** doing regular paid work for: he is in the employ of The Sunday Times
 **[[employable]]** *adj*
