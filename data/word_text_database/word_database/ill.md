**ill** *adj* **worse**, **worst**
**1** not in good health
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unwell]], [[sick]], [[poorly]] (*informal*), [[diseased]], [[weak]], [[crook]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *slang*), [[ailing]], [[frail]]
**2** [[bad]], [[harmful]], or hostile: ill effects
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harmful]], [[bad]], [[damaging]], [[evil]], [[foul]], [[unfortunate]], [[destructive]], [[detrimental]]
**3** promising an unfavourable outcome: ill omen
**4** **ill at ease** unable to relax
 *n*
**5** [[evil]] or harm
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[problem]], [[trouble]], [[suffering]], [[worry]], [[injury]], [[hurt]], [[strain]], harm
 *adv*
**6** badly, wrongly: the title ill befits him
**7** with difficulty; [[hardly]]: we can ill afford another scandal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hardly]], [[barely]], [[scarcely]], [[just]], [[only just]], [[by no means]], [[at a push]]
