**fault** *n*
**1** [[responsibility]] for something wrong
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[responsibility]], [[liability]], [[guilt]], [[accountability]], [[culpability]]
**2** a [[defect]] or [[failing]]: they shut the production line to remedy a fault
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[failing]], [[weakness]], [[defect]], [[deficiency]], [[flaw]], [[shortcoming]], [[blemish]], [[imperfection]]
**3** a [[weakness]] in a person's character
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[failing]], [[weakness]], [[defect]], [[deficiency]], [[flaw]], [[shortcoming]], [[blemish]], [[imperfection]]
**4** (*geol*) a fracture in the earth's crust with displacement of the rocks on either side
**5** (*tennis*, *squash*, *etc.*) a serve that bounces outside the proper service court or fails to get over the net
**6** (in showjumping) a penalty mark for [[failing]] to clear, or refusing, a fence
**7** **at fault** to be to [[blame]] for something wrong
**8** **[[find fault with]]** to seek out minor imperfections in
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[find fault with something or someone]]**: [[criticize]], [[complain]] about, whinge about (*informal*), whine about (*informal*), quibble, carp at, take to task, pick holes in, nit-pick (*informal*)
**9** **[[to a fault]]** more than is usual or necessary: generous [[to a fault]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[to a fault]]**: excessively, unduly, in the extreme, overmuch, immoderately
 *vb*
**10** to [[criticize]] or [[blame]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[criticize]], [[blame]], [[complain]], [[condemn]], [[moan about]], [[censure]], hold (someone) responsible, [[find fault with]]
**11** (*geol*) to undergo or cause to undergo a fault
 **[[faultless]]** *adj* 
 **[[faultlessly]]** *adv*
