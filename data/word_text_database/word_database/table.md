**table** *n*
**1** a piece of furniture consisting of a flat top supported by legs: a coffee table
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[counter]], [[bench]], [[stand]], [[board]], [[surface]], [[work surface]]
**2** a set of facts or figures arranged in rows and columns: a league table
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[list]], [[chart]], [[tabulation]], [[record]], [[roll]], [[register]], [[diagram]], [[itemization]]
**3** a group of people sitting round a table for a meal, game, etc.: the whole table laughed
**4** (*formal*) the food provided at a meal or in a particular house: he keeps a good table
**5** **turn the tables** to cause a complete reversal of circumstances
 *vb* **-bling**, **-bled**
**6** (*Brit** & **Austral*) to [[submit]] (a motion) for discussion by a meeting
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[submit]], [[propose]], [[put forward]], [[move]], [[suggest]], [[enter]], [[file]], [[lodge]]
**7** (*US*) to suspend discussion of (a proposal) indefinitely
