**outrage** *n*
**1** deep [[indignation]], [[anger]], or [[resentment]]: she felt a sense of outrage that he should abandon her like that
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indignation]], [[shock]], [[anger]], [[rage]], [[fury]], [[hurt]], [[resentment]], [[scorn]]
**2** an extremely vicious or cruel act; gross violation of decency, morality, or honour: there have been reports of another bombing outrage in the capital
 *vb* **-raging**, **-raged**
**3** to cause deep [[indignation]], [[anger]], or [[resentment]] in (someone): they were outraged by the news of the assassination
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[offend]], [[shock]], [[upset]], [[wound]], [[insult]], [[infuriate]], [[incense]], [[madden]]
