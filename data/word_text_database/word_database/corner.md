**corner** *n*
**1** the place or [[angle]] formed by the meeting of two converging lines or surfaces
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[angle]], [[joint]], [[crook]]
**2** the [[space]] within the [[angle]] formed, as in a room
**3** the place where two streets meet
**4** a sharp [[bend]] in a road
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bend]], [[curve]]
**5** a remote place: far-flung corners of the world
**6** any secluded or private place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[space]], [[hideaway]], [[nook]], [[hide-out]]
**7** (*sport*) a free kick or shot taken from the corner of the field
**8** **cut corners** to take the shortest or easiest way at the expense of high standards
**9** **turn the corner** to pass the critical point of an illness or a difficult time
 *adj*
**10** on or in a corner: a corner seat
 *vb*
**11** to force (a person or animal) into a difficult or inescapable position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trap]], [[catch]], [[run to earth]]
**12** (of a vehicle or its driver) to turn a corner
**13** to obtain a monopoly of
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[monopolize]], [[take over]], [[dominate]], [[control]], [[hog]] (*slang*), [[engross]]
