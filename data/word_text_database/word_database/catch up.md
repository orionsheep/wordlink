**catch up** *vb*
**1** **be caught up in** to be unwillingly or accidentally involved in: hundreds of civilians have been caught up in the clashes
**2** **catch up on *or* with** to bring (something) up to date: he had a lot of paperwork to catch up on
**3** **catch up with** to reach or pass (someone or something): she ran to catch up with him
 *n* **catch-up**
**4** a meeting held in order to bring someone up to date with developments
**5** a facility for watching a television programme after it has been broadcast
**6** **play catch-up** (*informal*) to attempt to match a standard set by another person or organization
