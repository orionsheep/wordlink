**straight** *adj*
**1** continuing in the same direction without bending; not curved or crooked
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[direct]], [[unswerving]]
**2** [[even]], [[level]], or [[upright]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[level]], [[even]], [[right]], [[square]], [[true]], [[smooth]], [[aligned]], [[horizontal]]
**3** in keeping with the facts; accurate
**4** [[outright]] or [[candid]]: a straight rejection
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[frank]], [[plain]], [[straightforward]], [[blunt]], [[outright]], [[honest]], [[candid]], [[forthright]]
**5** in [[continuous]] succession
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[successive]], [[consecutive]], [[continuous]], [[running]], [[solid]], [[nonstop]]
**6** (of an alcoholic drink) [[undiluted]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[undiluted]], [[pure]], [[neat]], [[unadulterated]], [[unmixed]]
**7** not wavy or curly: straight hair
**8** in good order
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[in order]], [[organized]], [[arranged]], [[neat]], [[tidy]], [[orderly]], [[shipshape]]
**9** (of a play or acting style) [[straightforward]] or serious
**10** [[honest]], [[respectable]], or [[reliable]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[honest]], [[just]], [[fair]], [[reliable]], [[respectable]], [[upright]], [[honourable]], [[law-abiding]]
**11** heterosexual
**12** (*slang*) [[conventional]] in views, customs, or appearance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[conventional]], [[conservative]], [[bourgeois]]
**13** (*informal*) no longer owing or being owed something: if you buy the next round we'll be straight
 *adv*
**14** in a straight line or [[direct]] course
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[directly]], [[precisely]], [[exactly]], [[unswervingly]], [[by the shortest route]], [[in a beeline]]
**15** [[immediately]]; [[at once]]: get straight back here
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[immediately]], [[directly]], [[promptly]], [[instantly]], [[at once]], [[straight away]], [[without delay]], [[forthwith]]
**16** in a [[level]] or [[upright]] position: he sat up straight
**17** continuously; uninterruptedly: we waited for three hours straight
**18** (often foll. by *out*) frankly; candidly: she asked me straight out
**19** **go straight** (*informal*) to reform after having been a criminal
**20** **[[straight away]]** *or ***straightaway** [[at once]]
 *n*
**21** a straight line, form, part, or position
**22** (*Brit*) a straight part of a racetrack
**23** a heterosexual person
