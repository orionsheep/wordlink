**fancy** *adj* **-cier**, **-ciest**
**1** special, unusual, and [[elaborate]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[elaborate]], [[decorative]], [[extravagant]], [[intricate]], [[baroque]], [[ornamental]], [[ornate]], [[embellished]]
**2** (often used ironically) superior in quality
**3** (of a price) higher than expected
 *n*, *pl* **-cies**
**4** a sudden spontaneous [[idea]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[whim]], [[thought]], [[idea]], [[desire]], [[urge]], [[notion]], [[humour]], [[impulse]]
**5** a sudden or irrational liking for a person or thing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[whim]], [[thought]], [[idea]], [[desire]], [[urge]], [[notion]], [[humour]], [[impulse]]
**6** (*old-fashioned*, *or* *literary*) a person's imagination
 *vb* **-cies**, **-cying**, **-cied**
**7** (*Brit* *informal*) to be physically attracted to (another person)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be attracted to]], [[find attractive]], [[lust after]], [[like]], [[take to]], [[be captivated by]], [[have a thing about]] (*informal*), [[have eyes for]]
**8** (*informal*) to have a [[wish for]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wish for]], [[want]], [[desire]], [[hope for]], [[long for]], [[crave]], [[yearn for]], [[thirst for]]
**9** to picture in the imagination
**10** to [[think]] or [[suppose]]: I fancy I am redundant here
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suppose]], [[think]], [[believe]], [[imagine]], [[reckon]], [[conjecture]], [[think likely]]
**11** **fancy oneself** to have a high opinion of oneself
 *interj*
**12** Also: **fancy that!** an exclamation of surprise
 **[[fancily]]** *adv*
