**oblige** *vb* **obliging**, **obliged**
**1** to [[compel]] someone by legal, moral, or physical means to do something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[compel]], [[make]], [[force]], [[require]], [[bind]], [[constrain]], [[necessitate]], [[impel]]
**2** to [[make]] (someone) indebted or grateful for a favour: I am obliged to you for your [[help]]
**3** to do a favour to someone: she obliged the guests with a song
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[help]], [[assist]], [[benefit]], [[please]], [[humour]], [[accommodate]], [[indulge]], [[gratify]]
