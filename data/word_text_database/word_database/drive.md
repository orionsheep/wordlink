**drive** *vb* **driving**, **drove**, **driven**
**1** to [[guide]] the movement of (a vehicle)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[operate]], [[manage]], [[direct]], [[guide]], [[handle]], [[steer]]
**2** to transport or be transported in a vehicle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go]] (by car), [[ride]] (by car), [[motor]], [[travel by car]]
**3** to [[force]] into a specified state: work drove him to despair
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[force]], [[press]], [[prompt]], [[spur]], [[prod]], [[constrain]], [[coerce]], [[goad]]
**4** to [[push]] or [[propel]]: he drove the nail into the wall with a hammer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[push]], [[propel]]
**5** (*sport*) to hit (a ball) very hard and straight
**6** (*golf*) to strike (the ball) with a driver
**7** to chase (game) from cover
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[herd]], [[urge]], [[impel]]
**8** **drive home** to make (a point) clearly understood by emphasis
 *n*
**9** a [[journey]] in a driven vehicle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[run]], [[ride]], [[trip]], [[journey]], [[spin]] (*informal*), [[outing]], [[excursion]], [[jaunt]]
**10** a road for vehicles, esp. a private road leading to a house
**11** a special [[effort]] made by a group of people for a particular purpose: a charity drive
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[campaign]], [[push]] (*informal*), [[crusade]], [[action]], [[effort]], [[appeal]]
**12** [[energy]], [[ambition]], or [[initiative]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[initiative]], [[energy]], [[enterprise]], [[ambition]], [[motivation]], [[zip]] (*informal*), [[vigour]], [[get-up-and-go]] (*informal*)
**13** (*psychol*) a motive or interest: sex drive
**14** a sustained and powerful military offensive
**15** the means by which power is transmitted in a machine
**16** (*sport*) a hard straight shot or stroke
