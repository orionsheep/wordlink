**outlet** *n*
**1** a means of expressing one's feelings: the shock would give her an outlet for her own grief
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[channel]], [[release]], [[medium]], [[avenue]], [[vent]], [[conduit]]
**2**
**A** a [[market]] for a product: there is a huge sales outlet for personal computers
 **B** a [[shop]] or organization selling the goods of a particular producer or wholesaler or manufacturer: her own brand is now sold to outlets throughout the world
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shop]], [[store]], [[supermarket]], [[market]], [[boutique]], [[emporium]] (*old-fashioned*), [[hypermarket]]
**3** an [[opening]] permitting escape or [[release]]: make sure the exhaust outlet is not blocked
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pipe]], [[opening]], [[channel]], [[exit]], [[duct]]
