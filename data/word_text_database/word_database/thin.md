**thin** *adj* **thinner**, **thinnest**
**1** having a relatively small distance between opposite surfaces: a thin mattress
**2** much narrower than it is long: push a thin stick up the pipe in order to clear it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[narrow]], [[fine]], [[attenuated]]
**3** (of a person or animal) having no excess body fat
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slim]], [[spare]], [[lean]], [[slight]], [[slender]], [[skinny]], [[skeletal]], [[bony]]
**4** made up of only a few, widely separated, people or things: thin hair
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wispy]], [[thinning]], [[sparse]], [[scarce]], [[scanty]]
**5** not dense: a thin film of dust
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fine]], [[delicate]], [[flimsy]], [[sheer]], [[skimpy]], [[gossamer]], [[diaphanous]], [[filmy]]
**6** [[unconvincing]] because badly thought out or badly presented: the evidence against him was extremely thin
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unconvincing]], [[inadequate]], [[feeble]], [[poor]], [[weak]], [[superficial]], [[lame]], [[flimsy]]
**7** (of a voice) high-pitched and not very loud: a thin squeaky voice
 *adv*
**8** in order to produce something thin: roll the dough very thin
 *vb* **[[thinning]]**, **thinned**
**9** to make or become thin or [[sparse]]
 **[[thinly]]** *adv* 
 **[[thinness]]** *n*
