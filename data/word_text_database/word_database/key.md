**key** *n*
**1** a specially shaped metal instrument for moving the bolt of a lock so as to lock or unlock a door, suitcase, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[opener]], [[door key]], [[latchkey]]
**2** an instrument that is turned to operate a valve, clock winding mechanism, etc.
**3** any of a set of levers pressed to operate a computer, typewriter, or musical keyboard instrument
**4** a scale of musical notes that starts at one specific note
**5** something that is crucial in providing an explanation or interpretation
**6** a means of achieving a desired end: education is the key to success in most walks of life today
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[answer]]
**7** a list of explanations of symbols, codes, or abbreviations
**8** pitch: he spoke in a low key
 *adj*
**9** of great importance: key prosecution witnesses have been giving evidence
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[essential]], [[leading]], [[major]], [[main]], [[important]], [[necessary]], [[vital]], crucial
 *vb*
**10** to harmonize with: training and educational programmes uniquely keyed for local needs
**11** to adjust or fasten (something) with a key or some similar device
**12** to enter (information or instructions) into a computer by means of a keyboard
 See also **key in**
