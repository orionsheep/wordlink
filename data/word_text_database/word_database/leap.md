**leap** *vb* **leaping**, **leapt*** or ***leaped**
**1** to [[jump]] suddenly from one place to another
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jump]], [[spring]], [[bound]], [[bounce]], [[hop]], [[skip]]
**2** (often foll. by *at*) to move or react quickly
**3** to [[jump]] over
 *n*
**4** the act of jumping
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jump]], [[spring]], [[bound]], [[vault]]
**5** an abrupt or important [[change]] or [[increase]]: it's been a huge leap to the Premiership from where I started with Wigan
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rise]], [[change]], [[increase]], [[soaring]], [[surge]], [[escalation]], [[upsurge]], [[upswing]]
**6** **a leap in the dark** an action performed without knowledge of the consequences
**7** **by leaps and bounds** with unexpectedly rapid progress
