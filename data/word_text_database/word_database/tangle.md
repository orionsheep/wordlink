**tangle** *n*
**1** a confused or complicated mass of things, such as hair or fibres, knotted or coiled together: a tangle of wires
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[knot]], [[twist]], [[web]], [[jungle]], [[coil]], [[entanglement]]
**2** a complicated problem or situation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mess]], [[jam]] (*informal*), [[fix]] (*informal*), [[confusion]], [[complication]], [[mix-up]], [[shambles]], [[entanglement]]
 *vb* **-gling**, **-gled**
**3** to [[twist]] (things, such as hair or fibres) together in a confused mass
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[twist]], [[knot]], [[mat]], [[coil]], [[mesh]], [[entangle]], [[interweave]], [[ravel]]
**4** to come into conflict: the last thing she wanted was to tangle with the police
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[tangle with someone]]**: come into conflict with, come up against, cross swords with, dispute with, contend with, contest with, lock horns with
**5** to catch or trap in a net, ropes, etc.: the string of the kite had got [[tangled]] in the branches
 **[[tangled]]** *adj*
