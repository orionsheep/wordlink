**push** *vb*
**1** (sometimes foll. by *off* or *away* etc) to apply steady [[force]] to in order to [[move]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shove]], [[force]], [[press]], [[thrust]], [[drive]], [[knock]], [[sweep]], [[plunge]]
**2** to [[thrust]] (one's way) through something, such as a crowd
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: make *or* [[force]] your way, [[move]], [[shoulder]], [[inch]], [[squeeze]], [[thrust]], [[elbow]], [[shove]]
**3** (sometimes foll. by *for*) to be an advocate or promoter (of): there are many groups you can join to push for change
**4** to [[spur]] or [[drive]] (oneself or another person) in order to achieve more effort or better results: you must be careful not to push your children too hard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[urge]], [[encourage]], [[persuade]], [[spur]], [[press]], [[incite]], [[impel]]
**5** (*informal*) to sell (narcotic drugs) illegally
 *n*
**6** the act of pushing; [[thrust]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shove]], [[thrust]], [[butt]], [[elbow]], [[nudge]]
**7** (*informal*) [[drive]] or determination: everything depends on him having [[the push]] to obtain the money
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drive]], [[go]] (*informal*), [[energy]], [[initiative]], [[enterprise]], [[ambition]], [[vitality]], [[vigour]]
**8** (*informal*) a special effort to achieve something: when this push spent itself it was obvious the bid had failed
**9** **[[the push]]** (*Brit** & **NZ* *informal*) dismissal from employment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[the push]]**: dismissal, the sack (*informal*), discharge, the boot (*slang*), your cards (*informal*)
