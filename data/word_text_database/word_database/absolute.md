**absolute** *adj*
**1** [[total]] and [[complete]]: he ordered an immediate and absolute ceasefire
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[complete]], [[total]], [[perfect]], [[pure]], [[sheer]], [[utter]], [[outright]], [[thorough]]
**2** with [[unrestricted]] power and authority: she has absolute control with fifty per cent of the shares
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[supreme]], [[sovereign]], [[unlimited]], [[ultimate]], [[full]], [[unconditional]], [[unrestricted]], [[pre-eminent]]
**3** undoubted or certain: I was telling the absolute truth
**4** not dependent on or relative to anything else
**5** [[pure]]; unmixed: absolute alcohol
 *n*
**6** a principle or rule believed to be unfailingly correct
**7** **the Absolute** (*philosophy*) that which is totally unconditioned, [[perfect]], or [[complete]]
