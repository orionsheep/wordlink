**scrape** *vb* **scraping**, **scraped**
**1** to move (a rough or sharp object) across (a surface)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rake]], [[sweep]], [[drag]], [[brush]]
**2** (often foll. by *away* or *off*) to [[remove]] (a layer) by rubbing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clean]], [[remove]], [[scour]]
**3** to produce a grating sound by rubbing against (something else)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grate]], [[grind]], [[scratch]], [[squeak]], [[rasp]]
**4** to injure or damage by scraping: he had scraped his knees
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[graze]], [[skin]], [[scratch]], [[bark]], [[scuff]], [[rub]]
**5** **scrimp and scrape** See **scrimp**
 *n*
**6** the act or sound of scraping
**7** a scraped place: a scrape on the car door
**8** (*informal*) an awkward or embarrassing situation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[predicament]], [[difficulty]], [[fix]] (*informal*), [[mess]], [[dilemma]], [[plight]], [[tight spot]], [[awkward situation]]
**9** (*informal*) a conflict or struggle
 **[[scraper]]** *n*
