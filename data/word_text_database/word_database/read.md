**read** *vb* **reading**, **read**
**1** to look at and [[understand]] or take in (written or printed matter)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[understand]], [[interpret]], [[comprehend]], [[construe]], [[decipher]], [[see]], [[discover]]
**2** to look at and say aloud
**3** to have a certain wording: the memorandum read as follows
**4** to [[interpret]] in a specified way: it can be read as satire
**5** to [[interpret]] the significance or meaning of: an astrologer who reads Tarot
**6** to [[register]] or [[show]]: the meter reads 100
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[register]], [[show]], [[record]], [[display]], [[indicate]]
**7** to make out the true nature or mood of: she had read his thoughts
**8** to [[interpret]] (signs, characters, etc.) other than by visual means: to read Braille
**9** to have sufficient knowledge of (a language) to [[understand]] the written word
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[understand]], [[interpret]], [[comprehend]], [[construe]], [[decipher]], [[see]], [[discover]]
**10** to undertake a course of study in (a subject): to read economics
**11** to gain knowledge by reading: he read about the war
**12** to hear and [[understand]], esp. when using a two-way radio: we are reading you loud and clear
**13** (*computers*) to obtain (data) from a storage device, such as magnetic tape
 *n*
**14** matter suitable for reading: this book is a very good read
**15** a spell of reading
