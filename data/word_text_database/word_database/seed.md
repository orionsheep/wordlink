**seed** *n*
**1** (*botany*) the mature fertilized [[grain]] of a plant, containing an [[embryo]] ready for germination. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**seminal**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grain]], [[pip]], [[germ]], [[kernel]], [[egg]], [[embryo]], [[spore]], [[ovum]]
**2** such seeds used for sowing
**3** the source, [[beginning]], or origin of anything: the seeds of dissent
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beginning]], [[start]], [[germ]]
**4** (*chiefly* *Bible*) [[descendants]]; [[offspring]]: the seed of David
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[offspring]], [[children]], [[descendants]], [[issue]], [[progeny]] (*old-fashioned*)
**5** (*sport*) a player ranked according to his or her ability
**6** **go *or* run to seed**
**A** (of plants) to produce and shed seeds after flowering
 **B** to lose strength or usefulness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[go or run to seed]]**: decline, deteriorate, degenerate, decay, go downhill (*informal*), let yourself go, go to pot
 *vb*
**7** to plant (seeds) in (soil)
**8** (of plants) to produce or shed seeds
**9** to remove the seeds from (fruit or plants)
**10** to scatter silver iodide in (clouds) in order to cause rain
**11** to arrange (the draw of a tournament) so that outstanding teams or players will not meet in the early rounds
 **[[seedless]]** *adj*
