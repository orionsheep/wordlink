**simple** *adj*
**1** easy to understand or do: in simple English; simple exercises
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[uncomplicated]], [[clear]], [[plain]], [[understandable]], [[lucid]], [[recognizable]], [[comprehensible]], [[intelligible]]
**2** [[plain]] and not elaborate: a simple red skirt; a simple answer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plain]], [[natural]], [[classic]], [[unfussy]], [[unembellished]], [[bare-bones]]
**3** not combined or complex: simple diagnostic equipment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pure]], [[mere]], [[sheer]], [[unalloyed]]
**4** leading an [[uncomplicated]] life: I am a simple man myself
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[artless]], [[innocent]], [[naive]], [[natural]], [[sincere]], [[unaffected]], [[childlike]], [[unsophisticated]]
**5** [[sincere]] or frank: a simple apology
**6** of [[humble]] background: the simple country girl
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unpretentious]], [[modest]], [[humble]], [[homely]], [[unfussy]], [[unembellished]]
**7** (*informal*) lacking in intelligence
**8** straightforward: a simple matter of choice
**9** (*music*) denoting a time where the number of beats per bar may be two, three, or four
 **[[simplicity]]** *n*
