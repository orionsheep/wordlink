**pose** *vb* **posing**, **posed**
**1** to take up a particular [[position]] to be photographed or drawn
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[position yourself]], [[sit]], [[model]], [[arrange yourself]]
**2** to behave in an affected way in order to impress others
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put on airs]], [[posture]], [[show off]] (*informal*)
**3** (often foll. by *as*) to pretend to be (someone one is not)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pose as something or someone]]**: impersonate, pretend to be, profess to be, masquerade as, pass yourself off as
**4** to create or be (a problem, threat, etc.): dressing complicated wounds has always posed a problem for doctors
**5** to put forward or ask: the question you posed earlier
 *n*
**6** a [[position]] taken up for an artist or photographer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[posture]], [[position]], [[bearing]], [[attitude]], [[stance]]
**7** behaviour adopted for effect
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[act]], [[façade]], [[air]], [[front]], [[posturing]], [[pretence]], [[mannerism]], [[affectation]]
