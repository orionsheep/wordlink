**riot** *n*
**1** a [[disturbance]] made by an unruly mob
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disturbance]], [[disorder]], [[confusion]], [[turmoil]], [[upheaval]], [[strife]], [[turbulence]], [[lawlessness]]
**2** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ*) an occasion of lively enjoyment
**3** a dazzling [[display]]: the pansies provided the essential riot of colour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[display]], [[show]], [[splash]], [[extravaganza]], [[profusion]]
**4** (*slang*) a very amusing person or thing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[laugh]] (*informal*), [[joke]], [[scream]] (*informal*), [[hoot]] (*informal*), [[lark]]
**5** **read the riot act** to reprimand severely
**6** **[[run riot]]**
**A** to behave without restraint
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run riot]]**: [[rampage]], go wild, be out of control
 **B** (of plants) to grow profusely
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run riot]]**: grow profusely, spread like wildfire
 *vb*
**7** to take part in a riot
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rampage]], [[run riot]], [[go on the rampage]]
