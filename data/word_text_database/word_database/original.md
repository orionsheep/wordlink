**original** *adj*
**1** [[first]] or [[earliest]]: the dining room also has attractive original beams
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[first]], [[earliest]], [[initial]]
**2** [[fresh]] and [[unusual]]; not copied from or based on something else: the composer's work has created some original and attractive choreography
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[new]], [[fresh]], [[novel]], [[unusual]], [[unprecedented]], [[innovative]], [[unfamiliar]], [[seminal]]
**3** able to think of or carry out [[new]] ideas or concepts: he is an excitingly original writer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[creative]], [[inspired]], [[imaginative]], [[artistic]], [[fertile]], [[ingenious]], [[visionary]], [[inventive]]
**4** being the [[first]] and genuine form of something, from which a copy or translation is made: all French recipes were translated from the original abridged versions
 *n*
**5** the [[first]] and genuine form of something, from which others are copied or translated: the original is in the British Museum
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prototype]], [[master]], [[pattern]]
**6** a person or thing used as a model in art or literature: she claimed to be the original on whom Lawrence based Lady Chatterley
 **[[originality]]** *n* 
 **[[originally]]** *adv*
