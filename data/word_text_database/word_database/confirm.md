**confirm** *vb*
**1** to [[prove]] to be true or valid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prove]], [[support]], [[establish]], [[back up]], [[verify]], [[validate]], [[bear out]], [[substantiate]]
**2** to reaffirm (something), so as to make (it) more definite: she confirmed that she is about to resign as leader of the council
**3** to [[strengthen]]: this cruise confirmed my first impressions of the boat's performance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strengthen]], [[establish]], [[fix]], [[secure]], [[reinforce]], [[fortify]]
**4** to formally make valid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ratify]], [[establish]], [[sanction]], [[endorse]], [[authorize]]
**5** (*Christianity*) to administer the rite of confirmation to
