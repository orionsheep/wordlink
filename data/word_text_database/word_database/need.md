**need** *vb*
**1** to [[require]] or be in [[want]] of: they desperately need success
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[require]], [[want]], [[demand]], [[call for]], [[entail]], [[necessitate]]
**2** to be obliged: the government may need to impose a statutory levy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[have to]], [[be obliged to]]
**3** used to express [[necessity]] or [[obligation]] and does not add -s when used with singular nouns or pronouns: need he go?
 *n*
**4** the condition of lacking something: he has need of a new coat
**5** a [[requirement]]: the need for closer economic co-operation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[requirement]], [[demand]], [[essential]], [[necessity]], [[requisite]], [[desideratum]], [[must-have]]
**6** [[necessity]]: there was no need for an explanation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[necessity]], [[call]], [[demand]], [[obligation]]
**7** [[poverty]] or [[destitution]]: the money will go to those areas where need is greatest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[poverty]], [[deprivation]], [[destitution]], [[penury]]
**8** distress: help has been given to those in need
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[emergency]], [[want]], [[necessity]], [[urgency]], exigency
 See also **needs**
