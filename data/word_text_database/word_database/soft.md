**soft** *adj*
**1** [[easy]] to dent, shape, or cut: soft material
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pliable]], [[flexible]], [[supple]], [[malleable]], [[plastic]], [[elastic]], [[bendable]], [[mouldable]]
**2** not hard; giving way easily under pressure: a soft bed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[yielding]], [[elastic]]
**3** fine, [[smooth]], or fluffy to the touch: soft fur
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[velvety]], [[smooth]], [[silky]], [[feathery]], [[downy]], [[fleecy]]
**4** (of music or sounds) [[quiet]] and pleasing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quiet]], [[gentle]], [[murmured]], [[muted]], [[dulcet]], [[soft-toned]]
**5** (of [[light]] or colour) not excessively bright or harsh
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pale]], [[light]], [[subdued]], [[pastel]], [[bland]], [[mellow]]
**6** (of a breeze or climate) [[temperate]], [[mild]], or pleasant
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mild]], [[temperate]], [[balmy]]
**7** with [[smooth]] curves rather than sharp edges: soft focus
**8** [[kind]] or [[lenient]], often to excess
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lenient]], [[easy-going]], [[lax]], [[indulgent]], [[permissive]], [[spineless]], [[overindulgent]]
**9** [[easy]] to influence or make demands on
**10** (*informal*) feeble or silly; simple: soft in the head
**11** not strong or able to endure hardship
**12** (of a drug) nonaddictive
**13** (*informal*) requiring little effort; [[easy]]: a soft job
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[easy]], [[comfortable]], [[undemanding]], [[cushy]] (*informal*)
**14** (*chem*) (of water) relatively free of mineral salts and therefore easily able to make soap lather
**15** loving and [[tender]]: soft words
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[kind]], [[tender]], [[sentimental]], [[compassionate]], [[sensitive]], [[gentle]], [[tenderhearted]], [[touchy-feely]] (*informal*)
**16** (*phonetics*) denoting the consonants *c* and *g* when they are pronounced sibilantly, as in *cent* and *germ*
**17** **soft on**
**A** [[lenient]] towards: he was accused of being soft on criminals
 **B** experiencing romantic love for
 *adv*
**18** in a soft manner: to speak soft
 *interj*
