**compromise** (**kom**-prom-mize) *n*
**1** [[settlement]] of a dispute by concessions on each side: everyone pleaded for compromise; the compromise was only reached after hours of hard bargaining
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give-and-take]], [[agreement]], [[settlement]], [[accommodation]], [[concession]], [[adjustment]], [[trade-off]]
**2** the terms of such a [[settlement]]
**3** something midway between different things
 *vb* **-mising**, **-mised**
**4** to settle (a dispute) by making concessions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meet halfway]], [[concede]], [[make concessions]], [[give and take]], [[strike a balance]], [[strike a happy medium]], [[go fifty-fifty]] (*informal*)
**5** to put (oneself or another person) in a dishonourable position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[undermine]], [[expose]], [[embarrass]], [[weaken]], [[prejudice]], [[discredit]], [[jeopardize]], dishonour
 *adj*
**6** being, or having the nature of, a compromise: a compromise solution
 **[[compromising]]** *adj*
