**escape** *vb* **-caping**, **-caped**
**1** to [[get away]] or [[break]] free from (confinement)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get away]], [[flee]], [[take off]], [[fly]], [[bolt]], [[slip away]], [[abscond]], [[make a break for it]], [[do a Skase]] (*Austral* *informal*)
**2** to manage to [[avoid]] (something dangerous, unpleasant, or difficult)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[avoid]], [[miss]], [[evade]], [[dodge]], [[shun]], [[elude]], [[duck]] (*informal*), [[steer clear of]]
**3** (of gases, liquids, etc.) to [[leak]] gradually
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[leak out]], [[flow out]], [[gush out]], [[emanate]], [[seep out]], [[exude]], [[spill out]], [[pour forth]]
**4** to [[elude]]; be forgotten by: those little round cakes whose name escapes me
 *n*
**5** the act of escaping or state of having escaped
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[getaway]], [[break]], [[flight]], [[break-out]]
**6** a way of avoiding something difficult, dangerous, or unpleasant: his frequent illnesses provided an escape from intolerable stress
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[avoidance]], [[evasion]], [[circumvention]]
**7** a means of [[relaxation]] or relief: he found temporary escape through the local cinema
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[relaxation]], [[recreation]], [[distraction]], [[diversion]], [[pastime]]
**8** a leakage of gas or liquid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[leak]], [[emission]], [[outpouring]], [[seepage]], [[issue]], [[emanation]]
