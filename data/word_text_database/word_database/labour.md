**labour** * or * (*US** & **Austral*, *sometimes* *Canad*) **labor** *n*
**1** productive [[work]], esp. physical [[work]] done for wages
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[work]], [[effort]], [[employment]], [[toil]], [[industry]]
**2** the people involved in this, as opposed to management
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[workers]], [[employees]], [[workforce]], [[labourers]], [[hands]]
**3** the final stage of pregnancy, leading to [[childbirth]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[childbirth]], [[birth]], [[delivery]], [[parturition]]
**4** difficult [[work]] or a difficult job
 *vb*
**5** to do physical [[work]]: the girls were labouring madly on it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[work]], [[toil]], [[strive]], [[work hard]], [[sweat]] (*informal*), [[slave]], [[endeavour]], [[slog away]] (*informal*)
**6** to [[work hard]] (for something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[struggle]], [[work]], [[strain]], [[work hard]], [[strive]], [[grapple]], [[toil]], [[make an effort]]
**7** to make one's way with difficulty: she was now labouring down the return length
**8** to emphasize too persistently: I have laboured the point
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[overemphasize]], [[stress]], [[elaborate]], [[exaggerate]], [[strain]], [[dwell on]], [[overdo]], [[go on about]]
**9** (usually foll. by *under*) to be at a disadvantage because of a mistake or false belief: she laboured under the illusion that I understood her
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be disadvantaged by]], [[suffer from]], [[be a victim of]], [[be burdened by]]
