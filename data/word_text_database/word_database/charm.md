**charm** *n*
**1** the quality of attracting, fascinating, or delighting people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attraction]], [[appeal]], [[fascination]], [[allure]], [[magnetism]]
**2** a [[trinket]] worn on a bracelet
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trinket]]
**3** a small object worn for supposed magical powers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[talisman]], [[amulet]], [[fetish]]
**4** a [[magic]] [[spell]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spell]], [[magic]], [[enchantment]], [[sorcery]], makutu (*NZ*)
 *vb*
**5** to [[attract]], [[fascinate]], or [[delight]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attract]], [[delight]], [[fascinate]], [[entrance]], [[win over]], [[enchant]], [[captivate]], [[beguile]]
**6** to influence or obtain by personal charm: you can easily be charmed into changing your mind
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[persuade]], [[seduce]], [[coax]], [[beguile]], [[sweet-talk]] (*informal*)
**7** to protect as if by [[magic]]: a charmed life
 **[[charmer]]** *n* 
 **[[charmless]]** *adj*
