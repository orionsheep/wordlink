**past** *adj*
**1** of the time before the present: the past [[history]] of the world
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[former]], [[early]], [[previous]], [[ancient]], [[bygone]], [[olden]]
**2** no longer in existence: past happiness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[over]], [[done]], [[ended]], [[finished]], [[gone]]
**3** immediately [[previous]]: the past year
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[last]], [[previous]]
**4** [[former]]: a past president
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[previous]], [[former]], [[one-time]], ex-
**5** (*grammar*) indicating a tense of verbs used to describe actions that have been begun or completed at the time of speaking
 *n*
**6** **the past** the period of time before the present: a familiar face from the past
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[former times]], [[long ago]], [[days gone by]], [[the olden days]]
**7** the [[history]] of a person or nation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[background]], [[life]], [[history]], [[past life]], [[life story]], [[career to date]]
**8** an earlier disreputable period of someone's [[life]]: a man with a bit of a past
**9** (*grammar*)
**A** the past tense
 **B** a verb in the past tense
 *adv*
**10** [[on]] or onwards: I called but he just walked past
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[on]], [[by]], [[along]]
**11** at a time before the present; ago: three years past
 *prep*
**12** [[beyond]] in time: it's past midnight
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[after]], [[beyond]], [[later than]]
**13** [[beyond]] in place: a procession of mourners filed past the coffin
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[by]], [[across]], [[in front of]]
**14** [[beyond]] the limit of: riches past his wildest dreams
**15** **not put it past someone** to consider someone capable of (a particular action): I wouldn't put it past him to double-cross us
**16** **past it** (*informal*) unable to do the things one could do when younger
