**stab** *vb* **[[stabbing]]**, **stabbed**
**1** to [[pierce]] with a sharp pointed instrument
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pierce]], [[stick]], [[wound]], [[knife]], [[thrust]], [[spear]], [[jab]], [[transfix]]
**2** (often foll. by *at*) to make a [[thrust]] (at); [[jab]]
**3** **stab someone in the back** to do harm to someone by betraying him or her
 *n*
**4** a [[stabbing]]
**5** a sudden, usually unpleasant, sensation: a stab of jealousy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[twinge]], [[prick]], [[pang]], [[ache]]
**6** (*informal*) an [[attempt]]: you've got to have a stab at it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attempt]], [[go]] (*informal*), [[try]], [[endeavour]]
**7** **stab in the back** an act of betrayal that harms a person
