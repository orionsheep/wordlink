**twist** *vb*
**1** to [[turn]] one end or part while the other end or parts remain still or [[turn]] in the opposite direction: never twist or wring woollen garments
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[coil]], [[curl]], [[wind]], [[wrap]], [[screw]], [[twirl]]
**2** to [[distort]] or be distorted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[distort]], [[screw up]], [[contort]], [[mangle]], [[mangulate]] (*Austral* *slang*)
**3** to [[wind]] or twine: the wire had been twisted twice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intertwine]]
**4** to force or be forced out of the natural form or position: I twisted my knee
**5** to [[change]] the meaning of; [[distort]]: he'd twisted the truth to make himself look good
**6** to revolve or rotate: he twisted the switch to [[turn]] the radio off
**7** to wrench with a turning action: he twisted the wheel sharply
**8** to follow a winding course: the road twisted as it climbed
**9** to dance the twist
**10** **twist someone's arm** to persuade or coerce someone
 *n*
**11** the act of twisting: she gave a dainty little twist to her parasol
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wind]], [[turn]], [[spin]], [[swivel]], [[twirl]]
**12** something formed by or as if by twisting: there's a twist in the cable
**13** a decisive [[change]] of direction, aim, meaning, or character: the latest revelations give a new twist to the company's boardroom wranglings
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[development]], [[emphasis]], [[variation]], [[slant]]
**14** an unexpected [[development]] in a story, play, or film
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[surprise]], [[change]], [[turn]] (*informal*), [[development]], [[revelation]], [[reveal]]
**15** a [[bend]]: a twist of the mountain road
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[curve]], [[turn]], [[bend]], [[loop]], [[arc]], [[kink]], [[zigzag]], [[dog-leg]]
**16** a distortion of the original shape or form
**17** a jerky pull, wrench, or [[turn]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wind]], [[turn]], [[spin]], [[swivel]], [[twirl]]
**18** **the twist** a dance popular in the 1960s, in which dancers vigorously twist the hips
**19** **round the twist** (*slang*) mad or eccentric
 **[[twisty]]** *adj*
