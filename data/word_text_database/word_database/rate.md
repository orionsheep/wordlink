**rate** *n*
**1** a quantity or amount considered in relation to or measured against another quantity or amount: he was publishing at the rate of about 10 books a year
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[degree]], [[standard]], [[scale]], [[proportion]], [[ratio]]
**2** a [[price]] or [[charge]] with reference to a [[standard]] or [[scale]]: an exchange rate
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[charge]], [[price]], [[cost]], [[fee]], [[figure]]
**3** the [[speed]] of progress or change: crime is increasing at an alarming rate
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[speed]], [[pace]], [[tempo]], [[velocity]], [[frequency]]
**4** a [[charge]] made per unit for a commodity or service
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[charge]], [[price]], [[cost]], [[fee]], [[figure]]
**5** relative quality: a third-rate power
**6** **[[at any rate]]** in any case
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[at any rate]]**: in any case, anyway, anyhow, at all events
 *vb* **rating**, **rated**
**7** to assign a position on a [[scale]] of relative values: he is rated as one of the top caterers in the country
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evaluate]], [[consider]], [[rank]], [[reckon]], [[value]], [[measure]], [[estimate]], [[count]]
**8** to [[estimate]] the [[value]] of: we rate your services highly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evaluate]], [[consider]], [[rank]], [[reckon]], [[value]], [[measure]], [[estimate]], [[count]]
**9** to [[consider]] or regard: it could hardly be rated a success
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evaluate]], [[consider]], [[rank]], [[reckon]], [[value]], [[measure]], [[estimate]], [[count]]
**10** to [[be worthy of]]: it barely rates a mention
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deserve]], [[merit]], [[be entitled to]], [[be worthy of]]
**11** (*informal*) to have a high opinion of: the cognoscenti have always rated his political skills
