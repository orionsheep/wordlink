**jar**^2 *vb* **[[jarring]]**, **jarred**
**1** to have an irritating or unpleasant effect: sometimes a light remark jarred on her father
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[irritate]], [[annoy]], [[offend]], [[nettle]], [[irk]], [[grate on]], [[get on your nerves]] (*informal*)
**2** to be in disagreement or conflict: their very different temperaments jarred
**3** to [[jolt]] or [[bump]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jolt]], [[rock]], [[shake]], [[bump]], [[rattle]], [[vibrate]], convulse
 *n*
**4** a [[jolt]] or shock
 **[[jarring]]** *adj*
