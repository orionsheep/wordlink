**[[take off]]** *vb*
**1** to remove (a garment)
**2** (of an aircraft) to become airborne
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[take off]]**: lift off, take to the air
**3** (*informal*) to set out on a journey: taking off for the Highlands
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[take off]]**: depart, go, leave, disappear, abscond, decamp, slope off
**4** (*informal*) to become successful or popular: the record took off after being used in a film
**5** to deduct (an amount) from a price or total
**6** to withdraw or put an end to: the bus service has been taken off because of lack of demand
**7** (*informal*) to mimic (someone)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[take someone off]]**: parody, imitate, mimic, mock, caricature, send up (*Brit* *informal*), lampoon, satirize
 *n* **takeoff**
**8** the act or process of making an aircraft airborne
**9** (*informal*) an act of mimicry
