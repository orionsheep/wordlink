**part** *n*
**1** a [[piece]] or [[portion]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[piece]], [[share]], [[proportion]], [[percentage]], [[bit]], [[section]], [[scrap]], [[portion]]
**2** one of several equal divisions: a salad dressing made with two parts oil to one part vinegar
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[component]], [[bit]], [[unit]], [[constituent]]
**3** an actor's [[role]] in a play
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[role]], [[representation]], [[persona]], [[portrayal]], [[depiction]], [[character part]]
**4** a person's duty: his ancestors had done their part nobly and well at Bannockburn
**5** an involvement in or contribution to something: he was jailed for his part in the fraud
**6** a [[region]] or [[area]]: he's well known in these parts; the weather in this part of the country is extreme
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[region]], [[area]], [[district]], [[neighbourhood]], [[quarter]], [[vicinity]]
**7** (*anatomy*) an [[area]] of the body
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[organ]], [[member]], [[limb]]
**8** a [[component]] that can be replaced in a vehicle or machine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[component]], [[bit]], [[unit]], [[constituent]]
**9** (*US** & **Canad** & **Austral*) same as **parting**
**10** (*music*) a melodic line assigned to one or more instrumentalists or singers
**11** **for my part** as far as I am concerned
**12** **for the most part** generally
**13** **in part** to some degree; partly
**14** **on the part of** on behalf of
**15** **part and parcel of** an essential ingredient of
**16** **play a part**
**A** to pretend to be what one is not
 **B** (foll. by *in*) to have something to do with: examinations play a large part in education and in schools
**17** **take part in** to participate in
**18** **take someone's part** to support someone, for example in an argument
**19** **take something in good part** to respond to (teasing or criticism) with good humour
 *vb*
**20** to [[divide]] or [[separate]] from one another: her lips parted in laughter; the cord parted with a pop
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[divide]], [[separate]], [[break]], [[tear]], [[split]], [[rend]] (*literary*), [[detach]], [[sever]]
**21** to go away from one another: we parted with handshakes all round
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[part company]], [[separate]], [[split up]]
**22** to [[split]]: the path parts here
**23** to arrange (the hair) in such a way that a line of scalp is left showing
**24** **part from** to cause (someone) to give up: I was astonished at the way Henry parted his audience from their money
**25** **part with** to give up: check carefully before you part with your cash
 *adv*
**26** to some extent; partly: this book is part history, part travelogue
