**let off** *vb*
**1** to excuse from (work or duties): I'll let you off homework for a week
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[let someone off]]**: excuse, release, discharge, pardon, spare, forgive, exempt, exonerate
**2** to spare (someone) the expected punishment: lots were let off because they couldn't be bothered to prosecute anybody
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[let someone off]]**: excuse, release, discharge, pardon, spare, forgive, exempt, exonerate
**3** to explode or fire (a bomb, gun, or firework)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[let something off]]**: fire, explode, set off, discharge, detonate
**4** to release (liquid, air, or steam)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[let something off]]**: emit, release, leak, exude, give off
