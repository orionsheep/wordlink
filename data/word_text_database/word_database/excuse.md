**excuse** *n*
**1** an [[explanation]] offered to [[justify]] an action which has been criticized or as a [[reason]] for not fulfilling an obligation, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[justification]], [[reason]], [[explanation]], [[defence]], [[grounds]], [[plea]], [[apology]], vindication
 *vb* **-cusing**, **-cused**
**2** to put forward a [[reason]] or [[justification]] for (an action, fault, or offending person)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[justify]], [[explain]], [[defend]], [[vindicate]], [[mitigate]], [[apologize for]], [[make excuses for]]
**3** to [[pardon]] (a person) or [[overlook]] (a fault)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[forgive]], [[pardon]], [[overlook]], [[tolerate]], [[acquit]], [[turn a blind eye to]], [[exonerate]], [[make allowances for]]
**4** to [[free]] (someone) from having to carry out a task, obligation, etc.: a doctor's letter excusing him from games at school
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[free]], [[relieve]], [[exempt]], [[release]], [[spare]], [[discharge]], [[let off]], [[absolve]]
**5** to allow to leave
**6** **be excused** (*euphemistic*) to go to the toilet
**7** **excuse me!** an expression used to catch someone's attention or to [[apologize for]] an interruption, disagreement, etc.
 **[[excusable]]** *adj*
