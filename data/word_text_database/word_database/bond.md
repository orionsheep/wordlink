**bond** *n*
**1** something that binds, fastens, or holds together
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fastening]], [[tie]], [[chain]], [[cord]], [[shackle]], [[fetter]], [[manacle]]
**2** something that brings or holds people together; [[tie]]: a bond of friendship
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tie]], [[union]], [[coupling]], [[link]], [[association]], [[relation]], [[connection]], [[alliance]]
**3** **bonds** something that restrains or imprisons
**4** a written or spoken [[agreement]], esp. a [[promise]]: a marriage bond
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[agreement]], [[word]], [[promise]], [[contract]], [[guarantee]], [[pledge]], [[obligation]], [[covenant]]
**5** (*chem*) a means by which atoms are combined in a molecule
**6** (*finance*) a certificate of debt issued in order to raise funds
**7** (*S African*) the conditional pledging of property, esp. a house, as security for the repayment of a loan
**8** (*law*) a written acknowledgment of an [[obligation]] to pay a sum or to perform a [[contract]]
**9** **in bond** (*commerce*) securely stored until duty is paid
 *vb*
**10** to [[hold]] or be held together; [[bind]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fix]], [[hold]], [[bind]], [[connect]], [[glue]], [[stick]], [[paste]], [[fasten]]
**11** to form a friendship
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[form friendships]], [[connect]]
**12** to put or [[hold]] (goods) in bond
