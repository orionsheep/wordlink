**draw** *vb* **drawing**, **drew**, **drawn**
**1** to [[sketch]] (a picture, pattern, or diagram) with a pen or pencil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sketch]], [[design]], [[outline]], [[trace]], [[portray]], [[paint]], [[depict]], [[mark out]]
**2** to cause (a person or thing) to move closer or further away from a place by pulling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pull]], [[drag]], [[haul]], [[tow]], [[tug]]
**3** to bring, [[take]], or [[pull]] (something) out of a container: he drew a gun and laid it on the table
**4** to [[take]] (something) from a particular source: the inhabitants drew water from the well two miles away
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[extract]], [[take]], [[remove]]
**5** to move in a specified direction: she drew alongside me
**6** to attract: she drew enthusiastic audiences from all over the country
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entice]]
**7** to formulate or decide: he drew similar conclusions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deduce]], [[make]], [[take]], [[derive]], [[infer]]
**8** to cause to flow: the barman nodded and drew two pints
**9** to choose or be given by lottery: Brazil have drawn Spain in the semi-final
**10** (of two teams or contestants) to finish a game with an equal number of points
**11** (*archery*) to bend (a bow) by pulling the string
**12** to cause (pus) to discharge from an abscess or wound
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[extract]], [[take]], [[remove]]
 *n*
**13** a raffle or lottery
**14** (*informal*) a person, place, show, or event that attracts a large audience
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[appeal]], [[pull]] (*informal*), [[charm]], [[attraction]], [[lure]], [[temptation]], [[fascination]], [[allure]]
**15** a contest or game ending in a [[tie]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tie]], [[deadlock]], [[stalemate]], [[impasse]], dead heat
 See also **drawback**
