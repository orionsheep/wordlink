**pay** *vb* **pays**, **paying**, **paid**
**1** to [[give]] (money) in [[return]] for goods or services: Willie paid for the drinks; nurses are not very well paid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reward]], [[compensate]], [[reimburse]], [[recompense]], [[requite]], [[remunerate]] (*formal*), [[front up]]
**2** to [[settle]] (a debt or obligation) by giving or doing something: he has paid his debt to society
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[settle]]
**3** to suffer: she paid dearly for her mistake
**4** to [[give]] (a compliment, regards, attention, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give]], [[extend]], [[present with]], [[grant]], [[hand out]], [[bestow]]
**5** to profit or benefit (someone): it doesn't always pay to be honest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be profitable]], [[make money]], [[make a return]]
**6** to make (a visit or call)
**7** to [[yield]] a [[return]] of: the account pays 5% interest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bring in]], [[earn]], [[return]], [[net]], [[yield]]
**8** **pay one's way**
**A** to contribute one's share of expenses
 **B** to remain solvent without outside help
 *n*
**9** money given in [[return]] for work or services; a [[salary]] or wage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wages]], [[income]], [[payment]], [[earnings]], [[fee]], [[reward]], [[salary]], [[allowance]]
