**prove** *vb* **proving**, **proved**, **proved*** or ***proven**
**1** to [[establish]] the truth or validity of: such a claim is difficult to prove scientifically
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[verify]], [[establish]], [[determine]], [[show]], [[confirm]], [[demonstrate]], [[justify]], [[substantiate]]
**2** (*law*) to [[establish]] the genuineness of (a will)
**3** to [[show]] (oneself) to be: he proved equal to the task
**4** to be found to be: it proved to be a trap
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn out]], [[come out]], [[end up]]
**5** (of dough) to rise in a warm place before baking
 **[[provable]]** *adj*
