**signal** *n*
**1** any [[sign]], [[gesture]], sound, or action used to communicate information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sign]], [[gesture]], [[indication]], [[mark]], [[note]], [[expression]], [[token]]
**2** anything that causes immediate action: this is the signal for a detailed examination of the risk
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cue]], [[sign]], [[prompting]], [[reminder]]
**3**
**A** a variable voltage, current, or electromagnetic wave, by which information is conveyed through an electronic circuit
 **B** the information so conveyed
 *adj*
**4** (*formal*) very important: a signal triumph for the government
 *vb* **-nalling**, **-nalled** (*US*) **-naling**, **-naled**
**5** to communicate (information) by signal
 **[[signally]]** *adv*
