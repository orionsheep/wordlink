**frame** *n*
**1** an open [[structure]] that gives shape and support to something, such as a building
**2** an enclosing [[case]] or border into which something is fitted: the window frame
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[casing]], [[framework]], [[structure]], [[shell]], [[construction]], [[skeleton]], [[chassis]]
**3** the system around which something is built up: caught up in the frame of the revolution
**4** the [[structure]] of the human [[body]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[physique]], [[build]], [[form]], [[body]], [[figure]], [[anatomy]], [[carcass]]
**5** one of a series of exposures on film used in making motion pictures
**6** a television picture scanned by electron beams at a particular frequency
**7** (*snooker*)
**A** a single game in a match
 **B** a wooden triangle used to arrange the red balls in formation before the start of a game
**8** (*slang*) a frame-up
**9** **[[frame of mind]]** a state of mind: in a complacent [[frame of mind]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[frame of mind]]**: mood, state, attitude, humour, temper, outlook, disposition, mind-set
 *vb* **framing**, **framed**
**10** to construct by fitting parts together
**11** to create and develop (plans or a policy)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[devise]], [[draft]], [[compose]], [[sketch]], [[put together]], [[draw up]], [[formulate]], [[map out]]
**12** to construct (a statement) in a particular kind of language
**13** to provide or [[enclose]] with a frame
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mount]], [[case]], [[enclose]]
**14** (*slang*) to conspire to incriminate (someone) on a false charge
