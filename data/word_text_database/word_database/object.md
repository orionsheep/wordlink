**object**^2 *vb*
**1** to express disapproval or opposition: my colleagues objected strongly to further delays
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[protest against]], [[oppose]], [[argue against]], [[draw the line at]], [[take exception to]], [[cry out against]], [[complain against]], [[expostulate against]]
**2** to state as one's reason for opposing: he objected that his small staff would be unable to handle the added work
 **[[objector]]** *n*
