**overtake** *vb* **-taking**, **-took**, **-taken**
**1** (*chiefly* *Brit*) to move past (another vehicle or person) travelling in the same direction
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pass]], [[leave behind]], [[overhaul]], [[catch up with]], [[get past]], [[outdistance]], go by *or* past
**2** to do better than (someone) after catching up with him or her
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[outdo]], [[top]], [[exceed]], [[eclipse]], [[surpass]], [[outstrip]], [[get the better of]], [[outclass]]
**3** to come upon (someone) suddenly or unexpectedly: a mortal tiredness overtook him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: befall (*archaic*, *literary*), [[hit]], [[happen to]], [[catch off guard]], [[catch unawares]]
