**farm** *n*
**1** a tract of land, usually with a house and buildings, cultivated as a unit or used to rear livestock
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[smallholding]], [[ranch]] (*chiefly* *US** & **Canad*), [[farmstead]], [[station]] (*Austral** & **NZ*), [[vineyard]], [[plantation]], [[croft]] (*Scot*), [[grange]], [[homestead]]
**2** a unit of land or water devoted to the growing or rearing of some particular type of fruit, animal, or fish: a salmon farm; an ostrich farm
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[smallholding]], [[ranch]] (*chiefly* *US** & **Canad*), [[farmstead]], [[station]] (*Austral** & **NZ*), [[vineyard]], [[plantation]], [[croft]] (*Scot*), [[grange]], [[homestead]]
 *vb*
**3**
**A** to [[cultivate]] (land)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cultivate]], [[work]], [[plant]], grow crops on
 **B** to rear (animals or fish) on a farm
**4** to do agricultural [[work]] as a way of life
**5** to collect and keep the profits from (a tax district or business)
 See also **farm out**
