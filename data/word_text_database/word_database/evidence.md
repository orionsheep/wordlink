**evidence** *n*
**1** something which provides ground for belief or disbelief: there is no evidence that depression is inherited
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[proof]], [[grounds]], [[demonstration]], [[confirmation]], [[verification]], [[corroboration]], [[authentication]], [[substantiation]]
**2** (*law*) matter produced before a court of law in an attempt to [[prove]] or disprove a point in issue
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[testimony]], [[statement]], [[submission]], [[avowal]]
**3** **in evidence** on [[display]]; apparent
 *vb* **-dencing**, **-denced**
**4** to [[show]] clearly; [[demonstrate]]: you evidenced no talent for music
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[show]], [[prove]], [[reveal]], [[display]], [[indicate]], [[witness]], [[demonstrate]], [[exhibit]]
