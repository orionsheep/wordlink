**noble** *adj*
**1** having or showing high moral qualities: a noble cause
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[worthy]], [[generous]], [[upright]], [[honourable]], [[virtuous]], [[magnanimous]]
**2** belonging to a class of people who hold titles and high social rank
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[aristocratic]], [[lordly]], [[titled]], [[patrician]], [[blue-blooded]], [[highborn]] (*old-fashioned*)
**3** [[impressive]] and magnificent: a noble beast
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dignified]], [[great]], [[imposing]], [[impressive]], [[distinguished]], [[splendid]], [[stately]]
**4** (*chem*) (of certain metals) resisting oxidation
 *n*
**5** a person who holds a title and high social rank
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: lord *or* lady, [[peer]], [[aristocrat]], nobleman *or* woman
 **[[nobly]]** *adv*
