**whisk** *vb*
**1** to move or take somewhere swiftly: I was whisked away in a police car
**2** to [[brush]] away lightly: the waiter whisked the crumbs away with a napkin
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flick]], [[whip]], [[sweep]], [[brush]]
**3** to [[beat]] (cream or eggs) with a whisk or fork until frothy or stiff
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beat]], [[mix vigorously]], [[stir vigorously]], [[whip]], fluff up
 *n*
**4** the act or an instance of whisking: a whisk of a scaly tail
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flick]], [[sweep]], [[brush]], [[whip]]
**5** a utensil for beating cream or eggs until frothy or stiff
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beater]], [[mixer]], [[blender]]
