**[[blow up]]** *vb*
**1** to explode or cause to explode
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[blow something up]]**: explode, bomb, blast, detonate, blow sky-high
**2** to inflate with air
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[blow something up]]**: inflate, pump up, fill, expand, swell, enlarge, puff up, distend
**3** to increase the importance of (something): an affair blown up out of all proportion
**4** (*informal*) to lose one's temper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[blow up]]**: lose your temper, rage, erupt, see red (*informal*), become angry, hit the roof (*informal*), fly off the handle (*informal*), go crook (*Austral** & **NZ* *slang*), blow your top
**5** (*informal*) to reprimand (someone)
**6** (*informal*) to enlarge (a photograph)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[blow something up]]**: magnify, increase, extend, expand, widen, broaden, amplify
**7** to come into existence with sudden force: a crisis had blown up
 *n* **blow-up**
**8** (*informal*) an enlarged photograph
