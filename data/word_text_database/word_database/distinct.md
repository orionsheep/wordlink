**distinct** *adj*
**1** not the same; [[different]]: these two areas produce wines with distinct characteristics
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[different]], [[individual]], [[separate]], [[discrete]], [[unconnected]]
**2** clearly seen, heard, or recognized: it is not possible to draw a distinct line between the two categories; there's a distinct smell of burning
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[striking]], [[dramatic]], [[outstanding]], [[noticeable]], [[well-defined]]
**3** [[clear]] and [[definite]]: there is a distinct possibility of rain
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[definite]], [[marked]], [[clear]], [[decided]], [[obvious]], [[evident]], [[noticeable]], [[conspicuous]]
**4** [[obvious]]: a distinct improvement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[definite]], [[marked]], [[clear]], [[decided]], [[obvious]], [[evident]], [[noticeable]], [[conspicuous]]
 **[[distinctly]]** *adv*
