**sanction** *n*
**1** [[permission]] granted by [[authority]]: official sanction
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[permission]], [[backing]], [[authority]], [[approval]], [[authorization]], O.K. *or* okay (*informal*), stamp *or* seal of [[approval]]
**2** support or [[approval]]: they could not exist without his sanction
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[permission]], [[backing]], [[authority]], [[approval]], [[authorization]], O.K. *or* okay (*informal*), stamp *or* seal of [[approval]]
**3** something that gives binding force to a law, such as a [[penalty]] for breaking it or a reward for obeying it
**4** **sanctions** coercive measures, such as boycotts and trade embargoes, taken by one or more states against another guilty of violating international law
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ban]], [[boycott]], [[embargo]], [[exclusion]], [[penalty]], coercive measures
 *vb*
**5** to officially [[approve]] of or [[allow]]: they do not want to sanction direct payments
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[permit]], [[allow]], [[approve]], [[endorse]], [[authorize]]
**6** to confirm or ratify
