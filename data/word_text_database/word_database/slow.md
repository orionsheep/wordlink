**slow** *adj*
**1** taking a longer time than is usual or expected
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prolonged]], [[protracted]], [[long-drawn-out]], [[lingering]], [[gradual]]
**2** lacking speed: slow movements
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unhurried]], [[sluggish]], [[leisurely]], [[lazy]], [[ponderous]], [[dawdling]], [[laggard]], [[lackadaisical]]
**3** adapted to or producing slow movement: the slow lane
**4** (of a clock or watch) showing a time earlier than the correct time
**5** not quick to understand: slow on the uptake
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stupid]], [[dim]] (*informal*), [[dense]], [[thick]], [[dozy]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[obtuse]], [[braindead]] (*informal*)
**6** dull or uninteresting: the play was very slow
**7** not easily aroused: he is slow to anger
**8** (of business) not busy; slack
**9** (of a fire or oven) giving off low heat
**10** (of photographic film) requiring a relatively long exposure time: a slow film
 *adv*
**11** in a slow manner
 *vb*
**12** (often foll. by *up* or *down*) to decrease or cause to decrease in speed or activity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decelerate]], [[brake]]
 **[[slowly]]** *adv* 
 **[[slowness]]** *n*
