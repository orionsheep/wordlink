**lift** *vb*
**1** to [[rise]] or [[raise]] upwards to a higher place: the breakdown truck was lifting the lorry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[raise]], [[pick up]], [[hoist]], [[draw up]], [[elevate]], [[uplift]], [[heave up]], [[upraise]]
**2** to move upwards: he slowly lifted his hand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[raise]], [[pick up]], [[hoist]], [[draw up]], [[elevate]], [[uplift]], [[heave up]], [[upraise]]
**3** to [[raise]] in status or estimation: lifted from poverty
**4** to [[revoke]] or [[cancel]]: the government lifted its restrictions on imported beef
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[revoke]], [[end]], [[remove]], [[withdraw]], [[stop]], [[cancel]], [[terminate]], [[rescind]]
**5** to [[remove]] (plants or underground crops) from the ground for harvesting
**6** to [[disappear]] or [[disperse]]: the tension lifted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disappear]], [[clear]], [[vanish]], [[disperse]], [[dissipate]], [[rise]], [[be dispelled]]
**7** (*informal*) to plagiarize (music or writing)
 *n*
**8** a compartment raised or lowered in a vertical shaft to transport people or goods to another floor in a building
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[elevator]] (*chiefly* *US*), [[hoist]], [[paternoster]]
**9** a [[ride]] in a car or other vehicle as a passenger
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ride]], [[run]], [[drive]], [[hitch]] (*informal*)
**10** a [[rise]] in morale or feeling of cheerfulness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[boost]], [[encouragement]], [[stimulus]], [[pick-me-up]], [[fillip]], [[shot in the arm]] (*informal*), [[gee-up]]
**11** the act of lifting
**12** the force that lifts airborne objects
