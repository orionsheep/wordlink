**into** *prep*
**1** to the inner part of: they went into the house
**2** to the middle of so as to be surrounded by: into the bushes
**3** against; up against: he drove into a wall
**4** used to indicate the result of a change: they turned the theatre into a garage
**5** (*maths*) used to indicate division: three into six is two
**6** (*informal*) interested in: I'm really into healthy food
