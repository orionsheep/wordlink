**prospect** *n*
**1** (*pl*) [[chances]] or opportunities for [[future]] success: a job with impossible workloads and poor career prospects
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[possibilities]], [[chances]], [[future]], [[potential]], [[expectations]], [[outlook]], [[scope]]
**2** expectation, or something anticipated: she was terrified at the prospect of bringing up two babies on her own
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[idea]], [[outlook]]
**3** (*old-fashioned*) a [[view]] or [[scene]]: a prospect of spires, domes, and towers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[view]], [[landscape]], [[scene]], [[sight]], [[outlook]], [[spectacle]], vista
 *vb*
**4** (sometimes foll. by *for*) to [[search]] for gold or other valuable minerals
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[look]], [[search]], [[seek]], [[dowse]]
