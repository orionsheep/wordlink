**forward** *adj*
**1** directed or moving ahead
**2** at, in, or near the [[front]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[leading]], [[first]], [[head]], [[front]], [[advance]], [[foremost]]
**3** overfamiliar or disrespectful
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[presumptuous]], [[familiar]], [[bold]], [[cheeky]], [[brash]], [[pushy]] (*informal*), [[brazen]], [[shameless]]
**4** well developed or [[advanced]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[future]], [[advanced]], [[premature]], [[prospective]]
**5** of or relating to the [[future]] or favouring change
 *n*
**6** an attacking player in any of various sports, such as soccer
 *adv*
**7** same as **forwards**
 *vb*
**8** to [[send]] (a letter, etc.) on to an ultimate destination
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[send on]], [[send]], [[post]], [[pass on]], [[dispatch]], [[redirect]]
**9** to [[advance]] or [[promote]]: the veneer of street credibility he had used to forward his career
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[further]], [[advance]], [[promote]], [[assist]], [[hurry]], [[hasten]], [[expedite]]
