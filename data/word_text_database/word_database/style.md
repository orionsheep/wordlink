**style** *n*
**1** a [[form]] of appearance, [[design]], or production: I like that style of dress
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[design]], [[form]], [[cut]]
**2** the [[way]] in which something is done: a new style of command
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[manner]], [[way]], [[method]], [[approach]], [[technique]], [[mode]]
**3** [[elegance]] or refinement of manners and dress: he has bags of style
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[elegance]], [[taste]], [[chic]], [[flair]] (*informal*), [[polish]], [[sophistication]], [[panache]], [[flamboyance]], [[élan]]
**4** a distinctive [[manner]] of expression in words, music, painting, etc.: a painting in the Expressionist style
**5** popular [[fashion]] in dress and looks: the old ones had gone out of style
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fashion]], [[trend]], [[mode]], [[vogue]], [[rage]]
**6** a fashionable or showy [[way]] of life: the newly rich could dine in style
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[luxury]], [[ease]], [[comfort]], [[elegance]], [[grandeur]], [[affluence]]
**7** the particular kind of spelling, punctuation, and [[design]] followed in a book, journal, or publishing house
**8** (*botany*) the stemlike part of a flower that bears the stigma
 *vb* **styling**, **styled**
**9** to [[design]], [[shape]], or [[tailor]]: neatly styled hair
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[design]], [[cut]], [[tailor]], [[fashion]], [[shape]], [[arrange]], [[adapt]]
**10** to [[name]] or [[call]]: Walsh, who styled himself the Memory Man
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[call]], [[name]], [[term]], [[label]], [[entitle]], [[dub]], [[designate]]
