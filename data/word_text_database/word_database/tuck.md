**tuck** *vb*
**1** to [[push]] or [[fold]] into a small space or between two surfaces: she tucked the letter into her handbag
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[push]], [[stick]], [[stuff]], [[slip]], [[ease]], [[insert]], [[pop]] (*informal*)
**2** to thrust the loose ends or sides of (something) into a confining space, so as to make it neat and secure: he tucked his shirt back into his trousers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[push]], [[stick]], [[stuff]], [[slip]], [[ease]], [[insert]], [[pop]] (*informal*)
**3** to make a tuck or tucks in (a garment)
 *n*
**4** a [[pleat]] or [[fold]] in a part of a garment, usually stitched down
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fold]], [[gather]], [[pleat]], [[pinch]]
**5** (*Brit* *informal*) [[food]], esp. cakes and sweets
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[food]], [[grub]] (*slang*), [[kai]] (*NZ* *informal*), [[nosh]] (*slang*)
