**have** *vb* **has**, **having**, **had**
**1** to [[possess]]: he has a collection of classic cars; I have an iron constitution
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[own]], [[keep]], [[possess]], [[hold]], [[retain]], [[boast]], [[be the owner of]]
**2** to [[receive]], [[take]], or [[obtain]]: I had a long letter
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get]], [[obtain]], [[take]], [[receive]], [[accept]], [[gain]], [[secure]], [[acquire]]
**3** to [[hold]] in the mind: she always had a yearning to be a schoolteacher
**4** to [[possess]] a knowledge of: I have no German
**5** to [[experience]] or be affected by: a good way to have a change
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[experience]], [[go through]], [[undergo]], [[meet with]], [[come across]], [[run into]], [[be faced with]]
**6** to [[suffer]] from: to have a blood pressure problem
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suffer]], [[experience]], [[undergo]], [[sustain]], [[endure]], [[be suffering from]]
**7** to [[gain]] control of or advantage over: you have me on that point
**8** (*slang*) to cheat or outwit: I've been had
**9** to show: have mercy on me
**10** to [[take]] part in; [[hold]]: I had a telephone conversation
**11** to cause to be done: have my shoes mended by Friday
**12** to eat or drink
**13** (*vulgar*, *slang*) to have sexual intercourse with
**14** to tolerate or allow: I won't have all this noise
**15** to [[receive]] as a guest: we have visitors
**16** to be pregnant with or [[give birth to]] (offspring)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give birth to]], [[bear]], [[deliver]], [[bring forth]], [[beget]]
**17** used to form past tenses: I have gone; I had gone
**18** **have had it** (*informal*)
**A** to be exhausted or killed
 **B** to have lost one's last chance
**19** **have it off** (*Brit* *vulgar*, *slang*) to have sexual intercourse
**20** **[[have to]]** used to express compulsion or necessity: you'd [[have to]] wait six months
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[have to]]**: must, should, be forced, ought, be obliged, be bound, have got to, be compelled
 *n*
**21** **haves** (*informal*) people who have wealth, security, etc.: the haves and the have-nots
 See also **have on**
