**devil** *n*
**1** (*theol*) **[[the Devil]]** the chief spirit of evil and enemy of God
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[the Devil]]**: Satan, Lucifer, Prince of Darkness, Mephistopheles, Evil One, Beelzebub, Old Nick (*informal*)
**2** any [[evil spirit]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evil spirit]], [[demon]], [[fiend]], [[atua]] (*NZ*), [[wairua]] (*NZ*)
**3** a [[person]] regarded as wicked
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[brute]], [[monster]], [[beast]], [[barbarian]], [[fiend]], [[terror]], [[swine]], [[ogre]]
**4** a [[person]]: lucky devil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[person]], [[individual]], [[soul]], [[creature]], [[thing]], [[beggar]]
**5** a [[person]] regarded as daring: be a devil!
**6** (*informal*) something difficult or annoying
**7** **between [[the devil]] and the deep blue sea** between equally undesirable alternatives
**8** **give [[the devil]] his due** to acknowledge the talent or success of an unpleasant [[person]]
**9** **talk of [[the devil]]!** used when an absent [[person]] who has been the subject of conversation arrives unexpectedly
**10** **[[the devil]]** used as an exclamation to show surprise or annoyance: what [[the devil]] is she doing here?
 *vb* **-villing**, **-villed** (*US*) **-viling**, **-viled**
**11** to prepare (food) by coating with a highly flavoured spiced mixture
**12** (*chiefly* *Brit*) to do routine literary work for a lawyer or author
