**charge** *vb* **charging**, **charged**
**1** to ask (an [[amount]] of money) as a [[price]]
**2** to enter a debit against a person's account for (a purchase)
**3** to [[accuse]] (someone) formally of a crime in a court of law
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accuse]], [[indict]], [[impeach]], [[incriminate]], [[arraign]]
**4** to make a [[rush]] at or sudden [[attack]] upon
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attack]], [[assault]], [[assail]]
**5** to [[fill]] (a glass)
**6** to cause (an accumulator or capacitor) to take and store electricity
**7** to [[fill]] or saturate with liquid or gas: old mine workings charged with foul gas
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fill]], [[load]]
**8** to [[fill]] with a feeling or mood: the emotionally charged atmosphere
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fill]], [[load]]
**9** (*formal*) to command or assign: the president has charged his foreign minister with trying to open talks
 *n*
**10** a [[price]] charged for something; [[cost]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[price]], [[rate]], [[cost]], [[amount]], [[payment]], [[expense]], [[toll]], [[expenditure]]
**11** a formal [[accusation]] of a crime in a court of law
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accusation]], [[allegation]], [[indictment]], [[imputation]]
**12** an onrush or [[attack]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attack]], [[rush]], [[assault]], [[onset]], [[onslaught]], [[stampede]], [[sortie]]
**13** [[custody]] or guardianship: in the charge of the police
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[care]], [[trust]], [[responsibility]], [[custody]], [[safekeeping]]
**14** a person or thing committed to someone's [[care]]: a nanny reported the father of one of her charges to social workers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[duty]], [[office]], [[responsibility]], [[remit]]
**15**
**A** a cartridge or shell
 **B** the explosive required to fire a gun
**16** (*physics*)
**A** the attribute of matter responsible for all electrical phenomena, existing in two forms, positive and negative
 **B** the total [[amount]] of electricity stored in a capacitor or an accumulator
**17** **in charge of** in control of and responsible for: in charge of defence and foreign affairs
