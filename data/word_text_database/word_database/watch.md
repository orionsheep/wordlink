**watch** *vb*
**1** to [[look at]] or [[observe]] closely and attentively
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[look at]], [[observe]], [[regard]], [[eye]], [[see]], [[view]], [[contemplate]], [[eyeball]] (*slang*)
**2** to [[look after]] (a child or a pet)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guard]], [[keep]], [[mind]], [[protect]], [[tend]], [[look after]], [[shelter]], [[take care of]]
**3** to maintain a careful interest in or control over: it reminds me to watch my diet
**4** **watch for** to be keenly alert to or cautious about: the vigilant night watchman hired to watch for thieves
**5** **watch it!** be careful!
 *n*
**6** a small portable [[timepiece]] worn strapped to the wrist or in a waistcoat pocket
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wristwatch]], [[timepiece]], [[chronometer]]
**7** the act or an instance of watching
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guard]], [[surveillance]], [[observation]], [[vigil]], [[lookout]]
**8** (*naut*) any of the periods, usually of four hours, during which part of a ship's crew are on duty
**9** **[[keep]] a close watch on** to maintain a careful interest in or control over: he keeps a close watch on party opinion
**10** **[[keep]] watch** to be keenly alert to danger; [[keep]] [[guard]]
**11** **on the watch** on the [[lookout]]
 **[[watcher]]** *n*
