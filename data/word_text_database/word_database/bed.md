**bed** *n*
**1** a piece of furniture on which to sleep
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bedstead]], [[couch]], [[berth]], [[cot]], [[divan]]
**2** a [[plot]] of [[ground]] in which plants are grown
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plot]], [[area]], [[row]], [[strip]], [[patch]], [[ground]], [[land]], [[garden]]
**3** the [[bottom]] of a river, lake, or sea
**4** any underlying structure or part
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[base]], [[footing]], [[basis]], [[bottom]], [[foundation]], [[underpinning]], [[groundwork]], [[bedrock]]
**5** a layer of rock
**6** **get out of bed on the wrong side** (*informal*) to begin the day in a bad mood
**7** **go to bed with** to have sexual intercourse with
 *vb* **bedding**, **bedded**
**8** (foll. by *down*) to go to or put into a place to sleep or rest
**9** to have sexual intercourse with
**10** to place firmly into position: the poles were bedded in concrete
**11** (*geol*) to form or be arranged in a distinct layer
**12** to plant in a bed of soil
