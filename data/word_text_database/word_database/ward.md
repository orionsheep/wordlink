**ward** *n*
**1** a [[room]] in a hospital for patients requiring similar kinds of care: the maternity ward
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[room]], [[department]], [[unit]], [[quarter]], [[division]], [[section]], [[apartment]] (*US*), [[cubicle]]
**2** one of the districts into which a town, [[parish]], or other [[area]] is divided for administration or elections
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[district]], [[constituency]], [[area]], [[division]], [[zone]], [[parish]], [[precinct]]
**3** Also called: **ward of court** (*law*) a person, esp. a child whose parents are dead, who is placed under the control or protection of a guardian or of a court
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dependant]], [[charge]], [[pupil]], [[minor]], protégé
 See also **ward off**
