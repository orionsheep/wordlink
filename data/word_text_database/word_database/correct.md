**correct** *adj*
**1** free from error; [[true]]: the correct answer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accurate]], [[right]], [[true]], [[exact]], [[precise]], [[flawless]], [[faultless]], O.K. *or* okay (*informal*)
**2** in conformity with accepted standards: in most cultures there is a strong sense of correct sexual conduct
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[right]], [[standard]], [[appropriate]], [[acceptable]], [[proper]], [[precise]]
 *vb*
**3** to make free from or put [[right]] errors
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rectify]], [[remedy]], [[redress]], [[right]], [[reform]], [[cure]], [[adjust]], [[amend]]
**4** to indicate the errors in (something)
**5** to [[rebuke]] or punish in order to improve: I stand corrected
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rebuke]], [[discipline]], [[reprimand]], [[chide]], [[admonish]], [[chastise]], [[chasten]], [[reprove]]
**6** to make conform to a [[standard]]
 **[[correctly]]** *adv* 
 **[[correctness]]** *n*
