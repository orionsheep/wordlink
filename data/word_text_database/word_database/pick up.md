**[[pick up]]** *vb*
**1** to lift or raise: he picked up his glass
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pick something or someone up]]**: lift, raise, gather, take up, grasp, uplift
**2** to obtain or purchase: a couple of pictures she had picked up in a flea market in Paris
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pick something up]]**: obtain, get, find, buy, discover, purchase, acquire, locate
**3** to improve in health or condition: business had picked up after a difficult first quarter 
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pick up]]**: improve, recover, rally, get better, bounce back, make progress, perk up, turn the corner
**4** to learn as one goes along: she had a good ear and picked up languages quickly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pick something up]]**: learn, master, acquire, get the hang of (*informal*), become proficient in
**5** to raise (oneself) after a fall or setback: she picked herself up and got on with her life
**6** to resume; return to
**7** to accept the responsibility for paying (a bill)
**8** to collect or give a lift to (passengers or goods)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pick something or someone up]]**: collect, get, call for
**9** (*informal*) to become acquainted with for a sexual purpose
**10** (*informal*) to arrest
**11** to receive (sounds or signals)
