**evil** *n*
**1** a force or power that brings about [[wickedness]] and harm: the battle between good and evil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wickedness]], [[bad]], [[vice]], [[sin]], [[wrongdoing]], [[depravity]], [[badness]], [[villainy]]
**2** a [[wicked]] or morally wrong act or thing: the evil of racism
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[act of cruelty]], [[crime]], [[ill]], [[horror]], [[outrage]], [[misfortune]], [[mischief]], affliction
 *adj*
**3** (of a person) deliberately causing great harm and misery; [[wicked]]: an evil dictator
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wicked]], [[bad]], [[malicious]], [[immoral]], [[sinful]], [[malevolent]], [[depraved]], [[villainous]]
**4** (of an act, idea, etc.) causing great harm and misery; morally wrong: what you did was deeply evil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harmful]], [[disastrous]], [[destructive]], [[dire]], [[catastrophic]], [[pernicious]] (*formal*), [[ruinous]]
**5** very [[unpleasant]]: it was fascinating to see people vanish as if we had some very evil smell
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[offensive]], [[nasty]], [[foul]], [[unpleasant]], [[vile]], [[noxious]], [[disagreeable]], [[pestilential]]
 **[[evilly]]** *adv*
