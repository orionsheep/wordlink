**sight** *n*
**1** the ability to [[see]]; [[vision]]. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**visual**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vision]], [[eyes]], [[eyesight]], [[seeing]], [[eye]]
**2** an instance of [[seeing]]
**3** the [[range of vision]]: the cemetery was out of sight
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[view]], [[range of vision]], [[visibility]]
**4** anything that is seen
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spectacle]], [[show]], [[scene]], [[display]], [[exhibition]], [[vista]], [[pageant]]
**5** point of [[view]]; judgment: nothing has changed in my sight
**6** (*informal*) anything unpleasant to [[see]]: she looked a sight in the streetlamps
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[eyesore]], [[mess]], [[monstrosity]]
**7** a device for guiding the [[eye]] in aiming a gun or making an observation with an optical instrument
**8** an aim or observation made with such a device
**9** **sights** anything worth [[seeing]]: the great sights of Barcelona
**10** **a sight** (*informal*) a great deal: it's a sight warmer than in the hall
**11** **a sight for sore [[eyes]]** a welcome sight
**12** **catch sight of** to glimpse
**13** **know someone by sight** to be able to recognize someone without having ever been introduced
**14** **lose sight of**
**A** to be unable to [[see]] (something) any longer
 **B** to forget: we lose sight of priorities
**15** **on sight** as soon as someone or something is seen
**16** **set one's sights on** to have (a specified goal) in mind
**17** **sight unseen** without having seen the object concerned: he would have taken it sight unseen
 *vb*
**18** to [[see]] (someone or something) briefly or suddenly: the two suspicious vessels were sighted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spot]], [[see]], [[observe]], [[distinguish]], [[perceive]], [[make out]], [[discern]], behold (*archaic*, *literary*)
**19** to aim (a firearm) using the sight
