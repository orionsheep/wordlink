**commission** *n*
**1** an [[order]] for a piece of work, esp. a work of art or a piece of writing
**2** a [[duty]] given to a person or group to perform
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[duty]], [[task]], [[mission]], [[mandate]], [[errand]]
**3** the [[fee]] or [[percentage]] paid to a salesperson for each sale made
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fee]], [[cut]] (*informal*), [[percentage]], [[royalties]], [[rake-off]] (*slang*)
**4** a group of people appointed to perform certain duties: a new parliamentary commission on defence
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[committee]], [[board]], [[representatives]], [[commissioners]], [[delegation]], [[deputation]]
**5** the act of committing a sin or crime
**6** (*military*) the rank or authority officially given to an officer
**7** authority to perform certain duties
**8** **in *or* out of commission** in *or* not in working [[order]]
 *vb*
**9** to place an [[order]] for: a report commissioned by the United Nations; a new work commissioned by the BBC Symphony Orchestra
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[appoint]], [[order]], [[contract]], [[select]], [[engage]], [[delegate]], [[nominate]], [[authorize]]
**10** (*military*) to give a commission to
**11** to prepare (a ship) for active service
**12** to grant authority to
