**tackle** *vb* **-ling**, **-led**
**1** to deal with (a problem or task) in a determined way
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[undertake]], [[attempt]], [[embark upon]], [[get stuck into]] (*informal*), have a go *or* stab at (*informal*)
**2** to confront (someone) about something: I intend to tackle both management and union on this issue
**3** to attack and fight (a person or animal)
**4** (*sport*) to [[attempt]] to get the ball away from (an opposing player)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intercept]], [[stop]], [[challenge]]
 *n*
**5** (*sport*) an [[attempt]] to get the ball away from an opposing player
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[block]], [[challenge]]
**6** the equipment required for a particular sport or occupation: fishing tackle
**7** a set of ropes and pulleys for lifting heavy weights
**8** (*naut*) the ropes and other rigging aboard a ship
