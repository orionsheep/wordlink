**[[knock off]]** *vb*
**1** (*informal*) to finish work: around ten, the day shift knocked off
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[knock off]]**: stop work, get out, call it a day (*informal*), finish work, clock off, clock out
**2** (*informal*) to make or do hastily or easily: she knocked off 600 books in all during her long life
**3** (*informal*) to take (an amount) off the price of (an article): I'll [[knock off]] 10% if you pay cash
**4** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) to steal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[knock something off]]**: steal, take, nick (*slang*, *chiefly* *Brit*), thieve, rob, pinch (*Brit* *informal*)
**5** (*slang*) to kill
**6** (*slang*) to stop doing something; used as a command: knock it off!
