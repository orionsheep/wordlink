**flesh** *n*
**1** the soft part of the body of an animal or human, esp. muscular [[tissue]]. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**carnal**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fat]], [[muscle]], [[tissue]], [[brawn]]
**2** (*informal*) excess [[weight]]; [[fat]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fatness]], [[fat]], [[adipose tissue]], [[corpulence]], [[weight]]
**3** the [[meat]] of animals as opposed to that of fish or, sometimes, fowl
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meat]]
**4** the thick soft part of a fruit or vegetable
**5** **the flesh** sexuality or sensuality: pleasures of the flesh
**6** **flesh and blood** human beings or human nature: it is almost more than flesh and blood can bear
**7** **in the flesh** in person; actually present
**8** **one's own flesh and blood** one's own family
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[your own flesh and blood]]**: family, blood, relations, relatives, kin, kith and kin, blood relations, kinsfolk, ainga (*NZ*), rellies (*Austral* *slang*)
**9** **press the flesh** (*informal*) to shake hands with large numbers of people, esp. in political campaigning
