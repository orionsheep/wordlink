**rig** *vb* **rigging**, **rigged**
**1** to [[arrange]] in a dishonest way, for profit or advantage: he claimed that the poll was rigged
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fix]] (*informal*), [[engineer]] (*informal*), [[arrange]], [[manipulate]], [[tamper with]], [[gerrymander]]
**2** to set up or prepare (something) hastily ready for use
**3** (*naut*) to [[equip]] (a vessel or mast) with (sails or rigging)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[equip]], [[fit out]], [[kit out]], [[outfit]], [[supply]], furnish
 *n*
**4** an apparatus for drilling for oil and gas
**5** (*naut*) the arrangement of the sails and masts of a vessel
**6** apparatus or equipment
**7** (*informal*) an [[outfit]] of clothes
**8** (*US** & **Canad** & **Austral*) an articulated lorry
 See also **rig up**
