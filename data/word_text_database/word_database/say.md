**say** *vb* **saying**, **said**
**1** to [[speak]] or [[utter]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[speak]], [[utter]], [[voice]], [[express]], [[pronounce]]
**2** to [[express]] (an idea) in words: I can't say what I feel
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[state]], [[declare]], [[remark]], [[announce]], [[maintain]], [[mention]], [[assert]], [[affirm]]
**3** to [[state]] (an opinion or fact) positively: I say you are wrong
**4** to indicate or show: the clock says ten to nine
**5** to recite: to say grace
**6** to report or allege: they say we shall have rain today
**7** to [[suppose]] as an example or possibility: let us say that he is lying
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suppose]], [[imagine]], [[assume]], [[presume]]
**8** to [[convey]] by means of artistic expression: what does the artist have to say in this picture?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suggest]], [[express]], [[imply]], [[communicate]], [[disclose]], [[give away]], [[convey]], [[divulge]]
**9** to make a case for: there is much to be said for it
**10** **go without saying** to be so obvious as to need no explanation
**11** **to say the least** at the very least
 *adv*
**12** approximately: there were, say, 20 people present
**13** for example: choose a number, say, four
 *n*
**14** the right or [[chance to speak]]: the opposition has hardly had a say in these affairs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[chance to speak]], [[vote]], [[voice]]
**15** [[authority]], esp. to [[influence]] a decision: he has a lot of say
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[influence]], [[power]], [[control]], [[authority]], [[weight]], [[clout]] (*informal*), [[mana]] (*NZ*)
