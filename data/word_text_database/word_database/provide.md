**provide** *vb* **-viding**, **-vided**
**1** to make available
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[supply]], [[give]], [[distribute]], [[outfit]], [[equip]], [[donate]], [[furnish]], [[dispense]]
**2** to [[afford]]; [[yield]]: social activities providing the opportunity to meet new people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give]], [[bring]], [[add]], [[produce]], [[present]], [[serve]], [[afford]], [[yield]]
**3** (often foll. by *for* or *against*) to take careful precautions: we provide for the possibility of illness in the examination regulations
**4** (foll. by *for*) to support financially: both parents should be expected to provide for their children
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[provide for someone]]**: support, care for, keep, maintain, sustain, take care of, fend for
**5** **provide for** (*formal*) (of a law, treaty, etc.) to make possible: a bill providing for stiffer penalties for racial discrimination
 **[[provider]]** *n*
