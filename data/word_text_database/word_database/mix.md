**mix** *vb*
**1** to [[combine]] or [[blend]] into one mass or substance: mix the water, yeast, and flour into a smooth dough
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[combine]], [[blend]], [[merge]], [[join]], [[cross]], [[fuse]], [[mingle]], [[jumble]]
**2** to be able to [[combine]] into one substance: oil and water do not mix
**3** to form by combining different substances: to mix cement
**4** to do at the same time: to mix business and pleasure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[combine]], [[marry]], [[blend]], [[integrate]], [[amalgamate]], [[coalesce]], [[meld]]
**5** to be outgoing in social situations: he [[mixed]] well
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[socialize]], [[associate]], [[hang out]] (*informal*), [[mingle]], [[circulate]], [[consort]], [[hobnob]], [[fraternize]]
**6** (*music*) to balance and adjust individual performers' parts to make an overall sound by electronic means
 *n*
**7** something produced by mixing; [[mixture]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mixture]], [[combination]], [[blend]], [[fusion]], [[compound]], [[assortment]], [[alloy]], [[medley]]
**8** a [[mixture]] of ingredients, esp. one commercially prepared for making a cake
**9** (*music*) the sound produced by mixing
 See also **mix-up**
 **[[mixed]]** *adj*
