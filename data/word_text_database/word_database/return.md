**return** *vb*
**1** to [[come back]] to a former place or state
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[come back]], [[go back]], [[retreat]], [[turn back]], [[revert]], [[reappear]]
**2** to give, put, or send back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put back]], [[replace]], [[restore]], [[reinstate]]
**3** to repay with something of equivalent value: she returned the compliment
**4** to hit, throw, or play (a ball) back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[come back]], [[go back]], [[retreat]], [[turn back]], [[revert]], [[reappear]]
**5** to [[recur]] or [[reappear]]: as he relaxed his appetite returned
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recur]], [[repeat]], [[persist]], [[revert]], [[happen again]], [[reappear]], [[come again]]
**6** to [[come back]] or [[revert]] in thought or speech: let's return to what he said
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[come back]], [[go back]], [[retreat]], [[turn back]], [[revert]], [[reappear]]
**7** to earn or [[yield]] ([[profit]] or [[interest]])
**8** to answer or reply
**9** to vote into office
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[elect]], [[choose]], [[vote in]]
**10** (*law*) (of a jury) to deliver (a verdict)
 *n*
**11** the act or an instance of coming back
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reappearance]]
**12** the act of being returned
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[restoration]], [[reinstatement]], [[re-establishment]]
**13** replacement or [[restoration]]: the return of law and order
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[restoration]], [[reinstatement]], [[re-establishment]]
**14** something that is given or sent back
**15** (*sport*) the act of playing or throwing a ball back
**16** a [[recurrence]] or [[reappearance]]: the return of tuberculosis
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recurrence]], [[repetition]], [[reappearance]], [[reversion]], [[persistence]]
**17** the [[yield]] or [[profit]] from an investment or venture
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[profit]], [[interest]], [[gain]], [[income]], [[revenue]], [[yield]], [[proceeds]], [[takings]]
**18** a [[statement]] of one's taxable [[income]] (a **tax return**)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[statement]], [[report]], [[form]], [[list]], [[account]], [[summary]]
**19** an answer or reply
**20** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ*) short for **return ticket**
**21** **in return** in exchange
**22** **returns** [[statement]] of the votes counted at an election
**23** **by return (of post)** (*Brit*) by the next post back to the sender
**24** **many happy returns (of the day)** a conventional birthday greeting
 *adj*
**25** of or being a return: the team is keen on a return match
 **[[returnable]]** *adj*
