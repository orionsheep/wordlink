**supply** *vb* **-plies**, **-plying**, **-plied**
**1** to [[provide]] with something required: Nigeria may supply them with oil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[provide]], [[give]], [[furnish]], [[produce]], [[stock]], [[grant]], [[contribute]], yield
 *n*, *pl* **-plies**
**2** the act of providing something
**3** an amount available for use; [[stock]]: electricity supply
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[store]], [[fund]], [[stock]], [[source]], [[reserve]], [[quantity]], [[hoard]], [[cache]]
**4** **supplies** [[food]] and [[equipment]] needed for a trip or military campaign
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[provisions]], [[necessities]], [[stores]], [[food]], [[materials]], [[equipment]], [[rations]]
**5** (*econ*) the amount of a commodity that producers are willing and able to offer for sale at a specified price: supply and demand
**6** a person who acts as a temporary substitute
 *adj*
**7** acting as a temporary substitute: supply teachers
 **[[supplier]]** *n*
