**slump** *vb*
**1** (of commercial activity or prices) to [[decline]] suddenly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fall]], [[sink]], [[plunge]], [[crash]], [[collapse]], [[slip]]
**2** to [[sink]] or [[fall]] heavily and suddenly: she slumped back with exhaustion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sag]], [[hunch]], [[droop]], [[slouch]], loll
 *n*
**3** a severe [[decline]] in commercial activity or prices; [[depression]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recession]], [[depression]], [[stagnation]], [[inactivity]], hard *or* bad times
**4** a sudden or marked [[decline]] or failure: a slump in demand for oil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fall]], [[drop]], [[decline]], [[crash]], [[collapse]], [[reverse]], [[downturn]], [[trough]]
