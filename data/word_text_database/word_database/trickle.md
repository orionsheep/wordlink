**trickle** *vb* **-ling**, **-led**
**1** to flow or cause to flow in a [[thin stream]] or drops: tears trickled down her cheeks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dribble]], [[run]], [[drop]], [[stream]], [[drip]], [[ooze]], [[seep]], [[exude]]
**2** to move slowly or in small groups: voters trickled to the polls
 *n*
**3** a thin, irregular, or slow flow of something: a trickle of blood
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dribble]], [[drip]], [[seepage]], [[thin stream]]
