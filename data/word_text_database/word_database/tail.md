**tail** *n*
**1** the rear part of an animal's body, usually forming a long thin flexible part attached to the trunk. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**caudal**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[extremity]], [[appendage]], [[brush]], [[rear end]], [[hindquarters]], [[hind part]]
**2** any long thin part projecting or hanging from the back or [[end]] of something: the waiter produced menus from beneath the tail of his coat
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[train]], [[end]], [[trail]], [[tailpiece]]
**3** the last part: the tail of the procession
**4** the rear part of an aircraft
**5** (*astron*) the luminous stream of gas and dust particles driven from the head of a comet when it is close to the sun
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[train]], [[end]], [[trail]], [[tailpiece]]
**6** (*informal*) a person employed to [[follow]] and spy upon another
**7** **[[turn tail]]** to run away
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[turn tail]]**: run away, flee, run off, retreat, cut and run, take to your heels
**8** **with one's tail between one's legs** completely defeated and demoralized
 *adj*
**9** at the back: tail feathers
 *vb*
**10** (*informal*) to [[follow]] (someone) stealthily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[follow]], [[track]], [[shadow]], [[trail]], stalk
 See also **tail off**, **[[tails]]**
