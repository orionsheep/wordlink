**due** *adj*
**1** [[expected]] to happen, be done, or arrive at a particular time: he is due to return on Thursday
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[expected]], [[scheduled]]
**2** immediately [[payable]]: the balance is now due
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[payable]], [[outstanding]], [[owed]], [[owing]], [[unpaid]], [[in arrears]]
**3** [[owed]] as a debt: they finally agreed to pay her the money she was due
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[payable]], [[outstanding]], [[owed]], [[owing]], [[unpaid]], [[in arrears]]
**4** [[fitting]] or [[proper]]: he was found guilty of driving without due care and attention
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fitting]], [[deserved]], [[appropriate]], [[justified]], [[suitable]], [[merited]], [[proper]], [[rightful]]
**5** **due to** happening or existing as a direct result of someone or something else: the cause of death was chronic kidney failure due to diabetes
 *n*
**6** something that is [[owed]] or required
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[right]](s), [[privilege]], [[deserts]], [[merits]], [[comeuppance]] (*informal*)
**7** **give someone his *or* her due** to acknowledge someone's good points: I'll give him his due, he's resourceful
 *adv*
**8** [[directly]] or [[exactly]]: due west
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[directly]], [[dead]], [[straight]], [[exactly]], [[undeviatingly]]
