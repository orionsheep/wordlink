**swell** *vb* **swelling**, **swelled**, **swollen*** or ***swelled**
**1** (of a part of the body) to [[grow]] in size as a result of injury or infection: his face swelled and became pale
**2** to [[increase]] in size as a result of being filled with air or liquid: a [[balloon]] swells if you force in more air
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[expand]], [[increase]], [[grow]], [[rise]], [[balloon]], [[enlarge]], [[bulge]], [[dilate]]
**3** to [[grow]] or cause (something) to [[grow]] in size, numbers, amount, or degree: Israel's population is swelling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[rise]], [[grow]], [[mount]], [[expand]], [[accelerate]], [[escalate]], [[multiply]]
**4** (of an emotion) to become more intense: his anger swelled within him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[rise]], [[grow]], [[mount]], [[expand]], [[accelerate]], [[escalate]], [[multiply]]
**5** (of the seas) to [[rise]] in waves
**6** (of a sound) to become gradually louder and then die away
 *n*
**7** the waving movement of the surface of the open sea
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wave]], [[surge]], [[billow]]
**8** an [[increase]] in size, numbers, amount, or degree
**9** a [[bulge]]
**10** (*old-fashioned*, *informal*) a person who is wealthy, upper class, and fashionably dressed
**11** (*music*) an [[increase]] in sound followed by an immediate dying away
 *adj*
**12** (*slang*, *chiefly* *US*) excellent or fine
