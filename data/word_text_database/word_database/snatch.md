**snatch** *vb*
**1** to seize or [[grasp]] (something) suddenly: she snatched the paper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grab]], [[grip]], [[grasp]], [[clutch]]
**2** (usually foll. by *at*) to attempt to seize suddenly
**3** to take hurriedly: these players had snatched a few hours sleep
**4** to remove suddenly: she snatched her hand away
 *n*
**5** an act of snatching
**6** a small [[piece]] or incomplete [[part]]: snatches of song
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bit]], [[part]], [[fragment]], [[piece]], [[snippet]]
**7** a brief spell: snatches of sleep
**8** (*slang*, *chiefly* *US*) an act of kidnapping
**9** (*Brit* *slang*) a robbery: a wages snatch
