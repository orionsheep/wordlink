**but** *conj*
**1** contrary to expectation: he cut his hand but didn't cry
**2** in contrast; on the contrary: I like seafood but my husband doesn't
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[however]], [[still]], [[yet]], [[nevertheless]]
**3** other than: we can't do anything but wait
**4** without it happening: we never go out but it rains
 *prep*
**5** [[except]]: they saved all but one
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[except]] (for), [[save]], [[bar]], [[barring]], [[excepting]], [[excluding]], [[with the exception of]]
**6** **but for** were it not for: but for you, we couldn't have managed
 *adv*
**7** [[only]]: I can but try; he was but a child
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[only]], [[just]], [[simply]], merely
 *n*
**8** an objection: ifs and buts
