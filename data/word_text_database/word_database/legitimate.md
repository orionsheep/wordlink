**legitimate** *adj*
**1** [[authorized]] by or in accordance with law: legitimate accounting practices
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lawful]], [[legal]], [[genuine]], [[authentic]], [[authorized]], [[rightful]], [[kosher]] (*informal*), [[dinkum]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*), [[licit]]
**2** based on [[correct]] or acceptable principles of reasoning: a legitimate argument
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reasonable]], [[correct]], [[sensible]], [[valid]], [[warranted]], [[logical]], [[justifiable]], [[well-founded]]
**3** (of a child) born of parents legally married to each other
**4** of, relating to, or ruling by hereditary right: under their legitimate ruling house
**5** of or relating to serious drama as distinct from films, television, or vaudeville
 *vb* **-mating**, **-mated**
**6** to make, pronounce, or show to be legitimate
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[legitimize]], [[allow]], [[permit]], [[sanction]], [[authorize]], [[legalize]], [[pronounce lawful]]
 **[[legitimacy]]** *n* 
 **[[legitimately]]** *adv*
