**form** *n*
**1** the [[shape]] or [[appearance]] of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shape]], [[formation]], [[configuration]], [[structure]], [[pattern]], [[appearance]]
**2** a visible person or animal
**3** the particular mode in which a thing or person appears: wood in the form of [[paper]]
**4** a [[type]] or [[kind]]: shells were used as a form of currency
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[type]], [[sort]], [[kind]], [[variety]], [[class]], [[style]]
**5** physical or mental [[condition]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[condition]], [[health]], [[shape]], [[nick]] (*informal*), [[fitness]], [[trim]], [[fettle]]
**6** a printed [[document]], esp. one with spaces in which to fill details or answers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[document]], [[paper]], [[sheet]], [[questionnaire]], [[application]]
**7** the previous record of a horse, athlete, etc.
**8** (*Brit* *slang*) a criminal record
**9** (*education*, *chiefly* *Brit** & **NZ*) a group of children who are taught together
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[class]], [[year]], [[set]], [[rank]], [[grade]], [[stream]]
**10** manners and [[etiquette]]: it is considered bad form not to wear a tie
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[procedure]], [[etiquette]], [[use]], [[custom]], [[convention]], [[usage]], [[protocol]], [[wont]], [[kawa]] (*NZ*), [[tikanga]] (*NZ*)
**11** the [[structure]] and arrangement of a work of art or piece of writing as distinguished from its content
**12** a bench
**13** a hare's nest
**14** any of the various ways in which a word may be spelt or inflected
 *vb*
**15** to give [[shape]] to or take [[shape]], esp. a particular [[shape]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arrange]], [[combine]], [[line up]], [[organize]], [[assemble]], [[draw up]]
**16** to come or bring into existence: glaciers dammed the valley bottoms with debris behind which lakes have formed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[establish]], [[start]], [[launch]]
**17** to [[make]] or [[construct]] or be made or constructed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[make]], [[produce]], [[fashion]], [[build]], [[create]], [[shape]], [[construct]], [[forge]]
**18** to train or mould by instruction or example
**19** to [[acquire]] or [[develop]]: they've formed this impression; we formed a bond
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[develop]], [[pick up]], [[acquire]], [[cultivate]], [[contract]]
**20** to be an element of: they had formed part of a special murder unit
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[constitute]], [[make up]], [[compose]], [[comprise]]
