**wring** *vb* **wringing**, **wrung**
**1** Also: **wring out** to squeeze water from (a cloth or clothing) by twisting it tightly
**2** to twist (a neck) violently
**3** to clasp and twist (one's hands) in anguish
**4** to grip (someone's hand) vigorously in greeting
**5** to obtain by forceful means: to wring concessions from the army
**6** **wring someone's heart** to make someone feel sorrow or pity
