**change** *n*
**1** the fact of becoming different
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[alteration]], [[innovation]], [[transformation]], [[modification]], [[mutation]], [[metamorphosis]], [[difference]], [[revolution]]
**2** [[variety]] or [[novelty]]: they wanted to print some good news for a change
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[variety]], [[break]] (*informal*), [[departure]], [[variation]], [[novelty]], [[diversion]] (*Brit*)
**3** a different set, esp. of clothes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exchange]], [[trade]], [[conversion]], [[swap]], [[substitution]], [[interchange]]
**4** money exchanged for its equivalent in a larger denomination or in a different currency
**5** the balance of money when the amount paid is larger than the amount due
**6** coins of a small denomination
 *vb* **changing**, **changed**
**7** to make or become different
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[alter]], [[reform]], [[transform]], [[adjust]], [[revise]], [[modify]], [[reorganize]], [[restyle]]
**8** to [[replace]] with or [[exchange]] for another: the Swedish Communist Party changed its name to the Left Party
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exchange]], [[trade]], [[replace]], [[substitute]], [[swap]], [[interchange]]
**9** to give and receive (something) in return: slaves and masters changed places
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exchange]], [[trade]], [[replace]], [[substitute]], [[swap]], [[interchange]]
**10** to give or receive (money) in [[exchange]] for its equivalent sum in a smaller denomination or different currency
**11** to put on other clothes
**12** to get off one bus, train, or airliner, and onto another: there's no direct train, so you'll need to change at York
 **[[changeless]]** *adj*
