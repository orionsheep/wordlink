**stuff** *n*
**1** [[substance]] or [[material]]
**2** any collection of unnamed [[things]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[things]], [[gear]], [[possessions]], [[effects]], [[equipment]], [[objects]], [[tackle]], [[kit]]
**3** the raw [[material]] of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[substance]], [[material]], [[essence]], [[matter]]
**4** subject [[matter]], skill, etc.: this journalist knew his stuff
**5** woollen fabric
**6** **do one's stuff** (*informal*) to do what is expected of one
 *vb*
**7** to [[pack]] or [[fill]] (something) completely; [[cram]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cram]], [[fill]], [[pack]], [[crowd]]
**8** to [[force]], [[shove]], or [[squeeze]] (something somewhere): I stuffed it in my briefcase
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shove]], [[force]], [[push]], [[squeeze]], [[jam]], [[ram]]
**9** to [[fill]] (food such as poultry or tomatoes) with a seasoned mixture
**10** to [[fill]] (a dead animal's skin) with [[material]] so as to restore the shape of the live animal
**11** (*slang*) to frustrate or defeat
**12** **get stuffed!** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*) an exclamation of anger or annoyance with someone
**13** **stuff oneself *or* one's face** to eat a large amount of food
