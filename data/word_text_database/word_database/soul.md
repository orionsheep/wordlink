**soul** *n*
**1** the spiritual part of a [[person]], regarded as the centre of personality, intellect, will, and emotions: believed by many to survive the [[body]] after death
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spirit]], [[essence]], [[life]], [[vital force]], [[wairua]] (*NZ*)
**2** the essential part or fundamental nature of anything: the soul of contemporary America
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[embodiment]], [[essence]], [[epitome]], [[personification]], [[quintessence]], [[type]]
**3** deep and sincere feelings: you've got no soul
**4** Also called: **soul music** a [[type]] of music of Black American origin using blues and elements of jazz, gospel, and pop
**5** a [[person]] regarded as a good example of some quality: the soul of prudence
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[embodiment]], [[essence]], [[epitome]], [[personification]], [[quintessence]], [[type]]
**6** a [[person]]: there was hardly a soul there
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[person]], [[being]], [[individual]], [[body]], [[creature]], man *or* woman
**7** **the [[life]] and soul** (*informal*) a [[person]] who is lively, entertaining, and fun to be with: the [[life]] and soul of the campus
