**invest** *vb*
**1** (often foll. by *in*) to put (money) into an enterprise with the expectation of profit
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spend]], [[expend]], [[advance]], [[venture]], [[put in]], [[devote]], [[lay out]], [[sink in]]
**2** (often foll. by *in*) to [[devote]] (time or effort to a project)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spend]], [[expend]], [[advance]], [[venture]], [[put in]], [[devote]], [[lay out]], [[sink in]]
**3** to give power or authority to: invested with the powers of government
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[empower]], [[provide]], [[charge]], [[sanction]], [[license]], [[authorize]], [[vest]]
**4** (often foll. by *in*) to install someone (in an official position)
**5** (foll. by *with* or *in*) to credit or [[provide]] (a person with qualities): he was invested with great common sense
**6** **invest in** to buy: she invested in some barbecue equipment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[invest in something]]**: buy, get, purchase, pay for, obtain, acquire, procure
**7** **invest with** (*usually* *poetic*) to cover, as if with a coat: when spring invests the trees with leaves
 **[[investor]]** *n*
