**associate** *vb* **-ating**, **-ated**
**1** to [[connect]] in the mind
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[connect]], [[link]], [[ally]], [[identify]], [[join]], [[combine]], [[attach]], [[fasten]]
**2** to [[mix]] socially: addicts are driven to associate with criminals
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[socialize]], [[mix]], [[accompany]], [[mingle]], [[consort]], [[hobnob]]
**3** **be associated *or* associate oneself with** to be involved with (a group) because of shared views: she had long been associated with the far right
 *n*
**4** a [[partner]] in business
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[partner]], [[friend]], [[ally]], [[colleague]], [[mate]] (*informal*), [[companion]], [[comrade]], [[affiliate]], cobber (*Austral** & **NZ* *old-fashioned*, *informal*), [[E hoa]] (*NZ*)
**5** a [[companion]] or [[friend]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[partner]], [[friend]], [[ally]], [[colleague]], [[mate]] (*informal*), [[companion]], [[comrade]], [[affiliate]], cobber (*Austral** & **NZ* *old-fashioned*, *informal*), [[E hoa]] (*NZ*)
 *adj*
**6** having partial rights or subordinate status: an associate member
**7** joined with in business: an associate director
