**smooth** *adj*
**1** having an [[even]] surface with no roughness, bumps, or holes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[even]], [[level]], [[flat]], [[plane]], [[flush]], [[horizontal]]
**2** without obstructions or difficulties: smooth progress towards an agreement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[easy]], [[effortless]], [[well-ordered]]
**3** without lumps: a smooth paste
**4** free from jolts and bumps: a smooth landing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flowing]], [[steady]], [[regular]], [[uniform]], [[rhythmic]]
**5** not harsh in taste; [[mellow]]: an excellent smooth wine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mellow]], [[pleasant]], [[mild]], [[agreeable]]
**6** charming or [[persuasive]] but possibly insincere
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suave]], [[slick]], [[persuasive]], [[urbane]], [[glib]], [[facile]], [[unctuous]], smarmy (*Brit* *informal*)
 *adv*
**7** in a smooth manner
 *vb*
**8** (often foll. by *down*) to make or become [[even]] or without roughness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flatten]], [[level]], [[press]], [[plane]], [[iron]]
**9** (often foll. by *out* or *away*) to remove in order to make smooth: smoothing out the creases
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flatten]], [[level]], [[press]], [[plane]], [[iron]]
**10** to make calm; soothe
**11** to make easier: Moscow smoothed the path to democracy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ease]], facilitate
 *n*
**12** the smooth part of something
**13** the act of smoothing
 **[[smoothly]]** *adv*
