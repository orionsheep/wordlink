**lend** *vb* **lending**, **lent**
**1** to permit the temporary use of
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[loan]], [[advance]], [[sub]] (*Brit* *informal*)
**2** to [[provide]] (money) temporarily, often at interest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[loan]], [[advance]], [[sub]] (*Brit* *informal*)
**3** to contribute (some abstract quality): a painted trellis lends a classical air to any garden
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give]], [[provide]], [[add]], [[supply]], [[grant]], [[confer]], [[bestow]], [[impart]]
**4** **lend an ear** to listen
**5** **lend itself to** to be appropriate for: the building lends itself to loft conversion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[lend itself to something]]**: be appropriate for, suit, be suitable for, be appropriate to, be serviceable for
 **[[lender]]** *n*
