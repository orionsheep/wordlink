**hear** *vb* **hearing**, **heard**
**1** to perceive (a sound) with the sense of hearing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[overhear]], [[catch]], [[detect]]
**2** to [[listen to]]: I didn't want to hear what he had to say
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[listen to]]
**3** to be informed (of something); receive information (about something): I hear you mean to join the crusade
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[learn]], [[discover]], [[find out]], [[pick up]], [[gather]], [[ascertain]], [[get wind of]] (*informal*)
**4** (*law*) to give a hearing to (a case)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[try]], [[judge]], [[examine]], [[investigate]]
**5** **hear from** to receive a letter or telephone call from
**6** **hear! hear!** an exclamation of approval
**7** **hear of** to allow: she wouldn't hear of it
 **[[hearer]]** *n*
