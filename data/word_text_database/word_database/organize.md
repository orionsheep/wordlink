**organize** * or ***-nise** *vb* **-nizing**, **-nized*** or ***-nising**, **-nised**
**1** to [[plan]] and [[arrange]] (something): we organized a protest meeting in the village hall
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arrange]], [[run]], [[plan]], [[prepare]], [[set up]], [[devise]], [[put together]], [[take care of]], [[jack up]] (*NZ* *informal*)
**2** to [[arrange]] systematically: the files are organized in alphabetical order and by date
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put in order]], [[arrange]], [[group]], [[list]], [[file]], [[index]], [[classify]], [[inventory]]
**3** to form, join, or recruit (people) into a trade union: the seasonal nature of tourism makes it difficult for hotel workers to organize
 **[[organizer]]** * or ***-niser** *n*
