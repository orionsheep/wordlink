**people** *pl n*
**1** [[persons]] collectively or in general
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[humankind]], [[persons]], [[individuals]], [[folk]] (*informal*), [[men and women]], [[humanity]], [[mankind]], [[mortals]], [[the human race]]
**2** a group of [[persons]] considered together: young people
**3** *pl* **-ples** the [[persons]] living in a particular country: the American people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nation]], [[public]], [[community]], [[subjects]], [[population]], [[residents]], [[citizens]], [[folk]]
**4** one's [[family]] or ancestors: her people originally came from Skye
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[family]], [[parents]], [[relations]], [[relatives]], [[folk]], [[folks]] (*informal*), [[clan]], [[kin]], [[rellies]] (*Austral* *slang*)
**5** **the people**
**A** the mass of ordinary [[persons]] without rank or privileges
 **B** the body of [[persons]] in a country who are entitled to vote
 *vb* **-pling**, **-pled**
**6** to provide with inhabitants: the centre of the continent is sparsely peopled
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inhabit]], [[occupy]], [[settle]], [[populate]], [[colonize]]
