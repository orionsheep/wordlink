**star** *n*
**1** a planet or meteor visible in the clear night sky as a point of light
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[heavenly body]], [[celestial body]]
**2** a hot gaseous mass, such as the sun, that radiates energy as heat and light, or in some cases as radio waves and X-rays. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVES: </small>**astral**, **sidereal**, **stellar**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[heavenly body]], [[celestial body]]
**3** **stars** same as **horoscope**
**4** an emblem with five or more radiating points, often used as a symbol of rank or an award: the RAC awarded the hotel three stars
**5** same as **asterisk**
**6** a famous person from the sports, acting, or music professions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[celebrity]], [[big name]], [[megastar]] (*informal*), [[name]], [[luminary]], leading man *or* lady, hero *or* heroine, [[principal]], [[main attraction]]
**7** **see stars** to see flashes of light after a blow on the head
 *vb* **starring**, **starred**
**8** to [[feature]] (an actor) or (of an actor) to be featured as a star: he's starred in dozens of films
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[play the lead]], [[appear]], [[feature]], [[perform]]
**9** to mark (something) with a star or stars
