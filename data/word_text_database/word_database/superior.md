**superior** *adj*
**1** [[greater]] in quality, quantity, or usefulness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[better]], [[higher]], [[greater]], [[grander]], [[surpassing]], [[unrivalled]]
**2** [[higher]] in rank, position, or status: he was reprimanded by a superior officer
**3** believing oneself to be [[better]] than others
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[supercilious]], [[patronizing]], [[condescending]], [[haughty]], [[disdainful]], [[lordly]], [[lofty]], [[pretentious]]
**4** of very high quality or respectability: superior merchandise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[first-class]], [[excellent]], [[first-rate]], [[choice]], [[exclusive]], [[exceptional]], [[de luxe]], [[booshit]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[exo]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[sik]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[rad]] (*informal*), [[phat]] (*slang*), [[schmick]] (*Austral* *informal*)
**5** (*formal*) placed [[higher]] up: damage to the superior surface of the wing
**6** (*printing*) (of a character) written or printed above the line
 *n*
**7** a person of [[greater]] rank or status
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[boss]], [[senior]], [[director]], [[manager]], [[chief]] (*informal*), [[principal]], [[supervisor]], [[sherang]] (*Austral** & **NZ*) 
 **[[superiority]]** *n*
