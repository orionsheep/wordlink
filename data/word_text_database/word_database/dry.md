**dry** *adj* **drier**, **driest*** or ***dryer**, **dryest**
**1** lacking moisture
**2** having little or no rainfall
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dehydrated]], [[dried-up]], [[arid]], [[parched]], [[desiccated]]
**3** having the water drained away or evaporated: a dry gully for the most part of the year
**4** not providing milk: a dry cow
**5** (of the eyes) free from tears
**6** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) [[thirsty]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thirsty]], [[parched]]
**7** eaten without butter or jam: a dry cracker
**8** (of wine) not sweet
**9** [[dull]] and uninteresting: a dry subject
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dull]], [[boring]], [[tedious]], [[dreary]], [[tiresome]], [[monotonous]], [[run-of-the-mill]], [[humdrum]]
**10** (of humour) subtle and [[sarcastic]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sarcastic]], [[cynical]], [[low-key]], [[sly]], [[sardonic]], [[deadpan]], [[droll]], [[ironical]]
**11** prohibiting the sale of alcoholic liquor: a dry district
 *vb* **dries**, **drying**, **dried**
**12** to make or become dry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drain]], [[make dry]]
**13** to preserve (food) by removing the moisture
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dehydrate]], [[make dry]], [[desiccate]], [[sear]], [[parch]], dehumidify
 See also **dry out**
 **[[dryness]]** *n*
