**run** *vb* **running**, **ran**, **run**
**1** to [[move]] on foot at a rapid pace
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[race]], [[rush]], [[dash]], [[hurry]], [[sprint]], [[bolt]], [[gallop]], [[hare]] (*Brit* *informal*)
**2** to [[pass]] over (a distance or route) in running: being a man isn't about running the fastest mile
**3** to [[take part]] in (a [[race]]): I ran a decent [[race]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[take part]], [[compete]]
**4** to carry out as if by running: he is running errands for his big brother
**5** to [[flee]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flee]], [[escape]], [[take off]] (*informal*), [[bolt]], [[beat it]] (*slang*), [[leg it]] (*informal*), [[take flight]] (*old-fashioned*), [[do a runner]] (*slang*)
**6** to travel somewhere in a vehicle
**7** to give a lift to (someone) in a vehicle: one wet day I ran her down to the service
**8** to [[drive]] or maintain and [[operate]] (a vehicle)
**9** to travel regularly between places on a route: trains running through the night
**10** to [[move]] or [[pass]] quickly: he ran his hand across his forehead
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pass]], [[go]], [[move]], [[roll]], [[glide]], [[skim]]
**11** to [[function]] or cause to [[function]]: run the video tape backwards
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go]], [[work]], [[operate]], [[perform]], [[function]]
**12** to [[manage]]: she ran a small hotel
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[manage]], [[lead]], [[direct]], [[be in charge of]], [[head]], [[control]], [[operate]], [[handle]]
**13** to [[continue]] in a particular direction or for a particular time or distance: a road running alongside the Nile; a performing arts festival running in the city for six weeks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[continue]], [[go]], [[stretch]], [[reach]], [[extend]], [[proceed]]
**14** (*law*) to have legal force or effect: the lease runs for two more years
**15** to be subjected to or affected by: she ran a high risk of losing her hair
**16** to tend or incline: he was of medium height and running to fat
**17** to recur persistently or be inherent: the capacity for infidelity ran in the genes
**18** to [[flow]] or cause (liquids) to [[flow]]: sweat ran down her face
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flow]], [[pour]], [[stream]], [[go]], [[leak]], [[spill]], [[discharge]], [[gush]]
**19** to [[dissolve]] and spread: the soles of the shoes peeled off and the colours ran
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[melt]], [[dissolve]], [[liquefy]], [[go soft]]
**20** (of stitches) to unravel
**21** to spread or circulate: rumours ran around quickly
**22** to [[publish]] or be published in a newspaper or magazine: our local newspaper ran a story on the appeal
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[publish]], [[feature]], [[display]], [[print]]
**23** (*chiefly* *US** & **Canad*) to [[stand]] as a candidate for political or other office: due to announce she is running for president
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[compete]], [[stand]], [[contend]], [[be a candidate]], [[put yourself up for]], [[take part]]
**24** to get past or through: the oil tanker was hit as it tried to run the blockade
**25** to [[smuggle]] (goods, esp. arms)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[smuggle]], [[traffic in]], [[bootleg]]
**26** (of fish) to migrate upstream from the sea, esp. in order to spawn
**27** (*cricket*) to score (a run or number of runs) by hitting the ball and running between the wickets
 *n*
**28** the act or an instance of running: he broke into a run
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[race]], [[rush]], [[dash]], [[sprint]], [[gallop]], [[jog]], [[spurt]]
**29** a distance covered by running or a [[period]] of running: it's a short run of about 20 kilometres
**30** a [[trip]] in a vehicle, esp. for pleasure: our only treat is a run in the car to Dartmoor
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ride]], [[drive]], [[trip]], [[spin]] (*informal*), [[outing]], [[excursion]], [[jaunt]]
**31** free and unrestricted access: he had the run of the house
**32**
**A** a [[period]] of time during which a machine or computer operates
 **B** the amount of [[work]] performed in such a [[period]]
**33** a continuous or sustained [[period]]: a run of seven defeats
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sequence]], [[period]], [[stretch]], [[spell]], [[course]], [[season]], [[series]], [[string]]
**34** a continuous [[sequence]] of performances: the play had a long run
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sequence]], [[period]], [[stretch]], [[spell]], [[course]], [[season]], [[series]], [[string]]
**35** (*cards*) a [[sequence]] of winning cards in one suit: a run of spades
**36** type, class, or category: he had nothing in common with the usual run of terrorists
**37** a continuous and urgent demand: a run on the pound
**38** a [[series]] of unravelled stitches, esp. in tights
**39** a steeply inclined [[course]], esp. a snow-covered one used for skiing
**40** an [[enclosure]] for domestic fowls or other animals: the chicken run
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[enclosure]], [[pen]], [[coop]]
**41** (esp. in Australia and New Zealand) a tract of land for grazing livestock
**42** the migration of fish upstream in order to spawn
**43** (*music*) a rapid scalelike passage of notes
**44** (*cricket*) a score of one, normally achieved by both batsmen running from one end of the wicket to the other after one of them has hit the ball
**45** (*baseball*) an instance of a batter touching all four bases safely, thereby scoring
**46** **a run for one's money** (*informal*)
**A** a close competition
 **B** pleasure or success from an activity
**47** **in the long run** as an eventual outcome
**48** **on the run** escaping from arrest
**49** **the runs** (*slang*) diarrhoea
