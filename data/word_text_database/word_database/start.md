**start** *vb*
**1** to [[begin]] (something or to do something); come or cause to come into being: to start a war; this conflict started years ago
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[set about]], [[begin]], [[proceed]], [[embark upon]], [[take the first step]], [[make a beginning]]
**2** to set or be set in motion: he started the van
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[start up]], [[activate]], [[get something going]]
**3** to make a sudden involuntary movement from fright or surprise; [[jump]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jump]], [[shy]], [[jerk]], [[flinch]], [[recoil]]
**4** to [[establish]]; [[set up]]: to start a state lottery
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[establish]], [[begin]], [[found]], [[create]], [[launch]], [[set up]], [[institute]], [[pioneer]]
**5** to support (someone) in the first part of a career or activity
**6** (*Brit* *informal*) to [[begin]] quarrelling or causing a disturbance: don't start with me
**7** **to start with** in the first place
 *n*
**8** the first part of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beginning]], [[outset]], [[opening]], [[birth]], [[foundation]], [[dawn]] (*literary*), [[onset]], [[initiation]]
**9** the place or time at which something begins
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beginning]], [[outset]], [[opening]], [[birth]], [[foundation]], [[dawn]] (*literary*), [[onset]], [[initiation]]
**10** a signal to [[begin]], such as in a race
**11** a lead or advantage, either in time or distance, in a competitive activity: he had an hour's start on me
**12** a slight involuntary movement from fright or surprise: I awoke with a start
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jump]], [[spasm]], [[convulsion]]
**13** an opportunity to enter a career or [[begin]] a project
**14** **for a start** in the first place
