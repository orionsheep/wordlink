**lick** *vb*
**1** to pass the [[tongue]] over in order to [[taste]], wet, or clean
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[taste]], [[lap]], [[tongue]]
**2** to [[flicker]] over or round (something): flames licked the gutters
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flicker]], [[touch]], [[flick]], [[dart]], [[ripple]], [[play over]]
**3** (*informal*)
**A** to [[defeat]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beat]], [[defeat]], [[overcome]], [[rout]], [[outstrip]], [[outdo]], [[trounce]], vanquish
 **B** to thrash
**4** **lick into shape** to put into a satisfactory condition
**5** **lick one's wounds** to retire after a [[defeat]]
 *n*
**6** an instance of passing the [[tongue]] over something
**7** a small amount: a lick of paint
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dab]], [[touch]], [[stroke]]
**8** (*informal*) a blow
**9** (*informal*) a fast [[pace]]: a pulsating rhythm taken at a lick
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pace]], [[rate]], [[speed]], [[clip]] (*informal*)
**10** **a lick and a promise** something hastily done, esp. a hurried wash
