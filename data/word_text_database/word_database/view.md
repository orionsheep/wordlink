**view** *n*
**1** [[opinion]], judgment, or [[belief]]: in my view that doesn't really work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[opinion]], [[belief]], [[feeling]], [[attitude]], [[impression]], [[conviction]], [[point of view]], [[sentiment]]
**2** an understanding of or [[outlook]] on something: a specific view of human history
**3** everything that can be seen from a particular place or in a particular direction: there was a beautiful view from the window
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[scene]], [[picture]], [[sight]], [[prospect]], [[perspective]], [[landscape]], [[outlook]], [[spectacle]]
**4** [[vision]] or [[sight]], esp. range of [[vision]]: as they turned into the drive, the house came into view
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vision]], [[sight]], [[visibility]], [[perspective]], [[eyeshot]]
**5** a [[picture]] of a [[scene]]
**6** the act of seeing or observing
**7** **in view of** taking into consideration
**8** **on view** exhibited to the public
**9** **take a dim *or* poor view of** to [[regard]] (something) unfavourably
**10** **with a view to** with the intention of
 *vb*
**11** to [[consider]] in a specified manner: they viewed the visit with hardly disguised apprehension
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[regard]], [[see]], [[consider]], [[perceive]], [[treat]], [[estimate]], [[reckon]], [[deem]]
**12** to examine or inspect (a house or flat) carefully with a view to buying it
**13** to look at
**14** to watch (television, videos, etc.)
