**load** *n*
**1** something to be borne or conveyed; [[weight]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cargo]], [[delivery]], [[haul]], [[shipment]], [[batch]], [[freight]], [[consignment]]
**2** the amount borne or conveyed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cargo]], [[delivery]], [[haul]], [[shipment]], [[batch]], [[freight]], [[consignment]]
**3** something that weighs down or burdens: I have enough of a load to carry right now
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[oppression]], [[charge]], [[worry]], [[trouble]], [[weight]], [[responsibility]], [[burden]], [[onus]]
**4** (*electronics*) the power delivered by a machine, generator, or circuit
**5** an external force applied to a component or mechanism
**6** **a load of** (*informal*) a quantity of: a load of half-truths
**7** **get a load of** (*informal*) to pay attention to
 *vb*
**8** to place [[cargo]] or goods upon (a ship or vehicle)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fill]], [[stuff]], [[pack]], [[pile]], [[stack]], [[heap]], [[cram]], [[freight]]
**9** to [[burden]] or oppress
**10** to supply in abundance: other treats are loaded with fat
**11** to cause to be biased: the dice are loaded
**12** to put ammunition into (a firearm)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[make ready]], [[charge]], [[prime]]
**13** (*photog*) to insert film in (a camera)
**14** to [[weight]] or bias (a roulette wheel or dice)
**15** (*computers*) to transfer (a program) to a memory
