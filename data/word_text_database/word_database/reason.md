**reason** *n*
**1** a [[cause]] or [[motive]] for a belief or action: he had two reasons for his dark mood
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cause]], [[grounds]], [[purpose]], [[motive]], [[goal]], [[aim]], [[object]], [[intention]]
**2** the ability to [[think]] or argue rationally
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sense]], [[mind]], [[understanding]], [[judgment]], [[logic]], [[intellect]], [[sanity]], [[rationality]]
**3** an argument in favour of or a justification for something: there is every reason to encourage people to keep fit
**4** soundness of [[mind]]
**5** **by reason of** because of
**6** **within reason** within moderate or justifiable bounds
**7** **it stands to reason** it is logical or obvious
 *vb*
**8** to [[think]] logically in forming conclusions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deduce]], [[conclude]], [[work out]], [[make out]], [[infer]], [[think]]
**9** **reason with** to persuade by logical arguments into doing something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[reason with someone]]**: persuade, bring round, urge, win over, prevail upon (*formal*), talk into *or* out of
**10** **reason out** to [[work out]] (a problem) by reasoning
