**channel** *n*
**1** a band of radio frequencies assigned for the broadcasting of a radio or television signal
**2** a [[path]] for an electrical signal or computer data
**3** a [[means]] of access or communication: reports coming through diplomatic channels
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[means]], [[way]], [[course]], [[approach]], [[medium]], [[route]], [[path]], [[avenue]]
**4** a broad [[strait]] connecting two areas of sea
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strait]], [[sound]], [[route]], [[passage]], [[canal]], [[waterway]]
**5** the bed or [[course]] of a river, stream, or [[canal]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strait]], [[sound]], [[route]], [[passage]], [[canal]], [[waterway]]
**6** a navigable [[course]] through an area of water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strait]], [[sound]], [[route]], [[passage]], [[canal]], [[waterway]]
**7** a [[groove]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[duct]], [[artery]], [[groove]], [[gutter]], [[furrow]], conduit
 *vb* **-nelling**, **-nelled** (*US*) **-neling**, **-neled**
**8** to [[direct]] or [[convey]] through a channel or channels: tunnels that channel the pilgrims into the area; to channel funds abroad
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[direct]], [[guide]], [[conduct]], [[transmit]], [[convey]]
