**spread** *vb* **spreading**, **spread**
**1** to [[open]] out or [[unfold]] to the fullest width: spread the material out
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[open]] (out), [[extend]], [[stretch]], [[unfold]], [[sprawl]], [[unroll]]
**2** to [[extend]] over a larger expanse: the subsequent unrest spread countrywide
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grow]], [[increase]], [[expand]], [[widen]], [[escalate]], [[proliferate]], [[multiply]], [[broaden]]
**3** to apply as a coating: spread the paste evenly over your skin
**4** to be displayed to its fullest [[extent]]: the shining bay spread out below
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[extend]], [[open]], [[stretch]]
**5** to send or be sent out in all directions or to many people: the news spread quickly; the sandflies that spread the disease
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[circulate]], [[broadcast]], [[propagate]], [[disseminate]], [[make known]]
**6** to distribute or be distributed evenly: we were advised to spread the workload over the whole year
**7** **spread out** (of people) to [[increase]] the distance between one another (to [[widen]] the scope of a search, for example)
 *n*
**8** a spreading; distribution, dispersion, or [[expansion]]: the spread of higher education
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[development]], [[advance]], [[expansion]], [[proliferation]], [[dissemination]], [[dispersal]]
**9** (*informal*) a large meal
**10** (*informal*) the wingspan of an aircraft or bird
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[extent]], [[span]], [[stretch]], [[sweep]]
**11** (*informal*, *chiefly* *US** & **Canad*) a ranch or other large area of land
**12** a soft food which can be spread: cheese spread
**13** two facing pages in a book or magazine
**14** a widening of the hips and waist: middle-age spread
