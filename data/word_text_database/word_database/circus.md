**circus** *n*, *pl* **-cuses**
**1** a travelling company of entertainers such as acrobats, clowns, trapeze artists, and trained animals
**2** a public performance given by such a company
**3** (*Brit*) an open place in a town where several streets meet
**4** (*informal*) a hectic or well-published situation: her second marriage turned into a media circus
**5** (in ancient Rome) an open-air stadium for chariot races or public games
**6** a travelling group of professional sportspeople: the Formula One circus
