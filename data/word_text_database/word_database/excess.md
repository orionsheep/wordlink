**excess** *n*
**1** the state or act of going beyond normal or permitted limits
**2** an immoderate or abnormal amount
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[surfeit]], [[surplus]], [[overload]], [[glut]], [[superabundance]], [[superfluity]]
**3** the amount, number, etc. by which one thing exceeds another
**4** behaviour regarded as too extreme or immoral to be acceptable: a life of sex, drugs, and drunken excess
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[overindulgence]], [[extravagance]], [[profligacy]], [[debauchery]], [[dissipation]], [[intemperance]], [[indulgence]], [[prodigality]]
**5** **excesses** acts or actions that are unacceptably cruel or immoral: one of the bloodiest excesses of a dictatorial regime
**6** **in excess of** more than
**7** **to excess** to an extreme or unhealthy extent: he had started to drink to excess
 *adj*
**8** more than normal, necessary, or permitted: excess fat
 **[[excessive]]** *adj* 
 **[[excessively]]** *adv*
