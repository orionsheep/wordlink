**renew** *vb*
**1** to begin again
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recommence]], [[continue]], [[extend]], [[repeat]], [[resume]], [[reopen]], [[re-create]], [[reaffirm]]
**2** to take up again after a break: they wanted to renew diplomatic ties
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[recommence]], [[continue]], [[extend]], [[repeat]], [[resume]], [[reopen]], [[re-create]], [[reaffirm]]
**3** to make valid again: we didn't renew the lease
**4** to grow again
**5** to [[restore]] to a new or fresh condition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[restore]], [[repair]], [[overhaul]], [[mend]], [[refurbish]], [[renovate]], [[refit]], [[modernize]]
**6** to [[replace]] (an old or worn-out part or piece)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[replace]], [[refresh]], [[replenish]], [[restock]]
**7** to restate or [[reaffirm]] (a promise)
 **[[renewal]]** *n*
