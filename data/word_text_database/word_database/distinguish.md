**distinguish** *vb*
**1** to make, show, or [[recognize]] a difference: I have tried to distinguish between fact and theory
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[differentiate]], [[determine]], [[separate]], [[discriminate]], [[decide]], [[judge]], [[ascertain]], [[tell the difference]]
**2** to be a distinctive feature of: what distinguishes the good teenage reader from the less competent one?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[characterize]], [[mark]], [[separate]], [[single out]], [[set apart]]
**3** to [[make out]] by hearing, seeing, or tasting: she listened but could distinguish nothing except the urgency of their discussion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[make out]], [[recognize]], [[perceive]], [[know]], [[see]], [[tell]], [[pick out]], [[discern]]
**4** **distinguish oneself** to make oneself noteworthy
 **[[distinguishable]]** *adj* 
 **[[distinguishing]]** *adj*
