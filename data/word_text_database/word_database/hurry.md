**hurry** *vb* **-ries**, **-rying**, **-ried**
**1** to move or act, or cause to move or act, in great [[haste]]: the umpires hurried the players off the ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[fly]], [[dash]], [[scurry]], [[scoot]]
**2** to [[speed]] up the completion or progress of: eat a small snack rather than hurry a main meal
 *n*
**3** [[haste]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[haste]], [[speed]], [[urgency]], [[flurry]], [[quickness]]
**4** [[urgency]] or eagerness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[haste]], [[speed]], [[urgency]], [[flurry]], [[quickness]]
**5** **in a hurry** (*informal*)
**A** easily: a striking old guy, not the sort you'd forget in a hurry
 **B** willingly: he would not ease interest rates again in a hurry
