**relieve** *vb* **-lieving**, **-lieved**
**1** to lessen (pain, distress, boredom, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ease]], [[soothe]], [[alleviate]], [[relax]], [[comfort]], [[calm]], [[cure]], [[soften]]
**2** to bring assistance to (someone in need): a plan to relieve those facing hunger
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[help]], [[support]], [[aid]], [[sustain]], [[assist]], [[succour]]
**3** to free (someone) from an obligation: a further attempt to relieve the taxpayers of their burdens
**4** to take over the duties of (someone): the night nurse came in to relieve her
**5** to free (a besieged town or fort)
**6** **relieve oneself** to urinate or defecate
**7** to set off by contrast: painted walls are marginally [[relieved]] by some abstract prints
**8** (*informal*) to take from: the prince had [[relieved]] him of his duties
 **[[relieved]]** *adj*
