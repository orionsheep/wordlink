**threshold** *n*
**1** the lower horizontal part of an [[entrance]] or [[doorway]], esp. one made of stone or hardwood
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entrance]], [[doorway]], [[door]], [[doorstep]]
**2** any [[doorway]] or [[entrance]]: he had never been over the threshold of a pub before
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entrance]], [[doorway]], [[door]], [[doorstep]]
**3** the [[starting point]] of an experience, event, or venture: she was on the threshold of a glorious career
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[start]], [[beginning]], [[opening]], [[dawn]] (*literary*), [[verge]], [[brink]], [[outset]], [[inception]]
**4** the point at which something begins to take effect or be noticeable: the threshold for basic rate tax; he has a low boredom threshold
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[limit]], [[margin]], [[starting point]], [[minimum]]
