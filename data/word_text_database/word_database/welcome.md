**welcome** *vb* **-coming**, **-comed**
**1** to [[greet]] the arrival of (a guest) cordially
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[greet]], [[meet]], [[receive]], [[embrace]], [[hail]], [[karanga]] (*NZ*), [[mihi]] (*NZ*), [[haeremai]] (*NZ*)
**2** to [[receive]] or accept (something) gladly: I would welcome a chance to speak to him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accept gladly]], [[appreciate]], [[embrace]], [[approve of]], [[be pleased by]], [[give the thumbs up to]] (*informal*), [[be glad about]], express pleasure *or* satisfaction at
 *n*
**3** the act of [[greeting]] or receiving someone or something in a specified manner: the President was given a warm welcome
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[greeting]], [[welcoming]], [[reception]], [[acceptance]], [[hail]], [[hospitality]], [[salutation]] (*formal*), [[haeremai]] (*NZ*)
 *adj*
**4** gladly received or admitted: I wouldn't want to stay where I'm not welcome
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wanted]]
**5** encouraged or invited: you are welcome to join us at one of our social events
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[free]]
**6** bringing pleasure: a welcome change
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pleasing]], [[appreciated]], [[acceptable]], [[pleasant]], [[desirable]], [[refreshing]], [[delightful]], [[gratifying]]
**7** **you're welcome** an expression used to acknowledge someone's thanks
 **[[welcoming]]** *adj*
