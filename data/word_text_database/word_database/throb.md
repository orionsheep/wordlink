**throb** *vb* **[[throbbing]]**, **throbbed**
**1** to [[pulsate]] or [[beat]] repeatedly, esp. with abnormally strong force: her eardrums were [[throbbing]] with pain
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pulsate]], [[pound]], [[beat]], [[pulse]], [[thump]], [[palpitate]]
**2** (of engines, drums, etc.) to have a strong rhythmic [[vibration]] or [[beat]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vibrate]], [[pulsate]], [[reverberate]], [[shake]], [[judder]] (*informal*)
 *n*
**3** the act or sensation of [[throbbing]]: he felt a throb of fear; the throb of the engines
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vibration]], [[throbbing]], [[reverberation]], [[judder]] (*informal*), [[pulsation]]
 **[[throbbing]]** *adj*, *n*
