**warm** *adj*
**1** feeling or having a moderate degree of [[heat]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[balmy]], [[mild]], [[temperate]], [[pleasant]], [[fine]], [[bright]], [[sunny]], [[agreeable]]
**2** giving [[heat]]: warm clothing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thermal]], [[winter]], [[thick]], [[chunky]], [[woolly]]
**3** (of colours) predominantly red or yellow in tone
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mellow]], [[relaxing]], [[pleasant]], [[agreeable]], [[restful]]
**4** [[kindly]] or [[affectionate]]: warm embraces
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[affable]], [[kindly]], [[friendly]], [[affectionate]], [[loving]], [[tender]], [[amicable]], [[cordial]]
**5** (*informal*) [[near]] to finding a hidden object or guessing facts, for example in a children's game
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[near]], [[close]], [[hot]], [[near]] to the truth
 *vb*
**6** to make warm
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[warm up]], [[heat]], [[thaw]] (out), [[heat up]]
**7** **warm to**
**A** to become fonder of: I warmed to him when he defended me
 **B** to become more excited or enthusiastic about: he had warmed to his theme
 See also **[[warm up]]**
 **[[warmly]]** *adv* 
 **[[warmness]]** *n*
