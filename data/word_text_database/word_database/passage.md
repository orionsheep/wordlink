**passage** *n*
**1** a [[channel]] or opening providing a [[way]] through
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[alley]], [[way]], [[close]] (*Brit*), [[course]], [[road]], [[channel]], [[route]], [[path]]
**2** a [[hall]] or [[corridor]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[corridor]], [[hall]], [[lobby]], [[vestibule]]
**3** a [[section]] of a written work, speech, or [[piece]] of music
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[extract]], [[reading]], [[piece]], [[section]], [[text]], [[excerpt]], [[quotation]]
**4** a [[journey]] by ship
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[journey]], [[crossing]], [[trip]], [[trek]], [[voyage]]
**5** the act of passing from one place or condition to another: Ireland faced a tough passage to qualify for the World Cup finals
**6** the right or freedom to pass: the aid convoys were guaranteed safe passage through rebel-held areas
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[safe-conduct]], [[right to travel]], [[freedom to travel]], [[permission to travel]]
**7** the establishing of a law by a law-making body
