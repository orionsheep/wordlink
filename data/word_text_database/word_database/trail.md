**trail** *n*
**1** a rough [[path]] across open country or through a forest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[path]], [[track]], [[route]], [[way]], [[course]], [[road]], [[pathway]], [[footpath]]
**2** a [[route]] along a series of roads or paths that has been specially planned to let people see or do particular things: a nature trail through the woods
**3** a print, mark, or [[scent]] left by a person, animal, or object: a trail of blood was found down three flights of stairs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tracks]], [[path]], [[marks]], [[wake]], [[trace]], [[scent]], [[footprints]], [[spoor]]
**4** something that trails behind: a vapour trail
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wake]], [[stream]], [[tail]]
**5** a sequence of results from an event: a trail of mishaps
 *vb*
**6** to [[drag]] or [[stream]] along the ground or through the air behind someone or something: part of her sari trailed behind her on the floor
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drag]], [[draw]], [[pull]], [[sweep]], [[haul]], [[tow]], [[dangle]], [[droop]]
**7** to [[lag]] behind (a person or thing): Max had arrived as well, trailing behind the others
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lag]], [[follow]], [[drift]], [[wander]], [[linger]], [[trudge]], [[plod]], [[meander]]
**8** to [[follow]] or [[hunt]] (an animal or person), usually secretly, by following the [[marks]] or [[tracks]] he, she, or it has made: the police had trailed him the length and breadth of the country
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[follow]], [[track]], [[chase]], [[pursue]], [[dog]], [[hunt]], [[shadow]], [[trace]]
**9** to be falling behind in a race, match or competition: they trailed 2–1 at half-time
**10** to move wearily or slowly: we spent the afternoon trailing round the shops
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lag]], [[follow]], [[drift]], [[wander]], [[linger]], [[trudge]], [[plod]], [[meander]]
