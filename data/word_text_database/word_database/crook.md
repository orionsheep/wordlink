**crook** *n*
**1** (*informal*) a dishonest person
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[criminal]], [[rogue]], [[cheat]], [[thief]], [[shark]], [[villain]], [[robber]], [[racketeer]], [[skelm]] (*S African*)
**2** a bent or curved place or thing: she held the puppy in the crook of her arm
**3** a bishop's or shepherd's staff with a hooked end
 *adj*
**4** (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*)
**A** ill
 **B** of poor quality
 **C** unpleasant; bad
**5** **[[go (off) crook]]** (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) to lose one's temper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[go (off) crook]]**: lose your temper, be furious, rage, go mad, lose it (*informal*), crack up (*informal*), see red (*informal*), blow your top
**6** **go crook at *or* on** (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) to rebuke or upbraid
 *vb*
**7** to bend or curve
