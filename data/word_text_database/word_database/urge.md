**urge** *n*
**1** a strong [[impulse]], inner [[drive]], or [[yearning]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[impulse]], [[longing]], [[wish]], [[desire]], [[drive]], [[yearning]], [[itch]] (*informal*), thirst
 *vb* **urging**, **[[urged]]**
**2** to [[plead]] with or press someone to do something: he [[urged]] his readers to do the same
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[beg]], [[exhort]] (*formal*), [[plead]], [[implore]], [[beseech]], [[entreat]]
**3** to [[advocate]] earnestly and persistently: I have long [[urged]] this change
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[advocate]], [[recommend]], [[advise]], [[support]], [[counsel]]
**4** (often foll. by *on*) to force or hasten onwards: something very powerful [[urged]] him on
