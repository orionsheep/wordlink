**indulge** *vb* **-dulging**, **-dulged**
**1** (often foll. by *in*) to [[yield to]] or [[gratify]] (a whim or desire for): to indulge in new clothes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gratify]], [[satisfy]], [[feed]], [[give way to]], [[yield to]], [[pander to]], [[gladden]]
**2** to allow (someone) to have or do everything he or she wants: he had given her too much, indulged her in everything
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spoil]], [[pamper]], [[cosset]], [[humour]], [[give in to]], [[coddle]], [[mollycoddle]], [[overindulge]]
**3** to allow (oneself) the pleasure of something: he indulged himself
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[indulge yourself]]**: treat yourself, splash out, [[spoil]] yourself, luxuriate in something, [[overindulge]] yourself
**4** (*informal*) to take alcoholic drink
