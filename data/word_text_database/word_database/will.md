**will**^2 *n*
**1** a strong [[determination]]: a fierce will to survive
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[determination]], [[drive]], [[purpose]], [[commitment]], [[resolution]], [[resolve]], [[spine]], [[backbone]]
**2** [[desire]] or [[wish]]: a referendum to determine the will of the people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wish]], [[mind]], [[desire]], [[intention]], [[fancy]], [[preference]], [[inclination]]
**3** a document setting out a person's wishes regarding the disposal of his or her property after death
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[testament]], [[bequest]](s), [[last wishes]], [[last will and testament]]
**4** **at will** when and as one chooses: customers can withdraw money at will
 *vb* **willing**, **willed**
**5** to try to make (something) happen by wishing very hard for it: she willed herself not to cry
**6** to [[wish]] or [[desire]]: if he wills it, we will meet again
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wish]], [[want]], [[prefer]], [[desire]], [[see fit]]
**7** to [[leave]] (property) in one's will: the farm had been willed to her
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bequeath]], [[give]], [[leave]], [[transfer]], [[gift]], [[hand on]], [[pass on]], [[confer]]
