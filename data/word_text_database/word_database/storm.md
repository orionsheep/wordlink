**storm** *n*
**1** a violent weather condition of strong winds, rain, hail, [[thunder]], lightning, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tempest]] (*literary*), [[hurricane]], [[gale]], [[blizzard]], [[squall]]
**2** a violent [[disturbance]] or quarrel: a storm of protest from the opposition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[outburst]], [[row]], [[outcry]], [[furore]], [[outbreak]], [[turmoil]], [[disturbance]], [[strife]]
**3** (usually foll. by *of*) a heavy discharge of bullets or missiles
**4** **take a place by storm**
**A** to capture or overrun a place by a violent [[attack]]
 **B** to surprise people, but receive their praise, by being extremely successful at something
 *vb*
**5** to [[attack]] or capture (a place) suddenly and violently
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attack]], [[charge]], [[rush]], [[assault]], [[assail]]
**6** to shout angrily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rage]], [[rant]], [[thunder]], [[rave]], [[bluster]]
**7** to move or [[rush]] violently or angrily: she stormed into the study
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[stamp]], [[flounce]], [[fly]]
