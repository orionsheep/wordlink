**make** *vb* **making**, **made**
**1** to [[create]], construct, establish, or draw up; bring into being: houses made of stone; he will have to make a will
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[produce]], [[cause]], [[create]], [[effect]], [[lead to]], [[generate]], [[bring about]], [[give rise to]]
**2** to [[cause]] to [[do]] or be; [[compel]] or [[induce]]: please make her go away
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[force]], [[cause]], [[compel]], [[drive]], [[require]], [[oblige]], [[induce]], [[constrain]]
**3** to [[bring about]] or [[produce]]: don't make a noise
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[produce]], [[cause]], [[create]], [[effect]], [[lead to]], [[generate]], [[bring about]], [[give rise to]]
**4** to [[carry out]] or [[perform]]: he made his first trip to China in 1987; she made an obscene gesture
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[perform]], [[do]], [[effect]], [[carry out]], [[execute]]
**5** to appoint: they made him caretaker manager
**6** to come into a specified state or condition: to make merry
**7** to become: she will make a good diplomat
**8** to [[cause]] or ensure the success of: that news has made my day
**9** to [[amount to]]: 5 and 5 make 10
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[amount to]], [[total]], [[constitute]], [[add up to]], [[count as]], [[tot up to]] (*informal*)
**10** to [[earn]] or be paid: they must be making a fortune
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[earn]], [[get]], [[gain]], [[net]], [[win]], [[clear]], [[obtain]], [[bring in]]
**11** to have the qualities of or be suitable for: what makes this book such a good read?
**12** to prepare for use: she forgot to make her bed
**13** to be the essential element in: confidence makes a good salesperson
**14** to use for a specified purpose: they will make this town their base
**15** to deliver: he made a very good speech
**16** to consider to be: what time [[do]] you [[make it]]?
**17** to [[cause]] to seem or represent as being: her girlish pigtails made her look younger than she was; she made the experience sound most unpleasant
**18** to acquire: she doesn't make friends easily
**19** to engage in: they made war on the Turks
**20** to travel a certain distance or to a certain place: we can make at least three miles before it gets dark
**21** to arrive in time for: he didn't make the first act of the play
**22** to [[win]] or score: he made a break of 125
**23** (*informal*) to [[gain]] a place or position on or in: to make the headlines
**24** **make a day *or* night of it** to [[cause]] an activity to last a day or night
**25** **make eyes at** (*old-fashioned*) to flirt with or ogle
**26** **[[make it]]** (*informal*)
**A** to be able to attend: I'm afraid I can't [[make it]] to your party
 **B** to be successful
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[make it]]**: succeed, prosper, arrive (*informal*), [[get]] on, crack it (*informal*)
**27** **make like** (*slang*, *chiefly* *US** & **Canad*)
**A** to imitate
 **B** to pretend
**28** **make to *or* as if to *or* as though to** to act with the intention or with a show of doing something: she made as if to hit him
 *n*
**29** manufacturer; [[brand]]: what make of car is that?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[brand]], [[sort]], [[style]], [[model]], [[kind]], [[type]], [[variety]], [[marque]]
**30** the way in which something is made
**31** **on the make** (*slang*) out for profit or conquest
