**initiate** *vb* **-ating**, **-ated**
**1** to [[begin]] or set going: they wanted to initiate a discussion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[begin]], [[start]], [[open]], [[launch]], [[kick off]] (*informal*), [[embark on]], [[originate]], [[set about]]
**2** to accept (new members) into a group, often through secret ceremonies
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[introduce]], [[admit]], [[enlist]], [[enrol]], [[launch]], [[establish]], [[invest]], [[recruit]]
**3** to teach the fundamentals of a skill or knowledge to (someone)
 *n*
**4** a person who has been initiated, esp. recently
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[novice]], [[member]], [[pupil]], [[convert]], [[amateur]], [[newcomer]], [[beginner]], [[trainee]]
**5** a [[beginner]]
 **[[initiation]]** *n*
