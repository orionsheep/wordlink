**poor** *adj*
**1** having little money and few possessions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[impoverished]], [[broke]] (*informal*), [[hard up]] (*informal*), [[short]], [[needy]], [[penniless]], [[destitute]], [[poverty-stricken]]
**2** less than is necessary or expected: it was a poor reward for all his effort
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meagre]], [[inadequate]], [[insufficient]], [[lacking]], [[incomplete]], [[scant]], [[deficient]], [[skimpy]]
**3** (sometimes foll. by *in*) [[lacking]] in (something): a food which is rich in energy but poor in vitamins
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meagre]], [[inadequate]], [[insufficient]], [[lacking]], [[incomplete]], [[scant]], [[deficient]], [[skimpy]]
**4** [[inferior]]: poor quality
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inferior]], [[unsatisfactory]], [[mediocre]], [[second-rate]], [[rotten]] (*informal*), [[low-grade]], [[below par]], [[substandard]], [[half-pie]] (*NZ* *informal*), bodger *or* bodgie (*Austral* *slang*)
**5** disappointing or disagreeable: a poor play
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inferior]], [[unsatisfactory]], [[mediocre]], [[second-rate]], [[rotten]] (*informal*), [[low-grade]], [[below par]], [[substandard]], [[half-pie]] (*NZ* *informal*), bodger *or* bodgie (*Austral* *slang*)
**6** [[pitiable]]; [[unlucky]]: poor John is ill
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unfortunate]], [[unlucky]], [[hapless]], [[pitiful]], [[luckless]], [[wretched]], [[ill-starred]], [[pitiable]]
**7** **poor man's (something)** a cheaper substitute for (something): pewter, sometimes known as poor man's silver
