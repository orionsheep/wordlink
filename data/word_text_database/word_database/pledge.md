**pledge** *n*
**1** a solemn [[promise]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[promise]], [[vow]], [[assurance]], [[word]], [[undertaking]], [[warrant]], [[oath]], [[covenant]]
**2**
**A** something valuable given as a [[guarantee]] that a [[promise]] will be kept or a debt paid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guarantee]], [[security]], [[deposit]], [[bail]], [[collateral]], [[pawn]], [[surety]]
 **B** the condition of being used as [[security]]: in pledge
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guarantee]], [[security]], [[deposit]], [[bail]], [[collateral]], [[pawn]], [[surety]]
**3** a token: a pledge of good faith
**4** an [[assurance]] of support or goodwill, given by drinking a toast: we drank a pledge to their success
**5** **take *or* sign the pledge** to [[vow]] not to drink alcohol
 *vb* **pledging**, **pledged**
**6** to [[promise]] solemnly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[promise]], [[vow]], [[swear]], [[contract]], [[engage]], [[give your word]], [[give your oath]]
**7** to [[promise]] to give (money to charity, etc.)
**8** to bind by or as if by a pledge: I was pledged to secrecy
**9** to give (one's [[word]] or property) as a [[guarantee]]
**10** to drink a toast to (a person or cause)
