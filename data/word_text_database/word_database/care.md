**care** *vb* **caring**, **cared**
**1** to be worried or concerned: he does not care what people think about him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be concerned]], [[mind]], [[bother]], [[be interested]], [[be bothered]], [[give a damn]], [[concern yourself]]
**2** to like (to do something): anybody care to go out?
**3** **care for**
**A** to look after or provide for: it is still largely women who care for dependent family members
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[care for someone]]**: look after, [[mind]], tend, attend, nurse, minister to, watch over
 **B** to like or be fond of: he did not care for his concentration to be disturbed; I don't suppose you could ever care for me seriously
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[care for something or someone]]**: like, enjoy, take to, relish, be fond of, be keen on, be partial to
**4** **I couldn't care less** I am completely indifferent
 *n*
**5** careful or serious [[attention]]; [[caution]]: treat all raw meat with extreme care to avoid food poisoning
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[caution]], [[attention]], [[pains]], [[consideration]], [[heed]], [[prudence]], [[vigilance]], [[forethought]]
**6** [[protection]] or [[charge]]: the children are now in the care of a state orphanage
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[custody]], [[keeping]], [[control]], [[charge]], [[management]], [[protection]], [[supervision]], [[guardianship]]
**7** [[trouble]] or [[worry]]: his [[mind]] turned towards money cares
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[worry]], [[concern]], [[pressure]], [[trouble]], [[responsibility]], [[stress]], [[anxiety]], [[disquiet]]
**8** **care of** (written on envelopes) at the address of
**9** **in *or* into care** (*Brit** & **NZ*) (of a child) made the legal [[responsibility]] of a local authority or the state by order of a court
**10** **take care** to be careful
**11** **take care of** to look after: women have to take greater care of themselves during pregnancy
