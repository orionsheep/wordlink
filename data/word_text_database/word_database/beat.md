**beat** *vb* **[[beating]]**, **beat**, **beaten*** or ***beat**
**1** to [[strike]] with a series of violent blows
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[batter]], [[hit]], [[strike]], [[knock]], [[pound]], [[smack]], [[thrash]], [[thump]]
**2** to move (wings) up and down
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flap]], [[thrash]], [[flutter]], [[wag]]
**3** to [[throb]] rhythmically
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throb]], [[thump]], [[pound]], [[quake]], [[vibrate]], [[pulsate]], [[palpitate]]
**4** (*cookery*) to stir or whisk vigorously
**5** to shape (metal) by repeated blows
**6** (*music*) to indicate (time) by one's hand or a baton
**7** to produce (a sound) by striking a drum
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hit]], [[strike]], [[bang]]
**8** to [[overcome]] or [[defeat]]: he was determined to beat his illness
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[defeat]], [[outdo]], [[trounce]], [[overcome]], [[crush]], [[overwhelm]], [[conquer]], [[surpass]]
**9** to form (a [[path]] or track) by repeated use
**10** to arrive, achieve, or finish before (someone or something): she beat her team mate fair and square
**11** (foll. by *back* or *down* or *off* etc) to drive, push, or thrust
**12** to scour (woodlands or undergrowth) to rouse game for shooting
**13** (*slang*) to puzzle or baffle: it beats me
 *n*
**14** a stroke or blow
**15** the sound made by a stroke or blow
**16** a regular [[throb]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throb]], [[pounding]], [[pulse]], [[thumping]], [[vibration]], [[pulsating]], [[palpitation]], [[pulsation]]
**17** an assigned [[route]], as of a police officer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[route]], [[way]], [[course]], [[rounds]], [[path]], [[circuit]]
**18** the basic rhythmic unit in a piece of music
**19** pop or rock music characterized by a heavy rhythmic beat
 *adj*
**20** (*slang*) totally exhausted
 See also **beat up**
