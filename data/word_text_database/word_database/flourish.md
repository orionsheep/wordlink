**flourish** *vb*
**1** to be active, successful, or widespread; [[prosper]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thrive]], [[increase]], [[advance]], [[abound]], [[progress]], [[boom]], [[bloom]], [[blossom]], [[prosper]]
**2** to be at the peak of development
**3** to [[wave]] (something) dramatically
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wave]], [[brandish]], [[display]], [[shake]], [[wield]], flaunt
 *n*
**4** a dramatic waving or sweeping movement: she created a flourish with an imaginary wand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wave]], [[sweep]], [[brandish]], [[swish]], [[swing]], [[twirl]]
**5** an ornamental curly line in writing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[curlicue]], [[sweep]], [[decoration]], [[swirl]], [[plume]], [[embellishment]], [[ornamentation]]
**6** a fancy or extravagant action or part of something: he took his tie off with a flourish
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[show]], [[display]], [[parade]], [[fanfare]]
 **[[flourishing]]** *adj*
