**land** *n*
**1** the solid part of the surface of the [[earth]] as distinct from seas and lakes. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**terrestrial**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ground]], [[earth]], [[dry land]], [[terra firma]]
**2** [[ground]], esp. with reference to its use or quality: agricultural land
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[soil]], [[ground]], [[earth]], [[clay]], [[dirt]], [[sod]], [[loam]]
**3** rural or agricultural areas: he couldn't leave the land
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[countryside]], [[farmland]]
**4** (*law*) [[ground]] owned as [[property]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[property]], [[grounds]], [[estate]], [[real estate]], [[realty]], [[acreage]], [[homestead]] (*US** & **Canad*)
**5** a [[country]], [[region]], or area: to bring peace and riches to your land
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[country]], [[nation]], [[region]], [[state]], [[district]], [[territory]], [[province]], kingdom
 *vb*
**6** to come down or bring (something) down to [[earth]] after a flight or jump
**7** to transfer (something) or go from a ship to the shore: sacks of malt were landed from barges
**8** to come to or touch shore
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arrive]], [[dock]], [[put down]], [[moor]], [[alight]], [[touch down]], [[disembark]], [[come to rest]]
**9** (*informal*) to obtain: he landed a handsomely paid job in a bank
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gain]], [[get]], [[win]], [[secure]], [[acquire]]
**10** (*angling*) to retrieve (a hooked fish) from the water
**11** (*informal*) to deliver (a blow or punch)
 See also **land up**
