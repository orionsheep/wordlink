**inside** *prep*
**1** [[in]] or to the [[interior]] of: a bomb had gone off inside the parliament building
**2** [[in]] a period of time less than: they took the lead inside seven minutes
 *adj*
**3** on or of the inside: an article on the paper's inside pages
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inner]], [[internal]], [[interior]], [[inward]]
**4** by or from someone [[within]] an organization, esp. illicitly: inside information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[confidential]], [[private]], [[secret]], [[internal]], [[exclusive]], [[restricted]], [[privileged]], [[classified]]
**5** of or being the lane [[in]] a road which is nearer the side than other lanes going [[in]] the same direction: all the lorries were [[in]] the inside lane
 *adv*
**6** on, [[in]], or to the inside; [[indoors]]: when the rain started we took our drinks inside
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indoors]], [[in]], [[within]], [[under cover]]
**7** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*) [[in]] or into prison
 *n*
**8** the [[inner]] side, surface, or part of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[interior]], [[contents]], [[core]], [[nucleus]]
**9** **inside out** with the inside facing outwards
**10** **know inside out** to know thoroughly
