**soak** *vb*
**1** to put or lie in a liquid so as to become thoroughly wet
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[steep]]
**2** (usually foll. by *in* or *into*) (of a liquid) to [[penetrate]] or [[permeate]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[penetrate]], [[permeate]], [[seep]]
**3** (usually foll. by *in* or *up*) to take in; absorb: white clay soaks up excess oil
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[soak something up]]**: absorb, suck up, assimilate
 *n*
**4** a [[soaking]] or being soaked
**5** (*slang*) a person who drinks very heavily
 **[[soaking]]** *n*, *adj*
