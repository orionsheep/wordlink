**disgrace** *n*
**1** a condition of [[shame]], loss of reputation, or [[dishonour]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shame]], [[degradation]], [[disrepute]], [[ignominy]], [[dishonour]], [[infamy]], [[opprobrium]], [[odium]] (*formal*)
**2** a shameful person or thing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[scandal]], [[stain]], [[stigma]], [[blot]], [[blemish]]
**3** exclusion from confidence or trust: he was sent home in disgrace
 *vb* **-gracing**, **-graced**
**4** to [[bring shame upon]] (oneself or others)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shame]], [[humiliate]], [[discredit]], [[degrade]], [[taint]], [[sully]], [[dishonour]], [[bring shame upon]]
 **[[disgraceful]]** *adj* 
 **[[disgracefully]]** *adv*
