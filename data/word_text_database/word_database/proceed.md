**proceed** *vb*
**1** to advance or [[carry on]], esp. after stopping
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[continue]], [[go on]], [[progress]], [[carry on]], [[go ahead]], [[press on]], [[crack on]] (*informal*)
**2** (often foll. by *with*) to start or [[continue]] doing: he proceeded to pour himself a cup of tea
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[begin]], [[go ahead]]
**3** (*formal*) to walk or go
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go on]], [[continue]], [[progress]], [[carry on]], [[go ahead]], [[move on]], [[move forward]], [[press on]], [[crack on]] (*informal*)
**4** (often foll. by *against*) to start a legal action
**5** (*formal*) to [[arise]] (from): their mutual dislike proceeded from differences of political opinion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arise]], [[come]], [[issue]], [[result]], [[spring]], [[flow]], [[stem]], [[derive]]
