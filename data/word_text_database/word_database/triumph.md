**triumph** *n*
**1** the feeling of great [[happiness]] resulting from a [[victory]] or major [[achievement]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[joy]], [[pride]], [[happiness]], [[rejoicing]], [[elation]], [[jubilation]], [[exultation]]
**2** an outstanding [[success]], [[achievement]], or [[victory]]: the concert was a musical triumph
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[success]], [[victory]], [[accomplishment]], [[achievement]], [[coup]], [[feat]], [[conquest]], [[attainment]]
**3** (in ancient Rome) a procession held in honour of a victorious general
 *vb*
**4** to gain control or [[success]]: triumphing over adversity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[succeed]], [[win]], [[overcome]], [[prevail]], [[prosper]], [[vanquish]] (*literary*)
**5** to [[rejoice]] over a [[victory]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rejoice]], [[celebrate]], [[glory]], [[revel]], [[gloat]], [[exult]], [[crow]]
 **[[triumphal]]** *adj*
