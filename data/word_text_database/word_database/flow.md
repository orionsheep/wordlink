**flow** *vb*
**1** (of liquids) to [[move]] in a [[stream]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[run]], [[course]], [[rush]], [[sweep]], [[move]], [[pass]], [[roll]], [[flood]]
**2** (of blood, electricity, etc.) to circulate
**3** to [[move]] steadily and smoothly: a golf club with rich-looking cars flowing into it
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pour]], [[move]], [[sweep]], [[flood]], [[stream]]
**4** to be produced effortlessly: words flowed from him in a steady [[stream]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[issue]], [[follow]], [[result]], [[emerge]], [[spring]], [[proceed]], [[arise]], [[derive]]
**5** to hang freely: her hair loose and flowing down her back
**6** to be abundant: at the buffet lunch, wine flowed like water
**7** (of [[tide]] water) to rise
 *n*
**8** the act, rate, or manner of flowing: the abundant flow of water through domestic sprinklers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stream]], [[current]], [[movement]], [[motion]], [[course]], [[flood]], [[drift]], [[tide]]
**9** a continuous [[stream]] or discharge
**10** the advancing of the [[tide]]
