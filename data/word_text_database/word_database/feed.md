**feed** *vb* **feeding**, **fed**
**1** to give [[food]] to (a person or an animal)
**2** to give (something) as [[food]]: people feeding bread to their cattle
**3** to [[eat]] [[food]]: red squirrel feed in the pines
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[graze]], [[eat]], [[browse]], [[pasture]]
**4** to [[supply]] or prepare [[food]] for
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cater for]], [[provide for]], [[nourish]], [[provide with food]], [[supply]], [[sustain]], [[cook for]], [[wine and dine]]
**5** to provide what is needed for the continued existence, operation, or growth of: illustrations which will feed an older child's imagination; pools fed by waterfalls
 *n*
**6** the act of feeding
**7** [[food]], esp. that given to animals or babies
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[food]], [[fodder]], [[provender]], [[pasturage]]
**8** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) a [[meal]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meal]], [[spread]] (*informal*), [[dinner]], [[lunch]], [[tea]], [[breakfast]], [[feast]], [[supper]]
