**finish** *vb*
**1** to bring to an [[end]]; [[conclude]] or [[stop]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[end]], [[stop]], [[conclude]], [[wind up]], [[terminate]]
**2** to be at or come to the [[end]]; use up
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stop]], [[close]], [[complete]], [[conclude]], [[cease]], [[wrap up]] (*informal*), [[terminate]], [[round off]]
**3** to bring to a desired or [[complete]] condition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get done]], [[complete]], [[conclude]]
**4** to put a particular [[surface]] [[texture]] on (wood, cloth, or metal)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[coat]], [[polish]], [[stain]], [[texture]], [[wax]], [[varnish]], [[gild]], [[veneer]]
**5** **finish off**
**A** to [[complete]] by doing the last part of: she finished off her dissertation last week
 **B** to destroy or defeat completely: the physical demands of two young children nearly finished me off
**6** **finish with** to [[end]] a relationship with (someone)
