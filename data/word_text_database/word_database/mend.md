**mend** *vb*
**1** to [[repair]] something broken or not working
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[repair]], [[fix]], [[restore]], [[renew]], [[patch up]], [[renovate]], [[refit]], [[retouch]]
**2** to [[heal]] or [[recover]]: a wound like that will take a while to mend
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[heal]], [[improve]], [[recover]], [[get better]], [[be all right]], [[be cured]], [[recuperate]], [[pull through]]
**3** (esp. of behaviour) to [[improve]]; make or become better: if you don't mend your ways you'll be in serious trouble
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[improve]], [[reform]], [[correct]], [[revise]], [[amend]], [[rectify]], [[ameliorate]], emend
 *n*
**4** a mended area, esp. on a garment
**5** **[[on the mend]]** regaining one's health
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[on the mend]]**: convalescent, improving, recovering, getting better, recuperating
