**shower** *n*
**1**
**A** a kind of bathing in which a person stands upright and is sprayed with water from a nozzle
 **B** a device, room, or booth for such bathing
**2** a brief period of rain, hail, sleet, or snow
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deluge]]
**3** a sudden fall of many small light objects: a shower of loose gravel
**4** (*Brit* *slang*) a worthless or contemptible group of people
**5** (*US** & **Canad** & **Austral** & **NZ*) a party held to honour and present gifts to a prospective bride or prospective mother
 *vb*
**6** to take a shower
**7** to [[sprinkle]] with or as if with a shower: the walkers were showered by volcanic ash
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cover]], [[dust]], [[spray]], [[sprinkle]]
**8** to present (someone) with things liberally: he showered her with presents
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inundate]], [[heap]], [[lavish]], [[pour]], [[deluge]]
 **[[showery]]** *adj*
