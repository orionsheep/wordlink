**override** *vb* **-riding**, **-rode**, **-ridden**
**1** to set aside or disregard (a person or a person's decisions) by having superior authority or power: the managing director can override any decision he doesn't like
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[overrule]], [[cancel]], [[overturn]], [[repeal]], [[rescind]], [[annul]], [[nullify]], [[countermand]]
**2** to be more important than or replace (something): unsurprisingly the day-to-day struggle for survival overrode all moral considerations
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[outweigh]], [[eclipse]], [[supersede]], [[take precedence over]], [[prevail over]]
 **[[overriding]]** *adj*
