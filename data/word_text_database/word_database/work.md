**work** *n*
**1** physical or mental [[effort]] directed to doing or making something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[effort]], [[industry]], [[labour]], [[sweat]], [[toil]], [[exertion]], [[drudgery]], [[elbow grease]] (*facetious*)
**2** paid [[employment]] at a [[job]], [[trade]], or [[profession]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[employment]], [[business]], [[job]], [[trade]], [[duty]], [[profession]], [[occupation]], [[livelihood]]
**3** [[duties]] or tasks: I had to delegate as much work as I could
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[task]], [[jobs]], [[projects]], [[commissions]], [[duties]], [[assignments]], [[chores]], [[yakka]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*)
**4** something done or made as a result of [[effort]]: a work by a major artist
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[creation]], [[piece]], [[production]], [[opus]], [[achievement]], [[composition]], [[handiwork]]
**5** the place where a person is employed: accidents at work
**6** (*physics*, *old-fashioned*) the transfer of energy occurring when a force is applied to [[move]] a body
**7** **at work** working or in action: the social forces at work in society
 *adj*
**8** of or for work: work experience
 *vb*
**9** to do work; [[labour]]: no-one worked harder than Arnold
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[labour]], [[sweat]], [[slave]], [[toil]], [[slog]] (away), [[drudge]], [[peg away]], [[exert yourself]]
**10** to [[be employed]]: she worked as a waitress
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be employed]], [[be in work]]
**11** to make (a person or animal) [[labour]]
**12** to [[operate]] (a machine or a [[piece]] of equipment)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[operate]], [[use]], [[move]], [[control]], [[drive]], [[manage]], [[handle]], [[manipulate]]
**13** (of a machine or a [[piece]] of equipment) to [[function]], esp. effectively: he doesn't have to know how things work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[function]], [[go]], [[run]], [[operate]], [[be in working order]]
**14** (of a plan or system) to [[be successful]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[succeed]], [[work out]], [[pay off]] (*informal*), [[be successful]], [[be effective]], [[do the trick]] (*informal*), [[do the business]] (*informal*), [[get results]]
**15** to [[cultivate]] (land)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cultivate]], [[farm]], [[dig]], [[till]], [[plough]]
**16** to [[move]] gradually into a specific condition or position: he picked up the shovel, worked it under the ice, and levered
**17** to make (one's way) with [[effort]]: he worked his way to the top
**18** (*informal*) to [[manipulate]] to one's own advantage: they could see an angle and they'd know how to work it
