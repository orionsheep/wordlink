**contract** *n*
**1** a formal [[agreement]] between two or more parties
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[agreement]], [[commitment]], [[arrangement]], [[settlement]], [[bargain]], [[pact]], [[covenant]]
**2** a document setting out a formal [[agreement]]
 *vb*
**3** to make a formal [[agreement]] with (a person or company) to do or deliver (something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[agree]], [[negotiate]], [[pledge]], [[bargain]], [[undertake]], [[come to terms]], [[covenant]], [[make a deal]]
**4** to enter into (a relationship or marriage) formally: to contract a marriage without the approval of one's guardian
**5** to make or become smaller, narrower, or shorter
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[constrict]], [[confine]], [[tighten]], [[shorten]], [[compress]], [[condense]], [[shrivel]]
**6** to become affected by (an illness)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[catch]], [[get]], [[develop]], [[acquire]], [[incur]], [[be infected with]], [[go down with]], [[be afflicted with]]
**7** to draw (muscles) together or (of muscles) to be drawn together
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tighten]], [[narrow]], [[shorten]]
**8** to [[shorten]] (a word or phrase) by omitting letters or syllables, usually indicated in writing by an apostrophe
 **[[contractible]]** *adj*
