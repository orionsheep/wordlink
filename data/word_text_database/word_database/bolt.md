**bolt** *n*
**1** a [[bar]] that can be slid into a socket to [[lock]] a door, gate, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bar]], [[catch]], [[lock]], [[latch]], [[fastener]], [[sliding bar]]
**2** a metal [[rod]] or [[pin]] that has a head and a screw thread to take a nut
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pin]], [[rod]], [[peg]], [[rivet]]
**3** a flash (of lightning)
**4** a sudden movement, esp. in order to escape
**5** an arrow, esp. for a crossbow
**6** **a bolt from the blue** a sudden, unexpected, and usually unwelcome event
**7** **shoot one's bolt** to exhaust one's efforts
 *vb*
**8** to run away suddenly
**9** to [[secure]] or [[lock]] with or as if with a bolt
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lock]], [[close]], [[bar]], [[secure]], [[fasten]], [[latch]]
**10** to attach firmly (one thing to another) by means of a nut and bolt
**11** to eat hurriedly: bolting your food may lead to indigestion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gobble]], [[stuff]], [[wolf]], [[cram]], [[gorge]], [[devour]], [[gulp]], [[guzzle]]
**12** (of a horse) to run away without control
**13** (of vegetables) to produce flowers and seeds too soon
 *adv*
**14** **bolt upright** stiff and rigid
