**tramp** *vb*
**1** to walk long and far; [[hike]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trudge]], [[stump]], [[toil]], [[plod]], [[traipse]] (*informal*)
**2** to walk heavily or firmly across or through (a place): she tramped slowly up the beach
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[trudge]], [[stump]], [[toil]], [[plod]], [[traipse]] (*informal*)
 *n*
**3** a homeless person who travels about on foot, living by begging or doing casual work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vagrant]], [[derelict]], [[drifter]], [[down-and-out]], [[derro]] (*Austral* *slang*)
**4** a long hard walk; [[hike]]: we went for a long tramp over the downs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hike]], [[march]], [[trek]], [[ramble]], [[slog]]
**5** the sound of heavy regular footsteps: we could hear the tramp of the marching soldiers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tread]], [[stamp]], [[footstep]], [[footfall]]
**6** a small cargo ship that does not run on a regular schedule
**7** (*US** & **Canad** & **Austral** & **NZ* *derogatory*, *slang*) a woman considered promiscuous
