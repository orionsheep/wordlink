**tempt** *vb*
**1** to [[entice]] (someone) to do something, esp. something morally wrong or unwise: can I tempt you to have another whisky?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[entice]], [[lure]], [[lead on]], [[invite]], [[seduce]], [[coax]]
**2** to [[allure]] or [[attract]]: she was tempted by the glamour of a modelling career
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[attract]], [[allure]]
**3** **be tempted** to want to do something while knowing it would be wrong or inappropriate to do so: many youngsters are tempted to experiment with drugs
**4** **tempt fate *or* providence** to take foolish or unnecessary risks
 **[[tempter]]** * or * (*fem*) **[[temptress]]** *n*
