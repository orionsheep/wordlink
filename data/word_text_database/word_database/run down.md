**run down** *vb*
**1** to be rude about: he is busy running us down and insulting other Europeans
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run something or someone down]]**: criticize, denigrate, belittle, knock (*informal*), rubbish (*informal*), slag (off) (*slang*), disparage, decry
**2** to reduce in number or size: it should be possible to run down the existing hospitals almost entirely
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run something or someone down]]**: downsize, cut, reduce, trim, decrease, cut back, curtail, kennet (*Austral* *slang*), jeff (*Austral* *slang*)
**3** (of a device such as a clock or battery) to lose power gradually and cease to function
**4** to hit and knock to the ground with a moving vehicle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[run something or someone down]]**: knock down, hit, run into, run over, knock over
**5** to pursue and find or capture: while I was there, Moscow ran me down, convinced I was ready to defect
 *adj* **rundown**
**6** tired or ill
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exhausted]], [[weak]], [[drained]], [[weary]], [[unhealthy]], [[worn-out]], [[debilitated]], [[below par]]
**7** [[shabby]] or [[dilapidated]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dilapidated]], [[broken-down]], [[shabby]], [[worn-out]], [[seedy]], [[ramshackle]], decrepit
 *n* **rundown**
**8** a reduction in number or size
**9** a brief review or summary
