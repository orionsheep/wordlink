**name** *n*
**1** a word or [[term]] by which a person or thing is known. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**nominal**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[title]], [[nickname]], [[designation]], [[term]], [[handle]] (*slang*), [[epithet]], [[sobriquet]], moniker *or* monicker (*slang*)
**2** reputation, esp. a good one: he was making a name for himself
**3** a famous person: she's a big name now
**4** **[[call]] someone names *or* a name** to insult someone by using rude words to describe him or her
**5** **in name only** not possessing the powers or status implied by one's [[title]]: a leadership in name only
**6** **in the name of**
**A** for the sake of: in the name of decency
 **B** by the authority of: in the name of the law
**7** **name of the game** the most significant or important aspect of something: survival is the name of the game in wartime
**8** **to one's name** in one's possession: she hasn't a penny to her name
 *vb* **naming**, **named**
**9** to give a name to
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[call]], [[christen]], [[baptize]], [[dub]], [[term]], [[style]], [[label]], [[entitle]]
**10** to refer to by name: he refused to name his source
**11** to fix or [[specify]]: he named a time for the meeting
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nominate]], [[choose]], [[select]], [[appoint]], [[specify]], [[designate]]
**12** to [[appoint]]: she was named Journalist of the Year
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nominate]], [[choose]], [[select]], [[appoint]], [[specify]], [[designate]]
**13** to ban (an MP) from the House of Commons by mentioning him or her formally by name as being guilty of disorderly conduct
**14** **name names** to cite people in order to blame or accuse them
