**dance** *vb* **[[dancing]]**, **danced**
**1** to move the feet and body rhythmically in time to music
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prance]], [[trip]], [[hop]], [[skip]], [[sway]], [[whirl]], [[caper]], [[jig]]
**2** to perform (a particular dance): to dance a tango
**3** to [[skip]] or leap
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[caper]], [[trip]], [[spring]], [[jump]], [[bound]], [[skip]], [[frolic]], [[cavort]]
**4** to move in a rhythmic way: their reflection danced in the black waters
**5** **dance attendance on someone** to carry out someone's slightest wish in an overeager manner
 *n*
**6** a [[social]] meeting arranged for [[dancing]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ball]], [[social]], [[hop]] (*informal*), [[disco]], [[knees-up]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[discotheque]], [[B and S]] (*Austral* *informal*)
**7** a series of rhythmic steps and movements in time to music
**8** a piece of music in the rhythm of a particular dance
 **[[dancer]]** *n* 
 **[[dancing]]** *n*, *adj*
