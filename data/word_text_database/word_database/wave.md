**wave** *vb* **waving**, **waved**
**1** to move (one's hand) to and fro as a greeting
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[signal]], [[sign]], [[gesture]], [[gesticulate]]
**2** to [[direct]] (someone) to move in a particular direction by waving: I waved him on
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guide]], [[point]], [[direct]], [[indicate]], [[signal]], [[motion]], [[gesture]], [[nod]]
**3** to hold (something) up and move it from side to side in order to attract attention
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[brandish]], [[swing]], [[flourish]], [[wag]], [[shake]]
**4** to move freely to and fro: flowers waving in the wind
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flutter]], [[flap]], [[stir]], [[shake]], [[swing]], [[wag]], oscillate
 *n*
**5** one of a sequence of ridges or undulations that moves across the surface of the sea or a lake
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ripple]], [[breaker]], [[swell]], [[ridge]], [[roller]], [[billow]]
**6** a curve in the hair
**7** a sudden rise in the frequency or intensity of something: a wave of sympathy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[outbreak]], [[rash]], [[upsurge]], [[flood]], [[surge]], [[groundswell]]
**8** a widespread movement that advances in a body: a new wave of refugees
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stream]], [[flood]], [[surge]], [[spate]], [[current]], [[flow]], [[rush]], [[tide]]
**9** a prolonged spell of some particular type of weather: a heat wave
**10** the act or an instance of waving
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gesture]], [[sign]], [[signal]], [[indication]], [[gesticulation]]
**11** (*physics*) an energy-carrying disturbance travelling through a medium or space by a series of vibrations without any overall movement of matter
**12** **make waves** to cause trouble
