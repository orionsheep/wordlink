**gather** *vb*
**1** to come or bring together
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[congregate]], [[assemble]], [[collect]], [[meet]], [[mass]], [[come together]], [[muster]], [[converge]]
**2** to [[increase]] gradually in (pace, speed, or momentum)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[build up]], [[rise]], [[increase]], [[grow]], [[expand]], [[swell]], [[intensify]], [[heighten]]
**3** to prepare oneself for a task or challenge by collecting one's thoughts, strength, or courage
**4** to [[learn]] from information given; [[conclude]]: this is pretty important, I gather
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[understand]], [[believe]], [[hear]], [[learn]], [[assume]], [[conclude]], [[presume]], [[infer]]
**5** to draw (fabric) into small folds or tucks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fold]], [[tuck]], [[pleat]]
**6** to [[pick]] or [[harvest]] (crops)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pick]], [[harvest]], [[pluck]], [[reap]], [[garner]], glean
 *n*
**7** **gathers** small folds or tucks in fabric
