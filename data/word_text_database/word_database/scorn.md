**scorn** *n*
**1** open [[contempt]] for a person or thing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[contempt]], [[disdain]], [[mockery]], [[derision]], [[sarcasm]], disparagement
 *vb*
**2** to treat with [[contempt]]: she attacked the government for scorning her profession
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[despise]], [[reject]], [[disdain]], [[slight]], [[be above]], [[spurn]], [[deride]], [[flout]]
**3** to refuse to have or do (something) because it is felt to be undesirable or wrong: youths who scorn traditional morals
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[despise]], [[reject]], [[disdain]], [[slight]], [[be above]], [[spurn]], [[deride]], [[flout]]
 **[[scornful]]** *adj* 
 **[[scornfully]]** *adv*
