**large** *adj*
**1** having a relatively [[great]] size, quantity, or extent; [[big]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[big]], [[great]], [[huge]], [[heavy]], [[massive]], [[vast]], [[enormous]], [[tall]], [[supersize]]
**2** of wide or broad scope, capacity, or range; comprehensive: a large effect
 *n*
**3** **[[at large]]**
**A** as a whole; in general: both the Navy and the country [[at large]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[at large]]**: in general, generally, chiefly, mainly, as a whole, in the main
 **B** (of a dangerous criminal or wild animal) out of captivity; free
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[at large]]**: free, on the run, fugitive, at liberty, on the loose, unchained, unconfined
 **C** in full detail
 *vb* **larging**, **larged**
**4** **large it** (*Brit* *slang*) to enjoy oneself or celebrate in an extravagant way
 **[[largeness]]** *n*
