**dump** *vb*
**1** to [[drop]] or [[let fall]] in a careless manner: he dumped the books on the bed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[deposit]], [[throw down]], [[let fall]], [[fling down]]
**2** (*informal*) to abandon (someone or something) without proper care: the unwanted babies were dumped in orphanages
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get rid of]], [[tip]], [[dispose of]], [[unload]], [[jettison]], [[empty out]], throw away *or* out
**3** to [[dispose of]] (nuclear waste)
**4** (*commerce*) to sell (goods) in bulk and at low prices, usually in another country, in order to keep prices high in the home market
**5** (*computers*) to record (the contents of the memory) on a storage device at a series of points during a computer run
 *n*
**6** a place where rubbish is left
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rubbish tip]], [[tip]] (*Brit*), [[junkyard]], [[rubbish heap]], [[refuse heap]]
**7** (*informal*) a dirty, unattractive place: you're hardly in this dump out of choice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pigsty]], [[hole]] (*informal*), [[slum]], [[hovel]]
**8** (*military*) a place where weapons or supplies are stored
