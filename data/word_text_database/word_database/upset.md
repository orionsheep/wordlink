**upset** *adj*
**1** emotionally or physically [[disturbed]] or [[distressed]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[distressed]], [[shaken]], [[disturbed]], [[worried]], [[troubled]], [[hurt]], [[bothered]], unhappy
 *vb* **-setting**, **-set**
**2** to turn or [[tip over]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tip over]], [[overturn]], [[capsize]], [[knock over]], [[spill]]
**3** to disrupt the normal state or progress of: bad weather upset their plans
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mess up]], [[spoil]], [[disturb]], [[change]], [[confuse]], [[disorder]], [[unsettle]], [[disorganize]]
**4** to [[disturb]] mentally or emotionally
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[distress]], [[trouble]], [[disturb]], [[worry]], [[alarm]], [[bother]], [[grieve]], [[agitate]]
**5** to make physically ill: it still seems to upset my stomach
 *n*
**6** an unexpected [[defeat]] or [[reversal]], as in a contest or plans
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reversal]], [[shake-up]] (*informal*), [[defeat]]
**7** a disturbance or [[disorder]] of the emotions, mind, or body
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[illness]], [[complaint]], [[disorder]], [[bug]] (*informal*), [[sickness]], [[malady]]
