**touch** *vb*
**1** to cause or permit a part of the body to [[come into contact]] with (someone or something): the baking tin is too hot to touch
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[come into contact]], [[meet]], [[contact]], [[border]], [[graze]], [[adjoin]], [[be in contact]], [[abut]]
**2** to [[tap]], feel, or strike (someone or something): he touched me on the shoulder
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tap]]
**3** to come or bring (something) into [[contact]] with (something else): the plane's wheels touched the runway
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tap]]
**4** to [[move]] or [[disturb]] by [[handling]]: we shouldn't touch anything before the police arrive
**5** to have an effect on: millions of people's lives had been touched by the music of the Beatles
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[affect]], [[influence]], [[inspire]], [[impress]]
**6** to produce an emotional response in: the painful truth of it touched her
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move]], [[stir]], [[disturb]]
**7** to [[eat]] or [[drink]]: she hardly ever touched alcohol
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[consume]], [[take]], [[drink]], [[eat]], [[partake of]]
**8** to compare to in quality or attainment; [[equal]] or [[match]]: nothing can touch them for scope and detail
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[match]], [[rival]], [[equal]], [[compare with]], [[parallel]], [[hold a candle to]] (*informal*)
**9** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *slang*) to ask (someone) for a loan or gift of money
**10** to fondle in a sexual [[manner]]: I wouldn't let him touch me unless I was in the mood
**11** to strike, harm, or molest: I never touched him!
**12** **touch on *or* upon** to allude to briefly or in passing: these two issues may be touched upon during the talks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[touch on something]]**: refer to, cover, raise, deal with, mention, bring in, speak of, hint at
 *n*
**13** the sense by which the texture and other qualities of objects can be experienced when they come in [[contact]] with a part of the body surface, esp. the tips of the fingers. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**tactile**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[feeling]], [[handling]], [[physical contact]]
**14** the feel or texture of an object as perceived by this sense: she enjoyed the touch of the damp grass on her feet
**15** the act or an instance of something coming into [[contact]] with the body: he remembered the touch of her hand
**16** a gentle [[push]], [[tap]], or caress: the switch takes only the merest touch to operate
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[contact]], [[push]], [[stroke]], [[brush]], [[press]], [[tap]], [[poke]], [[nudge]]
**17** a [[small amount]]; [[trace]]: a touch of luxury
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bit]], [[spot]] (*Brit*), [[trace]], [[drop]], [[dash]], [[small amount]], [[jot]], [[smattering]]
**18** a particular [[manner]] or [[style]] of doing something: his songs always reveal his keen melodic touch
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[style]], [[method]], [[technique]], [[way]], [[manner]], [[trademark]]
**19** a detail of some work: final touches were now being put to the plans
**20** a slight attack: a touch of dysentery
**21** (in sports such as football or rugby) the area outside the lines marking the side of the pitch: he kicked the ball into touch
**22** the [[technique]] of fingering a keyboard instrument
**23** **a touch** slightly or marginally: it's nice, but a touch expensive
**24** **in touch**
**A** regularly speaking to, writing to, or visiting someone
 **B** having up-to-date knowledge or understanding of a situation or trend
**25** **lose touch**
**A** to gradually stop speaking to, writing to, or visiting someone
 **B** to stop having up-to-date knowledge or understanding of a situation or trend
**26** **out of touch**
**A** no longer speaking to, writing to, or visiting someone
 **B** no longer having up-to-date knowledge or understanding of a situation or trend
