**edge** *n*
**1** a [[border]] or line where something ends or begins: the edge of the city
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[border]], [[side]], [[limit]], [[outline]], [[boundary]], [[fringe]], [[verge]], [[brink]]
**2** a line along which two faces or surfaces of a solid meet
**3** the sharp cutting [[side]] of a blade
**4** [[keenness]], [[sharpness]], or urgency: there was a nervous edge to his voice
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sharpness]], [[point]], [[bitterness]], [[keenness]]
**5** **have the edge on** to have a slight advantage over
**6** **[[on edge]]** nervous and irritable
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[on edge]]**: tense, nervous, impatient, irritable, apprehensive, edgy, ill at [[ease]], on tenterhooks, adrenalized
**7** **set someone's teeth [[on edge]]** to make someone acutely irritated
 *vb* **edging**, **edged**
**8** to make, form, or be an edge or [[border]] for: a pillow edged with lace
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[border]], [[fringe]], [[hem]], [[pipe]]
**9** to move very gradually in a particular direction: I edged through to the front of the crowd
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inch]], [[ease]], [[creep]], [[slink]], [[steal]], [[sidle]], [[move slowly]]
