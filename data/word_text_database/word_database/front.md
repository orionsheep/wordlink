**front** *n*
**1** that part or side that is forward, or most often seen or used
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[foreground]], [[fore]], [[forefront]], [[nearest part]]
**2** a position or place directly before or ahead
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exterior]], [[face]], [[façade]], [[frontage]]
**3** the beginning, opening, or first part
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[head]], [[start]], [[lead]], [[forefront]]
**4** the position of leadership
**5** a promenade at a seaside resort
**6** (*military*)
**A** the total area in which opposing armies [[face]] each other
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[front line]], [[trenches]], [[vanguard]], firing line
 **B** the space in which a military unit is operating
**7** (*meteorol*) the dividing line between two different air masses
**8** an outward appearance: he put on a bold front
**9** (*informal*) a business or other activity serving as a respectable [[cover]] for another, usually criminal, organization
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disguise]], [[cover]], [[blind]], [[mask]], [[cover-up]], [[cloak]], [[façade]], [[pretext]]
**10** Also called: **front man**, **front woman** a nominal leader of an organization
**11** a particular field of activity: on the economic front
**12** a group of people with a common goal: the National Liberation Front
 *adj*
**13** of, at, or in the front
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[foremost]], at the front
 *vb*
**14** to [[face]] (onto)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[face onto]], [[overlook]], [[look out on]], [[have a view of]], look over *or* onto
**15** to be a front of or for
**16** to appear as a presenter in (a television show, etc.)
**17** to be the leader of (a band) on stage
