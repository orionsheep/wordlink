**swing** *vb* **swinging**, **swung**
**1** to move backwards and forwards; [[sway]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sway]], [[rock]], [[wave]], [[veer]], [[oscillate]]
**2** to [[pivot]] or cause (something) to [[pivot]] from a fixed point such as a hinge: the door swung open
**3** to [[wave]] (a weapon, etc.) in a sweeping motion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[brandish]], [[wave]], [[shake]], [[flourish]], [[wield]], [[dangle]]
**4** to move in a sweeping [[curve]]: the headlights swung along the street
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn]], [[swivel]], [[curve]], [[rotate]], [[pivot]]
**5** to alter one's opinion or mood suddenly
**6** to [[hang]] so as to be able to [[turn]] freely
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hang]], [[dangle]], [[suspend]]
**7** (*old-fashioned*, *slang*) to be hanged: you'll swing for this!
**8** (*informal*) to manipulate or influence successfully: it may help to swing the election
**9** (often foll. by *at*) to [[hit out]] with a sweeping motion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hit out]], [[strike]], [[swipe]] (*informal*), [[lash out at]], [[slap]]
**10** (*old-fashioned*) to play (music) in the style of swing
**11** (*old-fashioned*, *slang*) to be lively and modern
 *n*
**12** the act of swinging
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[swaying]], [[sway]]
**13** a sweeping stroke or punch
**14** a seat hanging from two chains or ropes on which a person may swing back and forth
**15** popular dance music played by big bands in the 1930s and 1940s
**16** (*informal*) the normal pace at which an activity, such as work, happens: I'm into the swing of things now
**17** a sudden or extreme change, for example in some business activity or voting pattern
**18** **go with a swing** to go well; be successful
**19** **in full swing** at the height of activity
