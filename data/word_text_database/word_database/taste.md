**taste** *n*
**1** the sense by which the [[flavour]] of a substance is distinguished by the taste buds
**2** the sensation experienced by means of the taste buds
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flavour]], [[savour]], [[relish]], [[smack]], [[tang]]
**3** a small amount eaten, sipped, or tried on the tongue
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bit]], [[bite]], [[mouthful]], [[sample]], [[dash]], [[spoonful]], [[morsel]], [[titbit]]
**4** a brief [[experience]] of something: a taste of the planter's life
**5** a [[liking]] for something: a taste for puns
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[liking]], [[preference]], [[penchant]], [[fondness]], [[partiality]], [[fancy]], [[appetite]], [[inclination]]
**6** the ability to appreciate what is beautiful and excellent: she's got very good taste in clothes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[refinement]], [[style]], [[judgment]], [[discrimination]], [[appreciation]], [[elegance]], [[sophistication]], [[discernment]]
**7** a person's typical preferences as displayed by what they choose to buy, enjoy, etc.: the film was good but a [[bit]] violent for my taste
**8** the quality of not being offensive or bad-mannered: that remark was in rather poor taste
 *vb* **tasting**, **tasted**
**9** to [[distinguish]] the taste of (a substance) by means of the taste buds: I've got a stinking cold and can't taste anything
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[distinguish]], [[perceive]], [[discern]], [[differentiate]]
**10** to take a small amount of (a food or liquid) into the mouth, esp. in order to [[test]] the [[flavour]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sample]], [[try]], [[test]], [[sip]], [[savour]]
**11** to have a [[flavour]] or taste as specified: the pizza tastes delicious
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[have a flavour of]], [[smack of]], [[savour of]]
**12** to have a brief [[experience]] of (something): they have tasted democracy and they won't let go
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[experience]], [[know]], [[undergo]], [[partake of]], [[encounter]], [[meet with]]
