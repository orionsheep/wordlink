**pass** *vb*
**1** to [[go by or past]] (a person or thing)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go by]] *or* past, [[overtake]], [[drive past]], [[lap]], [[leave behind]], [[pull ahead of]]
**2** to continue or extend in a particular direction: the road to Camerino passes through some fine scenery
**3** to [[go]] through or cause (something) to [[go]] through (an obstacle or barrier): the bullet passed through his [[head]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go]], [[move]], [[travel]], [[progress]], [[flow]], [[proceed]]
**4** to [[be successful in]] (a test or examination)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be successful in]], [[qualify]] (in), [[succeed]] (in), [[graduate]] (in), [[get through]], [[do]], [[gain a pass]] (in)
**5** to spend (time) or (of time) [[go by]]: the time passed surprisingly quickly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[elapse]], [[progress]], [[go by]], [[lapse]], [[wear on]], [[go past]], [[tick by]]
**6** to [[hand]] over or be handed over: she passed me her glass
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give]], [[hand]], [[send]], [[transfer]], [[deliver]], [[convey]]
**7** to [[be inherited by]]: his mother's small estate had passed to him after her death
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be left]], [[come]], [[be bequeathed]], [[be inherited by]]
**8** (*sport*) to [[hit]], [[kick]], or throw (the ball) to another player
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[kick]], [[hit]], [[loft]], [[head]], [[lob]]
**9** (of a law-making body) to agree to (a law or proposal): the bill was passed by parliament last week
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[approve]], [[accept]], [[decree]], [[enact]], [[ratify]], [[ordain]] (*formal*), [[legislate]] (for)
**10** to pronounce (judgment): the court is expected to pass sentence later today
**11** to [[move]] onwards or over: a flicker of amusement passed over his face
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go]], [[move]], [[travel]], [[progress]], [[flow]], [[proceed]]
**12** to [[exceed]]: Australia's population has just passed the 25 million mark
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exceed]], [[beat]], [[overtake]], [[go beyond]], [[surpass]], [[outstrip]], [[outdo]]
**13** to [[go]] without comment: the insult passed unnoticed
**14** to choose not to answer a question or not to make a bid or a play in card games
**15** to discharge (urine, etc.) from the body
**16** to [[come]] to an [[end]] or disappear: today's jitters will soon pass
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[end]], [[go]], [[cease]], [[blow over]]
**17** (foll. by *for* or *as*) to be likely to be mistaken for (someone or something else): the few sunny days that pass for summer in this country
**18** (*old-fashioned*) to take place: what passed at the meeting?
**19** **pass away *or* on** (*euphemistic*) to die
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pass away or on]]**: die, pass on, expire, pass over, snuff it (*informal*), [[kick]] the bucket (*slang*), shuffle off this mortal coil, cark it (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*)
 *n*
**20** a successful result in an examination or test
**21** (*sport*) the [[transfer]] of a ball from one player to another
**22** a [[route]] through a range of mountains where there is a [[gap]] between peaks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gap]], [[route]], [[canyon]], [[gorge]], [[ravine]]
**23** a [[permit]] or [[licence]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[licence]], [[ticket]], [[permit]], [[passport]], [[warrant]], [[authorization]]
**24** (*military*) a document authorizing leave of absence
**25** (*bridge*, *etc.*) an instance of choosing not to answer a question or not to make a bid or a play in card games
**26** **a pretty pass** a bad state of affairs
**27** **make a pass at** (*informal*) to try to persuade (someone) to have sex
 See also **pass out** etc
