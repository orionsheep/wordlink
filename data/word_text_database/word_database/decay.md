**decay** *vb*
**1** to [[decline]] gradually in health, prosperity, or quality
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decline]], [[diminish]], [[crumble]], [[deteriorate]], [[fall off]], [[dwindle]], [[lessen]], [[wane]]
**2** to [[rot]] or cause to [[rot]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rot]], [[spoil]], [[crumble]], [[deteriorate]], [[perish]], [[decompose]], [[moulder]], [[go bad]]
**3** (*physics*) (of an atomic nucleus) to undergo radioactive disintegration
 *n*
**4** the process of something rotting: too much sugar can cause tooth decay
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rot]], [[corruption]], [[mould]], [[blight]], [[decomposition]], [[gangrene]], [[canker]], [[caries]]
**5** the state brought about by this process
**6** (*physics*) disintegration of a nucleus, occurring spontaneously or as a result of electron capture
