**value** *n*
**1** the desirability of something, often in terms of its usefulness or exchangeability
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[importance]], [[benefit]], [[worth]], [[merit]], [[point]], [[service]], [[sense]], [[profit]]
**2** an amount of money considered to be a fair exchange for something: scrap metal with a value of £100
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cost]], [[price]], [[worth]], [[rate]], [[market price]], [[face value]], [[asking price]], [[selling price]]
**3** something [[worth]] the money it [[cost]]: the set meal was great value
**4** **values** the moral [[principles]] and beliefs of a person or group
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[principles]], [[morals]], [[ethics]], [[mores]], [[standards of behaviour]], (moral) [[standards]]
**5** (*maths*) a particular number or quantity represented by a figure or symbol
 *vb* **-uing**, **-ued**
**6** to [[assess]] the [[worth]] or desirability of (something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evaluate]], [[price]], [[estimate]], [[rate]], [[cost]], [[assess]], [[set at]], [[appraise]]
**7** to hold (someone or something) in high regard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[appreciate]], [[rate]] (*slang*), [[prize]], [[regard highly]], [[respect]], [[admire]], [[treasure]], [[esteem]]
 **[[valued]]** *adj* 
 **[[valueless]]** *adj* 
 **[[valuer]]** *n*
