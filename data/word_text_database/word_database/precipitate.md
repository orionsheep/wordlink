**precipitate** *vb* **-tating**, **-tated**
**1** to cause to happen earlier than expected: the scandal could bring the government down, precipitating a general election
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quicken]], [[trigger]], [[accelerate]], [[advance]], [[hurry]], [[speed up]], [[bring on]], [[hasten]]
**2** to condense or cause to condense and fall as snow or rain
**3** (*chem*) to cause to be deposited in solid form from a solution
**4** to [[throw]] from a height: the encircled soldiers chose to precipitate themselves into the ocean
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw]], [[launch]], [[cast]], [[hurl]], [[fling]], let fly
 *adj*
**5** done rashly or hastily
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hasty]], [[rash]], [[reckless]], [[impulsive]], [[precipitous]], [[impetuous]], [[heedless]]
**6** [[rushing]] ahead
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sudden]], [[quick]], [[brief]], [[rushing]], [[rapid]], [[unexpected]], [[swift]], abrupt
 *n*
**7** (*chem*) a precipitated solid
