**ruin** *vb*
**1** to destroy or [[spoil]] completely: the suit was ruined
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spoil]], [[damage]], [[mess up]], [[blow]] (*slang*), [[screw up]] (*informal*), [[botch]], [[make a mess of]], crool *or* cruel (*Austral* *slang*)
**2** to cause (someone) to lose money: the first war ruined him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bankrupt]], [[break]], [[impoverish]], [[beggar]], pauperize
 *n*
**3** the state of being destroyed or decayed
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[destruction]], [[fall]], [[breakdown]], [[defeat]], [[collapse]], [[wreck]], [[undoing]], [[downfall]]
**4** loss of wealth or position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bankruptcy]], [[insolvency]], [[destitution]]
**5** a destroyed or decayed building or town
**6** something that is severely damaged: my heart was an aching ruin
