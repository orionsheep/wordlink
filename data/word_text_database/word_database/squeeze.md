**squeeze** *vb* **squeezing**, **squeezed**
**1** to [[grip]] or [[press]] (something) firmly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[press]], [[crush]], [[squash]], [[pinch]]
**2** to [[crush]] or [[press]] (something) so as to extract (a liquid): squeeze the tomato and strain the juice; freshly squeezed lemon juice
**3** to push (oneself or a thing) into a confined space
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cram]], [[press]], [[crowd]], [[force]], [[stuff]], [[pack]], [[jam]], [[ram]]
**4** to [[hug]] (someone) closely
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hug]], [[embrace]], [[cuddle]], [[clasp]], [[enfold]]
**5** to obtain (something) by great effort or [[force]]: to squeeze the last dollar out of every deal
 *n*
**6** a squeezing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[press]], [[grip]], [[clasp]], [[crush]], [[pinch]], [[squash]], [[wring]]
**7** a [[hug]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hug]], [[embrace]], [[clasp]]
**8** a [[crush]] of people in a confined space
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[crush]], [[jam]], [[squash]], [[press]], [[crowd]], [[congestion]]
**9** (*chiefly* *Brit** & **NZ*) a restriction on borrowing made by a government to control price inflation
**10** an amount extracted by squeezing: a squeeze of lime
**11** **put the squeeze on someone** (*informal*) to put pressure on someone in order to obtain something
