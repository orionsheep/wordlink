**extreme** *adj*
**1** of a high or the [[highest]] degree or intensity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[great]], [[highest]], [[supreme]], [[acute]], [[severe]], [[maximum]], [[intense]], [[ultimate]]
**2** exceptionally [[severe]] or unusual: people can survive extreme conditions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[severe]], [[radical]], [[strict]], [[harsh]], [[rigid]], [[drastic]], [[uncompromising]]
**3** (of an opinion, political group, etc.) beyond the limits regarded as acceptable; [[fanatical]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[radical]], [[excessive]], [[fanatical]], [[immoderate]]
**4** [[farthest]] or [[outermost]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[farthest]], [[furthest]], [[far]], [[remotest]], [[far-off]], [[outermost]], most distant
 *n*
**5** either of the two limits of a scale or range
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[limit]], [[end]], [[edge]], [[opposite]], [[pole]], [[boundary]], [[antithesis]], [[extremity]]
**6** **go to extremes** to be unreasonable in speech or action
**7** **in the extreme** to the [[highest]] or [[furthest]] degree: the effect was dramatic in the extreme
 **[[extremely]]** *adv*
