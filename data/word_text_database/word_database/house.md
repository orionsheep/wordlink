**house** *n*
**1** a building used as a [[home]]; dwelling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[home]], [[residence]], dwelling (*formal*, *literary*), pad (*slang*, *old-fashioned*), [[homestead]], [[abode]], [[habitation]] (*formal*), [[domicile]], [[whare]] (*NZ*)
**2** the people in a house
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[household]], [[family]]
**3** a building for some specific purpose: beach house
**4** a [[family]] or [[dynasty]]: the House of Windsor
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dynasty]], [[tribe]], [[clan]]
**5** a commercial [[company]]: auction house
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[firm]], [[company]], [[business]], [[organization]], [[outfit]] (*informal*)
**6** a law-making body or the hall where it meets
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[assembly]], [[parliament]], [[Commons]], [[legislative body]]
**7** a division of a large school: he was captain of the house rugby team
**8** the audience in a theatre or cinema
**9** (*astrol*) any of the 12 divisions of the zodiac
**10** (*informal*) a brothel
**11** **get on like a house on fire** (*informal*) (of people) to get on very well together
**12** **[[on the house]]** (usually of drinks) paid for by the management
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[on the house]]**: free, for free (*informal*), for nothing, free of charge, gratis
**13** **put one's house in order** to settle or organize one's affairs
 *adj*
**14** (of wine) sold unnamed by a restaurant, at a lower price than wines specified on the wine list: house red
 *vb* **housing**, **housed**
**15** to give accommodation to
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accommodate]], [[quarter]], [[take in]], [[put up]], [[lodge]], [[harbour]], [[billet]]
**16** to [[contain]] or [[cover]] (something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[contain]], [[keep]], [[hold]], [[cover]], [[store]], [[protect]], [[shelter]]
