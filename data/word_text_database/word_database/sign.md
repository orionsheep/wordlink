**sign** *n*
**1** something that indicates a fact or condition that is not immediately or outwardly observable: a sign of tension
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indication]], [[evidence]], [[mark]], [[signal]], [[symptom]], [[hint]], [[proof]], [[gesture]]
**2** a [[gesture]], [[mark]], or [[symbol]] intended to convey an idea or information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[symbol]], [[mark]], [[device]], [[logo]], [[badge]], [[emblem]]
**3** a [[board]] or [[placard]] displayed in public and intended to advertise, inform, or warn
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[notice]], [[board]], [[warning]], [[placard]]
**4** a conventional [[mark]] or [[symbol]] that has a specific meaning, for example £ for pounds
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[symbol]], [[mark]], [[device]], [[logo]], [[badge]], [[emblem]]
**5** (*maths*)
**A** any [[symbol]] used to [[indicate]] an operation: a minus sign
 **B** a [[symbol]] used to [[indicate]] whether a number or expression is positive or negative
**6** a visible [[indication]]: no sign of the enemy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indication]], [[evidence]], [[mark]], [[signal]], [[symptom]], [[hint]], [[proof]], [[gesture]]
**7** an [[omen]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[omen]], [[warning]], [[portent]], [[foreboding]], [[augury]], [[auspice]]
**8** (*med*) any [[evidence]] of the presence of a disease or disorder
 *vb*
**9** to write (one's name) on (a document or letter) to show its authenticity or one's agreement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[autograph]], [[initial]], [[inscribe]]
**10** to communicate using sign language
**11** to make a sign to someone so as to convey an idea or information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gesture]], [[indicate]], [[signal]], [[beckon]], [[gesticulate]]
**12** to engage or be engaged by signing a contract: he signed for another team
