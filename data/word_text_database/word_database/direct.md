**direct** *adj*
**1** [[shortest]]; [[straight]]: a direct [[route]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quickest]], [[shortest]]
**2** without intervening people: they secretly arranged direct links to their commanders
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[first-hand]], [[personal]], [[immediate]]
**3** [[honest]]; [[frank]]: he was polite but very direct
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[straightforward]], [[open]], [[straight]], [[frank]], [[blunt]], [[honest]], [[candid]], [[forthright]]
**4** diametric: the direct opposite
**5** in an unbroken line of descent: a direct descendant
 *adv*
**6** directly; [[straight]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[non-stop]], [[straight]]
 *vb*
**7** to [[conduct]] or [[control]] the affairs of
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[control]], [[run]], [[manage]], [[lead]], [[guide]], [[handle]], [[conduct]], [[oversee]]
**8** to give orders with authority to (a person or group)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[order]], [[command]], [[instruct]], [[charge]] (*formal*), [[demand]], [[require]], [[bid]]
**9** to tell (someone) the way to a place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guide]], [[show]], [[lead]], [[point the way]], [[point in the direction of]]
**10** to [[address]] (a letter, parcel, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[address]], [[send]], [[mail]], [[route]], [[label]]
**11** to [[address]] (a look or remark) at someone: the look she directed at him was one of unconcealed hatred
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[aim]], [[point]], [[level]], [[train]], [[focus]]
**12**
**A** to provide guidance to (actors, cameramen, etc.) in (a play or film)
 **B** to supervise the making or staging of (a film or play)
