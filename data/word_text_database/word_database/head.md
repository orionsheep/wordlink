**head** *n*
**1** the upper or front part of the body that contains the [[brain]], eyes, mouth, nose, and ears
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[skull]], [[crown]], [[pate]], [[nut]] (*slang*), [[loaf]] (*slang*)
**2** a person's [[mind]] and mental abilities: I haven't any head for figures
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mind]], [[reasoning]], [[understanding]], [[thought]], [[sense]], [[brain]], [[brains]] (*informal*), [[intelligence]]
**3** the most forward part of a thing: the head of a queue
**4** the highest part of a thing; upper end: the head of the pass
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[top]], [[crown]], [[summit]], [[peak]], [[crest]], [[pinnacle]]
**5** something resembling a head in form or function, such as the [[top]] of a tool
**6** the position of leadership or [[command]]
**7** the person commanding most authority within a group or an organization
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[leader]], [[president]], [[director]], [[manager]], [[chief]], [[boss]] (*informal*), [[captain]], [[master]], [[sherang]] (*Austral** & **NZ*)
**8** (*botany*) the [[top]] part of a plant, where the leaves or flowers grow in a cluster
**9** a culmination or crisis: the matter came to a head in December 1928
**10** the froth on the [[top]] of a glass of beer
**11** the pus-filled tip of a pimple or boil
**12** part of a computer or tape recorder that can read, write, or erase information
**13** the source of a river or stream
**14** the side of a coin that usually bears a portrait of the head of a monarch, etc.
**15** a headland or promontory: Beachy Head
**16** pressure of water or steam in an enclosed space
**17** *pl* **head** a person or animal considered as a unit: the cost per head of Paris's refuse collection; six hundred head of cattle
**18** a headline or heading
**19** (*informal*) short for **headache**
**20** **give someone his *or* her head** to allow someone greater freedom or responsibility
**21** **go to one's head**
**A** (of an alcoholic drink) to make one slightly drunk
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[go to your head]]**: intoxicate
 **B** to make one conceited: success has gone to his head
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[go to your head]]**: make someone conceited, puff someone up, make someone full of themselves
**22** **[[head over heels]] (in love)** very much (in love)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[head over heels]]**: completely, thoroughly, utterly, intensely, wholeheartedly, uncontrollably
**23** **keep one's head** to remain calm
**24** **not make head nor tail of** not to understand (a problem, etc.)
**25** **off one's head** (*slang*) very foolish or mentally unstable
**26** **on one's own head** at one's own risk
**27** **over someone's head**
**A** to a higher authority: the taboo of going over the head of their immediate [[boss]]
 **B** beyond a person's [[understanding]]
**28** **put our *or* your *or* their heads together** (*informal*) to consult together
**29** **turn someone's head** to make someone conceited
 *vb*
**30** to be at the front or [[top]] of: Barnes headed the list
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lead]], [[precede]], [[be the leader of]], be *or* go first, be *or* go at the front of, [[lead the way]]
**31** to [[be in charge of]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be in charge of]], [[run]], [[manage]], [[lead]], [[control]], [[direct]], [[guide]], [[command]]
**32** (often foll. by *for*) to go or cause to go (towards): to head for the Channel ports
**33** (*soccer*) to propel (the ball) by striking it with the head
**34** to provide with a heading
