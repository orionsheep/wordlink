**judge** *n*
**1** a public official with authority to hear cases and pass sentences in a court of law
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[magistrate]], [[justice]], [[beak]] (*Brit* *slang*), [[His]], Her *or* Your Honour
**2** a person appointed to determine the result of a competition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[referee]], [[expert]], [[specialist]], [[umpire]], [[umpie]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[mediator]], [[examiner]], [[connoisseur]], [[assessor]]
**3** a person whose opinion on a particular subject is usually reliable: a fine judge of character
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[critic]], [[assessor]], arbiter
 *vb* **judging**, **[[judged]]**
**4** to determine the result of (a competition)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[adjudicate]], [[referee]], [[umpire]], [[mediate]], [[officiate]], [[arbitrate]]
**5** to appraise critically: she hopes people judge her on her work rather than her appearance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[evaluate]], [[rate]], [[consider]], [[view]], [[value]], [[esteem]] (*formal*)
**6** to decide (something) after inquiry: we use a means test to judge the most needy cases
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[estimate]], [[guess]], [[assess]], [[calculate]], [[evaluate]], [[gauge]]
**7** to believe or [[consider]]: doctors [[judged]] that the benefits of such treatment outweighed the risk
