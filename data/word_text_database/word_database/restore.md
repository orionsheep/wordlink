**restore** *vb* **-storing**, **-stored**
**1** to [[return]] (something) to its original or former condition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[repair]], [[refurbish]], [[renovate]], [[reconstruct]], [[fix]] (up), [[renew]], [[rebuild]], [[mend]]
**2** to [[bring back]] to health or good spirits
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[revive]], [[build up]], [[strengthen]], [[refresh]], [[revitalize]]
**3** to [[return]] (something lost or stolen) to its owner
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[return]], [[replace]], [[recover]], [[bring back]], [[send back]], [[hand back]]
**4** to re-enforce or [[re-establish]]: he must restore confidence in himself and his government; they worked to restore the monarchy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reinstate]], [[re-establish]], [[reintroduce]]
**5** to [[reconstruct]] (a ruin, extinct animal, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[repair]], [[refurbish]], [[renovate]], [[reconstruct]], [[fix]] (up), [[renew]], [[rebuild]], [[mend]]
