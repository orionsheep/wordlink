**ruffle** *vb* **-fling**, **-fled**
**1** to disturb the smoothness of: the wind was ruffling Dad's hair
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disarrange]], [[disorder]], [[mess up]], [[rumple]], [[tousle]], [[dishevel]], [[muss]] (*US** & **Canad*)
**2** to [[annoy]] or [[irritate]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[annoy]], [[upset]], [[irritate]], [[agitate]], [[nettle]], [[fluster]], [[peeve]] (*informal*)
**3** (of a bird) to erect its feathers in anger or display
**4** to flick cards or pages rapidly
 *n*
**5** a strip of pleated material used as a trim
