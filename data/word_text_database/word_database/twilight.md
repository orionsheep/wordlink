**twilight** *n*
**1** the soft dim light that occurs when the sun is just below the horizon after [[sunset]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[half-light]], [[gloom]], [[dimness]], [[semi-darkness]]
**2** the period in which this light occurs: soon after twilight we started marching again
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dusk]], [[evening]], [[sunset]], [[early evening]], [[nightfall]], [[sundown]], [[gloaming]] (*Scot* *poetic*), [[close of day]], [[evo]] (*Austral* *slang*)
**3** a period in which strength, importance, etc., is gradually declining: the twilight of his political career
 *adj*
**4** of or relating to the period towards the end of the day: the twilight shift
**5** of or being a period of decline: he spent most of his twilight years working on a history of France
**6** denoting irregularity and obscurity: a twilight existence
 **[[twilit]]** *adj*
