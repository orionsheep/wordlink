**marshal** *n*
**1** (in some armies and air forces) an officer of the highest rank: Field Marshall
**2** an officer who organizes or controls ceremonies or public events
**3** (*US*) the chief police or fire officer in some states
**4** (formerly in England) an officer of the royal family or court
 *vb* **-shalling**, **-shalled** (*US*) **-shaling**, **-shaled**
**5** to [[arrange]] in [[order]]: she marshalled her facts and came to a conclusion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arrange]], [[group]], [[order]], [[line up]], [[organize]], [[deploy]], [[array]], [[draw up]]
**6** to assemble and [[organize]] people or vehicles in readiness for onward movement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arrange]], [[group]], [[order]], [[line up]], [[organize]], [[deploy]], [[array]], [[draw up]]
**7** to [[guide]] or [[lead]], esp. in a ceremonious way: she marshalled them towards the lecture theatre
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[conduct]], [[take]], [[lead]], [[guide]], [[steer]], [[escort]], [[shepherd]], [[usher]]
