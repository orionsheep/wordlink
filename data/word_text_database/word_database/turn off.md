**turn off** *vb*
**1** to leave (a road or path): turning off the main road
**2** (of a road or path) to lead away from (another road or path): a main street with alleys twisting and turning off it
**3** to cause (something) to stop operating by turning a knob, pushing a button, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[turn something off]]**: switch off, turn out, put out, stop, cut out, shut down, unplug, flick off
**4** (*informal*) to cause disgust or disinterest in (someone): keeping kids from getting turned off by mathematics
 *n* **turn-off**
**5** a road or other way branching off from the main thoroughfare
**6** (*informal*) a person or thing that causes dislike
