**trip** *n*
**1** a [[journey]] to a place and back, esp. for pleasure: they took a coach trip round the island
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[journey]], [[outing]], [[excursion]], [[day out]], [[run]], [[drive]], [[tour]], [[spin]] (*informal*)
**2** a false step; [[stumble]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stumble]], [[fall]], [[slip]], [[misstep]]
**3** the act of causing someone to [[stumble]] or [[fall]] by catching his or her foot with one's own
**4** (*informal*) a hallucinogenic drug experience
**5** a catch on a mechanism that acts as a switch
 *vb* **tripping**, **tripped**
**6** Also: **trip up** to [[stumble]] or cause (someone) to [[stumble]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stumble]], [[fall]], [[fall over]], [[slip]], [[tumble]], [[topple]], [[stagger]], [[misstep]]
**7** Also: **trip up** to trap or catch (someone) in a mistake
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[trip someone up]]**: catch out, trap, wrongfoot
**8** to walk lightly and quickly, with a dancelike motion: I could see Amelia tripping along beside him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[skip]], [[dance]], [[hop]], [[gambol]]
**9** (*informal*) to experience the effects of a hallucinogenic drug
