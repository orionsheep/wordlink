**surrender** *vb*
**1** to give oneself up physically to an enemy after defeat
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give in]], [[yield]], [[submit]], [[give way]], [[succumb]], [[cave in]] (*informal*), [[capitulate]]
**2** to give (something) up to another, under pressure or on demand: the rebels surrendered their arms
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give up]], [[abandon]], [[relinquish]], [[yield]], [[concede]], [[part with]], [[renounce]], [[waive]]
**3** to give (something) up voluntarily to another: he was surrendering his own chance for the championship
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give up]], [[abandon]], [[relinquish]], [[yield]], [[concede]], [[part with]], [[renounce]], [[waive]]
**4** to [[give in]] to a temptation or an influence
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[give in]], [[yield]], [[submit]], [[give way]], [[succumb]], [[cave in]] (*informal*), [[capitulate]]
 *n*
**5** the act or instance of surrendering
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[submission]], [[cave-in]] (*informal*), [[capitulation]], [[resignation]], [[renunciation]], [[relinquishment]]
