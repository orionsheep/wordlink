**retrieve** *vb* **-trieving**, **-trieved**
**1** to get or fetch back again
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get back]], [[regain]], [[recover]], [[restore]], [[recapture]]
**2** to bring back to a more satisfactory state: his attempt to retrieve the situation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[redeem]], [[save]], [[win back]], [[recoup]]
**3** to rescue or [[save]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[redeem]], [[save]], [[win back]], [[recoup]]
**4** to [[recover]] (stored information) from a computer system
**5** (of dogs) to find and fetch (shot birds and animals)
**6** to remember
 *n*
**7** the chance of being retrieved: beyond retrieve
 **[[retrievable]]** *adj* 
 **[[retrieval]]** *n*
