**slack** *adj*
**1** not tight, tense, or taut: the slack jaw hung open
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[limp]], [[relaxed]], [[loose]], [[lax]]
**2** careless in one's work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[negligent]], [[lazy]], [[lax]], [[idle]], [[inactive]], [[slapdash]], [[neglectful]], [[slipshod]]
**3** (esp. of water) moving slowly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slow]], [[quiet]], [[inactive]], [[dull]], [[sluggish]], [[slow-moving]]
**4** (of trade) not busy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slow]], [[quiet]], [[inactive]], [[dull]], [[sluggish]], [[slow-moving]]
 *n*
**5** a part that is slack or hangs [[loose]]: take up the slack
**6** a period of less busy activity
 *vb*
**7** to neglect one's duty or work in a [[lazy]] manner: stop slacking, you pair!
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shirk]], [[idle]], [[dodge]], [[skive]] (*Brit* *slang*), [[bludge]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*)
**8** (often foll. by *off*) to loosen or slacken
 **[[slackness]]** *n*
