**perfect** *adj*
**1** having all essential elements
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[faultless]], [[correct]], [[pure]], [[impeccable]], [[exemplary]], [[flawless]], [[foolproof]]
**2** [[faultless]]: a perfect circle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[faultless]], [[correct]], [[pure]], [[impeccable]], [[exemplary]], [[flawless]], [[foolproof]]
**3** [[correct]] or [[precise]]: perfect timing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exact]], [[true]], [[accurate]], [[precise]], [[correct]], [[faithful]], [[unerring]]
**4** [[utter]] or [[absolute]]: a perfect stranger
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[complete]], [[absolute]], [[sheer]], [[utter]], [[consummate]], [[unmitigated]]
**5** [[excellent]] in all respects: a perfect day
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[excellent]], [[ideal]], [[supreme]], [[superb]], [[splendid]], [[sublime]], [[superlative]]
**6** (*maths*) exactly divisible into equal integral or polynomial roots: 36 is a perfect square
**7** (*grammar*) denoting a tense of verbs used to describe a completed action
 *n*
**8** (*grammar*) the perfect tense
 *vb*
**9** to [[improve]] to one's satisfaction: he is in Paris to perfect his French
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[improve]], [[develop]], [[polish]], [[refine]]
**10** to make fully accomplished: he perfected the system
 **[[perfectly]]** *adv*
