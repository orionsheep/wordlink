**shed**^2 *vb* **shedding**, **shed**
**1** to get rid of: 250 workers shed by the company
**2** **shed tears** to cry
**3** **shed light on** to make (a problem or situation) easier to understand
**4** to [[cast off]] (skin, hair, or leaves): the trees were already beginning to shed their leaves
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cast off]], [[discard]], [[moult]], [[slough off]]
**5** to cause to flow off: this coat sheds water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]], [[spill]], [[scatter]]
**6** to separate or divide (a group of sheep)
