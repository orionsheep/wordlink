**shoot** *vb* **shooting**, **shot**
**1** to [[hit]], wound, or [[kill]] with a missile fired from a weapon
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[open fire on]], [[blast]] (*slang*), [[hit]], [[kill]], [[plug]] (*slang*), [[bring down]]
**2** to [[fire]] (a missile or missiles) from a weapon
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fire]], [[launch]], [[discharge]], [[project]], [[hurl]], [[fling]], [[propel]], [[emit]]
**3** to [[fire]] (a weapon)
**4** to hunt game with a gun for sport
**5** to send out or be sent out quickly and aggressively: he shot questions at her
**6** to move very rapidly: the car shot forward
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[speed]], [[race]], [[rush]], [[charge]], [[fly]], [[tear]], [[dash]], barrel (along) (*informal*, *chiefly* *US** & **Canad*)
**7** to go or pass quickly over or through: he was trying to shoot the white water
**8** to slide or push into or out of a fastening: she shot the bolt quickly
**9** (of a plant) to [[sprout]] (a new growth)
**10** to photograph or film (a subject, sequence, etc.)
**11** (*sport*) to [[hit]] or kick the ball at goal
 *n*
**12** the act of shooting
**13** a new growth or [[sprout]] of a plant
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sprout]], [[branch]], [[bud]], [[sprig]], [[offshoot]]
**14** (*chiefly* *Brit*) a meeting or party organized for hunting game with guns
**15** an area where game can be hunted with guns
**16** (*informal*) a photographic assignment: a fashion shoot in New York
