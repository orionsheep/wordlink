**sharp** *adj*
**1** having a [[keen]] [[cutting]] edge
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[keen]], [[jagged]], [[serrated]]
**2** tapering to an edge or point
**3** involving a [[sudden]] change in direction: a sharp bend on a road; a sharp rise in prices
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sudden]], [[marked]], [[abrupt]], [[extreme]], [[distinct]]
**4** moving, acting, or reacting quickly: sharp reflexes
**5** clearly defined: a sharp contrast
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clear]], [[distinct]], [[well-defined]], [[crisp]]
**6** [[quick]] to notice or understand things; [[keen]]-witted
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[quick-witted]], [[clever]], [[astute]], [[knowing]], [[quick]], [[bright]], [[alert]], [[penetrating]]
**7** [[clever]] in an underhand way: sharp practices
**8** [[bitter]] or [[harsh]]: a sharp response
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cutting]], [[biting]], [[bitter]], [[harsh]], [[barbed]], [[hurtful]], [[caustic]]
**9** shrill or [[penetrating]]: a sharp cry of horror
**10** having a [[bitter]] or [[sour]] taste
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sour]], [[tart]], [[pungent]], [[hot]], [[acid]], [[acrid]], [[piquant]]
**11** (of pain or cold) [[acute]] or [[biting]]: a sharp gust of wind
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[acute]], [[severe]], [[intense]], [[painful]], [[shooting]], [[stabbing]], [[piercing]], [[gut-wrenching]]
**12** (*music*)
**A** (of a note) raised in pitch by one semitone: F sharp
 **B** (of an instrument or voice) out of tune by being too high in pitch
**13** (*informal*) neat and stylish: a sharp dresser
