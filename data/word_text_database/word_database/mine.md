**mine**^2 *n*
**1** a place where minerals, esp. coal, ores, or precious stones, are dug from the ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pit]], [[deposit]], [[shaft]], [[colliery]], [[excavation]]
**2** a type of bomb placed in water or under the ground, and designed to destroy ships, vehicles, or people passing over or near it
**3** a profitable [[source]] or abundant [[supply]]: a mine of information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[source]], [[store]], [[fund]], [[stock]], [[supply]], [[reserve]], [[treasury]], wealth
 *vb* **mining**, **[[mined]]**
**4** to dig minerals from the ground: lead has been [[mined]] here for over three centuries
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dig up]], [[extract]], [[quarry]], [[unearth]], [[excavate]], [[hew]], [[dig for]]
**5** to dig a hole or tunnel, esp. in order to obtain minerals
**6** to place explosive mines in or on: the retreating troops had [[mined]] the bridge
