**effective** *adj*
**1** producing a desired result: polio's spread was checked by the creation of an effective vaccine in the 1950s
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[efficient]], [[successful]], [[useful]], [[active]], [[capable]], [[valuable]], [[helpful]], [[adequate]]
**2** officially coming into operation: the new rates become effective at the end of May
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[in operation]], [[official]], [[current]], [[legal]], [[active]], [[in effect]], [[valid]], [[operative]]
**3** [[impressive]]: a highly effective speech
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[powerful]], [[strong]], [[convincing]], [[persuasive]], [[telling]], [[impressive]], [[compelling]], [[forceful]]
**4** in reality, although not officially or in theory: he is in effective control of the company
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[virtual]], [[essential]], [[practical]], [[implied]], [[implicit]], [[tacit]], [[unacknowledged]]
 **[[effectively]]** *adv* 
 **[[effectiveness]]** *n*
