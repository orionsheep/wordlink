**full** *adj*
**1** holding as much or as many as possible
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[filled]], [[stocked]], [[brimming]], [[replete]], [[complete]], [[loaded]], [[saturated]]
**2** abundant in supply: full of enthusiasm
**3** having consumed enough food or drink
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[satiated]], [[having had enough]], [[replete]]
**4** (of the face or figure) [[rounded]] or [[plump]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plump]], [[rounded]], [[voluptuous]], [[shapely]], [[well-rounded]], [[buxom]], [[curvaceous]] (*informal*)
**5** [[complete]]: the full amount
**6** with all privileges or rights: full membership
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[comprehensive]], [[complete]], [[exhaustive]], [[all-embracing]]
**7** (*music*) powerful or [[rich]] in volume and sound
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rich]], [[strong]], [[deep]], [[loud]], [[distinct]], [[resonant]], [[sonorous]], [[clear]]
**8** (of a garment) containing a [[large]] amount of fabric
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[voluminous]], [[large]], [[loose]], [[baggy]], [[billowing]], [[puffy]], [[capacious]], [[loose-fitting]]
**9** **full of** engrossed with: she had been full of her own plans lately
**10** **full of oneself** full of pride or conceit
**11** **full up** [[filled]] to capacity
 *adv*
**12** completely or entirely
**13** directly or right: she hit him full in the face
**14** **full well** very or extremely well: we knew full well that she was watching every move we made
 *n*
**15** **in full** without omitting or shortening
**16** **to the full** thoroughly or fully
 **[[fullness]]** * or * (*esp* *US*) **[[fulness]]** *n*
