**expose** *vb* **-posing**, **-posed**
**1** to [[uncover]] (something previously covered)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[uncover]], [[show]], [[reveal]], [[display]], [[exhibit]], [[present]], [[unveil]], [[lay bare]]
**2** to [[reveal]] the truth about (someone or something), esp. when it is shocking or scandalous: an MP whose private life was recently exposed in the press
**3** to leave (a person or thing) unprotected in a potentially harmful situation: workers were exposed to relatively low doses of radiation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[make vulnerable]], [[subject]], [[leave open]], [[lay open]]
**4** **expose someone to** to give someone an introduction to or experience of (something new)
**5** (*photog*) to [[subject]] (a film) to light when using a camera
**6** **expose oneself** to [[display]] one's sexual organs in public
