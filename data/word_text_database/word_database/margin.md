**margin** *n*
**1** an [[edge]], [[rim]], or [[border]]: we came to the margin of the wood; people on the margin of society
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[edge]], [[side]], [[border]], [[boundary]], [[verge]] (*Brit*), [[brink]], [[rim]], [[perimeter]]
**2** the blank space surrounding the text on a page
**3** an additional amount or one beyond the minimum necessary: the margin of victory was seven lengths; a small margin of error
**4** (*chiefly* *Austral*) a payment made in addition to a basic wage, esp. for special skill or responsibility
**5** a limit beyond which something can no longer exist or function: the margin of physical survival
**6** (*econ*) the minimum return below which an enterprise becomes unprofitable
