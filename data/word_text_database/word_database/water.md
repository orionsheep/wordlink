**water** *n*
**1** a clear colourless tasteless [[liquid]] that is essential for plant and animal life, that falls as rain, and forms seas, rivers, and lakes. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVES: </small>**aquatic**, **aqueous**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[liquid]], [[H2O]], [[wai]] (*NZ*)
**2** any area of this [[liquid]], such as a [[sea]], river, or lake
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sea]], [[main]], [[waves]], [[ocean]], [[depths]], [[briny]] (*informal*)
**3** the surface of such an area of water: four-fifths of an iceberg's mass lie below water
**4** the level of the tide: at high water
**5** (*physiol*)
**A** any fluid discharged from the body, such as sweat, urine, or tears
 **B** the fluid surrounding a fetus in the womb
**6** **hold water** (of an argument or idea) to be believable or reasonable
**7** **of the first water** of the highest quality or the most extreme degree: he's a scoundrel of the first water
**8** **pass water** to urinate
**9** **water under the bridge** events that are past and done with
 *vb*
**10** to moisten or [[soak]] with water: keep greenhouse plants well watered
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sprinkle]], [[spray]], [[soak]], [[irrigate]], [[hose]], [[dampen]], [[drench]], [[douse]], [[fertigate]] (*Austral*)
**11** to give (an animal) water to drink
**12** (of the eyes) to fill with tears: our eyes were watering from the fumes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get wet]], [[cry]], [[weep]], [[become wet]], [[exude water]]
**13** (of the mouth) to fill with saliva in anticipation of food
 See also **water down**
 **[[waterless]]** *adj*
