**lookout** *n*
**1** the act of watching for danger or for an opportunity: on the lookout for attack
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[watch]], [[guard]], [[vigil]]
**2** a person or people keeping such a [[watch]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[watchman]], [[guard]], [[sentry]], [[sentinel]]
**3** a viewpoint from which a [[watch]] is kept
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[watchtower]], [[post]], [[observatory]], [[observation post]]
**4** (*informal*) [[worry]] or [[concern]]: that is my lookout rather than theirs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[concern]], [[business]], [[worry]]
**5** (*chiefly* *Brit*) chances or prospect: it's a bad lookout for Europe
 *vb* **look out**
**6** to be careful
**7** to [[watch]] out for: look out particularly for oils that have been flavoured
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[look out for something]]**: be careful of, beware, [[watch]] out for, pay attention to, be wary of, keep an eye out for
**8** to find and take out: little time to look out clothes that she might need
**9** (foll. by *on* or *over*) to face in a particular direction: looking out over the courtyard
