**ease** *n*
**1** lack of difficulty
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[straightforwardness]], [[simplicity]], [[readiness]]
**2** freedom from discomfort or worry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[peace of mind]], [[peace]], [[content]], [[quiet]], [[comfort]], [[happiness]], [[serenity]], [[tranquillity]]
**3** [[rest]], [[leisure]], or [[relaxation]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[comfort]], [[luxury]], [[leisure]], [[relaxation]], [[prosperity]], [[affluence]], [[rest]], [[repose]]
**4** freedom from poverty: a life of [[leisure]] and ease
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[comfort]], [[luxury]], [[leisure]], [[relaxation]], [[prosperity]], [[affluence]], [[rest]], [[repose]]
**5** **at ease**
**A** (*military*) (of a soldier) standing in a relaxed position with the feet apart
 **B** in a relaxed attitude or frame of mind
 *vb* **easing**, **eased**
**6** to make or become less difficult or severe: the pain gradually eased
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[relieve]], [[calm]], [[soothe]], [[lessen]], [[alleviate]], [[lighten]], [[lower]], [[relax]]
**7** to move into or out of a place or situation slowly and carefully
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move carefully]], [[edge]], [[slip]], [[inch]], [[slide]], [[creep]], [[manoeuvre]]
**8** **ease off *or* up** to [[lessen]] or cause to [[lessen]] in severity, pressure, tension, or strain: the rain eased off
