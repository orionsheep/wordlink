**knock** *vb*
**1** to give a [[blow]] or push to
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hit]], [[strike]], [[punch]], [[belt]] (*informal*), [[smack]], [[thump]], [[cuff]]
**2** to [[rap]] sharply with the knuckles: he knocked on the door of the guest room
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[bang]], [[strike]], [[tap]], [[rap]], [[thump]], [[pummel]]
**3** to make by striking: he knocked a hole in the wall
**4** to collide (with)
**5** to bring into a certain condition by hitting: he was knocked unconscious in a collision
**6** (*informal*) to [[criticize]] adversely
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[criticize]], [[condemn]], [[put down]], [[run down]], [[abuse]], [[slate]] (*informal*), [[censure]], [[denigrate]], [[nit-pick]] (*informal*)
**7** to emit a regular banging sound as a result of a fault: the engine was knocking badly
**8** **knock on the head** to prevent the further development of (a plan)
 *n*
**9**
**A** a [[blow]], push, or [[rap]]: he gave the table a knock
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[blow]], [[hit]], [[punch]], [[crack]] (*informal*), [[clip]], [[slap]], [[bash]], [[smack]]
 **B** the sound so caused
**10** the sound of knocking in an engine or bearing
**11** (*informal*) a misfortune, rejection, or [[setback]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[setback]], [[check]], [[defeat]], [[blow]], [[reverse]], [[disappointment]], [[hold-up]], [[hitch]]
**12** (*informal*) criticism
 See also **knock about**, **knock back** etc
