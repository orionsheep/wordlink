**temper** *n*
**1** a sudden outburst of anger: she stormed out in a temper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rage]], [[fury]], [[bad mood]], [[passion]], [[tantrum]], [[foulie]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[hissy fit]] (*informal*), [[strop]] (*Brit* *informal*)
**2** a tendency to have sudden outbursts of anger: you've got a temper all right
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[irritability]], [[irascibility]], [[passion]], [[resentment]], [[petulance]], [[surliness]], [[hot-headedness]]
**3** a mental condition of moderation and calm: he lost his temper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[self-control]], [[composure]], [[cool]] (*slang*), [[calmness]], [[equanimity]]
**4** a person's [[frame of mind]]: he was in a bad temper
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[frame of mind]], [[nature]], [[mind]], [[mood]], [[constitution]], [[humour]], [[temperament]], disposition
 *vb*
**5** to modify so as to make less extreme or more acceptable: past militancy has been tempered with compassion and caring
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[moderate]], [[restrain]], [[tone down]], [[soften]], [[soothe]], [[lessen]], [[mitigate]], [[assuage]]
**6** to reduce the brittleness of (a hardened metal) by reheating it and allowing it to [[cool]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strengthen]], [[harden]], [[toughen]], [[anneal]]
**7** (*music*) to adjust the frequency differences between the notes of a scale on (a keyboard instrument)
