**wear** *vb* **wearing**, **wore**, **worn**
**1** to carry or have (a garment or jewellery) on one's body as clothing or ornament
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be dressed in]], [[have on]], [[sport]] (*informal*), [[put on]]
**2** to have (a particular facial expression): she wore a scowl of frank antagonism
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[show]], [[present]], [[bear]], [[display]], [[assume]], [[put on]], [[exhibit]]
**3** to style (the hair) in a particular way: she wears her hair in a braid
**4** to [[deteriorate]] or cause to [[deteriorate]] by constant use or action
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deteriorate]], [[fray]], [[wear thin]]
**5** (*informal*) to accept: he won't be given a top job – the Party wouldn't wear it
**6** **[[wear thin]]** to lessen or become weaker: his patience began to [[wear thin]]
**7** **wear well** to remain in good condition for a long time
 *n*
**8** [[clothes]] that are suitable for a particular time or purpose: evening wear; beach wear
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[clothes]], [[things]], [[dress]], [[gear]] (*informal*), [[attire]], [[costume]], [[garments]], [[apparel]] (*old-fashioned*)
**9** [[deterioration]] from constant or normal use
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[damage]], [[wear and tear]], [[erosion]], [[deterioration]], [[attrition]], [[corrosion]], [[abrasion]]
**10** the quality of resisting the effects of constant use
 See also **wear off**
