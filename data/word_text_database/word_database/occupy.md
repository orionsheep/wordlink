**occupy** *vb* **-pies**, **-pying**, **-pied**
**1** to live, stay, or work in (a house, flat, or office)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inhabit]], [[own]], [[live in]], [[dwell in]], [[reside in]], [[abide in]]
**2** to keep (someone or someone's mind) busy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[engage]], [[involve]], [[employ]], [[divert]], [[preoccupy]], [[engross]]
**3** to [[take up]] (time or space)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[take up]], [[consume]], [[tie up]], [[use up]], [[monopolize]]
**4** to move in and take [[control]] of (a country or other place): soldiers have occupied the country's television station
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[invade]], [[take over]], [[capture]], [[seize]], [[conquer]], [[overrun]], [[annex]], [[colonize]], [[cybersquat]] (*computers*)
**5** to fill or [[hold]] (a position or office)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hold]], [[control]], [[dominate]], [[possess]]
