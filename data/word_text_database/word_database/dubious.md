**dubious** (**dew**-bee-uss) *adj*
**1** not entirely honest, safe, or reliable: this allegation was at best dubious and at worst an outright fabrication
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suspect]], [[suspicious]], [[crooked]] (*informal*), [[dodgy]] (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *informal*), [[questionable]], [[unreliable]], [[fishy]] (*informal*), [[disreputable]]
**2** [[unsure]] or undecided: she felt dubious about the entire proposition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unsure]], [[uncertain]], [[suspicious]], [[hesitating]], [[doubtful]], [[sceptical]], [[tentative]], [[wavering]]
**3** of [[doubtful]] quality or worth: she had the dubious honour of being taken for his mother
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suspect]], [[suspicious]], [[crooked]] (*informal*), [[dodgy]] (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *informal*), [[questionable]], [[unreliable]], [[fishy]] (*informal*), [[disreputable]]
