**advance** *vb* **-vancing**, **-vanced**
**1** to go or bring forward
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[progress]], [[proceed]], [[come forward]], [[make inroads]], [[make headway]]
**2** to make [[progress]]: this student has advanced in reading and writing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[progress]], [[proceed]], [[come forward]], [[make inroads]], [[make headway]]
**3** to further a cause: an association founded to advance the interests of ex-soldiers
**4** **advance on** to move towards someone in a threatening manner
**5** to [[present]] an idea for consideration
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suggest]], [[offer]], [[present]], [[propose]], [[advocate]], [[submit]], [[prescribe]], [[put forward]]
**6** to [[lend]] a sum of money
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lend]], [[loan]], supply on [[credit]]
 *n*
**7** a forward movement
**8** [[improvement]] or [[progress]]: the greatest advance in modern medicine
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[improvement]], [[development]], [[gain]], [[growth]], [[breakthrough]], [[step]], [[headway]], [[inroads]]
**9** a [[loan]] of money
**10** a payment made before it is legally due
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[down payment]], [[credit]], [[fee]], [[deposit]], [[retainer]], [[prepayment]], [[loan]]
**11** an increase in price: any advance on fifty pounds?
**12** **[[in advance]]** [[beforehand]]: you have to pay [[in advance]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[in advance]]**: [[beforehand]], earlier, ahead, previously
**13** **[[in advance]] of** ahead of in time or [[development]]
 *adj*
**14** done or happening before an event: advance warning
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prior]], [[early]], [[beforehand]]
