**lobby** *n*, *pl* **-bies**
**1** a room or [[corridor]] used as an [[entrance hall]] or [[vestibule]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[corridor]], [[passage]], [[entrance]], [[porch]], [[hallway]], [[foyer]], [[entrance hall]], [[vestibule]]
**2** a [[group]] which attempts to [[influence]] legislators on behalf of a particular interest
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pressure group]], [[group]], [[camp]], [[faction]], [[lobbyists]], [[interest group]], [[special-interest group]], [[ginger group]], [[public-interest group]] (*US** & **Canad*)
**3** (*chiefly* *Brit*) a hall in a legislative building used for meetings between legislators and members of the public
**4** (*chiefly* *Brit*) one of two corridors in a legislative building in which members vote
 *vb* **-bies**, **-bying**, **-bied**
**5** to attempt to [[influence]] (legislators) in the formulation of policy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[campaign]], [[press]], [[pressure]], [[push]], [[influence]], [[promote]], [[urge]], [[persuade]]
