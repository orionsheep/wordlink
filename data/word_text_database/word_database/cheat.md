**cheat** *vb*
**1** to [[defraud]]: he cheated her out of millions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deceive]], [[trick]], [[fool]], [[con]] (*informal*), [[mislead]], [[rip off]] (*slang*), [[fleece]], [[defraud]], [[scam]] (*slang*)
**2** to act dishonestly in order to gain some advantage or profit
**3** **cheat on** (*informal*) to be unfaithful to (one's spouse or lover)
 *n*
**4** a person who cheats
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deceiver]], [[sharper]], [[shark]], [[charlatan]], [[trickster]], conman *or* woman (*informal*), [[con artist]] (*informal*), [[double-crosser]] (*informal*), [[swindler]], [[rorter]] (*Austral* *slang*), [[rogue trader]]
**5** a fraud or deception
