**occlude** *vb* **-cluding**, **-cluded** (*formal*)
**1** to block or stop up (a passage or opening): the arteries are occluded by deposits of plaque
**2** to shut in or out: slowly occluding him from Nash's vision
**3** (*chem*) (of a solid) to absorb and retain (a gas or other substance)
 **[[occlusion]]** *n*
