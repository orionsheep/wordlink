**dip** *vb* **dipping**, **dipped**
**1** to [[plunge]] or be plunged quickly or briefly into a liquid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plunge]], [[immerse]], [[bathe]], [[duck]], [[douse]], [[dunk]]
**2** to put one's hands into something, esp. to obtain an object: she dipped into her handbag looking for change
**3** to [[slope]] downwards
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slope]], [[drop]] (down), [[descend]], [[fall]], [[decline]], [[sink]], [[incline]], [[drop away]]
**4** to switch (car headlights) from the main to the [[lower]] beam
**5** to undergo a slight [[decline]], esp. temporarily: sales dipped in November
**6** to [[immerse]] (farm animals) briefly in a chemical to rid them of insects
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plunge]], [[immerse]], [[bathe]], [[duck]], [[douse]], [[dunk]]
**7** to [[lower]] or be lowered briefly: she dipped her knee in a curtsy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[drop]] (down), [[fall]], [[lower]], [[sink]], [[descend]], subside
 *n*
**8** the act of dipping
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plunge]], [[ducking]], [[soaking]], [[drenching]], [[immersion]], [[douche]]
**9** a brief swim
**10** a liquid chemical in which farm animals are dipped
**11** a [[depression]], esp. in a landscape
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hollow]], [[hole]], [[depression]], [[pit]], [[basin]], [[trough]], [[concavity]]
**12** a momentary sinking down
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[nod]], [[drop]], [[lowering]], [[slump]], [[sag]]
**13** a creamy mixture into which pieces of food are dipped before being eaten
 See also **dip into**
