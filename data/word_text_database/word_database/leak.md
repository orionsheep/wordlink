**leak** *n*
**1**
**A** a [[crack]] or [[hole]] that allows the accidental [[escape]] or entrance of liquid, gas, radiation, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hole]], [[opening]], [[crack]], [[puncture]], [[aperture]], [[chink]], [[crevice]], fissure
 **B** such escaping or entering liquid, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[leakage]], [[discharge]], [[drip]], [[seepage]], [[percolation]]
**2** a [[disclosure]] of secret information
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disclosure]], [[exposé]], [[exposure]], [[admission]], [[revelation]], [[uncovering]], [[betrayal]], [[unearthing]]
**3** the loss of current from an electrical conductor because of faulty insulation
**4** the act or an instance of leaking
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[leakage]], [[discharge]], [[drip]], [[seepage]], [[percolation]]
**5** (*slang*) urination
 *vb*
**6** to enter or [[escape]], or allow to enter or [[escape]], through a [[crack]] or [[hole]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[escape]], [[pass]], [[spill]], [[release]], [[drip]], [[trickle]], [[ooze]], [[seep]]
**7** to make (secret information) public, esp. deliberately
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[disclose]], [[tell]], [[reveal]], [[pass on]], [[give away]], [[make public]], [[divulge]], let slip
 **[[leaky]]** *adj*
