**job** *n*
**1** a person's occupation or paid [[employment]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[position]], [[work]], [[calling]], [[business]], [[field]], [[career]], [[employment]], [[profession]]
**2** a piece of [[work]]; [[task]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[task]], [[duty]], [[work]], [[venture]], [[enterprise]], [[undertaking]], [[assignment]], [[chore]]
**3** the performance of a [[task]]: he made a good job of the repair
**4** (*informal*) a difficult [[task]]: they are having a job to fill his shoes
**5** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ* *informal*) a crime, esp. a robbery
**6** **just the job** (*informal*) exactly what is required
**7** **make the best of a bad job** to cope as well as possible in unsatisfactory circumstances
