**tilt** *vb*
**1** to move into a sloping position with one end or side higher than the other: Dave tilted his chair back on two legs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slant]], [[tip]], [[slope]], [[list]], [[lean]], [[heel]], [[incline]]
**2** to move (part of the body) slightly upwards or to the side: Marie tilted her head back
**3** to become more influenced by a particular idea or group: the party is tilting more and more to the right
**4** to compete against someone in a jousting contest
 *n*
**5** a [[slope]] or [[angle]]: a tilt to one side
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slope]], [[angle]], [[inclination]], [[list]], [[pitch]], [[incline]], [[slant]], [[camber]]
**6** the act of tilting
**7**
**A** a jousting contest, esp. in medieval Europe
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[joust]], [[fight]], [[tournament]], [[lists]], [[combat]], duel
 **B** a thrust with a lance delivered during a medieval [[tournament]]
**8** an attempt to win a contest: a tilt at the world title
**9** **at full tilt** at full speed or force
