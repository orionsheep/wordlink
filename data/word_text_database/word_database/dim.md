**dim** *adj* **dimmer**, **dimmest**
**1** badly lit
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[poorly lit]], [[dark]], [[gloomy]], [[murky]], [[shady]], [[shadowy]], [[dusky]], [[tenebrous]]
**2** not clearly seen; [[faint]]: a dim figure in the doorway
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unclear]], [[obscured]], [[faint]], [[blurred]], [[fuzzy]], [[shadowy]], [[hazy]], [[bleary]]
**3** not seeing clearly: eyes dim with tears
**4** (*informal*) slow to understand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stupid]], [[thick]], [[dull]], [[dense]], [[dumb]] (*informal*), [[daft]] (*informal*), [[dozy]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[obtuse]]
**5** not clear in the mind; obscure: a dim awareness
**6** lacking in brightness or lustre: a dim colour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cloudy]], [[grey]], [[gloomy]], [[dismal]], [[overcast]], [[leaden]]
**7** **take a dim view of** to disapprove of
 *vb* **dimming**, **dimmed**
**8** to become or cause to become dim
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: grow *or* become [[faint]], [[fade]], [[dull]], grow *or* become dim
**9** to cause to seem less bright
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn down]], [[fade]], [[dull]]
**10** (*US** & **Canad*) same as **dip**
 **[[dimly]]** *adv* 
 **[[dimness]]** *n*
