**void** *n*
**1** a feeling or condition of loneliness or deprivation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gap]], [[space]], [[lack]], [[hole]], [[emptiness]]
**2** an empty [[space]] or area
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[emptiness]], [[space]], [[vacuum]], [[oblivion]], [[blankness]], [[nullity]], vacuity
 *adj*
**3** having no official value or authority, because the terms have been broken or have not been fulfilled: the race was declared void and rerun
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[invalid]], [[null and void]], [[inoperative]], [[useless]], [[ineffective]], [[worthless]]
**4** (*old-fashioned*, *or* *literary*) empty: behold, the tomb is void!
**5** **void of** devoid of or without: the fact of being punished becomes void of all moral significance
 *vb*
**6** to make [[ineffective]] or [[invalid]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[invalidate]], [[nullify]], [[cancel]], [[withdraw]], [[reverse]], [[undo]], [[repeal]], [[quash]]
**7** to empty
**8** to discharge the contents of (the bowels or bladder)
