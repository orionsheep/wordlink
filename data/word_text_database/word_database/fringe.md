**fringe** *n*
**1** (*chiefly* *Brit*) hair cut short and hanging over the forehead
**2** an ornamental [[edge]] of hanging threads, tassels, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[border]], [[edging]], [[edge]], [[trimming]], [[hem]], [[frill]], [[flounce]]
**3** an outer [[edge]]: London's southern fringe
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[edge]], [[limits]], [[border]], [[margin]], [[outskirts]], [[perimeter]], [[periphery]], [[borderline]]
**4** the minor and less important parts of an activity or organization: two agents on the fringes of espionage activity
**5** a small group of people within a larger body, but differing from it in certain aims and ideas: the [[radical]] fringe of the Green Party
 *adj*
**6** (of theatre) [[unofficial]] or [[unconventional]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unofficial]], [[alternative]], [[radical]], [[innovative]], [[avant-garde]], [[unconventional]], unorthodox
 *vb* **fringing**, **[[fringed]]**
**7** to form a [[border]] for: sandy paths fringing the water's [[edge]]
**8** to decorate with a fringe: tinsel [[fringed]] the desk
