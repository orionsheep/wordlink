**lapse** *n*
**1** a temporary [[drop]] in standard as a result of forgetfulness or lack of concentration
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mistake]], [[failing]], [[fault]], [[failure]], [[error]], [[slip]], [[negligence]], [[omission]]
**2** a moment or instance of bad behaviour, esp. by someone who is usually well-behaved
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decline]], [[fall]], [[drop]], [[deterioration]]
**3** a period of time sufficient for a change to take place: we returned after a lapse of two years
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[interval]], [[break]], [[gap]], [[pause]], [[interruption]], [[lull]], [[breathing space]], [[intermission]]
**4** a gradual [[decline]] to a lower degree, condition, or state: its lapse from the tradition of Disraeli
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[decline]], [[fall]], [[drop]], [[deterioration]]
**5** (*law*) the loss of some right by neglecting to exercise or renew it
 *vb* **lapsing**, **[[lapsed]]**
**6** to [[drop]] in standard or fail to maintain a standard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slip]], [[fall]], [[decline]], [[sink]], [[drop]], [[slide]], [[deteriorate]], [[degenerate]]
**7** to [[decline]] gradually in status, condition, or degree
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[slip]], [[fall]], [[decline]], [[sink]], [[drop]], [[slide]], [[deteriorate]], [[degenerate]]
**8** to allow to [[end]] or become no longer valid, esp. through [[negligence]]: a bid that [[lapsed]] last July
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[end]], [[stop]], [[run out]], [[expire]], [[terminate]]
**9** (usually foll. by *into*) to drift (into a condition): she appeared to lapse into a brief reverie
**10** (often foll. by *from*) to turn away (from beliefs or standards)
**11** (of time) to [[slip]] away
 **[[lapsed]]** *adj*
