**belt** *n*
**1** a [[band]] of leather or cloth worn around the waist
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[waistband]], [[band]], [[sash]], [[girdle]], [[girth]], [[cummerbund]]
**2** an [[area]] where a specific thing is found; [[zone]]: a belt of high pressure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[zone]], [[area]], [[region]], [[section]], [[district]], [[stretch]], [[strip]], [[layer]]
**3** same as **seat belt**
**4** a [[band]] of flexible material between rotating shafts or pulleys to transfer motion or transmit goods: a [[fan belt]]; a conveyer belt
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[conveyor belt]], [[band]], [[loop]], [[fan belt]], [[drive belt]]
**5** (*informal*) a sharp blow
**6** **below the belt** (*informal*) unscrupulous or cowardly
**7** **tighten one's belt** to reduce expenditure
**8** **under one's belt** as part of one's experience: he had a string of successes under his belt
 *vb*
**9** to fasten with or as if with a belt
**10** to hit with a belt
**11** (*slang*) to give (someone) a sharp blow
**12** (foll. by *along*) (*slang*) to move very fast
