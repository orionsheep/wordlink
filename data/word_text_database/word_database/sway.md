**sway** *vb*
**1** to [[swing]] to and fro: red poppies swayed in the faint breeze
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move from side to side]], [[rock]], [[roll]], [[swing]], [[bend]], [[lean]]
**2** to [[lean]] to one side and then the other: entire rows swayed in time
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move from side to side]], [[rock]], [[roll]], [[swing]], [[bend]], [[lean]]
**3** to be unable to decide between two or more opinions
**4** to [[influence]] (someone) in his or her opinion or judgment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[influence]], [[affect]], [[guide]], [[persuade]], induce
 *n*
**5** [[power]] or [[influence]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[power]], [[control]], [[influence]], [[authority]], [[clout]] (*informal*)
**6** a swinging or leaning movement
**7** **hold sway** to have [[power]] or [[influence]]
