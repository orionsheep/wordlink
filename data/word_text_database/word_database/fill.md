**fill** *vb* (often foll. by *up*)
**1** to make or [[become full]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[top up]], [[fill up]], [[make full]], [[become full]], [[brim over]]
**2** to [[occupy]] the whole of: their supporters filled the entire stand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pack]], [[crowd]], [[squeeze]], [[cram]], [[throng]]
**3** to [[plug]] (a gap or crevice)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plug]], [[close]], [[stop]], [[seal]], [[cork]], [[bung]], [[block up]], [[stop up]]
**4** to meet (a requirement or need) satisfactorily: this book fills a major gap
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fulfil]], [[hold]], [[perform]], [[carry out]], [[occupy]], [[execute]], [[discharge]]
**5** to cover (a page or blank space) with writing or drawing
**6** to [[hold]] and [[perform]] the duties of (an office or position)
**7** to appoint or elect an occupant to (an office or position)
 *n*
**8** **one's fill** sufficient for one's needs or wants
