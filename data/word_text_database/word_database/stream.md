**stream** *n*
**1** a small [[river]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[river]], [[brook]], [[burn]] (*Scot*), [[beck]], [[tributary]], [[bayou]], [[rivulet]]
**2** any steady [[flow]] of water or other liquid
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flow]], [[current]], [[rush]], [[run]], [[course]], [[drift]], [[surge]], [[tide]]
**3** something that resembles a stream in moving continuously in a line or particular direction: the stream of traffic
**4** a fast and continuous [[flow]] of speech: the constant stream of jargon
**5** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ*) a class of schoolchildren grouped together because of similar ability
 *vb*
**6** to [[pour]] in a continuous [[flow]]: rain streamed down her cheeks
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flow]], [[run]], [[pour]], [[issue]], [[flood]], [[spill]], [[cascade]], [[gush]]
**7** (of a crowd of people or traffic or a herd of animals) to move in unbroken succession
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rush]], [[fly]], [[speed]], [[tear]], [[flood]], [[pour]]
**8** to send video or audio material over the internet so that the receiving system can play it almost simultaneously
**9** to float freely or with a waving motion: a flimsy pink dress that streamed out behind her
**10** (*Brit** & **NZ*) to group (schoolchildren) in streams
 **[[streaming]]** *n* 
 **[[streamlet]]** *n*
