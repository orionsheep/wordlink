**float** *vb*
**1** to rest on the surface of a fluid without sinking
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be buoyant]], [[hang]], [[hover]]
**2** to move lightly or freely across a surface or through air or water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[glide]], [[sail]], [[drift]], [[move gently]], [[bob]], [[coast]], [[slide]], [[be carried]]
**3** to move about aimlessly, esp. in the mind: a pleasant image floated into his mind
**4**
**A** to [[launch]] (a commercial enterprise, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[launch]], [[offer]], [[sell]], [[set up]], [[promote]], [[get going]]
 **B** to [[offer]] for sale on the stock market
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[launch]], [[offer]], [[sell]], [[set up]], [[promote]], [[get going]]
**5** (*finance*) to allow (a currency) to fluctuate against other currencies
 *n*
**6** an inflatable object that helps people learning to swim stay afloat
**7** (*angling*) an indicator attached to a baited line that moves when a fish bites
**8** a long rigid boatlike structure, of which there are usually two, attached to an aircraft instead of wheels so that it can land on and take off from water
**9** a decorated lorry that is part of a procession
**10** a small delivery vehicle: a milk float
**11** (*Austral** & **NZ*) a vehicle for transporting horses
**12** a sum of money used to cover small expenses or provide change
**13** the hollow floating ball of a ball cock
