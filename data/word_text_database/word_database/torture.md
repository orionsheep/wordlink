**torture** *vb* **-turing**, **-tured**
**1** to cause (someone) extreme physical pain, esp. to extract information, etc.: suspects were regularly [[tortured]] and murdered by the secret police
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[torment]], [[abuse]], [[persecute]], [[afflict]], [[scourge]], [[molest]], [[crucify]], [[mistreat]]
**2** to cause (someone) mental [[anguish]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[distress]], [[torment]], [[worry]], [[trouble]], [[rack]], [[afflict]], [[harrow]], inflict [[anguish]] on
 *n*
**3** physical or mental [[anguish]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[agony]], [[suffering]], [[anguish]], [[distress]], [[torment]], [[heartbreak]]
**4** the practice of torturing a person
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ill-treatment]], [[abuse]], [[torment]], [[persecution]], [[maltreatment]], [[harsh treatment]]
**5** something which causes great mental [[distress]]: she was going through the torture of a collapsing marriage
