**wall** *n*
**1** a vertical structure made of stone, brick, or wood, with a length and height much greater than its thickness, used to enclose, divide, or support. <small>RELATED ADJECTIVE: </small>**mural**
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[partition]], [[screen]], [[barrier]], [[enclosure]]
**2** anything that suggests a wall in function or effect: a wall of elm trees; a wall of suspicion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[barrier]], [[obstacle]], [[barricade]], [[obstruction]], [[check]], [[bar]], [[fence]], [[impediment]]
**3** (*anatomy*) any lining or membrane that encloses a bodily cavity or structure: cell walls
**4** **drive someone up the wall** (*slang*) to make someone angry or irritated
**5** **go to the wall** (*informal*) to be financially ruined
**6** **have one's back to the wall** (*informal*) to be in a very difficult situation, with no obvious way out of it
 *vb*
**7** to surround or enclose (an area) with a wall
**8** **wall in *or* up** to enclose (someone or something) completely in a room or place
 **[[walled]]** *adj*
