**merit** *n*
**1** [[worth]] or superior [[quality]]; excellence: the film had two sequels, neither of much merit
**2** an admirable or advantageous [[quality]]: the relative merits of film and video as a medium of communication
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[advantage]], [[value]], [[quality]], [[worth]], [[strength]], [[asset]], [[virtue]], [[strong point]]
**3** **have the merit of** to have a positive feature or [[advantage]] that the alternatives do not have: the first version has the merit of being short
**4** **on its merits** on its intrinsic qualities or virtues
 *vb* **-riting**, **-rited**
**5** to [[be worthy of]]; [[deserve]]: the issue merits much fuller discussion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deserve]], [[warrant]], [[be entitled to]], [[earn]], [[have a right to]], [[be worthy of]]
