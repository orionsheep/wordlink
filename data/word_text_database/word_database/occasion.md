**occasion** *n*
**1** a particular [[event]] or the [[time]] at which it happens
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[time]], [[moment]], [[point]], [[stage]], [[instance]], [[juncture]]
**2** a need or [[reason]] to [[do]] or be something: we barely knew him and never had occasion to speak of him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reason]], [[cause]], [[call]], [[ground]](s), [[excuse]], [[incentive]], [[motive]], [[justification]]
**3** a suitable [[time]] or [[opportunity]] to [[do]] something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[opportunity]], [[chance]], [[time]], [[opening]], [[window]]
**4** a special [[event]], [[time]], or [[celebration]]: a wedding day is a truly special occasion
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[function]], [[event]], [[affair]], [[do]] (*informal*), [[happening]], [[experience]], [[gathering]], [[celebration]]
**5** **on occasion** every so often
**6** **rise to the occasion** to meet the special demands of a situation
 *vb*
**7** (*formal*) to [[cause]], esp. incidentally
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cause]], [[produce]], [[lead to]], [[inspire]], [[result in]], [[generate]], [[prompt]], [[provoke]]
