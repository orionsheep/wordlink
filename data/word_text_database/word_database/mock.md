**mock** *vb*
**1** to behave with [[scorn]] or contempt towards a person or thing: her husband mocked her attempts to educate herself
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[laugh at]], [[tease]], [[ridicule]], [[taunt]], [[scorn]], [[sneer]], [[scoff]], [[deride]]
**2** to imitate or mimic, esp. in fun
**3** to defy or frustrate: the team mocked the visitors' attempts to score
 *n*
**4** **mocks** (*informal*) (in England and Wales) school examinations taken as practice before public exams
 *adj*
**5** [[sham]] or [[imitation]]: mock Georgian windows
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[imitation]], [[pretended]], [[artificial]], [[fake]], [[false]], [[dummy]], [[sham]], [[feigned]]
**6** serving as an [[imitation]] or substitute, esp. for practice purposes: a mock battle
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[imitation]], [[pretended]], [[artificial]], [[fake]], [[false]], [[dummy]], [[sham]], [[feigned]]
 See also **mock-up**
 **[[mocking]]** *n*, *adj*
