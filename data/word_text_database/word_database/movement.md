**movement** *n*
**1** the act, process, or an instance of moving
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move]], [[action]], [[motion]], [[manoeuvre]]
**2** the manner of moving: their movement is jerky
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[move]], [[action]], [[motion]], [[manoeuvre]]
**3**
**A** a [[group]] of people with a common ideology
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[group]], [[party]], [[organization]], [[grouping]], [[front]], faction
 **B** the organized [[action]] and campaigning of such a [[group]]: a successful movement to abolish child labour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: campaign, [[drive]], [[push]] (*informal*), [[crusade]]
**4** a trend or tendency: a movement towards shorter working hours
**5** (*finance*) a [[change]] in the price or value of shares, a currency, etc.: adverse currency movements
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[development]], [[change]], [[variation]], [[fluctuation]]
**6** (*music*) a principal self-contained [[section]] of a large-scale work, such as a symphony
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[section]], [[part]], [[division]], [[passage]]
**7** **movements** a person's location and activities during a specific time: police were trying to piece together the recent movements of the two men
**8**
**A** the evacuation of the bowels
 **B** the matter evacuated
**9** the mechanism which drives and regulates a watch or clock
