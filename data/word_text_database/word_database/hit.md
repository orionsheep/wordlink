**hit** *vb* **hitting**, **hit**
**1** to [[strike]] or [[touch]] (a person or thing) forcefully
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strike]], [[beat]], [[knock]], [[bang]], [[slap]], [[smack]], [[thump]], [[clout]] (*informal*)
**2** to come into violent contact with: a helicopter hit a Volvo
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[collide with]], [[run into]], [[bump into]], [[clash with]], [[smash into]], [[crash against]], [[bang into]]
**3** to propel (a ball) by striking
**4** (*cricket*) to score (runs)
**5** to [[affect]] (a person, place, or thing) badly: the airline says that its revenue will be hit
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[affect]], [[damage]], [[harm]], [[ruin]], [[devastate]], [[overwhelm]], [[touch]], [[impact on]]
**6** to [[reach]] (a point or place): the city's crime level hit new heights
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[reach]], [[gain]], [[achieve]], [[arrive at]], [[accomplish]], [[attain]]
**7** **hit the bottle** (*slang*) to start drinking excessive amounts of alcohol
**8** **hit the road** (*informal*) to set out on a journey
 *n*
**9** an impact or collision
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shot]], [[blow]]
**10** a [[shot]] or [[blow]] that reaches its target
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[blow]], [[knock]], [[stroke]], [[belt]] (*informal*), [[rap]], [[slap]], [[smack]], [[clout]] (*informal*)
**11** (*informal*) a person or thing that gains wide appeal: those early collections made her a hit with the club set
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[success]], [[winner]], [[triumph]], [[smash]] (*informal*), [[sensation]]
**12** a single visit to a website: over 500,000 hits a day to its site
 See also **hit on**
