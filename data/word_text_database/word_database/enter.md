**enter** *vb*
**1** to come or go into (a particular place): he entered the room
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: come *or* go in *or* into, [[arrive]], [[set foot in somewhere]], [[cross the threshold of somewhere]], [[make an entrance]]
**2** to [[join]] (a party or organization)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[join]], [[start work at]], [[begin work at]], [[enrol in]], [[enlist in]]
**3** to become involved in or take part in: 1500 schools entered the competition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[participate in]], [[join]] (in), [[be involved in]], [[get involved in]], [[play a part in]], [[partake in]], [[associate yourself with]], [[start to be in]]
**4** to become suddenly present or noticeable in: a [[note]] of anxiety entered his voice
**5** to [[record]] (an item) in a journal or [[list]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[record]], [[note]], [[register]], [[log]], [[list]], [[write down]], [[take down]], [[inscribe]]
**6** (*theatre*) to come on stage: used as a stage direction: enter Joseph
**7** to [[begin]] (a new process or period of time): the occupation of the square has entered its eleventh day
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[begin]], [[start]], [[take up]], [[move into]], [[commence]], [[set out on]], [[embark upon]]
