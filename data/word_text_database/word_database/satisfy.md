**satisfy** *vb* **-fies**, **-fying**, **-fied**
**1** to [[fulfil]] the desires or needs of (a person): his [[answer]] didn't satisfy me
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[content]], [[please]], [[indulge]], [[gratify]], [[pander to]], [[assuage]], [[pacify]], [[quench]]
**2** to provide sufficiently for (a need or desire): to satisfy public demand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[comply with]], [[meet]], [[fulfil]], [[answer]], [[serve]], [[fill]], [[observe]], [[obey]]
**3** to [[convince]]: that trip did seem to satisfy her that he was dead
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[convince]], [[persuade]], [[assure]], [[reassure]]
**4** to [[fulfil]] the requirements of: unable to satisfy the conditions set by the commission
 **[[satisfiable]]** *adj* 
 **[[satisfying]]** *adj*
