**reject** *vb*
**1** to [[refuse]] to accept, use, or believe
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[deny]], [[exclude]], [[veto]], [[relinquish]], [[renounce]], [[disallow]], [[forsake]], [[disown]]
**2** to [[deny]] to (a person) the feelings hoped for: the boy had been rejected by his mother
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rebuff]], [[jilt]], [[turn down]], [[spurn]], [[refuse]], [[say no to]], [[repulse]], [[unfollow]], [[unfriend]]
**3** to pass over or throw out as useless
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[discard]], [[decline]], [[eliminate]], [[scrap]], [[jettison]], throw away *or* out
**4** (of an organism) to fail to accept (a tissue graft or organ transplant)
 *n*
**5** a person or thing rejected as not up to standard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[failure]], [[loser]], [[flop]] (*informal*) 
 **[[rejection]]** *n*
