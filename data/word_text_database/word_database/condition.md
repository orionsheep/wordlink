**condition** *n*
**1** a particular [[state]] of being: the human condition; the van is in very poor condition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[state]], [[order]], [[shape]], [[nick]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[trim]]
**2** **conditions** [[circumstances]]: worsening weather conditions; the government pledged to improve living and working conditions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[circumstances]], [[situation]], [[environment]], [[surroundings]], [[way of life]], [[milieu]]
**3** a necessary [[requirement]] for something else to happen: food is a necessary condition for survival
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[requirement]], [[terms]], [[rider]], [[restriction]], [[qualification]], [[limitation]], [[prerequisite]], [[proviso]]
**4** a [[restriction]] or a [[qualification]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[requirement]], [[terms]], [[rider]], [[restriction]], [[qualification]], [[limitation]], [[prerequisite]], [[proviso]]
**5** a term of an agreement: the conditions of the lease are set out
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[requirement]], [[terms]], [[rider]], [[restriction]], [[qualification]], [[limitation]], [[prerequisite]], [[proviso]]
**6** [[state]] of physical [[fitness]], esp. good [[health]]: she is in a serious condition in hospital; out of condition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[health]], [[shape]], [[fitness]], [[trim]], [[form]], [[kilter]], [[state of health]], [[fettle]]
**7** an [[ailment]]: a heart condition
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ailment]], [[problem]], [[complaint]], [[weakness]], [[malady]], [[infirmity]]
**8** **on condition that** provided that
 *vb*
**9** to [[accustom]] or alter the reaction of (a person or animal) to a particular stimulus or [[situation]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[train]], [[teach]], [[adapt]], [[accustom]]
**10** to treat with a conditioner
**11** to make fit or healthy
**12** to influence or determine the [[form]] that something takes: he argued that the failure of Latin American industry was conditioned by international economic structures
 **[[conditioning]]** *n*, *adj*
