**repulse** *vb* **-pulsing**, **-pulsed**
**1** to be disgusting to: this act of feminist rage repulsed as many as it delighted
**2** to drive (an army) back
**3** to reject with coldness or discourtesy: she repulsed his advances
 *n*
**4** a driving back
