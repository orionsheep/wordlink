**toss** *vb*
**1** to [[throw]] (something) lightly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw]], [[pitch]], [[hurl]], [[fling]], [[launch]], [[cast]], [[flip]], [[sling]] (*informal*)
**2** to [[fling]] or be flung about, esp. in a violent way: the salty sea breeze tossing the branches of the palms
**3** to coat (food) with a dressing by gentle stirring or mixing: her technique for tossing Caesar salad
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[shake]]
**4** (of a horse) to [[throw]] (its rider)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw]], [[pitch]], [[lob]] (*informal*)
**5** to move (one's head) suddenly backwards, as in impatience
**6** to [[throw]] up (a coin) to decide between alternatives by guessing which side will land uppermost
**7** **toss and turn** to be restless when trying to sleep
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thrash]] (about), [[twitch]], [[wriggle]], [[squirm]], writhe
 *n*
**8** the act or an instance of tossing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw]], [[pitch]], [[lob]] (*informal*)
**9** the act of deciding between alternatives by throwing up a coin and guessing which side will land uppermost: Essex won the toss and decided to bat first
**10** **argue the toss** to waste time and energy arguing about an unimportant point
**11** **not give a toss** (*informal*) not to care at all
