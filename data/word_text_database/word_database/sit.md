**sit** *vb* **sitting**, **sat**
**1** to [[rest]] one's body upright on the buttocks: she had to sit on the ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[take a seat]], [[perch]], [[settle down]]
**2** to cause (someone) to [[rest]] in such a [[position]]: they sat their grandfather in the shade
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[place]], [[set]], [[put]], [[position]], [[rest]], [[lay]], [[settle]], [[deposit]]
**3** (of an animal) to [[rest]] with the rear part of its body lowered to the ground
**4** (of a bird) to [[perch]] or roost
**5** **sit on** (of a bird) to cover its eggs so as to hatch them
**6** to be located: the bank sits in the middle of the village
**7** to pose for a painting or photograph
**8** to occupy a seat in some official capacity: no police representatives will sit on the committee
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be a member of]], [[serve on]], [[have a seat on]], [[preside on]]
**9** (of a parliament or court) to be in session
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[convene]], [[meet]], [[assemble]], [[officiate]]
**10** to remain unused: his car sat in the garage
**11** (of clothes) to fit or hang in a certain way: that dress sits well on you
**12** to take (an examination): he's sitting his finals
**13** (*in combination*) to look after a specified person or thing for someone else: is someone going to dog-sit for you?
**14** **sit for** (*chiefly* *Brit*) to be a candidate for (a qualification): he sat for a degree in medicine
**15** **sit tight** (*informal*)
**A** to wait patiently
 **B** to maintain one's [[position]] firmly
