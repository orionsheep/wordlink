**pluck** *vb*
**1** to pull or [[pick]] off
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tug]], [[catch]], [[snatch]], [[clutch]], [[jerk]], [[yank]], [[tweak]], [[pull at]]
**2** to pull out the feathers of (a bird for cooking)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: pull out *or* off, [[pick]], [[draw]], [[collect]], [[gather]], [[harvest]]
**3** (foll. by *off* or *away* etc) to pull (something) forcibly or violently (from something or someone)
**4** to sound the strings of (a musical instrument) with the fingers or a plectrum
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tug]], [[catch]], [[snatch]], [[clutch]], [[jerk]], [[yank]], [[tweak]], [[pull at]]
 *n*
**5** [[courage]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[courage]], [[nerve]], [[bottle]] (*Brit* *slang*), [[guts]] (*informal*), [[grit]], [[bravery]], [[backbone]], [[boldness]]
**6** a pull or [[tug]]
 See also **pluck up**
