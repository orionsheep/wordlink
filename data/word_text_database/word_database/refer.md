**refer** *vb* **-ferring**, **-ferred**
 **refer to**
**1** to mention or allude to
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[refer to something or someone]]**: allude to, mention, cite, speak of, bring up
**2** to be relevant or relate (to): the word cancer refers to many quite specific different diseases
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[refer to something or someone]]**: relate to, concern, apply to, pertain to, be relevant to
**3** to seek information (from): he referred to his notes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[refer to something or someone]]**: consult, go, apply, turn to, look up
**4** to [[direct]] the attention of (someone) for information: the reader is referred to the introduction
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[direct]], [[point]], [[send]], [[guide]]
**5** to [[direct]] (a patient or client) to another doctor or agency: her GP referred her to a specialist
**6** to hand over for consideration or decision: to refer a complaint to another department
