**drift** *vb*
**1** to be carried along by currents of air or water
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[float]], [[go]] (aimlessly), [[bob]], [[coast]], [[slip]], [[sail]], [[slide]], [[glide]]
**2** to move aimlessly from one place or activity to another
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wander]], [[stroll]], [[stray]], [[roam]], [[meander]], [[rove]], [[range]]
**3** to [[wander]] away from a fixed course or [[point]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stray]], [[wander]], [[digress]], [[get off the point]]
**4** (of snow) to [[pile up]] in heaps
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pile up]], [[gather]], [[accumulate]], [[amass]], [[bank]] up
 *n*
**5** something piled up by the wind or current, as a snowdrift
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pile]], [[bank]], [[mass]], [[heap]], [[mound]], [[accumulation]]
**6** a general movement or development: there has been a drift away from family control
**7** the main [[point]] of an argument or speech: I was beginning to get his drift
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[meaning]], [[point]], [[gist]], [[direction]], [[import]], [[intention]], [[tendency]], [[significance]]
**8** the extent to which a vessel or aircraft is driven off course by winds, etc.
**9** a current of water created by the wind
