**plant** *n*
**1** a living organism that grows in the ground and lacks the power of movement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flower]], [[bush]], [[vegetable]], [[herb]], [[weed]], [[shrub]]
**2** the land, building, and [[equipment]] used in an industry or business
**3** a [[factory]] or workshop
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[factory]], [[works]], [[shop]], [[yard]], [[mill]], [[foundry]]
**4** mobile mechanical [[equipment]] for construction or road-making
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[machinery]], [[equipment]], [[gear]], [[apparatus]]
**5** (*informal*) a thing positioned secretly for discovery by someone else, often in order to incriminate an innocent person
 *vb*
**6** to [[set]] (seeds or crops) into the ground to grow: it's the wrong time of year for planting roses
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sow]], [[scatter]], [[transplant]], [[implant]], [[put in the ground]]
**7** to [[place]] firmly in position: I planted my chair beside hers
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[place]], [[put]], [[set]], [[fix]]
**8** to introduce into someone's mind: once Wendy had planted the idea in the minds of the owners, they quite fancied selling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[place]], [[put]], [[establish]], [[found]], [[fix]], [[insert]]
**9** (*slang*) to deliver (a blow or kiss)
**10** (*informal*) to position or [[hide]] (someone) in order to deceive or observe
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hide]], [[put]], [[place]], [[conceal]]
**11** (*informal*) to [[hide]] or secrete (something), usually for some illegal purpose or in order to incriminate someone
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hide]], [[put]], [[place]], [[conceal]]
