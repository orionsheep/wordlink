**wait** *vb*
**1** to [[stay]] in one place or [[remain]] inactive in expectation of something: the delegates have to wait for a reply
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stay]], [[remain]], [[stop]], [[pause]], [[rest]], [[linger]], [[loiter]], [[tarry]]
**2** to be temporarily delayed: the celebrations can wait
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be postponed]], [[be suspended]], [[be delayed]], [[be put off]], [[be put back]], [[be deferred]], [[be put on hold]] (*informal*), [[be shelved]]
**3** (of a thing) to be ready or be in store: waiting for her on the library table was the latest Jilly Cooper novel
 *n*
**4** the act or a period of waiting
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[delay]], [[gap]], [[pause]], [[interval]], [[stay]], [[rest]], [[halt]], [[hold-up]]
**5** **lie in wait for**
**A** to prepare an ambush for
 **B** to be ready or be in store for
