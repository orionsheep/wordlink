**express** *vb*
**1** to [[state]] (an idea or feeling) in words; utter: two record labels have expressed an interest in signing the band
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[state]], [[communicate]], [[convey]], [[articulate]], [[say]], [[word]], [[voice]], [[declare]]
**2** to [[show]] (an idea or feeling): his body and demeanour expressed distrust
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[show]], [[indicate]], [[exhibit]], [[demonstrate]], [[reveal]], [[intimate]], [[convey]], [[signify]]
**3** to [[indicate]] through a symbol or formula
**4** to squeeze out (juice, etc.)
**5** **express oneself** to [[communicate]] one's thoughts or ideas
 *adj*
**6** explicitly stated
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[explicit]], [[clear]], [[plain]], [[distinct]], [[definite]], [[unambiguous]], [[categorical]]
**7** deliberate and [[specific]]: she came with the express purpose of causing a row
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[specific]], [[exclusive]], [[particular]], [[sole]], [[special]], [[singular]], [[clear-cut]], [[especial]]
**8** of or for [[rapid]] transportation of people, mail, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fast]], [[direct]], [[rapid]], [[priority]], [[prompt]], [[swift]], [[high-speed]], speedy
 *n*
**9** a [[fast]] train stopping at only a few stations
**10** (*chiefly* *US** & **Canad*) a system for sending mail rapidly
 *adv*
**11** using a system for [[rapid]] transportation of people, mail, etc.: please send this letter express: it's very urgent!
 **[[expressible]]** *adj*
