**rise** *vb* **rising**, **rose**, **risen**
**1** to [[get up]] from a lying, sitting, or kneeling position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get up]], [[stand up]], [[get to your feet]]
**2** to get out of bed, esp. to begin one's day: she rises at 5 am every day to look after her horse
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[arise]] (*old-fashioned*)
**3** to move from a lower to a higher position or place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go up]], [[climb]], [[ascend]]
**4** to appear above the horizon: as the sun rises higher the mist disappears
**5** to [[slope upwards]]: the road crossed the valley then rose to a low ridge
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get steeper]], [[ascend]], [[go uphill]], [[slope upwards]]
**6** to [[increase]] in height or level: the tide rose
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[mount]]
**7** to swell up: dough rises
**8** to [[increase]] in strength or degree: frustration is rising amongst sections of the population
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grow]], [[go up]], [[intensify]]
**9** to [[increase]] in amount or value: living costs are rising at an annual rate of nine per cent
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[mount]]
**10** (*informal*) to respond (to a challenge or remark)
**11** to [[revolt]]: the people rose against their oppressors
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[rebel]], [[revolt]], [[mutiny]]
**12** (of a court or parliament) to adjourn
**13** to be resurrected
**14** to become erect or rigid: the hairs on his neck rose in fear
**15** to originate: that river rises in the mountains
**16** (*angling*) (of fish) to come to the surface of the water
 *n*
**17** the act or an instance of rising
**18** a piece of rising ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[upward slope]], [[incline]], [[elevation]], [[ascent]], kopje *or* koppie (*S African*)
**19** an [[increase]] in wages
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[pay increase]], [[raise]] (*US*), [[increment]]
**20** an [[increase]] in amount, cost, or quantity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[upturn]], [[upswing]], [[upsurge]], [[bounce]]
**21** an [[increase]] in height
**22** an [[increase]] in status or position
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[advancement]], [[progress]], [[climb]], [[promotion]]
**23** an [[increase]] in degree or intensity
**24** the vertical height of a step or of a flight of stairs
**25** **get *or* take a rise out of** (*slang*) to provoke an angry reaction from
**26** **give rise to** to cause the development of
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[give rise to something]]**: cause, produce, effect, result in, bring about
