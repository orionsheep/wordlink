**tool** *n*
**1**
**A** an [[implement]], such as a hammer, saw, or spade, that is used by hand to help do a particular type of work
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[implement]], [[device]], [[appliance]], [[machine]], [[instrument]], [[gadget]], [[utensil]], [[contraption]] (*informal*)
 **B** a power-driven [[instrument]]: [[machine]] tool
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[implement]], [[device]], [[appliance]], [[machine]], [[instrument]], [[gadget]], [[utensil]], [[contraption]] (*informal*)
**2** the cutting part of such an [[instrument]]
**3** a person used to perform dishonourable or unpleasant tasks for another: the government is acting as a tool of big business
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[puppet]], [[creature]], [[pawn]], [[stooge]] (*slang*), [[minion]], [[lackey]], [[flunkey]], [[hireling]]
**4** any object, skill, etc., used for a particular task or in a particular job: a skilled therapist can use photographs as tools
 *vb*
**5** to work, cut, or form (something) with a tool
