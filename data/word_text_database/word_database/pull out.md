**pull out** *vb*
**1**
**A** (of a motor vehicle) to draw away from the side of the road
 **B** (of a motor vehicle) to move out from behind another vehicle to overtake
**2** to depart: the train pulled out of the station
**3** to withdraw: several companies have pulled out of the student market
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **pull out (of)**: withdraw, quit
**4** to remove by pulling
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[pull something out]]**: produce, draw, bring out, draw out
**5** to abandon a situation
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **pull out (of)**: leave, abandon, get out, quit, retreat from, depart (*US*), evacuate
