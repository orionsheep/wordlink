**prevail** *vb*
**1** (often foll. by *over* or *against*) to prove superior; gain mastery: moderate nationalists have until now prevailed over the radicals
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[win]], [[succeed]], [[triumph]], [[overcome]], [[overrule]], [[be victorious]]
**2** to be the most important feature: a casual good-natured mood prevailed
**3** to be generally established: this attitude has prevailed for many years
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be widespread]], [[abound]], [[predominate]], [[be current]], [[be prevalent]], [[exist generally]]
**4** **prevail on *or* upon** to [[succeed]] in persuading: he had easily been prevailed upon to accept a lift
