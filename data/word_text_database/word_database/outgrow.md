**outgrow** *vb* **-growing**, **-grew**, **-grown**
**1** to grow too large for (clothes or shoes): it's amazing how quickly children outgrow their clothes
**2** to lose (a way of behaving or thinking) in the course of becoming more mature: most teenagers outgrow their moodiness as they near adulthood
**3** to grow larger or faster than (someone or something): the weeds threatened to outgrow and choke the rice plants
