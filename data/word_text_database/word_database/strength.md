**strength** *n*
**1** the state or quality of being physically or mentally strong
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[might]], [[muscle]], [[brawn]]
**2** the ability to withstand great [[force]], stress, or pressure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[toughness]], [[soundness]], [[robustness]], [[sturdiness]]
**3** something regarded as valuable or a source of [[power]]: his chief strength is rocketry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[strong point]], [[skill]], [[asset]], [[advantage]], [[talent]], [[forte]], [[speciality]]
**4** [[potency]] or [[effectiveness]], such as of a drink or drug
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[potency]], [[effectiveness]], [[efficacy]]
**5** [[power]] to convince: the strength of this argument
**6** degree of [[intensity]] or concentration of colour, light, sound, or flavour: a medium-strength cheese
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[force]], [[power]], [[intensity]]
**7** the total number of people in a group: at full strength; 50 000 men below strength
**8** **go from strength to strength** to have ever-increasing success
**9** **on the strength of** on the basis of or relying upon
