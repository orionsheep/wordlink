**reinforce** *vb* **-inforcing**, **-inforced**
**1** to give added emphasis to (an idea or feeling): his tired face reinforced his own weariness
**2** to make physically stronger or harder: the plastic panels were reinforced with carbon fibre
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[support]], [[strengthen]], [[fortify]], [[toughen]], [[stress]], [[prop]], [[supplement]], [[emphasize]]
**3** to give added [[support]] to (a military force) by providing more men or equipment: the army garrison had been reinforced with helicopters
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[increase]], [[extend]], [[add to]], [[strengthen]], [[supplement]]
 **[[reinforcement]]** *n*
