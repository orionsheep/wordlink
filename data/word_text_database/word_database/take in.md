**take in** *vb*
**1** to understand: I was too tired to take in all of what was being said
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[take something in]]**: understand, absorb, grasp, digest, comprehend, assimilate, get the hang of (*informal*)
**2** (*informal*) to cheat or deceive: don't be taken in by his charming manner
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[take someone in]]**: deceive, fool, con (*informal*), trick, cheat, mislead, dupe, swindle, scam (*slang*)
**3** to include: this tour takes in the romance and history of Salzburg, Vienna, and Munich
**4** to receive into one's house: his widowed mother lived by taking in boarders
**5** to make (clothing) smaller by altering the seams
**6** to go to: taking in a movie
