**tone** *n*
**1** sound with reference to its pitch, [[timbre]], or [[volume]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[volume]], [[timbre]]
**2** (*US** & **Canad*) same as **note**
**3** (*music*) an interval of two semitones, such as that between doh and ray in tonic sol-fa
**4** the quality or [[character]] of a sound: her tone was angry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[volume]], [[timbre]]
**5** general aspect, quality, or [[style]]: the tone of the conversation made him queasy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[character]], [[style]], [[feel]], [[air]], [[spirit]], [[attitude]], [[manner]], [[mood]]
**6** high quality or [[style]]: my car with its patches of rust lowered the tone of the neighbourhood
**7** the quality of a given [[colour]], as modified by mixture with white or black; [[shade]] or [[tint]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[colour]], [[shade]], [[tint]], [[tinge]], [[hue]]
**8** (*physiol*) the natural firmness of the tissues and normal functioning of bodily organs in health
 *vb* **toning**, **toned**
**9** to be of a matching or similar tone
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harmonize]], [[match]], [[blend]], [[suit]], [[go well with]]
**10** to give a tone to or correct the tone of
 **[[toneless]]** *adj* 
 **[[tonelessly]]** *adv*
