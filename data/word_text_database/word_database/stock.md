**stock** *n*
**1** the total amount of [[goods]] kept on the premises of a shop or business
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[goods]], [[merchandise]], [[wares]], [[range]], [[choice]], [[variety]], [[selection]], [[commodities]]
**2** a [[supply]] of something stored for future use
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[supply]], [[store]], [[reserve]], [[fund]], [[stockpile]], [[hoard]]
**3** (*finance*)
**A** the money raised by a company through selling shares entitling their holders to dividends, partial ownership, and usually voting rights
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[property]], [[capital]], [[assets]], funds
 **B** the proportion of this money held by an individual shareholder
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: shares, [[holdings]], [[securities]], [[investments]], [[bonds]], [[equities]]
 **C** the shares of a specified company or industry
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: shares, [[holdings]], [[securities]], [[investments]], [[bonds]], [[equities]]
**4** farm animals bred and kept for their meat, skins, etc.
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[livestock]], [[cattle]], [[beasts]], [[domestic animals]]
**5** the original type from which a particular race, family, or group is descended
**6** the [[handle]] of a rifle, held by the firer against the shoulder
**7** a liquid produced by simmering meat, fish, bones, or vegetables, and used to make soups and sauces
**8** a kind of plant grown for its brightly coloured flowers
**9** (*old-fashioned*) the degree of status a person has
**10** See **laughing stock**
**11** **in stock** stored on the premises or available for sale or use
**12** **out of stock** not immediately available for sale or use
**13** **take stock** to think carefully about a situation before making a decision
 *adj*
**14** staple; standard: stock sizes in clothes
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[regular]], [[usual]], [[ordinary]], [[conventional]], [[customary]]
**15** being a cliché; [[hackneyed]]: the stock answer
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hackneyed]], [[routine]], [[banal]], [[trite]], overused
 *vb*
**16** to [[keep]] ([[goods]]) for sale
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sell]], [[supply]], [[handle]], [[keep]], [[trade in]], [[deal in]]
**17** to obtain a [[store]] of (something) for future use or sale: to stock up on food
**18** to [[supply]] (a farm) with animals or (a lake or stream) with fish
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fill]], [[supply]], [[provide with]], [[equip]], [[furnish]], [[fit out]]
