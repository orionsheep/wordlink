**exit** *n*
**1** a [[way out]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[way out]], [[door]], [[gate]], [[outlet]], [[doorway]], [[gateway]], [[escape route]]
**2** the act of [[going]] out
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[departure]], [[withdrawal]], [[retreat]], [[farewell]], [[going]], [[goodbye]], [[exodus]], [[decamping]]
**3** (*theatre*) the act of [[going]] offstage
**4** (*Brit** & **Austral*) a point at which vehicles may [[leave]] a motorway
 *vb* **exiting**, **exited**
**5** to [[go away]] or out; [[depart]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[depart]], [[leave]], [[go out]], [[withdraw]], [[retire]], [[quit]], [[retreat]], [[go away]]
**6** (*theatre*) he or she goes offstage: used as a stage direction: exit bleeding from the room
