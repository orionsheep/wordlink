**standard** *n*
**1** a [[level]] of quality: cuisine of a high standard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[level]], [[grade]]
**2** an [[accepted]] [[example]] of something against which others are judged or measured: the work was good by any standard
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[criterion]], [[measure]], [[guideline]], [[example]], [[model]], [[average]], [[norm]], [[gauge]]
**3** a moral principle of behaviour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[principles]], [[ideals]], [[morals]], [[ethics]]
**4** a [[flag]] of a nation or cause
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[flag]], [[banner]], [[ensign]]
**5** an upright pole or beam used as a support: a lamp standard
**6** a song that has remained popular for many years
 *adj*
**7** of a [[usual]], medium, or [[accepted]] kind: a standard cost
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[usual]], [[normal]], [[customary]], [[average]], [[basic]], [[regular]], [[typical]], [[orthodox]]
**8** of [[recognized]] authority: a standard reference book
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[accepted]], [[official]], [[established]], [[approved]], [[recognized]], [[definitive]], [[authoritative]]
**9** denoting pronunciations or grammar regarded as correct and acceptable by educated native speakers
