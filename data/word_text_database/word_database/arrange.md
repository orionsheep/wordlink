**arrange** *vb* **-ranging**, **-ranged**
**1** to [[plan]] in advance: my parents had arranged a surprise party
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[plan]], [[agree]], [[prepare]], [[determine]], [[organize]], [[construct]], [[devise]], [[contrive]], [[jack up]] (*NZ* *informal*)
**2** to arrive at an agreement: they had arranged to go to the cinema
**3** to put into a proper or systematic [[order]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put in order]], [[group]], [[order]], [[sort]], [[position]], [[line up]], [[organize]], [[classify]], [[jack up]] (*NZ* *informal*)
**4** to [[adapt]] (a musical composition) for performance in a certain way
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[adapt]], [[score]], [[orchestrate]], [[harmonize]], [[instrument]]
