**reflect** *vb*
**1** (of a surface or object) to [[throw back]] (light, heat, or sound)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw back]], [[return]], [[mirror]], [[echo]], [[reproduce]]
**2** (of a [[mirror]]) to form an image of (something) by reflection
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[throw back]], [[return]], [[mirror]], [[echo]], [[reproduce]]
**3** to [[show]]: many of her books reflect her obsession with fine art
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[show]], [[reveal]], [[display]], [[indicate]], [[demonstrate]], [[manifest]]
**4** to [[consider]] carefully
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[consider]], [[think]], [[muse]], [[ponder]], [[meditate]], [[ruminate]], [[cogitate]], [[wonder]]
**5** **reflect on *or* upon** to cause to be regarded in a specified way: the incident reflects very badly on me
**6** to bring as a consequence: the programme reflected great credit on the technicians
