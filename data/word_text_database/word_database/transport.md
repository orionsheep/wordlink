**transport** *vb*
**1** to [[carry]] or [[move]] (people or goods) from one place to another, esp. over some distance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[convey]], [[take]], [[move]], [[bring]], [[send]], [[carry]], [[bear]], [[transfer]]
**2** (*history*) to [[exile]] (a criminal) to a penal colony
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[exile]], [[banish]], [[deport]]
**3** to have a strong emotional effect on: transported by joy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[enrapture]], [[move]], [[delight]], [[entrance]], [[enchant]], [[captivate]], ravish
 *n*
**4** the business or system of transporting goods or people: public transport
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[vehicle]], [[transportation]], [[conveyance]] (*old-fashioned*)
**5** (*Brit*) [[freight]] vehicles generally
**6** a [[vehicle]] used to transport troops
**7** a transporting or being transported
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[transference]], [[carrying]], [[delivery]], [[distribution]], [[transportation]], [[shipment]], [[freight]], [[haulage]]
**8** [[ecstasy]] or [[rapture]]: transports of [[delight]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ecstasy]], [[delight]], [[heaven]] (*informal*), [[bliss]], [[euphoria]], [[rapture]], [[enchantment]], [[ravishment]]
 **[[transportable]]** *adj*
