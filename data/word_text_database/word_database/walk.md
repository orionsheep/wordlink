**walk** *vb*
**1** to [[move]] on foot at a moderate rate with at least one foot always on the ground
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[travel on foot]]
**2** to pass through, on, or over on foot: to walk a short distance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stride]], [[stroll]], [[go]], [[move]], [[step]], [[march]], [[pace]], [[hike]]
**3** to walk somewhere with (a person or a dog)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[escort]], [[take]], [[see]], [[show]], [[partner]], [[guide]], [[conduct]], [[accompany]]
**4** **walking on air** very happy and excited
**5** **walk the streets** to wander about, esp. when looking for work or when homeless
 *n*
**6** a short journey on foot, usually for pleasure
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stroll]], [[hike]], [[ramble]], [[march]], [[trek]], [[trudge]], [[promenade]], [[saunter]]
**7** the action of walking rather than running
**8** a manner of walking: a proud slow walk
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[gait]], [[step]], [[bearing]], [[carriage]], [[tread]]
**9** a place or route for walking
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[path]], [[footpath]] (*Austral** & **NZ*), [[track]], [[way]], [[road]], [[lane]], [[trail]], [[avenue]], [[berm]] (*NZ*)
**10** **[[walk of life]]** social position or profession: people from all walks of life were drawn to her
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[walk of life]]**: area, calling, business, line, trade, class, field, career
 [[See]] also **walk into**, **walk out** etc
 **[[walker]]** *n*
