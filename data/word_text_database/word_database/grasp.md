**grasp** *vb*
**1** to [[grip]] firmly
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grip]], [[hold]], [[catch]], [[grab]], [[seize]], [[snatch]], [[clutch]], [[clinch]]
**2** to [[understand]]: his failure to grasp the gravity of the crisis
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[understand]], [[realize]], [[take in]], [[get]], [[see]], [[catch on]] (*informal*), [[comprehend]], [[catch]] *or* [[get]] the drift of
 *n*
**3** a very firm [[grip]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grip]], [[hold]], [[possession]], [[embrace]], [[clutches]], [[clasp]]
**4** [[understanding]] or [[comprehension]]: a good grasp of detail
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[understanding]], [[knowledge]], [[grip]], [[awareness]], [[mastery]], [[comprehension]]
**5** **within someone's grasp** almost certain to be accomplished or won: she now has that prize within her grasp
