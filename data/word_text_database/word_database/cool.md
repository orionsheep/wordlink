**cool** *adj*
**1** moderately [[cold]]: it should be served cool, even [[chilled]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cold]], [[chilled]], [[refreshing]], [[chilly]], [[nippy]]
**2** comfortably free of heat: it was one of the few cool days that summer
**3** [[calm]] and [[unemotional]]: a cool head
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[calm]], [[collected]], [[relaxed]], [[composed]], [[sedate]], [[self-controlled]], [[unruffled]], [[unemotional]], [[chilled]] (*informal*)
**4** [[indifferent]] or [[unfriendly]]: the idea met with a cool response
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[unfriendly]], [[distant]], [[indifferent]], [[aloof]], [[lukewarm]], [[offhand]], [[unenthusiastic]], [[unwelcoming]]
**5** calmly impudent
**6** (*informal*) (of a large sum of money) without exaggeration: a cool million
**7** (*informal*) sophisticated or elegant
**8** (of a colour) having violet, blue, or green predominating
**9** (*informal*) excellent; marvellous
 *vb*
**10** to make or become cooler
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lose heat]], [[cool off]]
**11** to [[calm]] down
 *n*
**12** [[coolness]]: in the cool of the evening
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[coldness]], [[chill]], [[coolness]]
**13** (*slang*) [[calmness]]; [[composure]]: he lost his cool and wantonly kicked the ball away
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[calmness]], [[control]], [[temper]], [[composure]], [[self-control]], [[poise]], [[self-discipline]], [[self-possession]]
 **[[coolly]]** *adv* 
 **[[coolness]]** *n*
