**feast** *n*
**1** a large and special meal for many people
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[banquet]], [[repast]], [[spread]] (*informal*), [[dinner]], [[treat]], [[hakari]] (*NZ*)
**2** something extremely pleasing: a feast of colour
**3** an annual religious [[celebration]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[festival]], [[holiday]], [[fête]], [[celebration]], [[holy day]], [[red-letter day]], [[religious festival]], saint's day
 *vb*
**4** to take part in a feast
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[eat your fill]], [[wine and dine]], [[overindulge]], [[consume]], [[indulge]], [[gorge]], [[devour]], [[pig out]] (*slang*)
**5** to give a feast to
**6** **feast on** to eat a large amount of: down come hundreds of vultures to feast on the remains
**7** **feast one's eyes on** to look at (someone or something) with a great deal of attention and pleasure
