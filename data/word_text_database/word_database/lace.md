**lace** *n*
**1** a delicate decorative fabric made from threads woven in an open web of patterns
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[netting]], [[net]], [[filigree]], [[meshwork]], [[openwork]]
**2** a [[cord]] or [[string]] drawn through eyelets to [[fasten]] a shoe or garment
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cord]], [[tie]], [[string]], [[lacing]], [[shoelace]], bootlace
 *vb* **[[lacing]]**, **[[laced]]**
**3** to [[fasten]] (shoes) with a lace
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[fasten]], [[tie]], [[tie up]], [[do up]], [[secure]], [[bind]], [[thread]]
**4** to draw (a [[cord]] or [[thread]]) through holes as when tying shoes
**5** to add a small amount of alcohol, a [[drug]], or poison to (food or drink)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[mix]], [[drug]], [[doctor]], [[add to]], [[spike]], [[contaminate]], [[fortify]], [[adulterate]]
**6** to [[intertwine]]; interlace
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[intertwine]], [[interweave]], [[entwine]], [[twine]], [[interlink]]
