**flatter** *vb*
**1** to [[praise]] insincerely, esp. in order to win favour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[praise]], [[compliment]], [[pander to]], [[sweet-talk]] (*informal*), [[wheedle]], [[soft-soap]] (*informal*), [[butter up]]
**2** to [[show to advantage]]: she wore a simple green cotton dress which she knew flattered her
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[suit]], [[become]], [[enhance]], [[set off]], [[embellish]], [[do something for]], [[show to advantage]]
**3** to make (a person) appear more attractive than in reality: a portrait that flattered him
**4** to cater to the vanity of (a person): I was flattered by her [[praise]]
**5** **flatter oneself** to believe, perhaps mistakenly, something good about oneself
 **[[flatterer]]** *n*
