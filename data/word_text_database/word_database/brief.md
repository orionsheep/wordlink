**brief** *adj*
**1** [[short]] in duration
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[short]], [[quick]], [[fleeting]], [[swift]], [[short-lived]], [[momentary]], [[ephemeral]], [[transitory]]
**2** [[short]] in length or extent; scanty: a brief bikini
**3** terse or concise
 *n*
**4** a condensed statement or written synopsis
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[summary]], [[résumé]], [[outline]], [[sketch]], [[abstract]], [[digest]], [[epitome]], [[rundown]]
**5** (*law*) a document containing all the facts and points of law of a case by which a solicitor instructs a barrister to represent a client
**6** (*RC Church*) a papal letter that is less formal than a bull
**7** Also called: **briefing** instructions
**8** **hold a brief for** to argue for; champion
**9** **in brief** in [[short]]; to sum up
 *vb*
**10** to [[prepare]] or [[instruct]] (someone) by giving a [[summary]] of relevant facts
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[inform]], [[prime]], [[prepare]], [[advise]], [[fill in]] (*informal*), [[instruct]], [[put in the picture]] (*informal*), keep (someone) posted
**11** (*English law*)
**A** to [[instruct]] (a barrister) by brief
 **B** to retain (a barrister) as counsel
 **[[briefly]]** *adv*
