**help** *vb*
**1** to [[assist]] (someone to do something)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[aid]], [[support]], [[assist]], [[cooperate with]], [[abet]], [[lend a hand]], [[succour]]
**2** to contribute to: to help Latin America's economies
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[aid]], [[support]], [[assist]], [[cooperate with]], [[abet]], [[lend a hand]], [[succour]]
**3** to [[improve]] a situation: a felt or rubber underlay will help
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[improve]], [[ease]], [[relieve]], [[facilitate]], [[alleviate]], [[mitigate]], [[ameliorate]]
**4**
**A** to [[refrain from]]: I couldn't help feeling foolish
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[resist]], [[refrain from]], [[avoid]], [[prevent]], keep from
 **B** to be responsible for: you must not blame him, [[he simply can't help it]]
**5** to serve (a customer)
**6** **help oneself** to take something, esp. food or drink, for oneself, without being served
 *n*
**7** the act of helping
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[assistance]], [[aid]], [[support]], [[advice]], [[guidance]], [[cooperation]], [[helping hand]]
**8** a person or thing that helps, esp. a farm worker or domestic servant
**9** a remedy: there's no help for it
 *interj*
**10** used to call for [[assistance]]
