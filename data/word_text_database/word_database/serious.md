**serious** *adj*
**1** giving cause for concern: the situation is serious
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[grave]], [[bad]], [[critical]], [[dangerous]], [[acute]], [[severe]]
**2** concerned with [[important]] matters: there are some serious questions that need to be answered
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[important]], [[crucial]] (*informal*), [[urgent]], [[pressing]], [[worrying]], [[significant]], [[grim]], [[momentous]]
**3** not cheerful; [[grave]]: I am a serious person
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[solemn]], [[earnest]], [[grave]], [[sober]], [[staid]], [[humourless]], [[unsmiling]]
**4** [[in earnest]]; [[sincere]]: he believes we are serious
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sincere]], [[earnest]], [[genuine]], [[honest]], [[in earnest]]
**5** requiring concentration: a serious book
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[thoughtful]], [[detailed]], [[careful]], [[deep]], [[profound]], [[in-depth]]
**6** (*informal*) impressive because of its substantial quantity or quality: serious money
 **[[seriously]]** *adv* 
 **[[seriousness]]** *n*
