**put up** *vb*
**1** to build or erect: I want to put up a fence round the garden
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[put something up]]**: build, raise, set up, construct, erect, fabricate
**2** to display (a poster, sign, etc.)
**3** to accommodate or be accommodated at: can you put me up for tonight?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[put someone up]]**: accommodate, house, board, lodge, quarter, take someone in, billet
**4** to increase (prices)
**5** to submit (a plan, case, etc.)
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[put something up]]**: offer, present, mount, put forward
**6** to offer: the factory is being put up for sale
**7** to give: they put up a good fight
**8** to provide (money) for: they put up 35% of the film's budget
**9** to nominate or be nominated as a candidate: the party have yet to decide whether to put up a candidate
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[put someone up]]**: nominate, put forward, offer, present, propose, recommend, submit
**10** **put up to** to incite to: I wonder who put them up to it?
**11** **put up with** (*informal*) to endure or tolerate
 *adj* **put-up**
**12** (*informal*) dishonestly or craftily prearranged: a put-up job
