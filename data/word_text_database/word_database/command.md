**command** *vb*
**1** to [[order]] or [[compel]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[order]], [[tell]], [[charge]] (*formal*), [[demand]], [[require]], [[direct]], [[bid]], [[compel]]
**2** to [[have authority over]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[have authority over]], [[lead]], [[head]], [[control]], [[rule]], [[manage]], [[handle]], [[dominate]]
**3** to deserve and get: a public figure who commands almost universal respect
**4** to look down over: the house commands a magnificent view of the sea and the islands
 *n*
**5** an authoritative [[instruction]] that something must be done
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[order]], [[demand]], [[instruction]], [[dictate]], [[requirement]], [[decree]], [[directive]], [[ultimatum]], [[commandment]]
**6** the [[authority]] to command
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[management]], [[power]], [[control]], [[charge]], [[authority]], [[supervision]]
**7** knowledge; [[control]]: a fluent command of French
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[domination]], [[control]], [[rule]], [[mastery]], [[power]], [[government]]
**8** a military or naval unit with a specific function
**9** (*computers*) a part of a program consisting of a coded [[instruction]] to the computer to perform a specified function
