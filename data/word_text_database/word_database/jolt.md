**jolt** *n*
**1** a severe [[shock]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[surprise]], [[blow]], [[shock]], [[setback]], [[bombshell]], [[bolt from the blue]]
**2** a sudden violent movement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jerk]], [[start]], [[jump]], [[shake]], [[bump]], [[jar]], [[jog]], lurch
 *vb*
**3** to [[surprise]] or [[shock]]: he was momentarily jolted by the news
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[surprise]], [[stun]], [[disturb]], [[stagger]], [[startle]], [[perturb]], [[discompose]]
**4** to [[bump]] against (someone or something) with a sudden violent movement
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[jerk]], [[push]], [[shake]], [[knock]], [[jar]], [[shove]], [[jog]], [[jostle]]
**5** to move in a jerking manner
