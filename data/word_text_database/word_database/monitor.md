**monitor** *n*
**1** a person or device that warns, checks, controls, or keeps a continuous record of something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[guide]], [[observer]], [[supervisor]], [[invigilator]]
**2** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ*) a pupil assisting a teacher with various duties
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[prefect]] (*Brit*), [[head girl]], [[head boy]], [[senior boy]], [[senior girl]]
**3** a screen used to display certain kinds of information, for example in airports or television studios
**4** a large predatory lizard inhabiting warm regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia
 *vb*
**5** to act as a monitor of
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[check]], [[follow]], [[watch]], [[survey]], [[observe]], [[keep an eye on]], [[keep track of]], [[keep tabs on]]
**6** to [[observe]] or record the condition or performance of a person or thing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[check]], [[follow]], [[watch]], [[survey]], [[observe]], [[keep an eye on]], [[keep track of]], [[keep tabs on]]
**7** to [[check]] a broadcast for acceptable quality or content
 **[[monitorial]]** *adj*
