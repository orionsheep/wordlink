**tune** *n*
**1** a [[melody]], esp. one for which [[harmony]] is not essential
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[melody]], [[air]], [[song]], [[theme]], [[strain]](s), [[jingle]], [[ditty]], [[choon]] (*slang*)
**2** the correct musical [[pitch]]: many of the notes are out of tune
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[harmony]], [[pitch]], [[euphony]]
**3** **call the tune** to be in control of the proceedings
**4** **change one's tune** to alter one's attitude or tone of speech
**5** **in *or* out of tune with** in *or* not in agreement or sympathy with: in tune with public opinion
**6** **to the tune of** (*informal*) to the amount or extent of
 *vb* **tuning**, **tuned**
**7** to [[adjust]] (a musical instrument) so each string, key, etc., produces the right note
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tune up]], [[adjust]]
**8** to make small adjustments to (an engine, machine, etc.) to obtain the proper or desired performance
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[regulate]], [[adapt]], [[modulate]], [[harmonize]], [[attune]], [[pitch]]
**9** to [[adjust]] (a radio or television) to receive a particular station or programme: the radio was tuned to the local station
 **[[tuner]]** *n*
