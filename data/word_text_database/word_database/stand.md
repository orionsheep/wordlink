**stand** *vb* **standing**, **stood**
**1** to [[be upright]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be upright]], [[be erect]], [[be vertical]]
**2** to [[rise]] to an upright [[position]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[get to your feet]], [[rise]], [[stand up]], [[straighten up]]
**3** to [[place]] (something) upright
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[put]], [[place]], [[position]], [[set]], [[mount]]
**4** to [[be]] situated: the property stands in a prime [[position]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be located]], [[be]], [[sit]], [[be positioned]], [[be]] situated *or* located
**5** to have a specified height when standing: the structure stands sixty feet above the river
**6** to [[be]] in a specified [[position]]: Turkey stands to gain handsomely
**7** to [[be]] in a specified state or condition: how he stands in comparison to others
**8** to remain unchanged or valid: the Conservatives were forced to let much of the legislation stand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[be valid]], [[continue]], [[exist]], [[prevail]], [[remain valid]]
**9** **stand at** (of a score or an account) to [[be]] in the specified [[position]]: now the total stands at nine
**10** to [[tolerate]] or [[bear]]: Christopher can't stand him
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[tolerate]], [[bear]], [[abide]], [[stomach]], [[endure]], [[brook]]
**11** to survive: stand the test of time
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[resist]], [[endure]], [[tolerate]], [[stand up to]]
**12** (often foll. by *for*) to [[be]] a candidate: to stand for president
**13** (*informal*) to buy: to stand someone a drink
**14** **stand a chance** to have a chance of succeeding
**15** **stand one's ground** to maintain a [[position]] in the face of opposition
**16** **stand to reason** to [[be]] obvious or logical: it stands to reason you will play better
**17** **stand trial** to [[be]] tried in a law court
 *n*
**18** a [[stall]] or counter selling goods: the hot dog stand
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[stall]], [[booth]], [[kiosk]], [[table]]
**19** a structure at a sports ground where people can [[sit]] or stand
**20** the act or an instance of standing
**21** a firmly held [[opinion]]: its firm stand on sanctions
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[position]], [[attitude]], [[stance]], [[opinion]], [[determination]]
**22** (*US** & **Austral*) a [[place]] in a law court where a witness stands
**23** a rack on which coats and hats may [[be]] hung
**24** a base, support, or piece of furniture in or on which articles may [[be]] held or stored: a guitar stand
**25** an effort to defend oneself or one's beliefs against attack or criticism: a last stand against superior forces
**26** (*cricket*) a long period at the wicket by two batsmen
**27** See **one-night stand**
