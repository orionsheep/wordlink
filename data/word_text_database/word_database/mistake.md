**mistake** *n*
**1** an [[error]] or [[blunder]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[error]], [[blunder]], [[oversight]], [[slip]], [[gaffe]] (*informal*), [[miscalculation]], [[faux pas]], barry *or* Barry Crocker (*Austral* *slang*)
**2** a misconception or misunderstanding
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[oversight]], [[error]], [[slip]], [[fault]], [[howler]] (*informal*), [[erratum]], barry *or* Barry Crocker (*Austral* *slang*)
 *vb* **-taking**, **-took**, **-taken**
**3** to [[misunderstand]] or [[misinterpret]]: the chaplain quite mistook her meaning
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[misunderstand]], [[misinterpret]], [[misjudge]], [[misread]], [[misconstrue]], [[misapprehend]]
**4** (foll. by *for*) to confuse a person or thing with another: they saw the HMS Sheffield and mistook her for the Bismarck
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small> **[[mistake something or someone for something or someone]]**: confuse with, take for, mix up with
**5** to choose badly or incorrectly: he mistook his path
