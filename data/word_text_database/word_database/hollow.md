**hollow** *adj*
**1** having a [[hole]] or space within; not solid: a hollow tree
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[empty]], [[vacant]], [[void]], [[unfilled]]
**2** curving inwards: hollow cheeks
**3** (of sounds) as if echoing in a hollow place
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[dull]], [[low]], [[deep]], [[muted]], [[toneless]], [[reverberant]]
**4** without any real value or worth: a hollow enterprise, lacking purpose, and lacking soul
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[worthless]], [[useless]], [[vain]], [[meaningless]], [[pointless]], [[futile]], fruitless
 *adv*
**5** **beat someone hollow** (*Brit** & **NZ* *informal*) to defeat someone thoroughly
 *n*
**6** a [[cavity]] or space in something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cavity]], [[hole]], [[bowl]], [[depression]], [[pit]], [[basin]], [[crater]], [[trough]]
**7** a dip in the land
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[valley]], [[dale]], [[glen]], [[dell]], dingle
 *vb*
**8** (often foll. by *out*) to form a [[hole]] or [[cavity]] in
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[scoop out]], [[dig out]], [[excavate]], [[gouge out]]
 **[[hollowly]]** *adv*
