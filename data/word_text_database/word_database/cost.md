**cost** *n*
**1** the [[amount]] of money, time, or energy required to obtain or produce something
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[price]], [[worth]], [[expense]], [[charge]], [[damage]] (*informal*), [[amount]], [[payment]], [[outlay]]
**2** [[suffering]] or sacrifice: these were crucial truths which rugby never grasped, to its cost
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[loss]], [[suffering]], [[damage]], [[injury]], [[penalty]], [[hurt]], [[expense]], [[harm]]
**3** the [[amount]] paid for a commodity by its seller: to [[sell at]] cost
**4** **costs** (*law*) the expenses of a lawsuit
**5** **at all costs** regardless of any cost or effort involved
**6** **at the cost of** at the [[expense]] of losing: they eventually triumphed, but at the cost of many lives
 *vb* **costing**, **cost**
**7** to be obtained or obtainable in exchange for: calls cost 45p a minute
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[sell at]], [[come to]], set (someone) back (*informal*), [[be priced at]], [[command a price of]]
**8** to involve the [[loss]] or sacrifice of: a fall which almost cost him his life
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lose]], [[deprive of]], [[cheat of]]
**9** **costing**, **costed** to estimate the cost of producing (something)
