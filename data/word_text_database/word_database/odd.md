**odd** *adj*
**1** [[unusual]] or [[peculiar]]: his increasingly odd behaviour
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[peculiar]], [[strange]], [[unusual]], [[extraordinary]], [[bizarre]], [[offbeat]], [[freakish]], [[daggy]] (*Austral** & **NZ* *informal*)
**2** [[occasional]] or [[incidental]]: the odd letter from a friend abroad, the [[occasional]] postcard from a chum
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[occasional]], [[various]], [[random]], [[casual]], [[irregular]], [[periodic]], [[sundry]], [[incidental]]
**3** [[leftover]] or additional: we use up odd pieces of fabric to make up jerseys in wild designs
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[spare]], [[remaining]], [[extra]], [[surplus]], [[solitary]], [[leftover]], [[unmatched]], [[unpaired]]
**4** (of a number) not divisible by two
**5** being part of a pair or set when the other or others are missing: the drawer was full of odd socks
**6** somewhat more than the round number specified: I had known him for the past twenty-odd years
**7** **odd man *or* one out** a person or thing excluded from others forming a group or unit
 See also **odds**
