**succeed** *vb*
**1** to achieve an aim
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[triumph]], [[win]], [[prevail]]
**2** to turn out satisfactorily: Grandfather's plan succeeded
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[work out]], [[work]], [[be successful]]
**3** to [[do well]] in a specified field: how to succeed in show biz
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[make it]] (*informal*), [[do well]], [[be successful]], [[triumph]], [[thrive]], [[flourish]], [[make good]], [[prosper]]
**4** to come next in order after (someone or something): the first shock had been succeeded by a different kind of gloom
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[follow]], [[come after]], [[follow after]]
**5** to take over (a position) from (someone): Henry VIII succeeded to the throne in 1509; he will be succeeded as president by his deputy
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[take over from]], [[assume the office of]]
 **[[succeeding]]** *adj*
