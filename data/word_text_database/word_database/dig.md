**dig** *vb* **digging**, **dug**
**1** to cut into, break up, and [[turn over]] or remove (earth), esp. with a spade
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[turn over]]
**2** to [[excavate]] (a hole or tunnel) by digging, usually with an implement or (of animals) with claws
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[hollow out]], [[mine]], [[quarry]], [[excavate]], [[scoop out]]
**3** to obtain by digging: dig out potatoes
**4** to find by effort or searching: she dug out a torch from her bag
**5** (*informal*) to like or understand
**6** (foll. by *in* or *into*) to [[thrust]] or [[jab]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[poke]], [[drive]], [[push]], [[stick]], [[punch]], [[stab]], [[thrust]], shove
 *n*
**7** the act of digging
**8** an archaeological excavation
**9** a [[thrust]] or [[poke]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[poke]], [[thrust]], [[nudge]], [[prod]], [[jab]], [[punch]]
**10** a [[cutting remark]]
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[cutting remark]], [[crack]] (*slang*), [[insult]], [[taunt]], [[sneer]], [[jeer]], [[barb]], [[wisecrack]] (*informal*)
