**spoil** *vb* **spoiling**, **spoilt*** or ***spoiled**
**1** to make (something) less valuable, beautiful, or useful
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[ruin]], [[destroy]], [[wreck]], [[damage]], [[injure]], [[harm]], [[mar]], [[trash]] (*slang*), crool *or* cruel (*Austral* *slang*)
**2** to weaken the character of (a child) by giving it all it wants
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[overindulge]], [[indulge]], [[pamper]], [[cosset]], [[coddle]], [[mollycoddle]]
**3** (of oneself) to [[indulge]] one's desires: go ahead and spoil yourself
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[indulge]], [[pamper]], [[satisfy]], [[gratify]], [[pander to]]
**4** (of food) to become unfit for consumption
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[go bad]], [[turn]], [[go off]] (*Brit* *informal*), [[rot]], [[decay]], [[decompose]], [[curdle]], [[addle]]
**5** **be spoiling for** to have an aggressive urge for: he is spoiling for a fight
