**want** *vb*
**1** to feel a [[need]] or [[longing]] for: I want a job
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wish for]], [[desire]], [[long for]], [[crave]], [[covet]], [[hope for]], [[yearn for]], [[thirst for]]
**2** to [[wish]] or [[desire]] (to do something): we did not want to get involved
**3** (*Brit** & **Austral** & **NZ*) to have [[need]] of or [[require]] (doing or being something): what [[will]] you do when it wants cleaning?
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[need]], [[demand]], [[require]], [[call for]]
**4** [[should]] or [[ought]] (to do something): the last person you want to hire is someone who is desperate for a job
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[should]], [[need]], [[must]], [[ought]]
**5** **want for** to be lacking or deficient in: they were convinced I was wealthy and wanted for nothing
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lack]], [[need]], [[require]], miss
 *n*
**6** something that is needed, desired, or lacked: attempts to satisfy a number of wants
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[wish]], [[will]], [[need]], [[desire]], [[requirement]], [[longing]], [[appetite]], [[craving]]
**7** a [[lack]], [[shortage]], or [[absence]]: for want of opportunity
◇ <small>SYNONYMS</small>: [[lack]], [[need]], [[absence]], [[shortage]], [[deficiency]], [[famine]], [[scarcity]], [[dearth]]
**8** **in want of** needing or lacking: the Chinese peasant farmer may be in want of a roof, a job, a doctor nearby
